3 decades of VR headset expertise, multiple issued and pending patents, unique lens design, mounts any Android, Apple, HTC, Samsung

Shown here, the VR mount for this Kickstarter project: "The Gen 5". This image shows the prototype without the lens inserts and with the Galaxy S2 back mount. You can change out lenses with the Gen 5". You can change out cell phones with the Gen 5. Get a new back mount or get new lenses and swap them out when you need to. It is designed for hot swap lenses. Ideal lenses are the stacked Fresnel flat stamp 70-120 degree or the Erfle 65 degree lens, or the Plano Convex 92/95 degree lens. These lenses, or lens sets, can be purchased from various suppliers online for less than $30.00.

The phone slides into the back mount and is held in place by side clips on the back mount. You use your existing earphones or phone speaker for sound. Most smart phones have built-in position sensing.

Anybody that can program to an iPhone or an Android can program games or experiences that output their video in either side-by-side stereoscopy or same-image-both-eyes set-up. Existing games and content can be converted or you can write conversion utilities.

You can hot dip the whole mount in truck bed coating or black electrical tape-it for various amounts of blackout/immersion of the unit. (A famous game company spent millions on legal research to determine that due to past litigation from users of other gaming VR headsets from other companies, not ours, one cannot legally sell you a fully blacked-out headset mount.) You choose your safest blackout/immersion level based on your use and safety parameters.

Over 25 years of VR and wearable computer experience. Issued and filed patents dating to 1990 and back. Over 19 designs for headsets produced and delivered to clients including the first glasses-based computer.

The mount does not have an IPD (the distance between the center of each eye) adjust but you can adjust for your own IPD when you mount your lenses.

The resolution is contingent on your phone. Get the highest resolution phone you can find for the highest resolution experience and keep upgrading to keep your experience photo-realistic.

FAQ

  • What if a competing effort wants to buy your company or project before the project timeframe runs out?

    We would sell for the right price, in that case all Kickstarter participants for this program will either get a refund, a headset or a coupon of equal value for a product from the purchasing company

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 12:38 pm EDT
  • The Nintendo and Sega VR headsets had substantial legal liability research that they contracted and engaged in studies around. Were you able to take those studies into consideration?

    Yes.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 12:41 pm EDT
  • How is this better than other headsets?

    - You save hundreds of dollars.
    - You can use the phone you already paid for.
    - You can swap out and upgrade phones, with new back mounts, to get constantly improving resolution.
    - You can swap out lenses to get different immersion effects.
    - You can use your favorite headphones that you already own.
    - By using your phone the headset is already a networked VR device.
    - Most programmers already know how to program to IOS or Android.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 12:46 pm EDT
  • What should I know?

    - You have to provide the phone.
    - The first GEN5 comes with a back mount that fits the Galaxy S2 or any phone with the same external envelope shape as the Galaxy S2.
    - You have to provide the lenses but there are now a large number of "how to" sites to source those up for you.
    - Don't use VR to get high. That will often end up getting you sick. Do not use VR if you have photo-image triggered neurological issues.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 12:49 pm EDT
  • Will you be helping any community projects?

    We always encourage support of anti-cyber-bully programs like: http://www.cyberbullies.net

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 12:51 pm EDT
  • Can I tape or rubber band a bigger phone on the Galaxy 2 back mount before you get bigger back mounts made?

    You can do what ever you want with the mount once you have it. We rubber band a Galaxy Note 2 on the Galaxy S2 one and the Field of View really rocks out then.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 12:54 pm EDT
  • How much software is around?

    VR has been around since 1989, there has been a large amount of side-by-side stereoscopy game and experience code written for it and many utilities to convert. Go hunt up the old stuff and encourage the developers to port, use the new stuff that is coming out and, if you code, write some. Android and IOS is pretty easy to program too and you can also go to 3D websites with the browser on your phone.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 12:58 pm EDT
  • Does the top-of-head gravity offset tab come with the unit?

    Not initially. We have found that most people do not need the top of the head flap with the Galaxy S2. If you mount a heaver phone like a Note 2 or larger you may need one but it depends on your head, how tight you set the headstrap and other things. We intend to offer the top-of-head flap as an add-on later.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 1:01 pm EDT
  • Do I have to take the phone out of it's case to slide it in the back-mount?

    In the instance of the Galaxy S2, Yes.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 1:03 pm EDT
  • How long can I stay in a VR experience?

    It depends on your biology but most experts say that you should "go in" (To the VR World) for 15 minutes and out (into the real world) for 30 minutes. It will vary per user. Check with your general doctor and your eye doctor if you have any concerns. Military tests have kept people "in" for up to a week at a time but they were carefully monitored. Of course, in "The Matrix", they keep you "in" for the rest of your life but we won't do that to you.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 1:06 pm EDT
  • So if I buy this and black out the transparent plastic parts it will be super immersive?

    Yes. You are on your own if you do that. Going super-immersive, or "all the way in", can have compelling and/or dangerous effects. You might walk off a balcony or fall down stairs or walk in front of a car. Wear a seat belt in a chair if you go all the way in. You may not be able to tell where you are in the real world if the software is done well. Emotional connection with synthetic danger or attractive partners can have higher density than when you experienced it on a flat screen TV. As in real life: physical, neurological and emotional danger is enhanced in an "all the way in" VR experience with effective software. Use VR with caution and never while under the influence of anything. "Deepers", or those who go all the way in on a regular basis, often sit in the back seat of a car with the windows open, for fresh air, and the seat belt on for safety. This enhances the effect for driving or flying games. Never sit in the drivers seat as you might accidently release the brake.

    Last updated: Mon, Sep 9 2013 1:19 pm EDT
  • You have lots of warnings posted. Other headset projects don't have as many warnings posted. Why not?

    Nintendo, Sega, and others have spent many, many millions of dollars researching liability issues with wearables. Some of our team have spent decades developing these devices for games, defense and research projects. If another headset maker does not have AT LEAST this many advisories and warnings then they probably don't know what they are doing. If it touches your body, you have to let people know how it might affect their body.

    Last updated: Wed, Sep 11 2013 3:45 pm EDT

RHETTS is much more fun, much more exciting than all other word games due to its unique ways to form words and score points!

What is RHETTS?

Rhetts is a new word game in which the purpose of the game is the win the most points by forming words and by finding incorrect words in other players’ hands. Rhetts is based on the word “Rhetoric”. The game was solely created by Barry Tenser and was U.S. Copyrighted in 1989. Rhetts uses 108 alphabet letters, 52 red letters A-Z, 52 black letters A-Z, 4 of each letter total, and 2 red and 2 black blank wild card letters. A PDF download link to the original 1989 rules is available just above the copyright information which is further down on this webpage.

Why is Rhetts better and much more fun than all other word games?

There are more ways and unique ways to make words and to score points in Rhetts. These major differences make Rhetts much more enjoyable and exciting than all other word games along with being more enjoyable then many other non-word games as well.

Rhetts has alphabet letters which are much bigger, much brighter, much easier to see, and much easier to work with than those tiny tile word games and other word games too.

Rhett’s 2-color letter system is best, since many existing one color word systems can be dull and boring, and 3 or more colors would be an overly difficult complicated mess.

The score sheet below was designed in 1989 before I ever had a computer. Peel off sheet letters were partially used. That is why the word "RHETTS" appears slightly slanted.

Besides being a very fun and enjoyable word game for everybody, it is my strong belief that the Rhetts word game also has the potential to be a way to motivate children to learn more outside of the game.

Having a good vocabulary base and knowing proper spelling are needed to play well and to win in Rhetts. This knowledge of the language is also needed for the much more important life task of writing. Along with reading, writing is also a very important skill and part of learning, to communicate well and to succeed in life.

If playing Rhetts indirectly improves a child's vocabulary base, then that same child's vocabulary base will be improved for writing. With that in mind, I also believe that playing Rhetts could result in the increased motivation to write better and write more often.

Please consider, explore, and support the potential, multifaceted, learning motivations of Rhetts which could benefit children and society.

Playing the Rhetts game would also be a good influence & safe activity to replace any existing bad influences & harmful activities in some children's lives which would benefit society.

What else is in this future word game website?

The game software will be designed to be played on desktop computers, smart phones, and tablets. 3-day free trial members will have unlimited 24/7 play with up to 4 rounds of play. Paid members will have 24/7 unlimited access with up to 30 rounds of play. The game will be designed for 2-4 players. If a player does not finish word forming their words and the timer runs out, then the game will automatically submit any and all formed words up to that point. There will be virtual “rooms” in which a player can host a room or visit a room that is already hosted. The host has the ability to set the number of rounds, set the word making time limit per round, and to accept or deny other players from visiting the room. All players will start with a skill rating of 1500. If they win, their rating goes up and if they lose, their rating goes down. The rock bottom lowest rating a player can drop to is 700. Even if that player keeps losing after a rating of 700, there will not be any more reductions in rating points and no ratings less than 700 will occur. Ratings are needed so players can know if they are of similar playing skills or not. As just one example, it is good as to prevent a beginner from unknowingly playing an expert. If a low rating player beats a high rating player, then the weaker player will gain many rating points while the high rating player’s rating will drop many points. If a stronger player beats a weaker player, then the stronger player will only gain very few rating points and the weaker player may only lose 1 or 2 points. A game can also be played without changing anyone's rating which is called an unrated game. The room host can also determine if the game is rated or unrated before the game starts. Paid players will also have the option to save unfinished games in case someone has to leave the game and only if all players agree. There will also be a solitaire game which will be good for players to improve their skills. Before the game, players can pick 1, 1.5, 2, 2.5 and 3 minute time limits for total word making time before submitting their words for each round matchup. The host of the game can also select the number of rounds of play from 2-30 before the game begins. If one player challenges a word of another player, then the game software will immediately use its updated dictionary database to determine if the word is correct, in which it must exist as a word and it must be spelled correctly. Slang words and proper names do not count. A successful challenge will give the challenger a 10 point bonus while making all words of the challenged player in that round equaling -10 points. All players have up to 10 seconds to decide to challenge any other players’ word or not. Players may only challenge just one word from all players per round. There will be an option for online communication chat between players that can be turned on and off at any time by each player. Socializing is not a requirement to play. Any player may block any other players' communications for any reason. Abusive and harassing online chat is prohibited and reportable to the main office which can lead to the termination of service of the proven abusive player. Paid players may quit the service and terminate credit card billing for any reason whatsoever. People will also have the option to pay by credit card, check or money order for an entire year of unlimited service. Prepaying for the year cost less than monthly billing ($50 vs $60). Paid players can change their billing plan at any time.

FAQ

  • Why was the Rhetts game not shown and not sold to the public since 1989?

    Just months after inventing Rhetts in 1989, my life took on unexpected major life changes, problems and difficulties involving private matters that finally all ended in 2003. During those years, my game had no choice but to be put on indefinite hold and put in long term cold storage.

    By 2003, America was fully in a new widespread computer and internet age. My non-computer word game had a lot less value, much less demand and much less appeal in this new computer dominant gaming market. Then, there were very few software developers then there are today, and it would have cost about $200K to have the Rhetts game turned into an internet software game. I could not afford such a huge investment and the game had to remain in cold storage. As time went on, the number of software developers increased worldwide, resulting in the cost of software development going down, recently just costing about $60K to computerize the Rhetts game, though still too expensive for my budget. In 2013, I learned of the Kickstarter website and saw this as a means to get the very expensive funding required to get the Rhetts game computerized and put into the market for all people to enjoy.

    The 1989 Rhetts game was never sold in the market place. This is the first time it is being introduced to the general public.

    Last updated: Tue, Jul 9 2013 1:18 pm EDT
  • Why are there 2 long word making boxes instead of just one?

    Since larger letters would be easier to view and work with, larger word boxes were needed to fit them. This was also done for people who have had difficulty and unpleasant experiences working with other word games that have small letters.

    There is no word forming during the matchup, so the letters are smaller so claimed horizontal words of 4 players can fit on the screen.

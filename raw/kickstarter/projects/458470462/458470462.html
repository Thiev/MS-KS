FMC is a 3rd person mech RPG where you fight against robot monsters and other mechs in a competition for the good of your nation.

What Is FMC?  

FMC is an action RPG shooter set in a futuristic world where competition style combat has replaced war. You play as one of three main characters, each from a different nation with their own unique back story. But they all have the same goal for their own reasons. You must climb the ranks of the competition until you can challenge and defeat the number one ranked competitor and replace him for the sake of your nation. The competition itself takes place in a purpose built floating city constructed out of the strongest materials available so it can width stand the constant conflict between the competitor's Rigs. The ending of the game is a surprise not to be missed that only the best will survive.   

The Game Story 

The game is set in a post WW3 world where the war had lasted for many generations and changed the way the world worked. The war eventually became the way the nation would resolve and issue. If they wanted a natural resource they would fight over it. If the other nation did something they disagreed with they would attack on of there regions till they stoped of were defeated themselves. After many generation of fighting between the 3 great nation and all the small nation that got caught up in the war, the nation of Light who had stayed neutral in the war and were secretly working on military tech entered the war and brought it to an end by force. Once they had displayed there superior power they were able to force the nation to stop all conflicts and instead compete with each other in non lethal combat is a city they had built to replace the war. The city of Kronos was from then on where the nations fought with each other to decide world matters. This city came with a long list of rules and order which allowed the nations with the strongest competitors to shape the world outside. You start the game as 1 of 3 ex-military officers who have been given the mission to rise to the top ranks for your own reasons and depending on who you choose you will interact with people in the game from your past in different ways. 

Game Play 

Core Mechanic: 

At its core the game is an action shooter where you get to create a custom mech Rig that suits your personal play style and run around fighting AAUs (monsters) and other competitors in a large purpose built city. As your character gets stronger you can unlock abilities and items that you can use to do a variety of different things from capturing AAUs and using them as your personal army or give your Rig the ability to go Super Saiyan 3. The catch of most of the high end power ups being large power drain, which is where power management is important. Or if you want you can just put everything in to your weapons and armour and shoot your way through the game. It is all up to the player how they would like to play. Since we are working with Rigs here you can of course change up the way you want to play at any given time and if you want to spend the time planning you can configure your Rig to best suit not only yourself but the quest you are going on. 

 Quest System: 

The quest system divides up quests in to 4 different groups. 

 -The first is 'Jobs' and you can get jobs by talking to people or looking at the job board in the bar. Jobs are simple tasks that are ether created by the city or other competitors, they can be anything from defeat a certain competitor for revenge or collect an item that someone wants. All job tasks that are completed have a reward of money. 

- The second type are 'Missions' which you can find on the mission board in your nation's house. Missions are arena matches between two nations to resolve a real world dispute and are most commonly 1 vs 1. Once you accept a mission you are taken to the arena to fight your opponent, if you win then you get a prize of money and your patriot score goes up. However if you lose your patriot score goes down and can become negative. Your partiot score affects how other people from your nation interact from you, if it is high they will help you but if it is to low they will not even talk to you. 

-The third type are 'Quests' which can be unlocked by talking to people, finding special objects or wondering in to a quest zone. Quests are large tasks that require not just skill to complete but stamina as well. Quests can be anything from fight your way up the tower and defeat the Dragon King to find all the keys to unlock the grand vault. In most cases you can attempt a quest multiple times but not always and if you complete a quest you can never do it again. This is very important to remember for quests where at the end there is a choice of what prize you want or a large collection of items you can take away, because if you don't have the space to take it with you, you cannot go back for it. Completing a quest can also give you money or unlock a skill. 

-The last type is 'Rites of Passage', unlike the others rites of passage are always known to you and are the same for all three player options. Rites of passage are all contest based meaning you need to fight to win. They range from fighting the Bridge Guardians for the right to move between Districts to fighting all 7 of the Guardians of Kronos for the prize of picking which of their weapons you would like to have. All rights of passage unlock something in the game that you could not do before. Be it travel to places or unlock skills or get access to special abilities. Some rites of passage like the bridge guardians need to be done to be able to complete the game but most are optional. 

One last thing, an important thing to remember in this game is that just because you have been given the option to attempt something does not always mean that you are currently strong enough to do it. 

 Skill System:

As FMC is a mech based game the skill system is a simple streamlined one and how you improve a skill is the same way you would in real life and that is by using it. If you wanted to it is possible to unlock every skill there is but that would take a lot of time and force you to play in a way they would not necessarily feel right to you. A common theme trough out the game design is not to unlock the best items and skills but to find the ones that suit your play style the best. Every skill needs to be unlocked to start using it and this is done by finding loot chests which can be dropped by AAUs. Once you find a skill you can use it and every time you kill an AAU or Competitor while using that skill its skill points will increase until it maxes out. As its level goes up it will unlock other skills until every skill in that line is unlocked. This action takes no input from the player it all happens automatically. 

Special Items: 

Special items are things that you can use in your Rig to do a variety of things like healing yourself, repairing your mech, giving your mech a power up or even capturing disabled AAUs to latter use as your reinforcements. 

 Rig: 

The competitors RIgs are made up of 5 seperate body parts that you can mix and match to create your Rig. They are the head, torso, legs, right arm and left arm. Each body part has weight, armour and possible weapon slots. They can also have resistances to damage types or other power up bonuses. Depending on the players Rank and the parts they have on their Rig they can carry anywhere from 1 to 9 weapons at a time. Weapons come in a variety of sizes which will affect where you can holster them and they can be ether hand held or mounted. Hand held weapons are the most common in the game and you can use a max of 2 at any given time but you can use as many mounted weapons as you want plus 2 hand guns at the same time. That means that if you were able to find the right parts you could in theory fire 8 weapons at once. The last thing you can attach to your rig is specialty items, you have 5 spots on your Rig where you can attach items and no items do not stack. 

AU: 

AU stands for assault unit, this is a non Rig mech that is still piloted by a human. The AUs are piloted by people from the nation of Light and are most commonly part of a rite of passage. For example Sheldon one of the Officers of Kronos pilots the AU Ultera Crab. 

 AAUs: 

AAU stands for Autonomous Assault Unit, this is basically what you would consider a monster in most RPGs. In the world of Full Metal Combat the AAUs are AI robots that are scattered around the city to kill you and protect items. They are all built in the lower levels of the city and if disabled but not destroyed they will be eventually transported back to the factories bellow for repairs and redeployment.

Characters In The Game

I am not going to talk about all of the characters that are in the game for two reasons, the first is not all characters are locked down and second even if we were just to go through the ones that have some art it would take too long. So instead I will talk about the types of characters that are in the game and list some of the big ones. 

 7 Guardians of Kronos: The Guardians are the people from the Nation of Light that are responsible for order and safety in each of their respective Districts. They are ranked by their skill from 1 to 7, 7 being the best of the group but number 1 is still far from a push over. Each of the Guardians has a unique Rig that they use in arena matches when/if you challenge them. Defeating each of the Guardians gives you unique bonuses and if you defeat all 7 you get to keep one of their weapons. 

Shop keeps: Shop keepers are also from the Nation of Light except the bar tenders in the Nation Houses that are from their respective nations. Shop keeps are the NPCs that you talk to in the Sanctuary to get information, items, enter arena matches and configure your rig. 

Officers of Kronos: The Officers of Kronos are from the Nation of Light and are the ones in charge of maintaining the Districts. Each of them has a unique AU that they also use to fight with if challenged. The AUs are all hidden in Districts throughout the city and they each have their own special unlock for defeating them. For example one of them unlocks the AAU data base, this allows you to look up information on any AAU which is of great use to tactical players. Another gives you access to the cities teleportation system so you can teleport from anywhere in the city to a transportation point, this is a great way to escape without penalty for the player who like to gamble. And the last example I am going to mention is the Officers Key which grants you access in to several areas that are otherwise impossible to get in to. 

Old friends: Old friends are NPC competitors that you knew outside of the city. These NPCs will be a great help to you, giving you items and information as long as your patriot score is good. 

Game World Breakdown

The entire game world is contained inside a purpose build city, Kronos. Inside the city are 7 districts designed to create order by not letting competitors of low skill level in to the more dangerous areas. Inside each district is a Sanctuary tower. This is the only place in the city people can go outside of their Rigs. They are the safe havens where you can go to rest, buy items for your Rigs and if you choose compete in arena matches against your fellow competitors. Each Sanctuary also has a Guardian who is responsible for the safety and order of their District. While inside of the Sanctuary you can also challenge them to an Arena match which comes with unique rewards if you can best them and that is a big if. It is not recommended that you challenge a guardian till you are in Rank A (top), but if you choose to you can fight them at any Rank. Inside the Districts you also have a host of other building containing quests, training areas and other surprises. 

End Game Of this Funding 

This kick starter is not meant to pay for the entire game production as I don't want to compromise on my vision for this game but hopefully it will give us the time needed to make the game complete enough that an Alpha funding model could get us the rest of the way. As I have said tho no matter the money this game will get done because it is my dream, the only question is how fast it gets done. And that is where you can help. 

Where is the game At 

Phase 1 is creating a scaled version of the game world that will allow you to play the game from start to finish and have the game be code completed to a point where you can do everything in the game. 

Phase 2 is taking the code base and polishing it all so it works as smooth at is possibly can. And balancing every little piece of the game till it feels right. 

 Phase 3 is making a full scale version of the game world and polishing all of the art assets. 

Right now the game is in phase one with around 50% of the code being done, including but not limited to quest systems, rig management, travel from district to district, basic arena matches. Asset wise you can walk around the first Sanctuary and District, interact with all the shop keepers and enter 7 of the 12 buildings in the District. Also there is currently 35 different types of AAUs (monsters) in the District with more on the way soon. 

 Stretch Goals 

100K 

First stretch goal is to raise enough money that I could afford to pay the artists that have been volunteering time to work on the game. This would mean that they could spend more time working on the game which would increase both the speed of the project and the quality of the art work. Reaching this goal would also allow us to pay for sound effects and music. 

150K 

Second stretch goal would give us the resources to bring another artist on board which would free up more time for myself to work on multiplayer code which is something I would really like this game to have. 

 500K 

If by some miracle we were able to reach this level then we would have all the money needed to make this game exactly the way we want it to be and then release the final version of the game for free. 

My Story

Hi my name is Iain Nicholls and I have been obsessed with programming for the last 17 years and have been interested in making games for just as long. I have a Diploma in Games Programming and Bachelor in Interactive Entertainment majoring in games programming, I also have commercial experience as a programmer but not on games because the job market for games programmers in Australia is very small which is one of the reason I am making my own game. I have been working on this game on and off for a number of years now whenever I can find time and I would really LOVE to make this project a full time thing which is just not possible without this money as I have a beautiful wife and daughter to consider so I cannot afford to do the just go for it play. My main focus and goal for Full Metal Combat is not to make the most beautiful game or the most ground breaking or anything like that, my main goal is to make a game that is good old fashioned FUN. I want you to sit down to play this game and before you know it have lost hrs because you were engaged and having fun playing the game. A couple of games from my past that have been big influence on this game are Tyrian and Metal Fatigue both of which dealt with configuring your space ship of mech to best suit yourself and the mission. That plus the good old fun of just destroying anything that opposes you is the heart of this game.

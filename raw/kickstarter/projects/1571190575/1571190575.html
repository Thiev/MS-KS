FacePunchMaster: 8-bit Fist is a unique, RPG style beat 'em up, with a pixel inspired art style set in an expansive, 3D environment. It has been in development since June 2013, and an early BETA release was put in the iOS App Store and indiedb.com in February 2014.

I'm looking to fund the completion of FPM with the help of the awesome people on Kickstarter. I'd like to fund a few more months of development on the game to see it completed.

I'm hoping to release the game to a broad swath of platforms, including PC/Mac, iOS and Android devices, and the OUYA. I see development as an ongoing process: I'd like to continue adding chapter style quest lines to tell all the stories of Fist City and the world it exists in.

FacePunchMaster: 8-bit Fist is currently being developed by the one man team of Jared SaizdelaMora at Tastes Like Future! Games. Jared's background is in web and media design, mostly for education, non-profits, and youth outreach. He's worked on such games as the Walking History Project, an interactive AR experience centered in the Poughkeepsie Waterfront district, and the recent Neon City Runner: Death Run Redux.

 -----Story-----

FacePunchMaster:8-Bit Fist takes place in the dystopian Fist City, ten years after the ascent of it's latest municipal leader, Mr. Mayor. Conditions have deteriorated to the point of all out anarchy under the weight of the oppressive new regime. Street gangs terrorize the population, and mighty champions have carved out domains from the detritus of the once great city. Meanwhile, in response to the formation of the Secret Police Force, an omni-powerful law enforcement agency at the beck and call of Mr. Mayor, a rebellion has fomented against the man many see as a feckless and incompetent tyrant.

And so enters our protagonist; swept up in a secret police raid, the player must decide whether  to join the rebel forces and overthrow the current regime, or join the secret police and conquer the city from within.

-----Quests-----

There are many denizens and organizations in the world that require your assistance. In addition to the main quest line, there are many other quests and story lines for the player to explore and conquer. Simple quests yield rewards like experience, cashmoney, and rare loot, while more involved quest lines may grant you great and powerful abilities, or access to special services and vehicles.

-----Locations-----

While centered in Fist city, the story takes the player to many varied and colorful locations. Explore the creepy suburbs and ghoulish inhabitants of Mayberry Drive, Plumb the depths of the old sewer systems, travel back in time to deal with medieval mayhem in ye olde Bludgeonburg, and explore the many other locations of the FacePunchMaster universe.

The world of FacePunchMaster is organized into hub worlds; each hub world is unlocked by completing quests and challenges. Inside each hub world is a series of levels, as well as shops, quest npcs, and a diverse cacophony of pedestrians, combatants, street gangs, and general riff raff.

-----Champions-----

The completion of most levels will require the player to defeat one of the champions of fist city. Once a champion has been defeated, they are unlocked as a playable character. Each champion has 5 levels that can be unlocked with with experience (gained through combat and  questing) and cashmoney, the currency of the realm. With each level, the champion's abilities and stat boosts are unlocked. At level 5, the champion's alternate skin is unlocked, complete with an entire new set of attacks. Each champion's stats and abilities lend themselves to a different combat strategy.

-----Combat-----

Inspired by old school beat'em up mechanics emphasizing combinations and special attacks, FacePunchMaster: 8-Bit Fist's combat system is a unique twist on the standard beat'em up control scheme. Both the keyboard and touchscreen control setups are meant to evoke the deceptive simplicity of early D-Pad + 2 button controllers.

-Rage Meter-

Instead of a traditional mana/energy bar, a champion's special attacks require Rage. Some champions gain rage by dealing damage to others, and some gain rage by taking damage. Each character has a rage cap; exceeding this cap will allow the player to enter rage mode; their champion will turn red, they will gain a small boost to their speed and strength, and they will be able to perform their most devastating attack, the mega weapon attack. Rage depletes over time; some champions lose it more slowly than others.

-Standard attacks-

All characters possess a standard set of attacks. By utilizing various attacks while moving in different directions, the player can chain quick attacks, strong attacks, jump attacks, grapples, and special moves together to disable and damage their opponents. Each champion's unique move set and stats lend themselves toa different style of combat. Some champions excel at ranged combat, while others are more capable at receiving and dealing close combat damage. 

-Grappling-

While in close combat, the player can grapple enemies and perform grapple and toss attacks that turn enemies into dangerous projectiles. Most scenery objects can be picked up and thrown as well.

-Blocking and deflecting-

Sometimes, the best offense is a good defense.  By blocking incoming attacks, the player can lessen the damage and stunning effects of their enemies. Blocking damage reduces stamina, however; once the player's stamina reaches 0, their blocks will become ineffective.

With quick enough reflexes, the player may be able to utilize a deflection. Deflecting an enemy's attack will prevent all damage to the player, and redirect the attack at it's source, but the window for most attacks is very small. Failing a deflection temporarily stuns the player, leaving them vulnerable to counter attack.

-Weapon/Overdrive attacks-

Once a champion has been upgraded to level 1, the player is able to use that character's weapon attack. Weapon attacks are greatly varied between champions; some may unleash devastating close combat strikes, while others may launch projectile attacks, summon helper npcs, or grant the player temporary stat increases.

At level 2, the champion's overdrive attack is unlocked. Similar to the standard weapon attack, the overdrive attack requires the player to have a weapon equipped. Overdrive attacks are far more powerful than standard weapon attacks, but cost some health in addition to rage. Overdrive attacks are also much slower, and may leave the player open to counter attack if aimed incorrectly.

-Mega Weapon Attacks-

When a champion reaches level 3, the player will be able to utilize that champion's most powerful attack, the mega weapon attack. Like standard weapon attacks, mega weapon attacks require that the player have a weapon equipped. While in rage mode, the champion's standard weapon attack is replaced with the vastly more powerful mega weapon attack. Each champion's mega weapon attack is different, but all are very powerful.

-----Inventory-----

In their travels, the player will pick up plenty of randomly generated weapons, accessories, and items to aid them in their ventures through loot drops or purchasing at stores. 

-Weapons-

 In addition to offerings up to 2 stat bonuses, equipping a weapon is required to perform weapon and super move attacks. Each weapon also has a limited amount of durability; using special attacks diminishes durability, and if the durability goes under 0, the item is lost. Weapons can be repaired with cashMoney.

-Accessories-

 Accessories give the player up to 2 stat boosts. Like weapons, accessories also have a durability scale. When the player takes damage, the accessory's durability is lessened. When it reaches 0, the accessory will break and disappear.

-Items- 

There are many different types of items that the player can pickup and use; Items can restore health and stamina, boost your rage meter, grant you temporary bonuses, as well as offensive items that allow the player to summon lackey npcs and perform special attacks.  Most items are lost after use, however some very rare and valuable spell book items are not used up when activated.

-Common, Uncommon, and Rare Tickets- 

Tickets are rare drops found throughout the world by defeating harder enemies and completing quests. Tickets primarily serve to unlock the player's champion's higher levels, but they can also be exchanged at the Ticket Shop in Fist City Proper for uncommon and rare items.

-----Vehicles-----

In your travels through Fist City, you may stumble upon vehicles that will greatly aid your ability to travel. There's all sorts of zany vehicles; from cop cars and tanks to segways and golf carts - each with their own specialty. While still very early in their development, several vehicles are in the current demo.

-----Dungeon Mode-----

For players seeking to level up their champions, dungeon mode offers varied, unending, procedurally generated maps full of enemies for the player to conquer. While dungeon mode currently exists in game, I plan on giving it a major overhaul before the final release.

-----Multiplayer-----

Using their unlocked champions, players can play with friends in competitive multiplayer formats like Capture the flag, king of the hill, and team deathmatch, as well as co-operative campaign missions. By gaining cashmoney and rare tickets in game, the player will also be able to unlock deadly teams of themed AI warriors to aid them in their multiplayer battles.

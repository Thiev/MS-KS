About the Game

Endless Migration 2 is an artful, atmospheric video game about a flock of migratory birds lost in time.

While flying homeward, the flock strays off course into the Bermuda Triangle. Suddenly, the birds are pulled into a vortex, catapulted through a wormhole, and lost in time. As players progress through the multi-chapter story, they find themselves lost in places like the Ice Age during the final meteor showers that killed the dinosaurs, the hellscape of a post-apocalyptic future where man is pitted against machine, the high tides of the Industrial Age where coal-burning mills blot out the sun, China during the Han Dynasty and the advent of fireworks, the mythological age of Ancient Greece, and even the western front of World War One where explosions of mustard gas and dog-fighting airplanes make it nearly impossible to save the carrier pigeons flying above the battlefield. It is up to you to use your knowledge of the flock to guide your birds safely home to the wilderness of present-day Canada.

The original Endless Migration was a browser game created for Kongregate's Longest Lasting Game design contest in 2009. The game achieved more than we ever expected, won the Grand Prize, and has been played by millions of casual gamers since. (Here's a link to the archived contest page). It's really been wonderful to think about how many people love the game and still play it to this day. A simple but addictive mouse-avoider, Endless Migration was unique, subtle, and fun.

We want your help to completely revamp the Endless Migration concept. We're considering EM1 the prototype for a truly immersive and beautiful video game, with a wonderful narrative and the introduction of diverse environments and a multitude of bird species. We will release Endless Migration 2 on the iPhone as an accelerometer game and as a free browser game for PC and Mac. If we raise enough funding, we'd also love to do other platforms such as Android, iPad, X-Box Live, and Playstation Network.

Please share this project with your friends and family and help us make this dream come true. Thanks so much!

Why we need Kickstarter:

With your support, we will be able to turn this game into something it never could be without you. This means: more detail, refinement, content, platforms, and love.

Specifically, here is our wish list of things we need to really make this game a masterpiece:

  • Time to truly polish and expand the game! We came to Kickstarter, because we want to create one of the best games on the App Store. We want Endless Migration 2 to be a work of art. With seed funding from gracious backers like you, we can polish every aspect of the experience and add more and more great content. Right now, we're committed to 7 unique environments and approximately 20 bird species for the final game. But, we'd love to make that number grow!
  • Better computers, so that every member of the design team can switch to the mac platform and run the latest and greatest software.
  • More devices. We'd love to have multiple devices, so that each of us can test and debug the game individually. Since the mobile version of the game is going to be built around the accelerometer, the ability to test thoroughly and simultaneously on a number of generations of device is crucial to perfecting the controls and gameplay. ** Also, if we get enough backers and have funding to port to additional platforms such as iPad, Android, X-Box Live, PSN, etc. we will need additional devices.
  • Quality audio equipment and software. As a small indie developer, we probably aren't alone when we often feel forced to sacrifice sound design due to budget limitations. We'd love to be able to capture and create crisp sound effects with professional microphones and instruments.
  • Industry-standard software like Photoshop and Illustrator will drastically improve our capacity to make quality art, textures, and other visual elements. There are also a number of third-party plugins, particle emitters, and other software packages that we'd love to buy and use in the game for that extra level of detail and polish.
  • We'll be able to purchase quality third-party sound effects and other assets to continue to improve the game's visuals and sound-scape.
  • We'll market the game. We'll use a portion of the funding to mount an online marketing campaign using online and social media advertising.
  • We'll pay for start-up costs to create a proper business.

We've chosen $8000 as our funding goal, because it's realistic and it's just enough to supplement the amount we've personally invested in the project. With $8000, we'll be able to buy most of these things on our wishlist and create all the great rewards we're offering. Hopefully, this Kickstarter campaign is the beginning of new and wonderful things!

Guidelines for Creative Rewards:

We want backers to have a lot of freedom to create great in-game content, so these guidelines are meant to help steer creativity, not impede it.

Designing Birds: When you back us at a tier that lets you design your own bird, you truly get to take charge of virtually every aspect of character design for that species. Birds should be real or within a reasonable historical mythology. The Phoenix would be a good example of a mythic bird already found in the game. Maybe you like the idea of a gryphon, dragon, vampire bat, or other winged creature that isn't technically a bird. That's totally cool as long as it flies! By the way, one of the unique concepts driving EM2 is the concept of the flock as a passive entity. So, if you make a dragon, we aren't going to let it down all the planes with fireballs. You'll still have to keep the flock defensively oriented.

Remember, if you decide to make a terribly powerful creature (like a dragon) it will likely be rare in the game, so keep that in mind and decide if you want your creation to be a rare and powerful gem or a less powerful but more commonly occurring addition to the EM2 narrative.

Designing Adaptations: The max number of adaptations for a bird is 5, but you can design 1 to 5 based on your preference. Adaptations are meant to improve the abilities of a certain bird or to buff other birds in a flock, when a certain bird is present. As stated above, at the core of Endless Migration 2 is the concept of the flock as a defensive - not offensive - entity. Therefore, we are going to avoid any bird or adaptation that attacks or destroys airplanes / people / etc. However, it's okay if adaptations cause birds to destroy themselves or other birds in order to cast. (The Canary's Early Warning adaptation is already like this, where the canary sacrifices itself to save its flock-mates).

Adaptations can't be so overpowered that they make the game too easy to win, and they sort of have to fit with the bird you choose. If you are totally excited about a certain bird, but can't think of adaptations, we can help with them too.

Designing Level Environments: Think of your favorite historical epoch, a mythology or folklore that you love, an interesting contemporary environment, or a future reality and run away with it! It can be underground, above ground, in space, mythological, metaphysical, or realistic. We are pretty open to suggestions here. We're not looking for the impossible or overly goofy or stuff that infringes on copyright, so flying on the surface of the sun or across the blue screen of death on a Windows XP computer or through the first level of Mario Bros. aren't going to make sense. You'll be involved in creating everything for the level, ranging from the terrain to the specific hazards, challenges, and boss(es) that face the flock. So, try to think of cool places that will look great from an aerial perspective in the EM2 style and also pose unique and interesting challenges for a flock of birds in flight. Levels will last about 1 to 3 minutes each and may recur during survival mode.

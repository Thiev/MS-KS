Hi, my name is Kee-Won Hong, I'm a one man Indie team and I love making video games. Since my humble start modding the original Half-Life to my more recent work on commercial titles, I've had one goal: to design and release my own game. When I was laid off at the beginning of this year, I decided to turn a setback into an opportunity and create the game I've always wanted to make.

I wanted to combine the great action of a retro 2d shooter with the awesome customization of a modern action rpg. Take the best retro shooters like Megaman and Contra, mix with the character development of System Shock 2 or Deus Ex and place that in a gritty cyberpunk world like Akira or Ghost in the Shell. The result is Contract Work.

This project has been entirely self funded and developed and contains  ideas that I've been thinking about for years. Thank you for checking out my Kickstarter, and I'm excited to share the world of Contract Work with you.

2113 is a dystopia where massive corporations control the world through vast robotic armies, where your character is one of the last truly independent humans. Even the most powerful corporations depend on your unique skills to handle their delicate 'situations'. This contract work is high risk but higher reward, and for those with talent there is unlimited potential.

Play it on the web!

Contract Work is a web game, which means no installing or downloading plugins; Just play the game on your browser!

Mobile Contract Work? Ouya? Gamestick?

I'm planning on bringing Contract Work to iOS and Android eventually after the web release - if the campaign is funded, a likely stretch goal will be for mobile device support. I'm also looking into Contract Work on the Ouya & Gamestick!

Kill robots and get paid for it

Money drives the world of Contract Work .You'll be able to decide which contracts you want to take and how you want to handle them. Lucrative contracts come with larger risks, and you can even underbid a contract, adding more risk for a bigger payday. It's all up to you to decide.

Contracts will have many different locations and objectives, like defending data servers, stealing corporate intelligence or a hostile takeover of a rival HQ. Your robotic competition will take many forms, each presenting their own challenges. You never know who's going to show up at the office to make your day difficult.

Finally, you'll earn 'performance based bonuses' during contracts. You'll  get extra cash for chaining a kill streak, hacking every terminal, destroying every enemy plus many other bonuses. Customize your play style to maximize your profits!

Guns, lots of guns...

Contract work lets you customize each character the way you see fit. Each freelancer comes with a unique set of weapons, abilities and perks that you can purchase through your hard earned dollars. There are no skill trees here - if you can pay, you can play!

Once you've increased your net worth, you'll access specialist classes that unlock new items.An automation specialist can deploy remote defenses like turrets or barriers. Explosives experts will have access to proximity mines, explosive ammunition and rocket launchers. Hacking experts can even mind jack bots to turn them against their allies.

The final perk of being a freelancer is being able to sub-contract help for your missions. Your team could be your most valuable asset, but hire and fire carefully - this world is full of closet revolutionaries, corporate spies and other shady characters.

A world full of mystery and the freedom to explore it

The universe of 2113 is dangerous and in constant flux, and you'll make many decisions that will effect both you, the world and the outcome of your story. In this dark future, every action has consequence.Every dollar in your account increases the power of your character, but what was the cost to put it there?

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Will the game be completed if the funding goal is met?

Absolutely. I've been building software and games for years and understand what it takes to create, develop and deliver a product. There is nothing more important to me right now than getting this game completed...but you don't have to take my word for it, see for yourself:

Play the demo - Try it right now for free. It's not the final product but proves the technology and mechanics are working as promised.

Sign up for the closed beta - donating $25 gets you immediate access to the closed beta, which will be updated every week.

Follow the weekly blog and youtube updates to stay informed.

I noticed you have a pretty aggressive release date. Can you actually finish the game by then?

Yes! Most of the heavy technology work is already done, and HTML5/Impact makes updating quick and easy. In addition, after Contract Work goes live I can easily add additional content and features. I'm very confident that Contract Work will be available for everyone to play starting June 2013.

Will the game be fun? Will it deliver value for my money?

Yes! Beyond the planning I've done, I've created metrics tied into every release that will inform me how to best improve the game. Weekly beta updates will let you quickly tell me what you like (and don't like) about the direction of the game.

I'm very excited about this project and I've decided to donate at a higher tier. Will my reward be fulfilled?

Definitely! Your rewards are actually a critical part of the building of this game, not something that would take time away from development. Each reward helps me build a richer universe and also gives you a unique artifact to call your own.

Whoa. Designing something is a lot of pressure. What if I just want to donate at a higher level?

No worries. You can help as much or as little as you want based on your reward. Maybe you just want to write some copy or tweak some artwork - that's perfectly acceptable! You still get my undying gratitude, a special place in the credits and your input on the design.

I'm worried about all this design by committee. Will the game be unbalanced? Will it have offensive content?

Even though I'm collaborating with many partners to create this universe, I'm reserving final say on everything. I'll work with every partner as much as I can, but at the end of the day if content is offensive, unbalanced or a bad fit it won't be a part of the game (refunds will be given if applicable).

I donated at the basic level. Can I still be involved with the design of the game?

Yes! I'm always interested in your feedback, regardless of your donation. All donors are assured a spot in the public beta when it starts so you'll have plenty of opportunities to play the latest version and let me know what you think.

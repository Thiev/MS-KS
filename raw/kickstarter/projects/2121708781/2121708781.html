"The last thing that you remember is standing before the wizard Lakmir as he gestured wildly and chanted in an archaictongue. Now you find yourself staring at an entryway which lies at the edge of a forest."

So begins the legend of Shadowgate,the grandaddy of all 'point and click' fantasy adventure games - a title that paved the way for a host of fantasy games to follow. In Shadowgate, you move from room to room through the labyrinths of the living castle, confronting the devilish denizens and confounding the fiendish traps laid by the Warlock Lord. Your quest is to fulfill an ancient prophecy by gathering various objects of power along with three ancient artifacts. Once assembled, these relics of old comprise the Staff of Ages - the only weapon that can stop the traitorous sorcerers' plan of unleashing the Behemoth, a titan of tremendous power, on an unsuspecting world.

We are proud to announce that we are creating a new Shadowgate! While we published the original game on over ten different platforms, this Shadowgate is NOT A PORT. It's a re-imaginingthat includes a lot of new features, exciting updates and ingenious additions that will add even more to the mythology and expand upon the original story of our fantasy classic. This is theShadowgate that we always wanted to make and we are thrilled that we have the opportunity to capture the unique excitement of the original while expanding on the world and mythos like never before. And if you've never played a first-person adventure title, this is the one to start with!

Shadowgate will contain many cool features and options:

  • Rooms:More than 60 beautifully illustrated rooms featuring both newand familiar locations, offering a new play experience
  • Puzzles:Lots of newand updatedpuzzles that seamlesslyexpandon the original game
  • First Person Adventuring: Utilize your inventory, spell book, mapping system and on-screen help to complete your quest
  • Commands: Intuitive command-based UIallows you to interact extensively with the environments
  • Storytelling: Features the same greatstorytelling you expect from a Shadowgate adventure
  • Cinematic Score: A full, digitally-orchestrated soundtrack by composer Rich Douglas
  • 8-bit Score: A second retro soundtrack featuring Hiroyuki Masuno's original NES composition
  • Soundscapes: A complete atmospheric and puzzle-based sound design
  • Animations: Environmental and object-specific animation bring each location to life
  • Platforms: Supports Windows, Mac, iOS and Android tablets

Zojoi is a small gaming company, tucked away in the beautiful Blue Ridge Mountains of central Virginia. We started our careers in 1985 by designing and illustrating the groundbreaking game Shadowgate. We then went on to help design and create artwork for the other "MacVentures" (Dj vu, Dj vu II and Uninvited) on a multitude of platforms. Since that time, weve gone on to design, develop and/or executive produce everything from side-scrollers to massively multiplayer games including the SNES hit Road Runners Death Valley Rally, Aliens Online, Air Warrior III, Multiplayer Battletech: 3025, Sherlock Holmes and The Settlers of Catan.

Over the past ten years, we continued to work on the original adventures - porting each to the Game Boy Color, Palm and Pocket PC - as well as brainstorming and designing new titles. We started Zojoi because we love making first-person adventure games, we love playing them and we want to bring them to new and veteran adventure fans alike.

Bringing this new version of Shadowgate to fruition as an independent company is an expensive task - from reacquiring the license and redesigning the puzzles to developing awesome art/audio and programming the game. We really want to make Shadowgate happen so that's why we are turning to you.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Every game that the Zojoi team members have made over the last 27 years comes with challenges. It would be foolish to say that just because we have been in the games business a long time that we have everything and every angle covered. The key to any successful project is anticipating what the risks are and mitigating them. We are approaching the Shadowgate project with the same care that we would any gaming project we work on - whether it's a MMO or a first-person adventure.

To that end, we've been assessing the risks and developing mitigation plans during our pre-production phase. This includes how the command system, UI and inventory/spell book seamlessly work together and how rooms and audio tracks will be integrated via our scripting engine.

Here are the key areas weve chosen to concentrate on early in our development to ensure success:

- Terrific art and audio are great but an intuitive UI and solid, balanced game play is paramount to making a great product.
- Pre-production is key. We have documented everything that we are developing to ensure the games' success both creatively and technically.
- Developing for multiple platforms requires a ton of attention and we will continue to focus on each version and refine the gaming and UI experience, ensuring that no one platform suffers.
- Because most games come together during the polishing part at the end of a project, we have scheduled triple the amount of time for polishing the game.
- Testing is often overlooked in game development and brought on too late. We plan to bring QA on board asap.
- Assign an individual to manage the kickstarter campaign. The core team needs to concentrate on making the project that you funded.

While we use best practices to mitigate risks in the development of the game, there are some risks that cannot be foreseen or avoided that may affect the project and/or its launch. We will do everything we can to mitigate those as well and will definitely keep you informed throughout the project!

FAQ

  • What does the player do in Shadowgate?

    Shadowgate is a first-person adventure, presenting the action from a players perspective. Shadowgate is also location-based where you step from environment to environment and use your command system (Examine, Open, Close, Use, Etc.) to solve puzzles, defeat creatures and discover new locations.

    Last updated: Fri, Oct 26 2012 9:34 am EDT
  • What is a re-imagining?

    When we reacquired the Shadowgate license, we werent interested in doing a port of the original game. Instead, we wanted to look at the title in fresh way. So were building upon the original game by creating new story elements, adding new puzzles and replacing ones that we thought were too predictable. Additionally, we wanted to present the game in a way that we never had the opportunity to before, while still retaining the first-person adventure players enjoyed.

    Last updated: Fri, Oct 26 2012 9:34 am EDT
  • The video looked very cool! Where are you in development?

    Right now we are in the pre-production/concept phase. This means we are still working out all the important stuff including creative and technical bits. While we feel the style of the audio and room art is right on, there is a lot left to do. We have also begun experimenting with animations and particle effects in different environments to achieve the look we envision. Finally, weve started working on a custom scripting engine to allow flexibility in coding each individual game room.

    Last updated: Fri, Oct 26 2012 9:34 am EDT
  • Why are the portable versions coming out after the Windows/Mac versions?

    We want to focus on developing Shadowgate for PC and Mac first to ensure that the player experience is perfect before moving on to other platforms. We feel its best to design and implement the game to leverage the strengths of each platform.

    Last updated: Fri, Oct 26 2012 9:34 am EDT
  • Why did you choose these rewards?

    We love fantasy and decided that we would have rewards that we ourselves would want. That includes things like a cool box, a cloth map of the Shadowgate world, a poster and a soundtrack. We also thought the card game would be great if we had an expert develop it hence Coleman Charltons involvement. Finally, when we saw the first proofs of our staff design, we knew we had to make smaller replicas so more folks could get them.

    Last updated: Fri, Oct 26 2012 9:34 am EDT
  • Why do I need to add additional money for international shipping?

    One of the toughest things about a campaign is balancing the pledges for development against producing great rewards. We tried to make the cost of the tiers as low as possible for pledgers while making sure that we receive enough money to complete the game. To that end, we have to factor in the cost of shipping. Unfortunately, we discovered that international is just more expensive than we had hoped it would be.

    Last updated: Fri, Oct 26 2012 9:34 am EDT
  • I watched the video. Poor Aaron! Is he okay?!?!

    Poor Aaron? What about the rest of us? We TOLD him not to touch the staff! Did he listen? No, of course not. Aaron never listens. Seriously, were all fine. Thanks for asking.

    Last updated: Fri, Oct 26 2012 9:34 am EDT
  • Will you support the iPad 1?

    We are still looking at the iPad 1 but the difference in speed in comparison with the newer iPads is pretty huge. We will do everything we can to support it at this point it is doubtful the game will run adequately on it.

    Last updated: Sun, Oct 28 2012 9:51 pm EDT
  • Will my credit card or bank account be charged as soon as I pledge?

    No. Your card will only be charged when the campaign reaches it's goal and comes to an end.

    Last updated: Fri, Nov 2 2012 10:52 am EDT

 Prologue

Ever played an MMORPG? Have you ever gotten tired of grinding, or after months or years of investing your time and money into a game, found that the quests start to get repetitive and boring?

Let's change that, shall we?

For 15 years, I have been developing online multiplayer worlds with emphasis on User Generated Content. This is even before the days of YouTube, Instagram, Reddit, and Social Networking Sites, which made UGC mainstream.

When I first started designing and coding The King of Spades (a browser-based online massively multiplayer game) back in 1999, people were confused as to why I wanted to create a game world that will let players create their own quests and content and character designs, they were even more confused when a few years later, people paid real money for a virtual apartment or a custom piece of clothing or sometimes, even whole cities that live in this "virtual" world. To the minds of the skeptics, these worlds didn't "really exist". But they didn't really get it. To the minds of the players, this was an opportunity to experience something that they've imagined in a virtual space where others can also experience that with them. A virtual world that they can enjoy every once and a while, and in those moments forget about the gloom and doom and stresses of reality.

I had watched movies like eXistenZ and The Matrix many years ago, and to me, that was the next evolution of this virtual world: Immersive Virtual Reality in a seamless game world that you can experience with your friends (minus the evil agents that are trying to kill you).

This is the opportunity to make that world of amazing virtual reality experiences a reality.

What is it?

XViREnt (pronounced ex-vye-rent) stands for "Experiences in Virtual Reality Entertainment". It is an MMORPG Platform where players log into their hub, which is in the form of an apartment, condo, team base, or house (their virtual pad), and can interact with their environment by movement, or accessing virtual content sources, for example: a virtual TV, a virtual tabletop, a virtual book-case, even (future feature) a virtual phone! Every pad has an exit door, the door connects you to many other realities created by us and other developers. This will be a simple, seamless experience, and you'll be able to log in and out at your own pace without having to invest huge chunks of your time in every session. Both casual players and hardcore RolePlayers will enjoy the experience, because the game caters to both play styles.

And that is the key: This system is so convenient and so easy to use, that anyone can get right into it without ever having played an MMO or an FPS. We want everyone to experience this, even non-gamers.

And because it is an MMORPG Platform, it can be used to launch storylines and games developed and published by other game makers. One Avatar, infinite worlds, infinite story-lines, and infinite games.

The Premise

 Imagine starting your own TV Station in a virtual world beaming shows, entertainment, and role-played news events to thousands of users around the world. How about fighting the forces of evil with your loved one (who's physically a thousand miles away), and then end the night by sitting by the fireplace in your virtual apartment and watching a movie?

Imagine designing an android (the humanoid robot, not the O/S!) to be your butler in this virtual world, and have them do tasks for you like sending an e-mail or recommending a cool movie for you and your buddy. Imagine going into your virtual book-case and picking out a virtual book that has 3D popup elements.

I don't know about you, but that sounds like a virtual world I want to be a part of!

Think of it as "The Sims" meets "The Matrix" meets "eXistenZ"

Multiverses and Avatars

XViREnt will support multiple universes, each with its own realities, creatures, laws and owners. The player, however, can move their avatar back and forth between these realities seamlessly. This "Planeswalking" ability means that you don't have to recreate your avatar in each single reality, think of the history and adventures your avatar will write home about!

The Avatars, on the other hand are based on a "template", for example, one template could be a human form (with different varieties of body parts, colors, and shapes), another template could be a vampire, and a third could be a zombie, and so on. The player picks a template to set up their character, which comes comes with its own set of outfits, customizations, and animations. And because these templates are constantly being improved and added upon, XViREnt seamlessly patches the templates with new content as it becomes available by its developers and approved by the XViREnt submission and testing system. Every player is allowed to have up to ten Avatars in one XViREnt account.

Character Classes

Your XViREnt Avatar is a dynamic being, capable of learning new things and adapting to different environments and realities, but your character needs to specialize in one or more things in order for them to stand out as an individual.

To do that you need to select character classes from four tiers: Knowledge, Career, Combat, and Superpowers.

The Story

The first two realities we'll be creating in XViREnt are your "canvas" or virtual home (as a separate independent reality), and Rex Palarum. Rex Palarum is the spiritual successor of The King of Spades gaming universe, it will consist of several epic quests that will take you through multiple universes, and experience many amazing things to show off what XViREnt can offer. When you create your XViREnt avatar, you can move that avatar back and forth between any worlds in XViREnt, including those created by other developers and game makers. Meaning that once you complete the storyline in one world (like Rex Palarum) you can move on to other storylines created by other players and so on, for as long as developers keep creating stories, quests, and worlds. Technically, the "game" can go on for ever, and you never have to buy expansion packs that will break the bank, because the worlds and content is added on seamlessly into the existing platform.

The Rex Palarum story goes like this: "For eons, the four realms of reality (Earth and the known Universe, Atlantis, Hades, and Deva) have been enjoying relative stability and have been separated from each other by an inter-dimensional barrier. An angel named Neri'el, one of the guardians of Hades, has fallen, and escaped her post by stealing a key to unlock that barrier. And by doing so, she has shattered that inter-universal calm. Demons are now crossing over to our realm of reality, and what's worse: other, previously unknown realms of reality are now interfacing with our universe, and can potentially bring terrible devastation. You are a citizen of our universe, and fate is about to cross paths with you. Your future, will never be the same, and you may hold the key to stop the universe from falling into eternal darkness. That key, is the power of creation."

The Comic book (first issue has been completed and is going to be shipped in November) is meant to be a "companion" to the MMORPG. Reading the comics will introduce some of the background story and main characters in the XViREnt universe, and provides you with some clues that will come in handy during quests. You might even meet one of the main characters during your travels and they might send you on a few epic quests across different dimensions!

Note: We are doing a limited print run of the first issue of the comic, so it will only be available during this Kickstarter campaign, so make sure that you pledge so that you can get a copy!

User Generated Content (UGC)

The complaints I've heard time and time again from our players who keep coming up to TKOS even after years of hiatus trying out other MMORPGs is this: Many MMORPGs claim to be "Role Playing Games", but there's really not much "Role play" apart from creating your character in some random combination hoping it will be unique, picking up some cool combinations from the skill tree, then setting off on quests finding rare items, sure the worlds and graphics look beautiful and often times, the story writing is amazing, but the "role play" of those worlds compared to the games that invented the concept (games like D&D) are miles apart from meeting each other concept-wise. Even if you do find like-minded individuals to team up with and "Role play" with, the limitations of these game worlds is to what the content creators (meaning the game studio and/or publishers) have imagined for you. This is THEIR vision of how the world looks like, and how you should behave in it. You "role play" what they tell you to do. They might as well play the game for you!

This is where User Generated Content comes in. The UGC tools we provide will allow you to set up your events, quests, adventures, and activities that allow you to truly express yourself and your creativity. Think the game world's quests are boring? Huddle up with your friends and come up with an amazing adventure for others to play, then set up Non-Player Characters, puzzles, and contraptions so that players can participate in the mystery or adventure you've created for them. Think the map you're exploring is boring? How can you make it better and more exciting?

Ask not what the game can do for you, ask what YOU can do for the game.

So, our focus during the life-cycle of XViREnt is to build a full suite of tools to allow anyone to contribute creative content into the game without or with very little technical knowledge.

There are two sets of tools we are building:

1- In-game crafting and engineering (players can pick specialization classes and gather resources to create objects, NPCs, vehicles, buildings, and quests inside the game based on pre-implemented assets).

2- External User Generated Content SDK (Software Development Kit): We will open up the game world to game developers and content producers from all over the world so that they can contribute their own original content. Really, any creative person can contribute content using Unity as well as the SDK (Software Development Kit) and other tools that we are currently creating for you to facilitate creating UGC (for non programmers and non-techies) that can be plugged into XViREnt without a lot of effort.

And because you're helping us build this amazing, expansive universe with its infinite possibilities, we give you back for your creativity: You get to sell your own stuff in the game to other players for real money, we will take a small percentage to cover the VR app store costs and infrastructure, but you keep the biggest chunk of it!

Here are some examples of things you can generate or offer in this game world:

Artists: Draw a cool 3D or interactive virtual painting that players can show off to their guests. Maybe it provides a clue to a quest? No programming knowledge required to contribute your content!

Writers: Be the first of a generation of writers producing virtual books with content that pops up or interacts with the user! Or how about turning your story into an actual quest inside the game, complete with NPCs, dialog, excitement, and intrigue? No technical knowledge required to contribute your content! Write an event for players to participate in, taking them to an intriguing mystery mansion with creepy NPC's that you've designed!

Software Developers: Have a cool feature that you think will be useful to have in this virtual reality world? Contribute! Show off your mad programming skills!

3D Modellers, Animators, and Artists: Design a building, store, vehicle, outfit, or an entire landscape for other players to explore, purchase or use! Or create your own world populated by weird but magnificent creatures and invite friends over to hang out and marvel at your creation! No programming knowledge required, just drag and drop scripts that we provide to you for free via our SDK, which will in turn make your content and worlds come alive!

Unity 4 / 5 Game Developers: Think of a cool game? From tabletop games to full-blown cross-world adventures! We will give you an SDK that you can use on top of Unity to test and implement your game, once that's done, you submit it to us and we will integrate it into the core game world.

YouTube Channel owners: With your permission, we can extend your channel to be offered in this virtual game, add some cool dynamic or interactive elements that players can use while watching your channel! Maybe you want to report on mysterious activities in other dimensions? No technical knowledge required to contribute your content!

Fashionistas: Come up with some cool fashion styles for the players to buy and wear. No programming knowledge required to contribute your content!

Now for the cool part, because this world will be free to play, as a player you are under no obligation to commit to it, (full disclosure: some content like your own cool pad, special TV channels and complex board games may require a fee to buy, to cover the cost that the developers spent to bring you that content). The only caveat is that you would need to purchase a Virtual Reality headset (on launch we are only planning to support Oculus Rift at this time) and have a reliable and fast internet connection (to avoid lag issues associated with multi-player virtual reality gaming).

How to play

To be able to use XViREnt, the following are the requirements:

1. PC with HDMI or DVI video out port, and about 2 GB of free space (you'll need more in the future to support more virtual apps, content, and worlds)
2. A reliable, fast internet connection (i.e. dial up may not work so well)
3. Free subscription to XViREnt
4. Optional: Oculus VR Head-Mounted Display
5. Optional: Game controller (Xbox or PS4) plugged into the PC via USB
6. Optional: High end gaming computer or graphics card (so you can increase the coolness level of the graphics)
7. Optional: Body tracking suit (XSENS, PrioVR, etc), or Kinect.

Note: If you choose not to play with the Oculus Rift, it is still possible to play with traditional keyboard, and mouse, or controller (the mouse or controller will replace the POV/look controls), however, you will miss out on the immersive aspects of the game. This way, if you don't think you can afford the Oculus Rift right now, you can still play and take part and customize your virtual apartment and play through the Rex Palarum story until you get the chance to purchase the Oculus Rift.

Development

The development of the Proof of Concept has already started, we are using Unity 4 for prototyping, but we will be launching with Unity 5. We have a preliminary virtual reality (Oculus Rift support) apartment ready with a TV that can stream content, a book-case to read books, and a tabletop where players can play board games! We are up and running with a multiplayer capability of two customizable avatars in the same room with minimal to no lag. We have applied a lot of our proprietary code already, like terrain generation, NPC system, Artificial Intelligence, and so on, but there's lots of work that is remaining. The avatar is very basic so far, and we are working out some bugs right now, but once we've cleared it out, we'll post a demo video, so watch this space! Note though that the prototype demos will likely be private teasers only sent to a select few people, and will be posted as "unlisted videos" for a short time period of a few days given all the secret technologies we're working on! We know that what we're doing is super cool and we'd love for the whole world to see them, but for security reasons, they will be private and temporary, until we get to a more stable state.

In the mean-time, you can check out some of the 3D renders of some of the buildings and architecture that's going to be featured in XViREnt, including a Dojo, Gym, Mansion (premium pledge reward), office building, research center, and more.

This Kickstarter project is to fund the development of the beta and UGC tools ONLY, which is due to launch in November 2014 (possibly later if we get enough funding to add more fun features into it), we are currently in the process of setting up a worldwide cloud-based networking architecture to support thousands of concurrent users in this game world, for that we enlisted the help of MMO-expert and Senior Engineer Chris Lange.

If the funding exceeds expectations, then we may be able to push the launch date earlier, and have a lot of the features built into it from the get-go. Once the game launches, we will pay for the infrastructure via optional paid content from the world's first Virtual Reality App Store.

What will the funds be used for?

The funds will be used primarily to implement the following features for beta and launch (in order of priority), this includes licensing and other development costs.

1- Avatar Creation System: Creating your avatar and selecting the classes from the list of core classes.

2- Multiplayer support for up to 200 CCUs in the game (Concurrent Users)

3- Player housing in the form of the Player "Canvas", a.k.a. the Virtual Living Room: This is where your character lives when he or she is outside the game worlds, it's basically a home that you customize, can watch TV in, read interactive books, and play table top games.

4- Fulfilling player pledge rewards, including implementing any missing features to apply a virtual reward.

5- The XViREnt Launch Pad: This is where you create your Avatar(s), select a world to travel to, and access your Canvas.

6- The Tools, Software Development Kit, and "plugins" for third party developers to start creating and adding their own worlds and creations into XViREnt (UGC).

If we exceed the minimum required to achieve our goals, we will implement the following add-ons (in order of priority):

1- Exceed 200 CCUs (depending on how high the funding goes).

3- Rex Palarum: Storyline, quests and game world content, along with any implementations (weapon skills, classes, careers, etc) required to complete the storyline.

4- Advanced SDK and player crafting: Better features in the SDK, and in-game player crafting and resource gathering!

5- More cities, and worlds, including Atlantis, Hollow Earth, Time Worlds, and Space!

6- Advanced Character Designs and props: more Avatar options, more outfits, more weapons better quality (without sacrificing latency, which is very important in VR implementations), make your character more unique!

FAQ

  • What is the difference between XViREnt and Rex Palarum?

    XViREnt is the "experience", this is the system that includes the tools, the avatar creation room, a holographic training room, the world launchpad, and your own personal canvas (an advanced form of player housing, that's not just limited to a house but you can potentially become powerful enough to create an entire world for yourself).

    Rex Palarum is the first developer-created "world" that you can access from the XViREnt launchpad, this world includes four planes of reality representing the four primary elements: Earth, Deva, Hell, and Atlantis (Earth, Air, Fire, and Water), and this world takes you through an amazing adventure based on The King of Spades: Dream or Reality, a book written by Salem Al-Tamimi in 2002 and published for free. Because of the popularity of the book (it was downloaded 1.5 million times+ since being published), we have decided to implement that story as the first world in XViREnt, note though that it is technically a reboot, so the storyline and character bios have changed slightly.

    One Avatar can move from one reality to another back and forth seamlessly. Creatures, themes, and laws of physics vary from world to world, but the character can move across them without having to recreate a character in each world. The same combat skills, weapons, abilities, etc. can be used across all these worlds. These characters, for the lack of a better term, are akin to "Planeswalkers" (those of you that play MTG will know what we mean).

    What this means for developers is that they can focus on just creating the game world and all the cool entities in that world, without having to worry about creating an avatar/character creation system, or an RPG system, or an NPC/dialog system, or a GUI, or Virtual Reality HMD support, or massively multiplayer networking capabilities, we've done all that, and all that is extensible to any 3rd party world plugged into XViREnt.

  • Are you trying to compete with Second Life, or Minecraft, or IMVU?

    No. Second Life, Minecraft and IMVU are awesome games that we love due to the creativity that they inspire, and the open world sandbox feel to it. That being said, those three games and XViREnt are a completely different ballgame, they are open world sandbox and social virtual worlds. XViREnt is first and foremost an MMORPG, with an overarching storyline, goals, and limitations to what you can or can't do based on your skills and experience level. In addition, XViREnt is primarily being developed with virtual reality headsets, while the other virtual worlds came to existence before VR HMD's were made mainstream by the Oculus Rift. (Note: the three games mentioned in the question will possibly have Oculus Rift support as well, so keep an eye on any news coming from their end).

    Finally, just because we are building XViREnt, it doesn't mean that the players need to choose one game and abandon the rest, we think that competition is important for the economy and it also pushes us game developers to make bigger and better games to attract subscribers, and certainly a user can be a subscriber to all these different virtual worlds at the same time and enjoy what each of them has to offer.

The reason we have to charge for customizable player housing is due to the cost of managing these persistent entities in the system, as well as the added software and technology costs needed to make the customization possible.

  • When did development of XViREnt start?

    XViREnt started as a casual MMORPG development project (codenamed T3) back in 2009 with our infinitely evolving content technology in mind (the tech we implemented in The King of Spades from as far back as 1999). The bulk of the work was spent exploring and testing out technologies, character classes, combat, superpowers, effects, graphics, and lots of other things. When the Oculus Rift successfully got funded in November of 2012, leading the way for affordable VR HMD's, we shifted our focus to developing this for Virtual Reality on top of the infinitely evolving content technology, plus a couple of other "patent pending" inventions we came up with to make this virtual reality as amazing, seamless, fun, and productive as possible.

    Last updated: Fri, Mar 21 2014 8:00 am EDT
  • How many people internationally can this project support?

    Please take a look at our funding milestones list for a list of our funding goals and the corresponding number of simultaneous players we'll be supporting for those funding milestones. Note that "simultaneous connection" refers to people being inside the game world at the same time, it doesn't refer to the number of subscribers, the actual number of subscribers can be anywhere from 10 times to 100 times more than the limit of people in the game, since people from around the world will be logging in from different time zones. We have currently successfully tested with 20 CCUs with minimal or no lag, and the jump to a higher tier (like 100) is just a matter of upgrading a tier in our networking architecture. Because our networking architecture is on the cloud, these increases will be seamless as long as the funding is available. Please note, however, that there will be separation of regions for our network architecture, including North America, Europe, and Asia, this is necessary to ensure that there's minimal lag and that the experience is as enjoyable as possible for everyone. It will be possible for you to log in to a different region if you have to meet a friend, for example, but we can't guarantee that the game will run smoothly for you.

    Last updated: Fri, Mar 21 2014 8:00 am EDT
  • But isn't creating an MMO a huge and expensive undertaking and requires millions of dollars?

    XViREnt is run by a veteran team of online game developers. We've been building MMO's and MMORPGs for a couple of decades. In fact, you may have played some of the games our team has worked on, including classics like Electronic Arts & Origin Systems' Ultima Online, and UXO, SegaSoft's 10six (resurrected as Project Visitor) as well as relatively recent games like NCSoft's Tabula Rasa, and City of Heroes. Not only that, but our CTO Chris Lange is a Senior Engineer at Microsoft and has worked on the XBL and XBox One Live teams. And in regards to the infrastructure costs, by implementing our networking architecture on the cloud, we are able to gradually support an increasing user-base with minimal costs at first, and by the time the project becomes big and popular enough, the revenue streams will cover the increasing costs of supporting the rising load and bandwidth on the cloud.

    Finally, to throw a couple of buzzwords around: we are following the "lean startup" and "agile" methodologies in implementing this platform. Meaning we start with a small, core set of features first, test it to make sure that it works without a hitch, release that, then add on more features in subsequent phases, and so on, until all features have been completed.

  • I'm not really understanding how you say User Generated Content will not require programming experience, but yet you are going to offer a Software Development Kit for developers to contribute their content into the game? What gives?

    I'm glad you asked that!

    Let's clear the confusion:

    There are two types of content in the game: User Generated Content created by in-game tools, this includes creating worlds in your personal canvas, meaning designing your terrain, placing waterfalls and mountains, placing flora & fauna, and so on. For these types of content, you require a certain skill in the game. For example, to "create" a creature, you need to be a beastmaster, your skills allows you to create baby animals, like an egg of a dragon. You then place this dragon egg in the appropriate incubator, then the egg hatches, and you then raise the dragon (or sell it to other players who don't have your skill). Alternatively, you can be an architect and "create" a blueprint of a building, you turn that blueprint into a construction site (after you've purchased an appropriately sized plot of land from a landlord), then you (or others) will need to gather raw materials to turn that construction site into an actual building. Players of TheKingOfSpades project are familiar with this system and have used these tools to create an intricate, and huge gaming universe. We are now taking these tools into XViREnt.

    The second type of content, is developer content. If this was the Matrix, then the third party developer will be like a Neo, able to circumvant the laws of the game world, and do anything that their programming skills can do. As long as the content they create is of good quality, and passes our Quality Assurance testing, it will be integrated into XViREnt. Examples of this third party content includes:
    1- A new type of creature (maybe the developer has invented this cool looking type of creature never before seen in any game or movie, and wants other players to experience this creature, whether as a pet, or a mount, or a terrible monster guarding a sacred treasure).
    2- A new type of Avatar template (maybe a developer wants to create an Anime theme, which XViREnt currently does not have, so the developer will create avatar templates so players can create anime-type avatars).
    3- A scene or planet (maybe a developer has this idea for this cool, amazing looking planet after being inspired by James Cameroon's Avatar movie, so the developer creates this world in Unity 4, and after it's approved, it is "connected" into XViREnt via the Launchpad, now your Avatar will be able to visit this world).

  • I want to create a humanoid character, NPC, or Avatar template for XViREnt. How do I do it?

    First, you'll need to learn how to use UMA (Unity Multipurpose Avatar), an open source Avatar technology for Unity. Learn how to create your "race" in UMA, then when you're done, apply our "NPC", "Creature", or "Avatar" XViREnt scripts to your content, and submit it to us. We'll test it and make sure everything works and looks good, and if it does, we'll integrate it into the system. If you choose to sell your creation in the game, then you'll earn royalties.

  • I've created my own world, how do you know if it will be compatible with XViREnt?

    As long as your world is in the form of a Unity 4 scene and you submit the content to us (giving us the right to host it in XViREnt), then that's technically all you need to do. If you want to make entities in your world interactive (for example, for XViREnt to recognize a door as a portal that takes you to another location, or for XViREnt to recognize an enemy creature with loot), then you'll need to apply scripts from our SDK so that XViREnt can recognize that when our players explore your world. You will also need to identify spawn areas, these are the areas where our players' avatars can spawn inside your world. The area has to be fairly plain and open so that players can quickly get an overview of how the world looks like and exit if they don't like what they see.

    Note that the following types of scripts will need to be stripped off in order to make it compatible:
    Player controls, player camera, VR support, GUI, and Networking/multiplayer (these are all handled by XViREnt)

  • How do you handle the problem of User's environmental presence being disconnected from the Avatar experience?

    This is the "embedding" problem described on this neuroscience paper back in 2002: http://tarrlabwiki.cnbc.cmu.edu/images/7/70/TaWa02.pdf

    There are two parts to this problem, the first is the visual/auditory aspect, e.g. latency, resolution, and tracking prediction, and this problem is currently being solved by modern VR HMD's, like the Oculus Rift.

    The second problem is what is known by VR researchers as "Reality Affinity", that is, the correlation of your conscious belief in what you're seeing is real, and your subconscious belief. Not in a sense where belief is suspended consciously (as when you're reading a fictional story that you want to believe is true, despite the reality inconsistencies), but rather, the conscious acceptance that what you are experiencing is real.

    The ironic thing that, it's not hardware that solves that problem for us (although the hardware certainly helps with the immersive aspect, and taking care of the nausea issues), but it's XViREnt's story that is core in solving the second part of the embedding problem, and we thank Marie Laurie Ryan from Colorado State University for giving us the inspiration (http://pmc.iath.virginia.edu/text-only/issue.994/ryan.994).

    So, what is the answer?

    You'll just have to wait and experience it for yourself, because telling you will not convince you.

  • I think this project is too ambitious. Let's assume you don't get any development studios to create content in XViREnt, does the project become a fail?

    InEvoWare, the company that's building XViREnt is itself a gaming studio with steady revenue from consulting and third-party work that our team has been doing since 2008.

    While this Kickstarter campaign is primarily to release the Beta, 200 CCU multiplayer capabilities, pledge rewards, the UGC tools, and the SDK (which is why we're only asking for the bare minimum of $8000). After all this is done, our plan is to develop Rex Palarum as the first non-canvas world launched from XViREnt.

    Rex Palarum is a reboot of the story based on the book: The King of Spades: Dream or Reality. This book was the background storyline for our old browser-based game that we launched over a decade and a half ago. Funding for Rex Palarum will come either by exceeding our $8k minimum pledge, or through external funding sources we're currently negotiating. Rex Palarum in itself is a huge world (four actually), with many quests that will take the player at least a few months' worth of gameplay to complete, and these will be "intelligent" quests that requires the player to think, inspired by adventures brought to us by Infocom, Sierra, Lucasfilm/LucasArts games. Meaning that you need to seriously consider your actions, dialog-skipping and grinding does not work here.

    Some developers we've talked to are excited and have already jumped on board with creating content (from as small as music to play in the game worlds, to as large as entire maps). Other developers we've talked to are doubtful on this campaign's success, and prefer to do everything (architecture, networking, infrastructure, VR support, etc) from scratch on their own, which is perfectly fine obviously, we're not here to force anybody to do anything. We're just trying to help developers get faster from concept to launch, and save them lots of money and time while they're at it, and focus on doing what they do best: creating amazing things, not reinventing the wheel.

    That being said, the UGC tools we're creating will also allow non-developers to create content from within the game itself via their skills (this is like an advanced form of crafting and in-game world-building), so even without external third-party developers, the world still has room to grow, as it has been in The King of Spades project.

    Personally, I don't think the project is too "ambitious", maybe a lot of people have doubts because it's a new idea, and something possibly revolutionary. But if new, ambitious ideas weren't scary or risky, then everybody would be doing them.

    So, to answer your question, no, the project won't fail if we don't have external developer support, although the gaming universe will be much smaller than it would've been if other developers jumped on board.

    The project may seem too ambitious to those that don't know InEvoWare or the team behind it, but we've got it covered.

Pure, Unadulterated Combat mixed w/ Dark Humor (Comic Relief) and Satisfying, Non-Redundant Gameplay in a Post-Apocalyptic Setting.

We are currently in the process of giving the UI a graphics overhaul. The current user interface is a mere placeholder for what is to come. This screenshot does not yet represent the user interface that we are going for (in any shape or form), since it was purely for testing purposes. Due to the simplicity of our editor, we will be able to make quick & effective changes in the coming week.

6 months of research has gone into research on coding for an intermediate-level, C-based IDE, and a separate, unlimited layered map editor, which together form a perfect match for working on our project. Now that we have a solid engine and game layout with a playable map, we can begin adding NPC dialogues, combat sequences, and improved gui graphics.

Project: Rising Wasteland will initially be released on PC, although very simple porting methods are available for OSX and Linux already. We plan on releasing the game on all three platforms. We are using the same engine as Gemini Rue, except tweaked with various extensions and scripts that compliment a classic Fallout 2 style, 3rd-Person RPG. The good thing about using this engine is that there are lots and lots of modules, articles and support on every known issue and obstacle. We have already sorted through the grit, and have a solid foundation to get things going very quickly. In other words, the main coding headaches are a thing of the past. We are ready to get the ball rolling much quicker.

If we truly have proper funding, then we will purchase a Kinect and the appropriate software (already mapped out) to control any 3D model and record our own movements for characters in the game. This would truly add a realism to the game that is above and beyond.

There is a lot of drive and passion for this project, due to the team's identical fascination with the Post-Apocalyptic RPG genre. The entire reason that we feel our project will stand out (even against major studios with millions in their coffers), is that we refuse to settle for a cliché storyline and linear gameplay. Overall we do a great job of making use of all of the resources available online, however, there is a lot of room for premium content. The goal is to have a balance of the old school Fallout-inspired gameplay, with a healthy mix of innovative features to throw the player off. We won't give away any spoilers, but upon funding, we say it's a safe bet that you will enjoy our ideas. We tend to enjoy comic relief, twisted humor and enjoy coming up with outside-the-box encounters, quests, and ways to make the game have high replay value. Our goal on this Kickstarter is to post as many updates as possible, so that you can tell that our hearts are in this 100%.

You will play as either a male or female young adult, starting off in the outskirts of the small, yet highly fortified, town of Stellvia. We do not wish to give you any spoilers, so this will slightly limit what we have to say. We will say that you will have multiple choices that highly affect your place of residence and the tribesman in the outskirts. We will also not reveal why your character's features greatly contrast the mutated, three-fingered tribe.

There are mobsters guarding the town. These are no ordinary raider calibre guards; these are the real deal, and you will surely die if challenge/taunt them in the beginning of the game. Over time you can make a difference in how things are run in Stellvia (for the better or worse). We will be careful to make certain that even the most demented player will appreciate the cringe-worthy choices that are offered throughout the game. It's not like you'll lose karma in real life, right?

Chests will contain completely random content (based on your player level, to prevent traveling far ahead). You will not be able to use certain items until you are a high enough level, and/or satisfy stat requirements. Luckily, with a high enough crafting/makeshift skill, you will be able to design your own superior weaponry. If you're rich from gambling, then you can pay a blacksmith to craft for you. You can make the process cheaper by having the right materials (otherwise there is a steep fee if he has to gather it).

We will have one scene where a group of mobsters challenge their boss on account of him cussing them out and telling them that they are *insert expletive* for not scouting out to see how a dog was left in the middle of the outskirts sliced in half. This is, of course, a foreshadowing of events to come. You can save the dog on the rare event that you catch the unsettling group in the act at the right time (we love animals too). Other related events in Stellvia follow this gruesome scene.

There will be a scene with a guard outside of a cave near the outskirts, who says: "yea, I got 'chosen' to guard this cave. I don't know what in God's name is making those screaming noises, but if you come running out, I swear to God I will shoot the hell out of whatever horrific monster is following you. Go on in."

Of course, there will be some amusing dialogue options to follow his speech as well.

This project will be in a 3rd-person perspective. We have already jumped through the hoops in terms of rendering and importing characters into the game engine. We are able to resize any character simply by typing 1 line of code, so we don't have to have the character one perfect resolution ahead of time.

All character will have detailed, realistic idle animations that are meant to really have the player immersed, despite the 3rd-person perspective. We have already researched voice actors that will take a nice cut from this Kickstarter. There are people who have professional mics, and/or voice act for a living. We realize that bad voice acting is usually associated with a "bad game".

Part of the deal the mobs/organized raiders made with towns is that in exchange for food/resources, they would help guard the townsfolk from creatures/other raiders of the night. The other part of the deal was that townsfolk weren't allowed any weapon beyond a regular pistol with non-piercing ammunition. Anything found like this was confiscated and there were gun checks every couple of months.

This was so that the mobs could secretly/slowly take over the town (sometimes, unless they are lazy) and take full control and advantage over everyone using guerrilla force if necessary. It slowly becomes a mild form of slavery.

Each town will be different. For example, not every town will has to have a general store, a hospital and a residential area in different configurations. Instead, the main character can open/start these in the towns that lack them (either by personal funds or deals with other towns). Over time, you'll be able to see the amount of progress being made thanks to that one industry, etc.

These ideas will either be implemented by the full 1.0 release, through a patch, or by an expansion pack.

The US Dollar and Euro have dropped so much in value that the world calls to replace the global trading standard from US dollars to Chinese Yuan's. China starts flooding the global market with US bonds, which are still relatively valuable. Now other countries, including Europe (and in particular, the middle east) are buying them like hotcakes to own pieces of our economy. The US' economy then depleted further; the dollar now worthless, causing extreme inflation. 

     Fearing another depression, most people withdrew their life savings. Faith in the economy reached new depths, which made the situation during the Great Depression seem like a kiddy pool. Banks crashed and closed, unable to pay back those who had their faith and life savings ripped away. A broke United States began shutting down military bases in every country and region it had once stepped foot on; Europe, Japan, Korea, Africa—every last one of them, even Guantanamo Bay in Cuba. When asked about the prisoners held there, the base commander, a Marine Corps General, had men knock down the walls of the facility as the last of US forces withdrew from Cuba, releasing them into the Cuban populous. He then answered with another question: "what prisoners?"

     The Cubans were too weak to respond beyond their president condemning our actions publicly at the last United Nations Summit, held in France. The US, providing more than 40% of NATO’s funding until recently, was also not able to keep up with payments. NATO collapsed shortly after the UN’s disband. 

     Legitimate trade became almost nonexistent, as many countries were too fiscally weak to continue importing goods. Many companies closed down, which took away their ability to have legitimate exports for a trading contract. Upon China’s flooding of the market with US bonds, the US responded immediately by commandeering many of the larger corporations, then shut down all overseas factories, thus closing trade with China all together. This shattered China’s economy. The veritable destruction of the American economy and the US Dollar caused a global collapse. This collapse would be later known simply as “the Crisis”. What began as China spiting the United States over debt would snowball into something more horrific and unforeseen, rocking the world from the West and the East. 

 “Everyone drinks more during a recession; they want to forget.” – Christian Audigier

     Inflation causes economic and financial issues for corporations. Stocks are worthless. Budget cuts. This is because the McDouble at McDonalds went from $0.99 to $127.4498 (yes, 127 dollars and almost 45 cents). Unfortunately, companies couldn't quite afford to keep the manpower, with individual items on the value menus being worth as much as what a small tank of gas would have cost in 2044. Massive layoffs ensued, causing unemployment to skyrocket to 57%. Much like the market crash in 2007, and automotive bailout in 2009, similar events took place, but a bailout at this point would have cost tens of trillions of dollars; costs that every piggy bank in the US and all of Europe and Africa’s combined GDPs then multiplied several times would still not be able to cover. 

     The US government at the time tried to revalue the American currency, but the Chinese responded by doing the same with the Yuan, crushing what seemed like a promising idea. Even China hadn’t responded, that would have brought the price of the McDouble down to $52.712. Though a substantial improvement, it was still not a viable solution for the long run. On the global scale, America no longer had the money to continue funding the United Nations as it once used to. Up to that point, many countries could not pay their share for the United Nations, putting the fiscal responsibility and burden on America. America at this point was paying 37% of the UN. The price tag was now at an inflated price of $412 Billion. Even if it was a previous 2010 price tag of $3 billion, such an amount at this point would be just enough to support a family of four in America for one year with the same standard of living as that same family would have enjoyed in 2010.

“Occasionally the tree of Liberty must be watered with the blood of Patriots and Tyrants.” – Thomas Jefferson

     In the United States, a political post-Crisis revolution was taking place; one that would push the left further left, and the right further right. Liberals felt like more social benefits and reform of current social services would help improve the situation, citing Roosevelt’s intervention during the great depression. 

     Democrats have been pushed to socialists. The Socialist Party of America wins over anyone leaning left.  The socialism sweeping across America is unable to satiate everyone’s call for more money, benefits, protection, ending this short-lived revolution, only to make way for another one. 

     The Communist Party of America changes its aims strictly towards “workers’ rights, peace, equality, and justice”, also changing its name to the Agrarian Party, disclaiming they stand for the lesser known meaning of the word:  originally based on the Roman “agrarian laws”, which called for redistribution of land from the rich citizens who owned more land, to the poor.  Additionally, “Agrarian” sounds less extreme than “Communist”, easing the wavering conscious of those wanting to commit to a cause but not wanting to accept the extreme measures this move would call for. 

     But for this new party, when it focused on the term “equality” in their new aim, equality became the blatant and unconstitutional redistribution of wealth from rich to the poor.  The “1%”, as endangered as it already was, grew endangered.  Agrarian bankers and adept Agrarian hackers ransacked the accounts with programs and viruses that wired money to registered Agrarians, claiming they were simply being good Samaritans.  These bankers and hackers would then become known as Samaritans, perverting the definition of the term while simultaneously collapsing conglomerates and corporations overnight; thereby proving they were in love with the idea of the Agrarian Party, rather than the intended values.  The remaining wealthy salvaged what they could and took off with entourages of servants, crew and families to their various vacation homes in hopes of escaping this new world order. Some never made it to their destinations, while others arrived to angry mobs calling for what they believed to be retribution.

     The Republican party soon followed in this manner.  They began going back to early 20th century ideals of isolationism.  Although xenophobia was a dominating posture in the post-Crisis United States, the Republican party took this to new extremes.  They began openly calling themselves the new Isolationist Party.  Many of their new ideals and propaganda perpetuated xenophobia in the country.  They called for the repealing of NAFTA with Mexico, as the overall culture was far more alien than that of Canada. 

Isolationism ends up winning the hearts and minds of many Americans, but absolutely repels the Agrarians, causing tension on Capitol Hill.  With an Isolationist President pushing for conservative agendas and violent Agrarian opposition, civil conflicts and unrest soon followed.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

We are made up of a micro-sized development team, so there is always a chance of a delay by 3-6 months (from the estimated release date). Luckily, we have a solid, bug-free engine already, so it really is just a matter of utilizing funding for appropriate costs that allow us to work on the project everyday. The entire goal is to have backers so satisfied with their purchase, that they are thrilled for us to release an expansion.

Initially the game will be digital copies only, and we will attempt to become Greenlit on Steam shortly after a full game release.

Contagion is a Cooperative Zombie Survival Horror FPS game built on a heavily modified version of Valve's Source Engine. The game challenges players to conserve ammo, rely on your team, and work together to survive the zombie onslaught. Players will face traditional (NPC) zombies as well as former teammates who have been passed on and joined the walking deceased, fueling the carnage to come. The game has been in development for two and a half years after recovering from a disagreement with the original co-founder thus the delay.

Features

  • Play alone offline or online with friends (up to 8 players)
  • Multiple game modes.
  • Play as either Human or Zombie
  • Randomly generated weapon, ammo, and item placement.
  • Unpredictable and intelligent zombie spawning system that adjusts to the current situation..
  • A pathing and objective system that has you returning to the same map repeatedly without losing that fresh feel.
  • Full Steamworks support. (Trading Cards, Achievements etc.)

Gameplay as a human

  • FPS mechanics.
  • Escape Game-mode: work together or alone to complete objectives and make it to a randomly generated escape point.
  • Extraction Game-mode: Work together to find, protect, and extract helpless (NPC) survivors to a safe location.
  • Hunted Game-mode: With supplies low, fend off fellow survivors who would like nothing more than to be sifting through your pockets in this free for all, last man or woman standing game-mode.
  • Use stealth, distractions, and strategies to covertly avoid zombies or take them down quietly now that ammo is a rare and valuable commodity.

Gameplay as a zombie

  • Call upon and create your own zombie hoard to better break through fortifications and hunt your prey.
  • Selectively let the AI take control of your character so as to blend in with the mindless hoard in an attempt to camouflage yourself and avoid being singled out while also having the jump on unsuspecting survivors..
  • When the opportunity presents itself Grapple a player and sink your teeth into flesh causing both damage and a chance of infection which comes in handy if they manage to escape.
  • Zombie Vision enhances the senses allowing you to better see in the dark and track your prey.

We would also like to point out that Contagion will fully support Steam's Trading Card system and will release soon after launch.

  • Why can't I pledge using PayPal?

    Kickstarter uses Amazon Payments because it allows them to mark charges to be charged at a later date if a project is successful or if it isn't then release it so no one is charged.

    PayPal cannot do this and so does not meet the requirements needed to handle a KS project. They are also not currently built to handle crowd-funding which is something they are looking into.

    Until this changes PayPal payments cannot be paid directly to KS making it difficult to use for KS project funding. We are currently researching the many ways others have done it and so far many of them have cost time and money and don't fit our current schedule. We are still looking and have a few ideas we're bouncing around so it's not exactly a dead option at the moment.

    THAT SAID!!! There are other options in the form of Pre-paid Visa/MasterCard's or Virtual Credit Cards (listed at the top of the page). Can even set yourself up with a PayPal Prepaid Card!

  • What's with the Stretch Goals?

    Our stretch goals were put in place to help us achieve our goals faster as it's all coming out of pocket and what we're able to earn from sales knowing that we couldn't afford high end Marketing and PR.

    $75k - With this we can afford to purchase the 3rd party license bundles we'd require to begin work on Mac & Linux now rather than much later. It's pretty straight forward, we don't have the money in the bank to do it right now and paying developers for their hard work in creating Contagion must be a priority (If we reach the Stretch goal the licenses will be paid for immediately).

    $100k - You might be asking "Why are we paying for free DLC?" our plan is to provide free content and avoid DLC packs that cost you money which has grown into a more accepted trend. $25k more will not cover the cost of our planned New Game-Mode (Flatline), 2 New Maps, 4 New Characters, and 4 New Weapons but it will allow us to put the right people on it sooner and get it out there faster.

    $125k - Same as before we have another Game-Mode (Cruise), 2 New Maps, 4 New Characters, and 4 New Weapons which helps us finish up our plans for a total of 16 unique characters (may be seeing old faces from Zombie Panic), Game-Modes, and more. It again does not cover the costs but helps us to get it done sooner without hurting quality.

    $150k - Why this stretch goal? We've been releasing free content for Zombie Panic: Source for 6 years. We want to do the same with Contagion but that requires paying developers. I can't help saying that this will definitely not cover 3months let alone 6 years but it does help us commit our money and whatever we earn from Kickstarter in doing so. It also helps with the buffer after Kickstarter & Amazon take their cuts of 5% and 3% from the entirety of our final amount pledged after charges have cleared. Part of this Includes additional support for peripherals and more like Oculus Rift, Steam Box, and more. We are going to work towards all these goals either way but there is no denying that we as a start-up could use a hand.

    $250k - We're eager to put Contagion on consoles but this is quite expensive. To even get the certifications needed (not going into development costs, ratings, Marketing, and PR on a whole new level) this amount wouldn't cover it. We're asking for help where both the community and ourselves can put our money together to get it done.

    Those who think we're greedy even asking for $50k at this point should really do the research. We've already spent our family savings, just make it by with a second full time job to pay our way this far, and have not wasted a dime of it. If it's still confusing feel free to contact us or choose not to pledge. We won't be offended and for those that support us through videos, sharing links, pledging. We thank you for your understanding!

    Last updated: Tue, Oct 1 2013 5:20 pm EDT

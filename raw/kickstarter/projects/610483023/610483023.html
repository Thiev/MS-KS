A natural language processing text-based adventure game using unrecognised commands to learn, becoming better the more play it.

Aaron's Aardvark Adventure Zone is a Text-Based Adventure Game for the modern age intended to revolutionise a stale and aging genre, making a totally unique role-playing game.  The core idea of its development is this:  rather than programming then releasing, as such games were made before the Internet, the game is constantly available to play during development, with a process of amassing information about what people are entering, meaning the range of commands is pooled from the imaginations of hundreds, rather than just one or two.  Combine with that sophisticated language processing and you get a powerful storytelling tool; if every command is catered for, a much greater range of creativity is catered for too when playing the game.

The game is available to play - totally free of charge - at www.aaazgame.co.uk so check it out to see what your money will be spent on.  Currently it is in demo form and though it doesn't have every command by a long way that's because production hasn't taken place yet and not many people have played the game.  Once people have played it the ball will be set rolling and the ingenuity and creativity of the range of actions available will take off.

This is the story of a theme park focused on aardvarks run by Aaron A. Aaronson III, overrun by Mr Prickmeister, a jealous porcupine (he's a bit of a prick), but is actually about the player's descent into insanity and the underworld, becoming a space-pirate, getting attacked by (and not with) a carrot and trying out a lot of verbs on a range of aardvarks.  The development of the game is a game in itself; letting one's imagination run riot in this riotous world and using bizarre twisted logic to solve puzzles in interesting and mind-bending ways.

You can't be expected to contribute for nothing and we have a range of great rewards for you; in fact, 8 of them for £25, ranging up to obvious additions to the game for very reasonable prices. Since so much of the key programming for the game has already been done and I need only to add content I'm not asking for much and as such have been able to price all the rewards accordingly lowly.

The game itself is and is intended to remain free to play but there's plenty worth paying for. For £5 is the basic contributor reward, which allows you to take part in the metagame of contributing to the game itself. Many a fun hour has already been spent trying to see what the game doesn't already have. The game is tailored to people who contribute this. And there are plenty more cheap rewards, from large bundles of artwork to recognition for your contribution to even more games!

If you're willing to splash out a bit more you can really make your mark on games development history and get messages you have written or even yourself added to the game in prominent positions.

If the game is successfully funded, development will take place from July to September (and potentially longer) 2013. Now that most of the essential programming has been done all that needs to be added is content so those three months will be three months of constant hard work making the game bigger, better and more sophisticated. And the more money we get, the bigger it will be. Adding this stuff is easy, it just takes time, and that's why we're able to give so much away for so little money.

That's a very good question.  This is essentially a one-man project.  I've been helped and will continue to be helped by a large and supportive group but as all the output of the game is written, it should all be sourced from the same author.  I'm a Computer Scientist at Oxford with a bloody long summer holiday and a passion for computer games.  I have been working on Aaron's Aardvark Adventure Zone in my own time and forcing friends to be the "players" for a while now and think the game's ready to be taken to the next level of development - the global stage.

I have years of experience making games (as well as much more boring software) but that isn't enough.  What I bring to Aaron's Aardvark Adventure Zone is a Mathematical approach to rigorous, algorithmic development - essential for the natural language processing aspect - and the creativity of someone who has been making games, as just one of many creative media, for years.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

No games development is devoid of challenges, especially when making such an innovative game. It's the challenge that makes development fun. That said, you can already see for yourself that in the case of Aaron's Aardvark Adventure Zone, most of the core programming (the skeleton structure on which the game has been built) is done, so there will be much less risk involved in actually programming the game. Adding content is easy and shouldn't cause any snags in the development process, apart from trying to come up with new puns, until someday I run out of sarcastic error messages, in which case I don't know what I would do.

A significant struggle is the processing of all those user data and more development may be needed on the architecture through which we obtain these data, but that is a challenge we will not necessarily need to face and if it is needed it'll be relatively straightforward to overcome. I have been working on this game (on and off) for three years in my own time and in that time have done development work at multiple companies, as well as honing my Computer Science skills. I hope the game itself will convince you that I have the ability to pull it off. I want to do my bit for culture.

The main risk, to be quite honest, is tea overdose.

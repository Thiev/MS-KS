Victory at Sea is a naval combat game for mobile, PC & Mac where players take command of fleets in a desperate attempt to win the war.

Navigate your fleets around the Atlantic, Pacific and the Mediterranean theatres of war and engage in real time epic naval combat. Victory at Sea combines a simple touch control system with a large and detailed campaign that contains vast numbers of historically accurate ship variants, the likes of which have never been seen before on a mobile device. Victory at Sea will be easy to play but difficult to master and will be one of the largest and most engaging war combat games ever.

Our team here at Evil Twin Artworks have been developing games for mobile devices since the beginning of the app store. We are gamers with a penchant for strategic games and are disappointed with the current lack of in depth games in this genre on mobile devices. We want Victory at Sea to become the massive, immersive combat game all mobile devices deserve, taking full advantage of it’s intuitive touch control system. 

Victory at Sea is a highly successful table top game made by Mongoose Publishing, the company behind the Judge Dredd Miniatures Game, A Call to Arms: Star Fleet and Babylon 5: A Call to Arms games. The mobile game will encapsulate the vast amounts of information behind the table top game which will create a massive video game. The iOS / Android versions will also inherit the fast flowing rules that allows novices and veteran gamers alike to enjoy recreating the epic struggles between the mighty fleets of the era.

The funding from Kickstarter allow us to:

ABOUT VICTORY AT SEA

THE CAMPAIGN

Choose your theatre of war - the Atlantic, the Mediterranean or the Pacific. Manoeuvre your fleets into position ready to strike.

THE CONFLICT

Deploy ships from your available fleet to engage the enemy. In real time steer your ships using an innovative stance system that allows you to focus on individual ships while being able to order the rest of the fleet to fight independently. 

MANAGE YOUR FLEET 

Once combat is complete you’ll be able to use Warbonds to purchase new and better ships, as well as refit or salvage damaged ones. You will have access to a vast amount of historical vessels to help immerse yourself in the epic struggle of global warfare. You can unlock historical battles such as the Battle of the River Plate, the Battle of Midway, and sink the Bismarck in the Battle of the Denmark Strait. 

COMPETE AGAINST THE REST OF THE WORLD 

There will be an additional map that draws the results of battles from all the players across the world, showing how much is controlled by the axis fleets and how much is controlled by allies. 

ACHIEVEMENTS 

You will be rewarded for you endeavors with unique victory at sea achievements and medals.

There are many challenges in completing a project like this, but creating a fun and engaging game that is perfectly balanced and difficult to put down is the greatest challenge. Much of this is done by finessing statistics and vigorously play testing, fortunately this has been to a large degree in the miniature war game version, where stats rules have been finessed by a community of gamers for many years. We also want to involve the backers as much as possible in feeding back and helping us shape this into the best mobile war game on the market.

We also have an experienced team that specialises in every aspect of creating games for the mobile market, ready to take on all the challenges this development will throw at them.

FAQ

  • Does 5 pounds get the game or just a ship for the game?

    £5 gets the mobile version of the game with 1 unlocked ship. The PC/Mac version comes with all ships unlocked by default.

  • I see in the video ships firing at point blank range. Clearly ahistorical but I understand you want things to be pretty and visible on the IOS. So how are ranges of guns handled? Is everything massively scaled?

    We will be using all the stats and mechanics from the victory at sea game and range is handled in the same way as the game. The video and pics are more designed as a illustration and the final game would be more of a realistic sim.

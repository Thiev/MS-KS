Ultimate iOS snowball game with epic storyline where you save the world from aliens, zombies, lawn gnomes and Seven Deadly Snow Ninjas!

Spicy Rocket is what blossomed from searching the world over for the best upcoming talent possible. Harvesting the cream of the crop of students fresh out of colleges worldwide, and passionate professionals, creating a company capable of introducing new designers into the market and letting us attain our goal of creating games that matter.

See, at Spicy Rocket, entertainment is pretty awesome, sure, but games can be so much more. We strive to push the boundaries of what the definition of a game even is, and our quest has led us to some pretty interesting places!

That is where we came from; who we are, now, is a company focused upon story, upon sending a message and ensuring all our games redefine what quality means. From our first game, soon to be released Giddy Cook, to our current project you see here, Saligia, or our future designs, our team is dedicated to bringing awesome storylines and meaning to game genres which have never even be considered to use such before!

Also we promise to donate 5% of The Saligia Kickstarter profits to help fund other Kickstarter Projects. For more kindly visit http://kickingitforward.org

Saligia centers it's storyline around a father telling his child a story about how, when he was a kid himself, he got into a few snowball fights - this story might be more than a tiny bit embellished, and the distinction between how much actually happened, and how much was a child's imagination, is never really laid out clearly.

In the game itself, this begins with a highly intuitive set of controls for throwing, dodging, blocking, and moving around the game using touch controls. While a great deal of Saligia is focused upon PvP snowball death matches, the single player story mode includes even more:

Both of them will show how your friends are doing in comparison to you. Have you unlocked some special achievement and your friend yet hasn’t? Well, you’ll surely get higher on the leaderboard. That’s what we at Spicy Rocket call a healthy competition! In addition, the multiplayer mode offers you to play with your friend or just a quick auto-match (with a stranger). Be sure, that all of that doesn’t cause any awful lags or delays, it is fully real-time fun!

The various levels host a wide range of concepts, starting from something as simple as just tossing snowballs across a playground, to fending off an army of malicious lawn gnomes with a steampunk snow fortress, to bringing down a conspiracy-theorist wizard riding a fire breathing dragon.

A little girl comes home, having been pelted with snowballs, and runs into her father's waiting arms. As fathers are want to do, he of course consoles her, and uses it as an excuse to tell an elaborate story about how, when he was her age, the very same thing happened to him... with perhaps a few minor embellishments.

What started as a girl his age helping him up and teaching him the basics of how to defend himself in a snowball fight, quickly spirals out of control, soon leading to discoveries of an army of (possibly) sentient lawn gnomes seeking world domination, a cold war between the illuminati and templar conspiracies to shape the world as they see fit, the beginnings of the zombie apocalypse, and seven mysterious deadly snow ninjas - each representing one of the seven deadly sins.

Saligia is divided into seven story chapters, the first of which will be available upon the game's release, with new chapters being made available every few weeks, bringing with them a furthering of the depth of the plot, new faces and enemies, as well as new mini-games and level content.

Unlike most games that try to pull off a morality system, however, Saligia doesn't take the traditional black and white "good versus evil" route. Instead, we focus upon the concept of "all things in moderation". Too much pride and it leads to a fall, but without taking pride in your work, how can you ever hope to better yourself?

As the Avatar of SLOTH, Tim is a master of finding excuses to get out of doing things, or using his imagination to pretend he has taken on plentiful adventures. Tim's world is one where he is the hero, alongside his trusty stuffed toy dragoness, Obsidia, fighting against an endless array of unseen foes, always lurking in the shadows just out of sight.

While Tim's imagination can get a little carried away at times, and his multitude of conspiracy theories about reality are more than slightly over the top, it might just turn out that he's not entirely wrong, and that maybe someone really is out to get him after all...

Armed with a set of weighted dice which always land on the side he wants them to, and his multiclassed wizard/ninja character sheet, Tim is ready to take on the world!... So long as that doesn't involve having to actually, you know, do anything.

Well, let's say we were more than a little ambitious. If something's worth doing, it's worth doing so well it redefines the very definition of what "well done" even means!

Unfortunately, reality isn't so kind. We got about 70% of the way done finishing Saligia, and then those silly little things started to show up. You know the ones, bills going overdue, people needing to eat, hiring new talent actually requiring paying them, that kind of stuff. Ah, c'est la vie.

Fortunately, we no longer live in a world where you have to dance to the strings of a larger corporation, these days we have a better way: with Kickstarter and your help, we can retain all of our company values, producing only the absolute top-quality games that we would be proud to call our own, and without sacrificing that vision of beauty in both form and function that we feel our players deserve.

Were we to rely on traditional methods, we'd be forced to sacrifice far too much of what we can bring to the table, and once you fall into that pit, it's near impossible to crawl back out of once more. Gone is the age of being forced to push out mediocrity for want of a market share; with funding from your pledges, we can deliver a game that will show the world what the mobile market can attain with the right minds and a little push in the right direction, and deliver the kind of game we've only dreamed of playing!

Any funding you supply goes directly towards our capacity to provide the best features we can offer. With our current design model, we can ensure that our primary game will be able to be released with only £10,000. This will be divided between things like hiring an additional artist for producing art assets faster, covering bare-essential living costs for our employees, software licensing, and various similar things that we absolutely need to have to finish our project.

Anything beyond our stated goal of £10,000 will be spent upon rapidly accelerating the rate at which we're able to introduce features, and adding features we'd deemed neat, but not necessary for a great experience.

So what goes into our game design that sets it apart from the crowd? I'm glad you asked, self! Oh, and the people who are reading this. I guess they can know too.

Gameplay first: What good is a game that isn't fun? It kind of defeats the whole purpose, doesn't it? For Saligia, just throwing snowballs isn't "good enough" for us; if you can't bean a dragon in the face with a snowball, or have a western showdown with waterguns, we haven't done our job right.

Deep, rich story and a vibrant world: Just because a mobile game should be able to be picked up, played for five minutes, and dropped again at will, doesn't mean you should want to stop playing; it should have a storyline that can match the best books out there for being a page-turner!

Characters you can believe: Sure, they may be over the top and as many as they come, but there's a big difference between "eccentric" and "I call shenanigans". At Spicy Rocket, we believe that the willing suspension of disbelief should be a natural, unconscious act, not one that has to be consciously invoked.

Beauty: Be it of the eyes, the ears, or the controls themselves, it's our belief that a player should be in perpetual awe, but not distracted from the gameplay itself. We strive for that fine line between marveling in wonder, and not even realizing you're playing a game any longer.

Morality and Education: Games aren't just for kids, but that doesn't mean both kids and adults can't learn from them! From random, useful snippets of information that people can use in everyday life, to providing role models and an interactive display of how to live with other people without being at each other's throats, we take this part of our mandate seriously!

We've come a long way, and are roughly about 70% of the way done developing Saligia. Here's an idea of what we have done so far:

  • Script for whole game with 7 chapters and 70 scenes
  • Character designs
  • Regular level designs completed
  • First and second epic boss battles designed
  • Needed characters modeled
  • Playable alpha demo available
  • Artificial inteligence completed
  • Realtime multiplayer
  • Interactive sound system
  • Menu and map designs
  • 100% of game intro illustrated
  • 40% of 1st chapter illustrated
  • About 70% of the mechanics designed, programmed, implemented and tested
  • Level modeling, props, etc. about 50% completed

In short, the game's technically playable as is, but needs a lot more play testing to be as enjoyable as possible, and we're a bit behind on the art department side of things due to having only one 3D and one 2D artist to work with. A lot of the RPG elements are lingering somewhere in between the "finished design" stage and the "actually implemented" stage, as well.

With this in mind, the bulk of the pledge money we receive will be going towards an additional artist to vastly speed up production, and for testing/revising gameplay.

 

I'm sure we could talk about all the neat stuff you'll get access to for supporting Saligia, but we both know that you'd rather see it, so let's get to the goodies.

 

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

With Saligia, our challenges are mostly along the lines of managing to chew how much we bite off. Our team's more than capable of handling the technical challenges that we face, with the bulk of our issues rooted in being perhaps a little bit too ambitious for our own good.

When you strive for perfection, at what point do you mark it as "done" when there's always more that can be done? To this end, we've isolated which parts are absolutely required to get our message out, and keep the game as fun as possible in its core. From there, we've developed an upgradable system which, as we gain sales, we can add additional content such as new chapters, minigames, and features.

We've already covered virtually all of our start-up costs out of our own pocket, which means any money invested into this project goes directly into ensuring that Saligia will have more great features available upon release. Saligia will be playable and top notch right out of the box, but with each new feature added, we'll be raising the industry standard.

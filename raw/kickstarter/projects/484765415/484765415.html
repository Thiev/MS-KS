A shattered Sun, an eternal night, a frightened boy, a wish upon a star, a journey begins with only his courage and an unlikely friend

Thank you everyone who has supported us so far, we are grateful beyond words, but like how a Star has different stages within its lifecycle, our Kickstarter project has different milestones which, when reached, will allow us to accomplish a great deal more, and as you can see below, there is still a way to go!

Starlight is an adventure platform game riddled with puzzles, traps and opportunities for exploration, where you play as both Timothy Brave, a boy afraid of the dark, and Altair, a shooting star, on a journey through a world almost completely devoid of light. 

In a unique experience, the player controls both Timothy and Altair simultaneously and independently, facing many obstacles that the two must overcome. Some of these obstacles will hinder only Timothy, for example the darkness will drain his courage and prevent him from moving. Other obstacles will affect only Altair, such as reflective surfaces (like mirrors and water), which will repel him and limit his ability to illuminate and comfort Timothy, potentially ending the journey for them both. 

Players:    

 - Single Player

Game Length:

 - The initial play through would not take longer than 45 minutes, but as as the game expands and more levels become available, this time will increase.

 - The game encourages exploration and repeating previous levels to get uncover all the options, so despite our detailed level integration plan, without testing the complete version, it is hard to determine how long it would take to complete.

Target Platforms:

In order of production, our target platforms are: 

 - iPad    This is our intended launch platform, using the bigger screen to get the most out of the unique gameplay.

 - iPod/iPhone    With enough funding, the extra time and resources will let us launch to the smaller devices too. 

 -  Android    After gauging the success and desire for the game after launch and feedback from kick-starter, we will buy the tools required to make the android version too.

 - (Hopefully) Nintendo 3DS    This game has a great deal of potential, and no console will bring out as much as the Nintendo DS family, so we hope to launch it on that platform sometime next year. 

Estimated Release Dates:    

 - We intend to launch the iPad version of Starlight on April 17th, 2014.

 - If we reach our secondary funding goal, we aim to simultaneously launch the iPod/iPhone version, the extra money will help us speed up the required tweaks and testing. 

 - Depending on the initial success of the project, reaching our tertiary funding will set our Android release for June 2014.

 - We will wait a year to determine the value of future development, but intend to announce the games development for the DS by April 2015.

Target Market:   

 - Our initial market research show that children between the ages of 7 - 12 would be most drawn to the game.

 - Our Alpha testing and early design showed that teenage girls between the ages of 13 - 16 would show high levels of interest too.

 - Combing the results of our research and broader testing, along with our intended development, we predict that the market of young women between 18 - 23 would be appealed to by the games design, style and mechanics. 

The dynamic difficulty of the game (where the harder to find levels are the harder to play levels) and the many options for exploration and character development make it diverse enough for a variety of market groups, but we have isolated the core market above.

Target Audience:

 - Parents (primarily the mothers, but we have a different approach that can be used to market to the fathers of players too) of the children and teenagers in our Target Market

 - Teenage girls directly and young women between the ages 15 - 23

Target Prices: (Currently estimates as production costs depend on Kickstarters success)

 - The launch game, including the Tutorial, Level 1 and the first mini-game at least, will be a free to play trail.

 - The full launch game, going all the way to the end of the first set of levels (referred to as the first play through), will be significantly longer, and cost about £1.99. (This will be free for donators). 

 - The additional DLC will come as area bundles, that branch off from the different points on the original levels. Each bundle will in itself contain branches, and the cost would be close to that of the first play through, at £1.99 or perhaps more, depending on how far the branches go (The initial play through is not as long as the additional branches).

All these prices are subject to change as we factor in any additional costs of production, but the further we are along our Stretch Goals, the less likely any of these prices will increase.

When the Sun shattered and the pieces fell to the Earth, the world was dashed into an endless night. Timothy Brave was just a boy, afraid of his own shadow, kneeling by his bed while his nightlight began to dim. Desperate and afraid, he looked upon that shooting star, and made a simple wish:

"Please don't let the light go out. Please don't let the dark get in."

To his astonishment, the star came to him, introducing itself as Altair and filling his room with light. Timothy was filled with joy, dancing around to the light of the star, but Altair's miracle was not over.

"Timothy, this is all I can do to push back the darkness, but the light I cast will not last. The only light that will is the light of the Sun."

Timothy was scared and confused. He clung to his blanket and begged the star not to go.

"Like that lamp, my power will fade, but if you help me, you won't have to worry ever again."

From here, the star proposed a deal. Altair promised to light Timothy's path and never be far from his side, if in return, Timothy would guide Altair through this alien world and help him gather the fallen fragments of the Sun. 

Timothy was reluctant at first, but with the warm glow of the star by his side, he felt a strange kind of courage build up inside of him. He left the comforts of his room, and with Altair he felt he could face the darkness of the house, but when the time came to leave, the dark shadows dancing along the walls sent him into a state of panic.

But Altair illuminated the path to reveal the source to be nothing more than a cluster of toys, casting unusually large shadows. 

"See Timothy, I can always show you that there is nothing you need to fear."

Altair led Timothy to the window, where the faint lights of the city and the distant lights of the Sun Fragments could be seen, glittering in the distance. The path was not absolute darkness after all. This realisation gave Timothy conviction; he stood up, walked to the door, and took that first step into the world of Starlight.

Objectives:

Traverse the Starlight universe with Timothy and Altair, collecting the fallen pieces of the Sun, until they can finally be returned to the sky.

Obstacles:

Timothy is obstructed by areas of low light, physical objects, fearful images, and unknown events (sounds, flashes, etc) that occur within the darkness.

Altair is obstructed by reflective surfaces, his power weakens with any contact with water and he is pulled in by live cables and other darker energy sources you encounter throughout the game.

Character Development:

As the game progresses, Timothy is able to do more daring things, and stay in the darkness longer periods of time before panicking.

As Altair takes in star shards, he gains abilities, similar to those of the Sun throughout the different stages of its life cycle (e.g. Red Giant, Supernova, Neutron Star). The different powers have different effects on the environment, which allow Timothy to do different things as well. 

Summary:

Starlight is not just a standard adventure game; it is composed of real-time reactions to events and puzzle-solving elements. Timothy and Altair must gather the fallen pieces of the Sun and eventually return it to the sky, but Timothy doesn't just fear the darkness, he makes it "real" allowing him to interact with shadows, and even dropping into panic attacks if they are too threatening. This makes him dependent on Altair, who can freely manipulate the shape of the darkness, casting light onto various objects and projecting shadows across the walls while awakening new powers to affect the environment in different ways. However, Altair has his own weaknesses, unlike Timothy he cannot move or touch solid objects, and reflective surfaces and flowing water repel or weaken him, so Timothy must clear paths, find alternate routes and even face the darkness in order to help Altair progress. 

Timothy can:

- Walk over shadows

- Climb over various obstacles

- Move physical objects

- Protect Altair from a variety of harmful elements

- Collect Star fragments and other treasures

Altair can:

- Illuminate and reveal the true nature of various threats, changing Timothy's emotional state

- Cast light in various shapes

- Manipulate or disintegrate shadows

- Comfort Timothy and give him courage

- Bloom flowers and return colour and radiance to the world

Controls:

Timothy is controlled using a virtual joystick on the touch screen of the intended device. With this you can make him run, jump, push and climb, and there are other buttons that will allow Timothy to use other skills as he attains them.

Altair can be dragged around the screen through touch-move events, activating his unique powers with multi-touch gestures. He can be used to cast a light in a particular direction, illuminate a vast area, or throw projectile balls of light called "Solar Flares", to light multiple areas at once. As with Timothy, more gestures become available to Altair over time. 

Since there will be times where the two must be separated, there will be a switch button that will make the camera follow Altair instead of Timothy, and a call button that will make Altair return (if possible) to Timothy's side. 

Progression:

You progress through the game one level at a time, especially on the first play through; however, instead of following a linear path, the game branches out as you awaken new abilities, you can use them to explore additional areas of the same maps, and even find portals to entirely different levels. Every original level (even the tutorial) branches off into at least one other level or area, or both. You are encouraged to replay levels and use the different powers to reach places you otherwise may not have known even existed. 

Because of this mechanic, after your first play through, the level select options will become available, and with DLC, more areas will be added to discover, until you find the path that leads to the games conclusion, having enough Sun Fragments to bring the Sun back. 

Darkness:

The darkness is not just something to be feared, but also explored. Many things are hidden in the shadows, and as Altair and Timothy progress, there will be ways to illuminate more of the darkness at once. 

Non-essential discoveries include dying flowers that can be refreshed, toys, books and notes in various forms that can be found around every level, and even elements of the story, explaining what happened to the world after the lights went out.

Bonuses:

For each discovery, non-essential, essential and everything in-between, the player gains points. Certain discoveries and even activities will earn specific rewards, "Medals" that can be seen on Timothy's wall in the title screen. 

Some rewards are just bragging rights, others add toward the completion of the game, but some give the player access to additional content, including levels, artwork, and even sneak peaks at our other projects.

In Timothy's room, items you find along your journey will gather and be viewable. In his bookshelf you will find notes, text and even pictures he found. On his floor the different toys will begin to gather, and on his table, the places he has visited will be drawn out for you to view again at any time.

Both characters have had many make-overs, and while we are happy with Timothy's design, we may still change Altair a couple more times.

Beneficial:

Toys will comfort Timothy, and give him more courage (as well as more maximum courage), and can be found in most levels in some way, shape or form. 

Precarious objects will often fall when Timothy goes passed them, scaring him directly, or indirectly if he does not see what it was that made the sound. Finding them with the Star or collecting them with Timothy will reassure him, and make at least some of the sounds he hears from the darkness less threatening. 

Detrimental 

Some shadows will be useful to Timothy, as long as Altair is not too close, he can use them to climb up to higher places, but others will take a menacing form, which will stop Timothy even getting close, and unless you can dispel the shadow or reveal its source, Timothy may not be able to progress at all. 

Creative Genius is a new group of game designers, artists and programmers. We fund the company with part-time work and our own savings, and our motto "When we're not working, we're working" is testament to the effort we put in, on and off the clock, but there's a limit to what we can do with this effort alone. For over a year we have been building up our ideas, our designs and even our team, but we have reached certain financial, space and time barriers that are hindering our progress. We need equipment, we need external help to refine our incomplete soundtrack, we need more space to work from, but most importantly, we need licenses for expensive software. With the equipment we have, we could finish this project in two or three months, but it'd take 4 to 5 months to afford all the software we need, and we would not be able to do everything until we get it, so what would take maybe 3 months, would be pushed closer to 6 or 7. 

With the funding, not only would we have the software we need, but with the new equipment, we would be able to speed up production, and not only have the game finished by our desired date of March 14th, but with a lot more content and to much higher quality. 

Without enough previous experience, a bank loan would be difficult to attain, and the focus of paying it back, would take our attention away from creating the best possible product. 

We've spent almost a year trying to secure government grants but it's tricky, a lot of them are region or category restricted. Our options were running dry when we found out about Kick starter. Through this site, we could show people our ideas, and raise the funds we "need" to make them into reality, as well as have the opportunity to raise more, and really bring out our concepts' full potential. Not only that, but the more money we get from individuals, instead of bulk amounts, the more interest there is in our idea, and the more confidence it gives us in its potential success. 

With the money we could raise, it would not just launch this idea, but us as a company, and we could see at least three of our ideas being launched by the end of 2014, and with really big things happening in 2015, we just need that little extra push before our private funds run out, which will mean we will have to pull back a little from our work as Creative Genius, and take up full time jobs just to keep the company going. 

We've seen what Kickstarter has done for others, and we know it can do just as much for us, so please, have a look at what we've got to offer, even if it's just a look, because this is only the beginning of much greater things to come.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

As a company, we are still quite young, but as a group, most of us have been working together for a while and we have experience with working on larger projects than this with tighter deadlines and it's given us both insight and confidence in what we are doing now.

The biggest issues we are looking at are:

License and permissions:
Before releasing we need to have all the licences and permissions from our platforms. The licence costs have been included in our "Essential Fund", and we're confident we can reach that, but permissions will come from content and technical requirements. We doubt they will stop us, but they might delay our release, we plan to finish the game by Late February to give us time to overcome any issues faced when trying to get permission to launch the app in April as planned.

Funding:
Don't be intimidated by the amount we're asking for. The "Essential Fund" is what we need to finish to project, but the "Stretch Goals" are what we need to make it truly great. We have estimated that the initial release will include five levels and a tutorial, plus some mini-games, and we will have to add the rest later as DLC (Downloadable Content). However, the rest of the amount is to push us to have the complete "story mode" in the initial release, and only bonus levels as DLC. Speed of production and quality of scenery and sound (like video cutscenes, voices instead of text) depend on whether we can call in the extra help using the extra funds.

Time and space management:
Since all of us are working from different locations, it is difficult and arduous trying to collaborate. Right now we work with regular conference calls on Skype, email and phone for weekly updates, and we meet up at least once a month, but if we had one central location to work from, we could get things done so more easily and quickly. We can't afford it yet, but the extra money we need has been included in the "Bonus Funds" as it is not essential, but will really help us move forward and at a much greater pace.

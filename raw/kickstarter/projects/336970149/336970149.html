A choose your own adventure visual novel with you as the protagonist, ultimately making the choices that decide this sci-fi thriller.

Premise:

The game is a sci-fi thriller taking place in the near future, with a plot that focuses on player choices and character reactions. Characterization is crucial to the project.

Gameplay:

What is the game really about, and what experience are we going to provide you? Infection: First Contact can be described as the following:

  • A Visual Novel - Uncommon in America but widely embraced overseas, our story is a visual novel; a narrative told through text and images. Original musical compositions accompany the story, creating a unique, immersive experience.
  • A Choose Your Own Adventure - This classic genre allows you to create your own story as you go, making each play through reflect your own choices.
  • A Sci-Fi Thriller - The core of our story is an ensemble of characters dealing with the tension as it becomes apparent something is very, very wrong aboard the ship.

The Crew:

This is our current video showing an overview of each character. An update with audio will be coming shortly.

  • The Player - You are the most important character in this work, and it is you who drives the action of this story. Starting out as errand boy on the ship, your pivotal role ultimately dictates the narrative.
  • The Captain - Leader of the ship, The Captain is on his final journey before appointing his successor and returning to his family for good. A father figure to many, recent tensions concerning the succession put him in a tough position.
  • The Boatswain - The Captain's right hand man, The Boatswain assumes himself the guaranteed successor and inheritor of the ship. Smarmy without a hint of charm, he is not the most well-liked man on the ship, much to his surprise.
  • The Inventory Specialist - Responsible for the ship's cargo, he spends a lot of time unsupervised, leading to The Boatswain's suspicion. A passionate revolutionary, he is a character with strong opinions and stronger motivations.
  • The Navigation Technician - One of the few women on the ship, The Nav Tech is responsible with keeping the ship and The Nurse running properly. While not busy going over lines of code, she enjoys getting to know the rest of the crew.
  • The Engineer - A complete opposite to the messy and disorganized manner of The Nav Tech, The Engineer takes care of the inner mechanical workings on the ship. Short and small enough to get into the inner reaches of the ship, she is still nothing to mess with. She considers herself to be in a relationship with The Nav Tech, although the woman of her affections may object… if the Nav Tech knew of her affections at all..
  • The Doctor - Only on the ship for insurance reasons, The Doctor simply does her job and keeps to herself. Distant from the other members of the crew she mostly occupies herself with various Nuerohack, electronic programs used to simulate drugs.
  • The Nurse - A re-purposed entertainment bot, The Nurse has been programmed to do various medical tests and to assist The Doctor. Due to its original design, gender is really secondary since the face plate often changes throughout the story. Those who donate at a high enough level will be featured as one of The Nurses faces.
  • The Dog - Mascot character and lovable member of the crew. Do you believe in Dog?
  • The Ship - Oh, who could forget the ship? It, in many ways, is a driving force in the the game and has just as much personality as the rest of the crew. While we are working on getting some of our interior shots up we've decided to provide you with a 3D design below. It’s pretty damn cool.

Want to help us name The Ship?

It’s incredibly difficult to get a ship name right A fun name can easily come across as campy, and a supposedly inspiring one as overly dramatic. So in the spirit of you, the user, driving the story, we've decided to crowd source suggestions- which you can give at Ship@ElectricSunshineFactory.com for our consideration.

Put the your suggestion as the subject of the email to save time.

Our Goals:

When we set out to make Infection: First Contact we began looking at just what we wanted to accomplish, and worked to find the best way to execute those goals. With the game we hope to provide a quality gaming experience, that offers real emotional depth. The following goals lay at the heart of Infection: First Contact:

  • A Player Crafted Story - The choices you make in this game all have a depth to them and allow you to craft the narrative. We've made a game that does not take your choices lightly.
  • Multiple Outcomes - With every choice comes consequence, and although out game offers multiple endings, we've worked hard so that these outcomes have variety to them without forcing you to go through repetitive sections.
  • Rewarding Choices - No choice is wrong in this game, and they all lead to compelling endings. Every finale you bring yourself to is one we are proud of. We think that no choice should be punishing, that every end is a quality finish.

Stretch Goals:

Due to the nature of the game, and the way we have it programmed it, it is incredibly simple for us to export the game to other platforms and expand upon it. The following are stretch goals for this project, which are subject to change as we think of them or find other ways to provide you guys with more.

  • $17,500 (Female Player Option) - At this level of funding, we will be able to double the size of the game by offering the option to play as a woman. This will change the dialogue of certain game parts, open up new romantic plot lines, and offer a chance to pretend you are Ellen Ripley.
  • $20,000 (The Nintendo Option) - At this level of funding we will have the money required to apply for a Nintendo Game Development Kit so we can hopefully get the game released for the Nintendo 3DS, following in the footsteps of one of our inspirations, Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney. If successful the game would be distributed digitally.
  • $25,000 (The Android Apple Option) - At this level of funding we are going to be looking into getting the game on Android platforms, including the Kickstarter funded Ouya. We will also look at getting the game on iOS devices, but each of these require approvals that we can't guarantee. We can guarantee, however, that we will produce versions for these systems and work hard to have them approved.
  • $30,000 (Infection: Second Contact) - We have a sequel work in the planning stages, but right now we are focusing on this game full time and with our full dedication. Hitting this goal means we will begin work on a sequel to this planned trilogy of games, without having to take our full attention off this game. Everyone who has donated to this project will be given private updates on the progress of Infection: Second Contact as well.

How are we going to ensure that we get out game made?

Our game is already written, and it is written in pieces that allow us to program it in nearly any language and adapt it to different systems because of this piece system. The main thing stopping us from putting out game out now is our lack of art assets and music, two rather expensive things.

How are we going to handle shipping and printing our limited selection of physical copies?

We've run the numbers, run them again, and ran them a third time in case the postal service collapses. We are incredibly confident in our ability to send out these limited copies.

How are we going to ensure a quality project that handles these issues with tact?

We're going to be pretty transparent with how we spend our money, no one is getting a salary off our Kickstarter sales. Artists will be paid with the money, but all of the funds raised go into making our game better and paying costs. We will make this the best game we can, and that is a challenge that we are prepared to live up to.

FAQ

  • What platforms are you releasing on?

    We are releasing on Windows, Mac, and Linux. These three platforms are our primary goal, and each is equally important to us. If we reach our stretch goals, we would like to release our game on any platform that is a good fit. Because we are using the Ren’Py engine, it should be very simple to adapt our Visual Novel to additional platforms if possible. I would like to give a special mention to our game being released on Linux. Many of our earliest supporters requested that we cater to the Linux gaming community; they put their faith and support in us, which is why supporting them is just as important to us.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Why can’t you release this game or the rewards sooner?

    We may be able to, but we do not want to be a project that sets an unrealistic goal. The date posted takes into account shipping physical copies and the additional time required to write the female path- something we are really hoping for.
    That being said, we will likely release the digital copy of the game to every backer before that. We will release the game when it is of a quality we are happy with: tested and stable, and a product we are proud of. It’s become a little bit too common to release a broken game and then promise a patch. It is never acceptable to sell someone a broken product, and video games are no exception. You’ve supported us, and we will work hard to reward our backers with a quality product.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Why does the option to play as a female character need to be a stretch goal?

    Writing costs a great deal of money, and I’ve already stretched my own personal funding to the limit with concept art and getting the game itself written. A choice we made early on was that we wanted a female path. We have it mapped out already, and it’s something we are very passionate about. Given our financial situation, we decided that we should focus on the one story (the male path), and make it our first stretch goal to provide the female path.
    We wish to provide a unique experience through the female path. A lot of people like being able to choice the gender of their character, but most of all they like playing a good story. Quality is important to us, which is why the female path is not just other characters using she instead of he. Allowing for meaningful female representation in our game is important to us, and we hope to reach our stretch goal to do so. For more information, we have an opinion piece being written on this subject and more on our company blog. It also includes an interview with each of our writers, and it’s worth a read.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • How are you going to handle the sensitive issues presented in your game?

    Carefully, and with pride. We believe in the representation of groups that are not often seen in games in a prominent position. We realize that, in dealing with issues of race, gender, and sexuality, we run the risk of misrepresenting the unique individuals we are trying to portray. We have reached out to different communities and did a good deal of research to make sure we handle these issues well. If you would like more information about this, we have an opinion piece and interview up on our website. The link is available above, and it goes into a good deal of detail on how we strive to make strong characters and to reach out to different communities.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • How can I contact you for press?

    You can contact me for all things press related things by going to the contact information provided above.

    If requested, we can provide you with our press kit, additional information, interviews, and even a slimmed down tech demo.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • I’m not part of a major press organization, but I do run a blog or podcast. Can I still contact you and have a shot?

    I’m not part of a major game development organization, and we understand the importance of small media writers. No one is too small to be considered. You will receive a response promptly, and, if an interview just isn’t possible, we will still provide you with our basic press resources. If you want to ask us any questions, feel free to add them in an email and we will likely provide you with answers.
    Kickstarter is built on communities. We won’t hit our goal by denying small news sources and we are fans of small press. We love our game and therefor love to talk about it. We're very interested in hearing from our supporters. Shoot us an email!

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Can I use any images or video from this Kickstarter on my fan site, blog, news site, ect.?

    The legal information regarding everything is addressed in the final question of this FAQ. It really depends on which thing you're interested in using, so it can be a bit confusing and easy to make a mistake. We are not out to hound on anyone, especially if it’s for a press site or fan site. In fact, we have a specific email to contact us about those things to provide you with press kits and fan resources.
    If you have any doubts on something, email us using the legal address above.

    Every email we get is an exciting event.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Does your game contain nudity?

    No people appear nude in our game; however The Dog does appear nude. We are currently taking this concern seriously and are exploring options to allow users to choose whether or not The Dog appears in a space suit.

    (Joking, though Dog Space Suit has now been added to our list of things to do if we finish everything else.)

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Will the game be color blind friendly?

    Yes, any features that could possibly cause issues for people with color blindness will not be an issue for them. We have a great respect for first person shooters and others games who do so. One of our co-creators is color blind, and he will be a great resource ensuring our colorblind gamers will be able to enjoy our Visual Novel to the fullest extent.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Will the game have voice acting?

    The short answer is no. Amateur voice acting can easily sound out of place and high quality voice acting would require a very large sum of money. We do not believe the lack of voice acting will detract from the quality of our game. We are using a mixture of quality writing, fantastic character portraits and backgrounds, and our musical score to project the voice of the characters and the tone of any given scene.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Will the game only be released in English?

    Unfortunately, the answer is that we can only provide an English version of our game. Quality translation is something that takes a great deal of time, effort, and money if you want it done by professionals.
    That being said, there have been many fantastic fan-produced language patches released by game communities. I’m open to any fan translation efforts. I’m not a big industry insider who is going to crush an ambitious project by passionate fans. If you contact me, I will be able to work with you to make the creation of that patch easiest for the both of us.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • How transparent are you going to be with your spending?

    We will strive to be very transparent about our spending. We believe in keeping integrity and being honest with our supporters. Once funded, we will release a pie chart detailing where we intend the money to go. Once our project is finished, we will release another pie chart detailing where the money actually went. There are always last minute disasters, fees we cannot anticipate, taxes, and the possibility of going over or under budget in a category and we will keep our backers informed if these things crop up. We aim to conduct business very openly, and the use of our funds and support is something backers deserve to remain aware of.

    Last updated: Fri, Jul 12 2013 8:29 am EDT
  • Who do I contact if I have a legal question, comment, or concern?

    You can contact us using the legal concern email provided above for any questions you may have. We will reply as soon as we can.

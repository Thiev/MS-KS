About the Project

I'm very excited about this project, it was conceived about two and a half months ago and I've loved it ever since.  Pirate Treasure Attack! is a puzzle/platformer.  It follows the story of Captain Lou, who is cursed by an evil genie when he inadvertently wishes that "treasure would just fall from the sky."  

Originally, this game was going to be strictly a puzzle game with only a handful of stages that consisted of matching and collecting treasure with a mini-game and boss battle in each one, but the game has really evolved over the last couple of weeks and has finally started to shape into a true puzzle/platformer adventure!

Captain Lou and his crew were on an expedition to find a magic lamp, but when all they could find was a plain, old gravy boat, the crew abandoned their captain on an island in the middle of nowhere.  Not knowing a genie resides inside the seemingly ordinary gravy boat, Captain Lou unwittingly disturbs him and is cursed by treasure that falls from the sky.  But before he has the chance to ask the genie to remove the curse, the magic gravy boat is knocked into the ocean and Captain Lou's adventure begins!

You must help Captain Lou navigate across a vast world, battling greedy pirates and blood thirsty monsters, in search of the magic gravy boat to free him of his curse.

The game will be laid out in a classic over-world map style with different paths to follow and secrets to unlock.  Each level will be broken into sections which consist of different styles of play, for instance level 1-1 will be a side-scrolling level, level 1-2 will be a puzzle/matching level, and level 1-3 will be  challenge level.  Each level won't be set up in this fashion, the bar level will only be a puzzle/matching level, but it will have three to four mini-games that you must complete to progress.

Side-Scrolling Levels

In these levels, you will have a normal objective, to find special items or just to make it to the end and fight the boss.  Chests will still rain down on you during this type of level, but less frequently and in more of a physics-based manner.  These chests can't be matched, but they can be picked up and stacked to maneuver around tall obstacles, for use as counter-weight, or holding down switches.

Puzzle/Matching Levels

These levels consist of a "Match 3" style of play.  The player will navigate the hoard of falling treasure while avoiding enemies and booby traps to make matches and progress through the stage.  As if that wasn't enough, the game will only reveal the treasure inside each chest temporarily.  So, you have to memorize where the treasure is, make the matches before you run out of room, and try not to get killed.  

Puzzle levels will contain bad guys, mini-games, bosses and other mechanisms to keep the game play fresh and exciting.

For instance, the cursed doubloon and the golden goose are two treasures that will trigger special events.  The cursed doubloon will summon stage-specific minions to attack you, like the fiddler crab on the beach stage.

The golden goose will drop golden eggs on the stage as she flies around.  This is great, as long as you catch them!  When broken, the eggs will release baby birds that will attack you in an attempt to protect their mother.  

The puzzle levels will also contain several power-ups.

Here are three of my favorite power-ups so far: the cowbell will make Captain Lou move faster and also plays a funky, cowbell-heavy song. The golden pig will transform Captain Lou into Super Bacon, a hero with incredible powers...and a frying pan.  The compass will show Captain Lou the best matches available for a short period of time.

Mini-Games

The levels will have mini-games that fit their specific style, it won't be the same mini-game re-skinned for each level.  Mini-games will range from shooting targets to battle pinball, card games and many more. 

Challenge Levels

Sometimes it is nice to get a break from the normal routine, challenge levels won't use actions/controls typical to normal game play.  You may find yourself piloting a boat through treacherous water or leading a fleet of ships into battle (real-time strategy).  

Game Modes:

  • Adventure - A full-length adventure with innovative, new game play and plenty of levels to sink your teeth into
  • Multi-player - In this mode, both players will be on the same screen.  Two different colors of chests will fall down and each player will try to make matches that contain more of his color than the other (2 of 3, 3 of 5, etc.)  Badguys and booby traps released by a match will only harm the opposing player. Exclusive to OUYA, unless the stretch goal is met to add online multi-player.
  • Classic - Pirate Treasure Attack! isn't a remake, "classic" mode is just for people who want to do the memory/matching without the jumping around and fighting bad guys
  • Smart - 99% of educational gaming is boring, this won't be.  Well, it shouldn't be.  You might not find matching factors to be exciting, but it will add a new level of complexity to the game.  Matching factors won't be the only option.
  • Mini-games - Once you have unlocked a mini-game you will be able to come here and play them again, by yourself or with a friend

How far along is the project?

The game has grown rapidly over the past couple of months as you can see here: 

But there is still a lot to do.  The basic layout of the puzzle/matching level is done, but still has some bugs.  The side-scrolling and challenge levels have not been created yet and there is still a lot of art assets that need to be created.  We have a few backgrounds for levels, but ultimately we will re-design them to give the levels more depth.  

I'm very pleased with the look of the characters and objects, Brad has invested a lot of time and effort into emulating that 16-bit style that I was looking for.  Speaking of characters, let's meet Captain Lou. 

Our endearing, Captain Lou (finished concept, Right) went through several changes, but we finally got it right!  I wanted to create a fun-loving hero with a warm personality, one that always faces adversity with determination and a smile.

As of right now, it's just me (Eric Vest) and "Rad" Brad Branch.  The plan is to hire someone to program the game so I can work on music and coordinating the project, but if that falls through I plan on purchasing some game development software so I can put the game together myself and hire someone to do the music.  Brad is the game's only artist, but depending on how many of the stretch goals we hit, we may hire another artist as well.

UPDATE: Josh Guibault has joined the team!  Josh is the lead developer over at Evanced Games.  It's very exciting to have an industry professional on the team, and even though this is just a "fun side project" for him, Josh seems very excited about working on the game.  It's a relief to know someone very skilled at creating mobile games will be handling the programming side of the game.  Now I (Eric Vest) can focus on developing the levels, story, and creating an awesome soundtrack!  :D

The levels will be very diverse and so will the music; you'll hear everything from big band music to dub step.  I will be pulling inspiration from several sources including "Super Mario RPG", "The Twisted Tales of Spike McFang", classical orchestral music and more modern stuff as well.  

I have several things I want to accomplish during the kickstarter campaign, one of them is posting an example of the game music.  There are a couple examples of my work in the rewards section, but they don't necessarily represent the music that will be going into Pirate Treasure Attack!  So, check back often for updates!

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Well, the plan is to make enough money to cover the rest of the artwork and pay a programmer to put together the code. It may be difficult to find a like-minded and dedicated programmer, but if that's the case I plan to write the code myself and find someone to compose the music for the game instead.
That would just be a minor hiccup; not being able to balance work and family will be a major risk.

This kickstarter campaign won't raise enough money so that I can hire myself full-time, it'll be just enough to pay for art, programming, and other tidbits. During the development of this game I will be working full-time at my current job while putting together smaller projects for the website to boost ad revenue and coordinating and contributing to this project. Making sure I spend plenty of time with my wife and baby during the early stages of development won't be hard, but once the deadline starts creeping up it'll be a whole different ball game. On the flipside of that, when the project deadline is a year away, I may be prone to slack off a little more.

I don't have any special, preemptive measures to balance work and family aside from planning and scheduling, but whenever I feel overwhelmed with how much I have to do and I start feeling like I need to sacrifice time with my family I just say to myself, "I have the rest of my life to be successful, but I only have the rest of my life to spend with them." It might seem kind of silly, but the ability to keep things in perspective and set your priorities is invaluable when it comes to working on any project or just in everyday life. To keep from running into some major imbalance later down the road, I plan to schedule the work like a funnel, more tasks upfront and less tasks later. This should help us stay ahead and reduce production snags so close to the deadline.

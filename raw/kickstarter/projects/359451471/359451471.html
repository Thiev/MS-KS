Orc Attack is a 3D hack and slash with a revolutionary camera shared by all players that gives an awesome sensation of combat.

Orc Attack is a thrilling 3D co-op hack and slash adventure that puts players in control of a team of vile, disgusting Orcs as they embark on a mission to crush human invaders. Already a hit with critics, Orc Attack has won the People's Choice Award at the Develop Conference held in Brighton in July 2011, and was runner-up as the most promising indie game for 2012 at the Game Connection, held in Paris on December 2011.

Having been crushed and punished by humanity in so many past games, the time has come to let Orcs dish out some punishment of their own against the humans. Players control orcs and aim to defeat the evil humans who have come to exploit Orcish lands with a huge army and noisy and dirty machinery. Orc Attack features exciting co-op battles with a revolutionary camera shared by all players, giving an awesome sensation of combat.

Orc Attack has two different modes of play:

1) The Magic Mode, in which the Orcs performs magical spells that produce devastating effects on the enemies.

2) The Dirty Mode, where the Orcs use their gases to produce explosions with the same devastating effects on the enemies.

When people think about Orcs, the image of grubby and wild creatures that only think about (and we’re using the term “think” loosely) hunting, eating, fighting and sleeping is what immediately comes to mind and, to be sure, that image is mainly right. Orcs are in most cases dirty and savage creatures with very little concern that fight first without even bothering to ask questions. However, this time, the Orcs have a real reason to fight as evil humans have broken in their territories with their noisy and smoky machines to turn their land, air and water into poison.

By drinking poisoned water, eating sick animals and just breathing the polluted air, the unwary and ignorant Orcs become terribly ill, and are crushed in a battle by the humans due to their impaired state. The few survivors, sick and weakened, took refuge in the swamps. They are relentlessly pursued by ruthless Orc hunters on their trail, who are capable of tracking them quite easily, as the fugitive Orcs leave a stinking and thundering trace behind them, due to their illness.

The main symptom of their disease is tremendous flatulence. However, this weakness soon becomes a new strength for the sick Orcs with the help of a blind Orc master. The master teaches the Orc warriors how to use their unnatural gases to fight the enemy producing explosions and other powerful effects when battling the enormous human army. These gas powers, combined with warrior skills, will lead them to become new Orc heroes with the arduous task of expelling the nefarious humans and their machines from the Orc world.

This is the sad and stinky story behind this funny and frenzied game, but the main drive behind Orc Attack is co-op, brawling fun. Players will fight tremendous hordes of enemies, crushing many of them in just one blow and sending them flying into the air, or ignite a horde of them using a little of gas. If you like playing with your friends locally or online, and the thought of mythical beast men passing gas makes you want to shoot milk out of your nose, then Orc Attack is your game.

Orc Attack incorporates a revolutionary single-camera view with all players (up to 4 local or online) sharing the same camera, so you can always see your buddies fighting onscreen while getting a really impressive sensation of massive combat. Players will experience the desperate feeling of being surrounded by lots of enemies while fighting cooperatively with their comrades.

Orc Attack has 16 levels divided into four distinct environments. Players are given four Orc characters to choose from. As players progress through the game, it becomes harder and funnier. The Orcs will gain experience and earn new combos, more efficient gas powers, and will become stronger, faster, and more damage resistant. To enter each level, a determined number of sacred stones are required, which can be won by finishing prior levels and also by searching the sacred stones hidden throughout the game’s stages. There is also a store where new equipment and power ups can be purchased with runes picked up in combat missions.

Orc Attack players will evolve their avatars at will, leveling up and distributing their experience points among abilities such as strength, speed and resistance. They can also improve their avatar’s performance with purchases, made with the coins found along the combat missions, as well as buy different weapons, armors, power-ups and magic elixirs in the Orc Store.

We began planning Orc Attackback in 2010 and started work shortly thereafter. Each member of the team worked from his own home, designing and working through the preproduction stage. In early 2011, with some funds raised, we rented an office and began proper production. In summer 2011, we debuted the first running version of the game in public: it was featured in the Indie Showcase at the Develop Conference in Brighton, UK where we won the People's Choice Award. Production progressed steadily and by Christmas, Orc Attack was runner-up as the most promising indie game for 2012 at Game Connection in Paris, France. This year, Orc Attack received an excellent reception at PAX East, earning plenty of accolades from the gaming press.

Although Orc Attack is in the final production stages, we still need funding to finish it and if we raise the £20,000 we’re asking for, we will deliver a great game that we believe will exceed your expectations. Thank you for your support!

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

One of the main challenges we assume in developing our own videogame was to make it very funny, amusing and entertaining to be played online or local with our friends in the sofa.

To achieve these apparently simple objectives, we had to solve many different problems. Finally we have found the path, and results were good, but that was not enough for us. We needed to see the reaction of more people, so we introduced Orc Attack for the first time at the Brighton Develop Conference... and Orc Attack won the People's Choice Award !!!
After this spectacular triumph, we put all our efforts in improving and perfecting the game.

Up to date we have had some problems to solve, mainly those connected with the online. This can produce some delay in the launch of our game.
Although we are a little studio, we have a very high confidencce in ourselves and, over all, in our product. So we are sure that Orc Attack, in spite of possible little problems and delays, will be finally a success, specially with your support and with that of your friends.

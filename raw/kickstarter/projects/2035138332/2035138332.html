An open source, story inspired MMORPG using HTML5 technologies.

The game, in a few words, is diving into the heart of combat in a gladiator world. A story mirroring life, we're looking to the heroes in this game world to drive the story month by month. If this wasn't enough of a mission, we're using our passion to help grow the browser side of the game industry using only technologies that have been built off open standards, such as WebGL and Javascript. Our hope, is with commercial success and sponsoring open technology growth we can join the effort to drive these technologies to greater maturity and into the the mainstream game industry.

We would classically characterize this game as a massively multiplayer RPG, however some of the features in development currently don't exactly fit this mold perfectly. We have a soft spot for this genre and we've been itching to get a culturally significant story to run in tandem with a game world!

Rather than a story written inside of a game we arefocusing on the written story living outside of the game world, leaving the game world as the manifestation of our interpretation of the world depicted in the story. We're releasing a new chapter each month as a ebook in pdf format from our webpage for free.

You begin creating a character and personalizing it before you are thrust into the population of slaves and refugees. Forced to serve the entertainment and military appetites of mighty Romanesque empire, you scrounge and fight your way through the only hope you have, the tournament.

The tournament is where the best fight, warriors are hardened and blood is spilled in the name of survival. The winner earns freedoms, reward, title and great opportunity to become part of the story.

The gameplay focuses largely around a core mechanic presented to us in the story a non-voluntary martial arts culture hoping that a hero can find favor within the ranks of tyranny. Using a very different crafting system you're forced to forge ahead and survive through combat, strategy and social elements.

We are instrumenting gameplay to produce a high level of feedback and mechanics to bring you closer to the experience of a real fight. There are a lot of ways to make the combat in a game fun we realize, but our passion is for something gritty and that will line up with theexhilarationone experiences in a real fight.

We are beyond excited about this project. We've sold the farm, left jobs and started development. We realized that we truly love this project and just couldn't accept the idea working on it part time which is why we've come to kickstarter with this project at the concept phase.

We really believe in Kickstarter's vision and platform (not to mention they helped fund aShadowrun sequel http://bit.ly/I32oP5). We care alot about the "hows" in this project, and when it came to funding kickstarter was an obvious choice. We value transparency, sincerity and passion and feel that kickstarter helps foster these ideals.

We need to raise $250,000 to fund our amazing team to make this game successful. We realize this is a modest sum for a project like this -- we calculated very carefully the minimum needed to do this project correctly using only the bare essentials.

This project is in the concept/pre-production phase currently. Your support will ensure this raw game will become a reality something special. And if the pledges go beyond our target, we've got some great stretch goals in mind.

We need your help!

We strongly believe that the Internet has become what it is today due to open standards and open source projects. We see the browser as the hub and soul of the internet.

Our choice to use HTML5 technologies (webgl, javascript) is strongly based on real world experience using open source technologies to deliver services and software. We've also seen the results of closed source from several angles and felt it was the right choice for this project. Apart from our passion to experience this game, we really want to see the browser tool chain grow and mature so we can scaffold more power and ability to do more awesome stuff (like AAA games). We recognized that we needed to commit to the platform for better or worse for this project to have a hope of helping grow the platform in this direction.

Grateful for the contributions andleadershipof people like Mozilla and Google, we see lots of people working to grow the browser, making it more powerful and more capable. We recognized that we need more games built specifically for this platform and not just mobile games, but games that hardcore gamers will want to play. (For further reading/videos checkout the latest HTML5 presentation from Google IO:http://bit.ly/Qt0OI3or check out some demos over at the Mozilla Developer site:http://mzl.la/SuVmaj)

If were successful on kickstarter we are going to open up the code just after launch, and by open, we mean BSD licensed and on github. Lets do this!

Karsten McMinn started Eleventy Media because of his love of games. A life long gamer he grew up in code, games and PCs. Before leaving Electronic Arts to start Eleventy Media he was doing software and systems engineering on Facebook titles such as The Sims Social, Simcity Social, Fifa Super Stars and others. His background in games and open source are instrumental in the passion for this project.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Making games isn't difficult. Making great games, games worth people's time, is.

Capable people can go buy a license to a game engine and scaffold a prototype in a month or less. To make anything multiplayer and social takes being able to technically understand all the disciplines that go into them, something Karsten has been doing for a while. To get this level of understanding you have to be an engineer that touches every code base in a online game as well as work with the people in art, writing, game design and music.

Our team has the experience and potential to solve and tackle these challenges. We recognize that even great game designers and artists still fail miserably at times. Seeking an advisor is something that we've done not due to our lack of experience but because of our experience and desire to see many angles and make the flaws easier to spot.

One lesson we are constantly reminded of is that the right vision can yield a perfect plan, that when followed with relentless persistence will produce results that often will be cherished and truly worth people's time.

This project's main challenges are within the platform and using a opensource client and server. If we are successful we're very confident that we'll deliver an amazing experience to our customers.

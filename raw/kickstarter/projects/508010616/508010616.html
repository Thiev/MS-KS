A parody of old school RPGs where you are a new Dark Lord on a quest to amass monsters and allies on your side.

The game is an old school styled comedic parody RPG with some classic gameplay. It plays comically off the old RPG tropes that existed in games like Dragon Quest and Final Fantasy. While the game’s core is a comedy it will be a fun game with character progression and a story that will keep you moving forward.

  • Control the new Dark Lord and his other team members in a classic RPG style
  • Convince monster groups to fight for their new Dark Lord by beating their strongest fighter or finding a rare item to help them
  • Fight off the pompous new “Hero” and his lackeys
  • Find rare treasures and discover ways the Dark Lord “hides” it
  • Meet fun side characters such as Monster Merchants to sell gear to you and your team
  • Solve various puzzles throughout the game to reach your goal
  • Build up your Dark Castle as you grow in power

The last mighty Dark Lord was defeated over one-hundred years ago by the great Hero Ginn’regin. After the fall of every glorious Dark Lord, fate has always dictated that a new one takes his place quickly and ruthlessly, but not this time. Once our great Dark Lord was defeated, the Council of so-called Heroes convened to put in place laws to prevent what they deemed as “evil”. The Council of Heroes decided that all laws would solely be their decision as the populace cannot understand what evils can slip through. Through the years the list of laws, restrictions, and slavery have grown and grown to insure that what they define as “evil” never returned.

Peace spread across the land but at the cost of freedom and no new Dark Lord has risen to power to maintain the balance. Towns are now governed by appointed Heroes, lackeys, and lickspittles from the council to insure their safety and to make sure “evil” is not allowed. The ancestor of the great Ginn’regin that defeated the last Dark Lord has become the figurehead of the “good” movement. The various monster tribes have become divided, broken, and with no Dark Lord in place, they have been preoccupied with fighting each other, leaving the towns alone and whitewashed.

Heroes are picked and trained by the Council themselves. Anyone not deemed Heroic or goody-goody enough to be a true hero are given lesser roles to that the council deems appropriate. Some have been unhappy with their assignments, and while normally they are content to help where they can, unrest grows even among the ranks of the self-named forces of good. Except in the place of our glorious champion which is really where the story of our revolution begins.

Our small three man team has been working in the video game industry for over 5 to 6 years each with various game companies. We all have gotten to work with games in the development process and understand the work and planning that need to go into making a video game.

Jason Rothwell - After spending two years as a Quality Assurance Technician on games such as MX vs ATV: Untamed and Cars 2: Mater-national for Rainbow Studios and Puzzle Quest: Revenge of the Plague Lord and Dark Sector for D3 Publishing, he joined Quest Online LLC.  Working tirelessly for three years on Alganon as a Data Designer, he wrote story, progression, mechanics, and dialogue for over a thousand quests, tying them into an overarching storyline and laying the groundwork for the expansion, the Rise of the Ourobani.  He named and created the statistics for thousands of items, hundreds of NPCs, and was responsible for the entire crafting system for Alganon, including recipes, skill progression, and crafting quests and studies unique to that world.

Tim Brinton - Has worked over 6 years in the game industry. He has worked for several companies which include THQ, D3 Publisher of America, and Zynga. He spent several years working on many projects in Quality Assurance including titles as MX vs ATV: Untamed, Costume Quest, Stacking, WWE ‘12, and ChefVille. He has also worked closely with developers creating features for projects.

Elliot Kuykendall - Has work over 6 years in the game industry. The first 5 years were as one of the Test Leads in the Quality Assurance production group at THQ. He handled running different QA teams on various titles at THQ. His job also consisted of creating test plans, focus documents, conference calls with Developers, in addition to the tests of the game. The last year of work was working Development Quality Assurance with a Development team under Zynga. There he worked with a Design team on new features with the titles to insured these features were tested in scope with their design

Each of us on the team have been wanting to start making our own game projects and we want to combine our efforts together. Currently it is hard to get this opportunity within larger companies. Most companies won’t let you use your ideas unless you have already gotten designed titles under your belt.

Kickstarting allows us to be the Producers and Designers on the project. It will allows us to make a game that we really believe will be fun and enjoyable. Getting funded also allows us to put our full focus on the title and still be able to support ourselves and families.

With your support we can make this fun game and get to release it to everyone!

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

This will be our teams first title to release on our own. We know this is no simple task, but we understand how to properly plan and organize the project and ensure we are working towards the goal of completing a strong and fun title.

Currently we do not have an artist and composer for the game. Once funded, we will be using some of these funds to bring in people we know to fill these roles.

Making a game requires long hours and focus on working with our group. This is something the team understands completely and already has done with projects we’ve worked on with the companies we have worked for.

We have already set up the engine with SVN so everyone on the project can group edit our work files and we have create an online workspace for all our planning documentation.

FAQ

  • What engine will the game be made with?

    The team plans to use RPGmaker VX Ace.

    Last updated: Tue, Jul 9 2013 12:43 pm EDT
  • Why RPGmaker VX Ace?

    RPGmaker VX Ace is a simple editor on the surface that makes creating an old school RPG work really well. We plan to not use any of the pre-packaged assets and bring in our own into the engine. Just like any title being made through an already developed engine (example: Unreal engine) Additionally to the standard functions of RPGmaker, it allows us to use Ruby scripting to add or modify the engine to better suit our project.

    Last updated: Thu, Jun 20 2013 8:07 pm EDT
  • What platforms will So I’m A Dark Lord be released on?

    The initial plan is just for windows, but we will be looking into other platform to see what our other possibilities are.

    Last updated: Mon, Jun 24 2013 5:50 pm EDT
  • Why do you need to funds that you are requesting?

    We are starting with zero funds and need the ability to pay the team (we would like to also bring in an artist full time as well). The team will be focusing on the project full time which means we will not have any income coming from anything other than that what we have been funded. The team needs to be paid to continue to support their rent, bills and families. This amount should cover the cost of pay for a year of work and allow us to have funds for music, sound, art, taxes, and any other project needs. We don’t want to come up short and not have enough to finish the project that people want to see.

    Last updated: Tue, Jul 9 2013 12:43 pm EDT
  • Could the you do the project for less?

    Our team had a long discussion on what our funds needed to be for the game so our small team can devote themselves to the project for a solid year. This means that we needed to have funds the pay a salary for 12 months of work for each team member plus funds for contract work, like art and sound. We need a team that can focus on working on the game and still be able to pay rent and bills.

    If we cut the required funds needed to complete the Kickstarter we risk only have enough funds for a small amount of work before people would need to take extra jobs just to get by. In turn that could mean not completing the game which would break our promise to the people like you that supported us.

    Last updated: Tue, Jul 9 2013 12:43 pm EDT
  • Are you worried that you may not reach the goal?

    It is a bigger goal to hit, but not impossible. We just really need to reach more people with our game as we have seen other titles that manage to reach a large amount of people reach goals above what we need.

    Last updated: Tue, Jul 9 2013 12:43 pm EDT

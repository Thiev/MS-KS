 What is Trainz: A New Era?

Trainz: A New Era is far more than a train driving simulation. It offers something for every train fan - from the tools to create your own rail line, commands to operate multiple trains, and a choice of simple or realistic cab controls to hop inside the cab and become a driver in charge a 3,000 tonne freight train or a high speed passenger service.

Trainz: A New Era - more realism, more features, more tools, more content, and of course, the amazing graphics that you have been asking for.

With the support of our fans from all around the world, here are our plans that will deliver the best ever version of Trainz - Trainz Simulator: A New Era.

We've been busy working behind the scenes designing and creating an all new custom-built game engine, code-named "E2". This new engine will deliver a major upgrade in graphics capabilities and performance.

E2 features the latest technology including HDR (High Dynamic Range) rendering, whole-world real-time per-pixel lighting, native multi-threading support, 64-bit processing support and unlimited seamless streaming. With customized "graphics shaders" a huge new range of special effects will be possible such as reflective surfaces, heat haze effects, depth-of-field effects and atmospheric fog. 

By offloading major systems to separate CPU cores and maximising performance, E2 will support additional physical effects. For example smoke will be affected by local wind conditions or hitting an overpass, and weather (rain, snow, even autumn leaves) that will collide against solid objects.

So what does that all mean in layman's terms? It means E2 will support:

  • larger, more detailed routes/environments;
  • lighting which automatically casts shadows from all solid objects including terrain;
  • fully detailed and animated pedestrians, passengers, wildlife, cars, trucks and trains;
  • realistic environmental effects such as locally modelled environmental conditions including cloud formation, volumetric fogging, localised rain and snow effects, wind-blown trees and grass;
  • smoke billowing out of a tunnel or rain splashing in a puddle;
  • heat shimmer from hot diesel engines;
  • substantially improved in-editor performance.

In one sentence, E2 will provide a huge boost in power and performance to make routes more realistic and perform better than ever before.

DRIVING PHYSICS ENHANCEMENTS

From your feedback over the years, the most significant feature for many people is the accuracy of the simulation. This is why enhancements to the driving experience are at the very top of our T2 feature list.

For the technically-minded, our physics model uses a variety of configurable inputs such as engine power, train weight and rolling resistance, then takes into account factors like track gradient, curve resistance and track adhesion for each car in the train. We then calculate the forces at work 30 times a second and as a result, the train accelerates or decelerates and moves along the track accordingly.

T2 introduces brand new Real-time Physics Editing tools enabling users to enter known specifications then accurately monitor all the forces at work. Things like tractive effort, drawbar pull or brake pipe pressure can be monitored at a specific point along the train and input values adjusted “on-the-fly”. By analysing and tuning performance without leaving the simulation, real-world train characteristics can be matched perfectly. (It's a bit like running a virtual dynamometer car).

If physics aren't your thing, don't worry. You can leave the fine-tuning of your favourite loco performance to the experts and you will still have the choice of 'Realistic Mode' or 'Easy Mode' driving options.

  • What are the details of the Initial Goal Features?

    A: Procedural Animated Spline Junctions

    This feature includes underlying track system support and an updated set of track laying tools that will allow a user to lay track in Surveyor that automatically adds frogs (blades) and checkrails in the correct place on the tracks when making a junction. The blades will animate to indicate the direction of travel. Different switches (junction levers) can be placed to control the junction direction.

    B: Super-Elevated Track + Improved Cab Sway + Train Motion

    When laying track on curves, an angle modifier can be added to tilt the track in the direction of the curve. In addition, vehicle suspension will be modelled to accurately depict the angle of tilt (since this is dependent on speed and suspension). Using this suspension modelling system, cab sway and “car rocking” from both internal and external perspective will be implemented. When running on rough track, cars will wobble and shake independently.

    C: Smoke and Weather Affected by World Objects

    Collision volumes can be added to splines and map objects and the particle effects will then react to those collision volumes. Terrain will also support collision. This means rain will hit terrain or the roof of a station and splash, and smoke will hit the underside of a bridge or the top of a tunnel, change velocity and direction and flow out naturally.

    D: Interlocking Towers / Track Blocks (UK: Signal Boxes)

    Support will be added to allow the linking of signals and switches, and for a user to operate either mechanical levers (in older style towers) or console buttons (in newer towers) to adjust and lock a set. This will also provide a big helping hand to AI to “understand” the route better.

    Q. What is an interlocking tower?

    A. Some of the fundamental principles of interlocking include:
    Signals are locked in sets to prevent conflicting train movements to take place at the same time.
    Switches and other appliances in the route must be properly 'set' (in position) before a signal may allow train movements to enter that route.

    Once a route is set and a train is given a signal to proceed over that route, all switches and other movable appliances in the route are locked in position until either
    the train passes out of the portion of the route affected, or
    the signal to proceed is withdrawn and sufficient time has passed to ensure that a train approaching that route has had opportunity to come to a stop before passing the signal.

  • Can you explain each of the Add-ons in more detail?

    Name a Street and Street Sign: Add $20 to your pledge amount

(We may offer more options if possible, depending on popularity). The avatar can then be added to station platforms, engine cabs or anywhere in the world using the Surveyor Editor Module.     As per the Stationmaster reward level, we will create a high resolution 3D scenery object specifically designed for you. The item can contain up to 10,000 polygons. As an indication, this would include items such as your house, your car, a bridge or perhaps a bunch of items found in a factory yard.

Lords of Tyberis is a strategy role-playing game featuring diplomacy, intrigue, backstabbing and power. Can you rule Tyberis?

Inspiration and Game Play

Edit: While we will be selling premium accounts (and offering them as a reward) to help fund server costs long term, Lords of Tyberis is going to be free to play. We don't believe that a game created by the generosity of others should be too focused on profit.

A great thing about Lords of Tyberis is that since our system has already been play tested (see history) and we've had so many games, we've been able to utilize the stories and characters our players created to enrich the history of our game world. Many of the events in the game's backstory are player created, and that's important to us. Your actions won't just mean that some faceless NPC gets 5 wolf pelts, they'll write the game's history and effect your House's ranking in the game world. We're also looking at the idea of allowing the top ranked player in each House to serve as that House's Lord,

The game's inspiration, for me, wasn't just Game of Thrones. I loved playing classic strategy games like Civilization 2, but always felt the diplomacy in them was somewhat lacking. I also enjoyed online text based role playing, but that style has fallen out of favor, sadly, with the dominance of MMORPGs which replace story building and real interaction between players with scheduled raids, farming and keeping to a detailed script to defeat each boss. Lords of Tyberis focuses on letting players use their creativity and cunning in an roleplaying environment with as few rules as possible.

When joining up, you enter a lobby to talk to other players. You can join games either as a Lord, or as an heir if a current Lord is leaving and needs someone to learn his plans and take over his House. You get an avatar and choose your House, name your character (class selection comes later), and then it's largely up to you from there. You target territories to attack or strongholds to besiege on the interactive map, and the game rolls dice for your success. This is a quick demo of what gameplay will look like using our beta client screen.

There are many paths to power in LoT - you can do your best to remain honorable and true, hoping for your friends to reward you for it and for a boost to your honor rating. Or you can plot your way to increasing your holdings and power by military conquest. Or you can betray and deceive your way to the throne, which will drop your honor, but raise your player and House standing. IF you can take power, you must then keep it by placating your friends and crushing your enemies, and, most importantly, knowing who is who!

Each of 9 players will (generally) be vying for the throne. How do you get the throne? House Zale or House Garland must support you, and you or an ally wll need to take the Capitol, Sovereign Hill, with a roll of 9 or 10. The king gets a +2 territory bonus and can name one of his allies as Lord Chancellor (who recieves a +1 territory bonus), great huh!? Unfortunately, when you manage to finesse someone else into declaring you King, that means no one else can be King. There are now 7 other Houses who are probably not happy with this outcome and may try to take the capitol, kill your house, or turn your allies agaisnt you so they can take the crown for their own. A game doesnt end until all other Houses swear fealty to the monarch, and accept his or her distribution of lands and titles. After that, land gain/loss is tallied, and XP awarded.

At the end of the game, your stats are tallied up for things like how well you keep your word, how much land you gain, titles, foes slain, etc. This affects your player stats, house ranking, and your character's levels. There are 10 character classes you can choose from in Lords of Tyberis each with game changing special abilities. To take a character class, you must survive a game and gain power in some form. Once you do this enough, you'll get your first character level and be able to pick a class. Some classes are more martial and affect how well you gain territory or conduct a siege. Others affect the diplomacy elements. As you level up, your powers can change the game world in larger and larger ways. We'll be starting with just one all level server, but as more characters attain higher levels we'll be branching off into high level servers for them.

History of Tyberis

Lords of Tyberis is the second incarnation of a popular fan made forum game that was based on Game of Thrones. But now we've created our own game world and characters based upon Medieval England and Europe, but kept the same system that players have enjoyed for over a year. In the first version of the game, map updates had to be handled by a person, a DM of sorts. There weren't enough skilled DMs for all the players we ended up having, and so we had to find a better way.

Discussion centered around a web based game with coding to do the updates, allow for diplomacy in a chat window, and make the game accessible to an even wider audience. To do this we need money for an HTML5 programmer, web hosting, design, databases, artists, musicians and more. Most of our staff are volunteers or working for very cheap or a share in our profit, but people need to eat. People like Mossed Neuronn, our lead artist responsible for images and banners and game maps

and Rafael Smith at www.rafaelsmith.com who has agreed to make some music for our donators, and may even be able to score the entire game if we surpass our goal. Team Tiberis, and where to find us

For more history, lore, images, video, and information about the development team, please check our facebook page www.facebook.com/lordsoftyberis or discuss our game on www.Crowdfundingforum.com and of course check our official website www.lordsoftyberis.com

Our current team consists of:

Jarret Herrmann: Designer, Producer, Story writer. My years of writing and publishing books has prepared me for the challenge of building the world of Tyberis.

Thomas Clark: Front end programmer/designer: he's the wizard moving all the hidden gears in Tyberis.

Mossed Neuronn: Mossed is our main artist, and if you donate you'll get to see more of his impressive work!

Jon Fitch: Music supervisor

Rafael Langoni Smith: Composer

We also have a few musicians and artists on contract for supplementary work and presents for donators to our campaign. If we surpass our goal, we'll be able to bring them into the project in a much bigger way, which means better quality for you! Additional reach goals include allowing players to upload and host their own original maps, creating a snazzy introduction video/demo for the site, and creating special historical campaign scenarios.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Much is risked in war, and in game creation, too! Nothing is certain. But we have a team of devoted people with the skills and experience to make this game a success. We're going to work every day to make Lords of Tyberis a great experience for everyone! Except the players who get killed, and will inevitably rage and be butthurt. They probably won't enjoy it too much. Will you be one of them? If so, sorry in adance. That's certainly a risk to consider, the risk of butthurt!

We've been working on this project for half a year already and there have been small changes in the dev team, but we all get along pretty well and we're all focused on Tyberis. Once our programmers and artists have the ability to devote themselves fully to the project, we don't foresee it taking more than 3 months to have something playable for you, but it could take longer.

WHO AM I?

I am Gary “Grim” Blauvelt. I'm an illustrator and computer programmer. I've been a computer technician for most of my working years, but always wanted to fall into the realm of comics and video games, or both...or both together. I've been in and out of both for the last twenty years, but always had my eyes set on my big epic project which is what I'm about to tell you about.  

Currently I work on illustration commissions, background work for an upcoming cable TV Show, and a Machinima Partner/Director!

WHAT IS PROJECT EPICA REX ABOUT?
Epica Rex as I have been calling it now for three months, is the amalgam of fifteen years of dreaming up. Back when Ultima Online came out, me and my close friends dreamed up a game called “Legends of Fantasy Unearthed”, which we wanted to be the Ultima Online killer made in our image. We had grand designs, dynamic worlds and epic visions of people making a place of their own.

At it's heart, it is a multi-player, character skill driven based RPG mixed with a portion of survival, and another portion of world building, war and politics.


Its styling is retro pixel art in isometric view similar to Ultima 8/Crusader No Remorse. A style that I notice more people are putting into their own retro-styled games these days. Which is very exciting!

The story is that pieces of our world, Earth have been ripped up throughout time along with many people into a strange new world. Ruins both old and new lay about this new world from our old earth. People have either banded together to form lawful and defensive in nature or lawless and aggressive. You can choose how you want to live in this world...which is randomly generated each time you start a new game.

If you have ever played the Strategy RPG; Age of Wonders you have a pretty good idea of how character building and skills will work. You have some very basic stats like Life, Energy, Armor, Resistance. Everything else is a skill you buy which will make your character unique from anyone else. Want to be stronger to carry more? Buy strength or more meta-inventory (for building resources). One is geared toward lugging your primary inventory, the other is more interested in either trading or building. 

Epica Rex will not be some run of the mill fantasy RPG though. It will start off in what I call the Archaic Period, where you have low technology levels, rituals and magic to work with. As the game becomes more developed, more ages will get added in; up to 4 main ages. Aside from the Archaic period, you have the Steam Age which will introduce a level of machinery based off coal and steam, as well as the first use of guns and gunpowder. Then onto the Modern Age where the use of electronics and other fuels sources become prevalent. And lastly the Future Age where the world can use high advanced technology, to either improve your life or way of doing battle. The Future Age tech will bring back balance against using magic from the Archaic's, though if you subscribe to any age personally you will never be at a disadvantage. You may freely use whichever, mainly based on the skills you choose.

The worlds of Epica Rex are randomly generated and on top of that, extremely large.  The world is build on plates of 32x32 tiles, and there are 65,000 of these laid out north to south, east to west.  If each tile square was a yard, the land would measure 1200 x 1200 Miles (Or 1.4 Million Square Miles).   The architecture of the landscape is procedural and doesn't need to be directly stored unless it has been altered by player or administrator activity, which saves on memory usage for administrators worried about hard drive space.

Epica Rex is a multi-player only game. This is no true single player game – though you may start your own server and play alone if you wish. But the game at it's heart was meant for 2-64 people to join in at any one time and share the experience of your world personal world.  But you may have hundreds or thousands of people subscribed to your world overall.  Each world has its own user account system, passwords, etc.

Along with the game client, comes the game server. Each and every player can easily set up and run an Epica Rex server as soon as the game itself is installed.

Two players may even link up their servers and cross over between the two as long as each server is running. This can be repeated for any number of servers for any number of players.

Probably the best feature for those interested in running worlds is the ability to shape and edit your world as you see fit. You may include or exclude any level of technology, skill, spell, or even types of items and terrain features from being generated. You can make your world as large or small as you wish – you will even be able to turn off the random generator and completely make your world as you want it. You will be able to set options for starting skill packages players may buy. So for instance, you want a horror world where people play as Vampires, Werewolves, Hunters, and so on – you could set up skill packages for these archetypes as you see fit. Once a player selects such a package – they're stuck with it as long as you maintain the skill package rule.

WHY NOW?  At the time of Legends of Fantasy; 15 years ago, we didn't have any experience, the right knowledge or resources nor the technology to pull it off. I'm sure it could have been done. But it was beyond us. I kept most of the notes and over the years I would re-start it in various forms, get stuck and move on to something else. 

I think now, with all the knowledge I have gathered about programming – having cut my teeth sharply on such things as NWN Script (A C++ based scripting languge), Java Script and C# in Unity 3D, and of course the root of my programming BASIC (Specifically Dark Basic) I could probably figure out how to handle complex systems and I have already made a client/server tech demo which worked reasonably well for a first go at something of that nature.

I think now there are far more resources available for wanna-be game developers to be able to keep the team small and costs down.

But it's time for this project to grow up. Move out of the Darkbasic/Dark GDK and into something more cross-platform friendly such as Unity 3D (while still being a 2D game no less!).

I have already done a good chunk of the tile and character graphics for the game. The planning for how the game operates and is interfaced with is 90% worked out. It's just time to get funded and be able to get the right tools for the job and be able to maintain a higher level of visibility and professionalism on the project.

WHERE THE GAME IS NOW?In September and October two tech demo pre-alpha versions were released to test the concept. During this time I learned a lot about development and what needs to be done. I have since taken my code apart and I am in the process of producing code far more efficient and organized than my first go. I would suggest, if you haven't to take a look at the tech demo client/server. Currently though, since I want to cross into more platforms than just PC, I am looking into developing the game with the 2D Toolkit for UNITY. Mostly, I have been creating the hundreds (which will turn into thousands) of game sprites for the terrain and character sprites (which you can see pasted around this page). If this proves possible and sane for myself, the cross platform compatibility will be a huge step in getting more people into the game.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

The most challenging part of this game is going to be time. I have set a very loose goal of December 2013 (should it become funded) as the time in which Project Epica Rex will be in a playable stable state. But understand, I am both artist and programmer, boyfriend and father on this project and time comes and goes.

Indeed. This is a fairly complex project. Any game that involves multi-player elements is immediately far more complex than a single player game. Add in the fact that it is an RPG and building game, the scope is huge. However, I have already proven that most of these elements are perfectly feasible to do in my tech demo.

Refinement and platform handling are going to be challenges as well. Upon funding this project, it will switch from DARKBASIC/DARK GDK over to UNITY 3D (Using the 2D Toolkit). There will be a slight adjustment time as I acquaint myself with the intricacies of this platform. While I am familiar with both JAVA and C# (Through C++ programming) Unity itself will take an adjustment period.

Once funded, I will keep people very up to date on its development. And I hope to be able to get a public relations person on board to help out with that.

FAQ

  • What will the NPC's be like?

    This question was asked today via a private message. The easy answer is NPC's, "intelligent" NPC's fall into a couple of categories. Hostile, which will attempt to kill you. Cautious, will more likely respond with violence if you have your weapons out or they may be open to bribing to make them open to negotiation. Neutral; Will not attack unless directly provoked, and are open to trade. But these NPC's will unlikely be your allies or hirelings. Peaceful; These are NPC's who are open to trade and more willing to become hirelings.

    Neutrals will either teach you skills, or trade with you.

    Friendly NPC's will do the same, but there is a chance you can convince them to join you as a hireling. In order to do this you must have something they want, a bed to place them in and then assign them a task (which is usually based on their skill set).

    For administrators of a game server, you can create NPC's of any type. And you can even attach simple word context conversations to your NPC's (player clicks on a word that is highlighted in the NPC's text, information is given). An administrator can build a town, and set the populace as they wish. Unlike random NPC's, these NPC's will stick around. Randomly generated NPC's generally don't stay spawned in the world, unless they are tied to a VIP character somehow (ie: A named Bandit King, or a Scientist and his guards).

  • Platform Support

    I am currently looking into UNITY 3D and AGK. Both of which have multi-platform support. My goal is to support as many platforms as possible. UNITY would be ideal, since it supports the most platforms, but for a 2D game it may not be entirely ideal. AGK supports Windows, Mac, Android, iOS and Bada, and has plans to support browsers in the near future. AGK is geared more towards 2D games and would a good pick as well. I am still weighing the options, but both support Windows/Mac which was the lower set goal when I began working on ER.

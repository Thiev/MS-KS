Earthcore: Shattered Elements is a dynamic Action Card Game for PC, Mac, iOS and Android that combines card collecting with fast-paced combat and true tactical depth. Should the project be funded, you will be able to enjoy it absolutely for free later this year.

We have prepared a gameplay video to show you a sample battle. Check it out below and let us know what you think!

 Key game features:

  • Single Player (PvE): dramatic story-based campaign with dozens of quests, chapters and missions.
  • Multiplayer (PvP): robust online mode with tactical battles, special events, rankings and leagues.
  • Gameplay: groundbreaking mechanics, based on three core elements: Water, Nature, Fire. No mindless number crunching is necessary, just pure tactical thought.
  • Cross-Platform Experience: start playing on mobile, switch to iPad mid-day and continue playing on PC/MAC in the evening.
  • Hand-Painted Cards: over 200 gorgeous cards at launch with more coming to the game on regular basis.
  • Amazing Audio: featuring music by a prolific Polish composer Jerzy Adam Nowak.
  • Cinematic Experience: magical, mystical and mysterious, making you feel like a star of a fantasy film.
  • Deep Metagame: rebuild the city of Elemental Bearers and bring back prosperity to your people. 

We want to change the definition of what a TCG could be. Our goals are:

• To provide a gateway drug into the world of Collectible Card Games for beginners while satisfying TCG veterans with deeply strategic gameplay.

• To tell an interesting story set in an enchanted world, full of intrigue, mystery and plot twists, starring well developed characters.

• To create a fully cross-platform, free-to-play experience that allows you to play on any device you own without spending a dime.

• To give you a way to battle your friends and strangers in lightning-quick online multiplayer matches and advance to progressively more prestigious leagues.

• To keep you entertained for years to come by constantly updating the number of available cards and selectable story missions.

The basics are easy to grasp but individual card skills add immense strategic depth to the duels. Some cards provide protection to others, some can change their element, some move on the battlefield. Eventually, fights stop being about matching colors and turn into high-stake battles of the wit.

Each encounter is divided into two phases: Attack and Defense. One player’s Attack Phase is basically the other’s Defense Phase. There are six slots for cards on the game table, three per player. At the beginning of a round, players draw cards from their decks so that they have as many in their hand as there are free slots on their part of the table, plus one.

Once the players put their cards in the desired slots, the battle begins. Cards fight each other from left to right. Defeated cards land in the graveyard. If an empty slot is attacked, the victim loses a card from his deck, and if the deck is depleted, from his hand. If the hand is empty as well, the player loses the battle.

There are also elementless cards that lose to every element. To make up for it, those cards usually have the most powerful skills.Players may earn in-game Gold by completing story missions, participating in multiplayer battles or finishing quests and getting achievements. In addition, there are special buildings – such as the mines - that the player might construct and manage outside of combat to increase the amount of currency earned. Gold can then be spent on booster packs, traps that catch wild cards and, most importantly, fusion. By fusing together a few weaker cards, players create new, much more powerful units.

Welcome to the world of Earthcore: Shattered Elements, a land of magic, mystery and adventure. When the titular Earthcore shattered millennia ago, its fragments rained upon the planet, each containing an element inside. The land turned into a wasteland, part of it consumed by flames, part frozen, part overgrown with wildlife. The people struggle to rebuild but their efforts will be for naught unless balance is restored to the elements.

You become one of the last Elemental Bearers - an elite gathering of individuals sworn to protect the Earthcore, blessed with a unique power to command all the elements. For hundreds of years, Elemental Bearers have been questing to collect the shards of Earthcore and combine them back into what is essentially the heart of the world.

Guided by the mysterious Spark, you will learn to harness the might of the elements and battle the forces of Berak, a fallen Elemental Bearer that has been driven insane with lust for power. Along the journey, you will encounter dozens of interesting characters and hundreds of terrifying monsters.

Each card represents a character or creature under your command. By inspecting a card, you can see the element associated with it - fire, water, nature or none. There is also a six-level rarity indicator on every card. Finally, cards may have up to three specific skills and any number of shield points protecting them for damage.

TCG or CCG is a card game that is played using unique, non-standard sets of playing cards. Those cards resemble traditional cards in terms of shape and size but allow for strategic gameplay specific to the game they are featured in.

Cards in this kind of games are collectible and used to construct a deck - a player-made combination of cards that, depending on their composition, can have different tactical advantages and disadvantages. While minute-to-minute rules differ between TCGs, the overall goal is most often to collect all the most powerful cards, either by winning them in duels or purchasing booster packs, and constructing an unbeatable deck out of them.

Most TCGs are complicated, difficult to pick up and time consuming. Many of them require an hour or more for a comfortable play session and have steep learning curve. With dozens of cards in play and numerous resources to keep track of, such as Mana or global health, the matches are hard to follow and require long preparations.  

Earthcore: Shattered Elements is the first ever Action TCG because we are not afraid to defy tradition. Our game is dynamic, quick to play and easy to learn. Your only resource are your cards – once your opponent loses all of them, the battle is won.

And yet, depth-wise, we want to go above and beyond other TCGs. While we freely admit that there are less choices to make in a single round of the game than in similar games, we assure you that each choice is interesting and can make the difference between winning or losing.

Before going to Kickstarter, we decided to give ourselves a chance to pitch the game to a couple of publishers just to find out that such an ambitious game is unfundable even if we have half project completed. It’s a classic egg-chicken problem: publishers would invest into a game that has proven to be a successful product, ideally a revenue-genrerating blockbuster with a quickly increasing player base. The thing is: you first need to have the product (and release it) before proving to someone that it’s successful. Ironically, who needs a publisher when the self-released product turns out to be successful?

We have learned a lesson that in today’s market climate, with every publisher chasing the next casual hit making millions of dollars per day, funding a fully featured TCG such as Earthcore through traditional means is nearly impossible. Earthcore is not a mass market game - it is not supposed to be the next Candy Crush, nor is it the next GTA.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Earthcore is a complex cross-platform game and as in every project of this complexity, there are many risks involved in the development. Most of all, Earthcore requires a sophisticated approach to project management. Being aware of this, we have started preparing ourselves for the project very early. When it comes to planning, scheduling and executing we are risk averse. We make our decisions cautiously, with great consideration and detailed calculation. With our field-tested SCRUM based approach to game development, we’re confident we can deliver Earthcore according to the plan.

With our backers - YOU - onboard as our de facto publisher, we’re putting even greater effort into scheduling and on-time execution of all milestones we have planned for Earthcore. We have chosen a realistic funding goal that will support the required development, and we are committed to a realistic timeline with built-in contingencies should we encounter any unforeseen problems.

One of the challenges ahead is to develop and successfully release (and then maintain) a cross-platform game with a robust PvP mode that is available for both mobile & desktop platforms (plus consoles if stretch goals are met). Having the cross-platform approach in mind, we had decided to develop Earthcore using Unity 4.0 engine which greatly facilitates development across many different platforms. It’s our fifth consecutive project using Unity framework so we can comfortably say that we feel familiar with this environment and our development team is able to get through the development challenges in a smooth way.

As you may expect, building a true online game in which players compete head-to-head, communicate with each other and team up into guilds is a pretty complex thing requiring solid investments and months of development. Many gaming studios seeking funding on Kickstarter often do not realize what it takes to build a reliable solution to enable multiplayer online gaming for a successful title – a solution that does not crash out when thousands of backers and potentially millions of other players jump into the game on day one! The need to invest into reliable, scalable infrastructure such as game servers, back-end systems, player management and so on adds a completely new level of complexity and a risk to manage.

Fortunately, we are in a comfortable position as we had sorted those challenges and are prepared for a large scale interest. Here’s how: one of the things we are most proud is that over the past two years we have developed and field-tested our entire online gaming backend (all of this while being a relatively small 20 people studio). Our cloud-based infrastructure for online games has been successfully used in our BattleFriends game series that we had released on iOS and Android. It is the same infrastructure that we will be reusing in Earthcore: Shattered Elements and this is why we are guaranteeing a true multiplayer experience right from the start!

Last but not least, our development team is comprised on seasoned veterans who worked on a large number of different games in the past including major AAA titles. Everything presented on the screen has been painstakingly created by disciplined professionals: designers, graphic artists, programmers and composers. We are certain that with both our enthusiasm and know-how, we will manage to overcome all the potential hurdles.

FAQ

  • What languages will be available in Earthcore: SE?

    The initial release will be in English, French, Italian, German, Spanish and Polish. Russian is also a possibility at launch but we are not able to confirm that at this time due to additional challenges associated with non-Latin alphabet. Afterwards we wish to keep adding more languages so that all our fans may fully enjoy the epic story we are telling.

  • What platforms will Earthcore: SE be available on?

    Our initial target platforms are PC, Mac, iOS and Android. Should the first set of stretch goals be met, we are also planning adaptations for Linux and Kindle Fire. In addition, we are currently investigating further platforms, such as PS Vita, Ouya, 3DS and Wii U. Let us know what device you would like to play Earthcore on and watch this space for new information!

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • What are the differences between desktop & mobile versions?

    We are developing both versions concurrently so no version is a simple port of the other. Naturally, each version has a slightly different interface providing a native experience on each platform. Desktop versions (PC/Mac) will also have better quality graphical assets as we are not as constrained with CPU and memory limitations as is the case for the mobile editions.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • What does it mean that Earthcore: SE is free to play?

    Should our funding goal be met, anyone will be able to download the game after it is released absolutely free of charge. While Earthcore does feature microtransactions – such as booster packs or special avatars – there will be no non-vanity items that cannot be acquired through normal play. Additionally, due to our match-making system, it is impossible for players of wildly different power levels to face each other in ranked matches, so pay-to-win is absolutely out of question.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • What does it mean that Earthcore: SE is cross-platform?

    All the game data is held in the cloud so you can start playing on your PC, then continue the game on your phone, play a few sessions on your tablet and then finish up on a friend’s Mac. All you need to enjoy this feature is an Internet connection.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • What platforms will the Beta be available on?

    The initial Beta will be available on PC and Mac.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • How will I get the in-game rewards?

    You will receive a personalized code that will immediately unlock all the content that you are entitled to. This content will be available to you on all supported platforms without the need to enter the code again, as long as you keep using the same in-game account.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • Will the in-game rewards affect the game balance?

    Not to a significant degree as our match-making system prevents players of wildly different power levels to compete with each other in ranked multiplayer matches. Some of the most powerful rewards might make your progression in the Single Player campaign a bit faster but not to the point you will not have a satisfying experience. Our designers worked hard on making the in-game rewards truly valuable but not game-breaking and we are sure you will agree they succeeded.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • When will Earthcore: SE come out?

    The PC, Mac, iOS and Android versions of Earthcore: Shattered Elements will be released to the public by December 2014. While our current plan is to release the first version way before then, we prefer to give ourselves some room for changes resulting from your feedback and potential additional funding.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • What are the shipping costs for physicial goods?

    Shipping is free to US, EU, UK and Canadian backers. If you live outside of the countries/regions mentioned here, please add $20 (€15 / £12) to help offset shipping costs.

    Last updated: Mon, Feb 24 2014 1:13 pm EST
  • When will you ship the rewards?

    Digital reward codes will be sent to you in the days leading up to the launch of the first official release. You will receive all the physical rewards by December 2014.

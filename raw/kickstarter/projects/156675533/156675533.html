The Great War is a strategic WWI computer game which is based on the core values of classic board games. Keep it simple but strategic!

At June the 28th 2014 the starting point of the Great War, the assassination of Franz Ferdinand of Austria, will reach its 100th anniversary.

For this upcoming event we plan to create a strategic computer game which focuses on World War I and will transfer the player into this decade full of armed conflicts, political changes and technical improvements.

Game type: round based strategy computer game

Game engine: UNITY 3D

Target platforms: Windows
possible offset: Linux and Mac; tablet OS
out of scope: consoles

Gameplay:
Single player
Multiplayer (LAN- and Online-capable)

Languages: English, German, Spanish, Russian, French, Italian, Turkish

Estimated release date: Q3 2014

For detailed information: thegreatwar1914.com

Besides the strategic aspect of the game the atmosphere is one of our core focus areas. The brutal acts of world war one are long ago and not so well known as world war two or modern wars. We want the player to dive into the difficulties of this time and to show him how military tactics failed to keep pace with advances in technology. Impressive defense systems were built and the trench warfare was used on an unprecedented scale. Outflanking maneuvers like the âRace to the Seaâ where adopted to avoid fortified places. New offensive weapons such as tanks and airplanes were used in battle for the first time in history.

The game should reflect these circumstances and give the player the possibility to plunge into the darkness of these days. He will be able to change the face of Europe with every battle. At the battlefields the landscape of the map will change bit by bit until there is only a vast no-man's-land there. Craters will appear in the battle zone and soldiers will dig trenches along borders. The player will easily be able to identify hard-fought areas on the map.

The idea is to create goals in the game similar to quests in MMOS to ensure a variable game development and to generate additional tasks apart from the main goal to win the war with units and strategy. With this feature we want to ensure, that the game stays exciting even when the player frequently chooses the same country.Â 

The Quests will be a mixture based on historical events like âThe general staff ordered you to capture the fortress of Verdunâ and general applicable events like âHelp to free a prisoner of war by sending a spy to a certain enemy capitalâ. Because some Quests will trigger other quests there is the chance to dive into interesting storylines. If a player carries out the given tasks he earns popularity or gains reputation this will be important to keep its nation calm and win the war.

Each quest will be illustrated and will have a historical background. We will show pictures of the events to get the player into the story.

As a supreme commander the player will occasionally get quests from the government. To please his government he can accept and fulfill these missions. The more quests the player finishes victoriously the more reputation he earns. If he declines a quest he loses reputation.

The player has to be aware of not falling from grace within his empire. If his reputation goes down too far he will be replaced by a better general and the player loses his game.

The government will also gain popularity points which reflect the mood of the population and how much they like their government. With more food supply and less conscription the popularity of the government will rise but of course the supply of soldiers will decline.

Another way to gain points is to conquer provinces. Therefore each province has a certain number of reputation and popularity points depending on its historical importance. This will focus the battles around some areas that nobody will be able to afford to lose.

World war one was a global war because every nation was part of an alliance. If one alliance was activated, an endless chain of official and secret pacts came into force. Even though this game is a war game, diplomacy will be a core part of the game.

There are two types of diplomacy:

Official diplomacy
Official diplomacy is the diplomacy between nations and is visible to all and known to all, e.g.: formal alliance.

Secret diplomacy
Secret diplomacy is the diplomacy with ethnic groups within a country or official nations. The catch is, it is only known by the involved parties.Â  Only spys will be able to uncover these secret agreements.

Nation:
For example the player of Germany can make a secret pact with Spain, that if Italy enters the war, Spain enters the war on the side of Germany, or a secret right of passage can be negotiated or secret peace negotiations can be carried out with one nation.

Ethnic group:
A regional independence movement can be supported (e.g. Ireland), intelligence from an area of an ethnic group can be delivered (e.g. clearing fog of war in this area) or the ethnic group can be persuaded to carry out sabotage acts and more.

Like you can move tanks and infantry on the map, you will also be able to take control over âspecial personsâ. Those will be historical characters that will be treated as spies with special abilities. They (e.g. Lenin or AtatÃ¼rk) can only be developed once in a game. The idea is to create a race for historical persons like the race for world wonders in Civilization.

As long as they are not unmasked and stopped by an enemy spy they move undetected, even through provinces that belong to an opponent. To protect his historical characters the player can escort them with spies.

When such a person appears in the game, all players will get a warning. We will explain some of these abilities of special persons with Rasputin as example.

Rasputin. Once successfully sent to a capital of a nation Rasputin will diminish the attack strength of the troops and will increase the chances of revolts within this nation.

We derive these abilities from the historical Person Rasputin who was a spiritual Leader in Russia and managed to find his way into the family of the Russian Czar by helping the haemophilic son of the Russian Czar Alexander. Rasputin was a ruthless character who influenced politics through the Czar family. He managed to oust the minister of war and get his own but corrupted minister elected. This corruption lead to severe supply shortages for the Russian troops in the field. Through his drunkenness, sexual promiscuity and his willingness to accept bribes he added greatly to the destruction of the public opinion of the Czar in Russia.

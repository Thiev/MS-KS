Possibly the best retro beat ‘em up/Platform/Adventure/Puzzle game ever?

Download Draculas Castle Demo v1.0

(For Windows 98/Me/2000/XP/Vista/W7)

Possibly the best retro beat ‘em up/Platform/Adventure/Puzzle game ever? Really?

Ok, I know that’s a bit of a grand claim, but it may well be the case with this one. How often do you get a game that covers all of these genres? I have spent over 13 years thinking and designing this one to get it “just right”. To be brief, I wanted something that was easy to get into, but not shallow. Many casual games look very nice but just don’t seem to have enough substance to make an interesting play for more than 5 minutes. On the other hand, a grand epic which takes 10 minutes to get past the opening cinematic and another 20 to create the character can be a bit much! I find nothing wrong with either approach, I just wanted a ‘pick up and go’ type of game that also had some depth to it. The main feature that I have been trying to aim for, however, is perhaps answered by this question: 

‘What game would I have made in 1983 if I had more memory and processing power back then?’ 

(For the technical guys imagine a Spectrum with 1Mb RAM, a less restrictive screen VDU and a 32Mhz Z80! What about a Commodore 64 with a 16Mhz 6510 CPU/system bus and 1Mb RAM and a VIC colour RAM expansion? Or perhaps imagine if SGI workstations were available for £250!). I have tried to keep the vision as close to that ideal as possible and have hopefully incorporated all the fun and atmosphere and most importantly, game play that went along with the games of that era. One key aspect that I was trying to avoid is the over-cluttered complication of more modern titles, in game play as well as graphical style. Simple and fun but with lasting appeal. That’s what I’m trying to achieve here. 

What’s so special about it?

Key Points:

* Up to 12 human (or AI) controlled players on screen at once! As far as I know this has never been done before!

* Completely editable game content including characters, level design and all associated graphics/sounds!

* This mix of beat'em up, adventure, puzzle and platform game has never been seen before now!

The Game:

Dracula’s Castle is a spoof horror platform adventure puzzle game with fighting included. For those of you old enough to remember, it’s probably best described as Datasoft’s ‘Bruce Lee’ mixed with ‘The Castles of Dr.Creep’ with a smattering of many others (Jet Set Willy, Hero of the Golden Talisman, Cauldron, Yie Ar Kung Fu, Pitfall, etc). For those of you who don’t remember them, it’s probably not really like anything you’ve tried before! And for those that do, there is far more depth to this game. Here’s how the story goes… A group of famous heroes have gone out ‘on the town’ in Transylvania and have all had one too many drinks. The problem being, they are too wasted to give the taxi driver the right directions home and so they end up being taken to and dropped off outside Dracula’s Castle. So they decide to walk into the courtyard to see if anyone is in and BANG! The portcullis comes down behind them and they are trapped within the castle walls! The only way out now, is to navigate through each room, sometimes by having to find a key and then open the locked exit to the next screen and eventually to freedom. The problem is, the castle belongs to Count Dracula, and he will pursue the characters throughout the game attempting to stop them, along with his cronies who will make appearances on various screens. On some screens there may be closed doors or cupboards which may contain items which will aid/hinder the characters in their battle against Dracula and his evil minions. These can generally in some way be used to keep the nasties at bay for a while, or maybe protect the character from a dangerous part of the castle itself. However, sometimes an item is cursed and will prove to be a hindrance in some way or sometimes even fatal! All the characters, apart from being able to jump, climb and swim have a range of different fighting moves that they can perform; each one as individual as the next. These various attack moves may be more effective against some nasties than others. Falling on someone will damage them too! The heavier the better!

The full game will have 12 characters. 4 of which are “heroes” and the other 8 which are nasties, all controlled by joystick, joy pad or by the computer. In adventure mode, the game will involve up to 4 human hero characters trying to get the key on each screen to exit to the next, whilst avoiding/attacking the nasties (some or all which may be human controlled) along the way and also picking up items which might help them with their objectives. The adventure game will be cleverly designed so that players can opt to play their favourite game type by choosing to go in a certain path. For example, if you prefer puzzle solving over combat, there will be a route through the castle which requires more thought to get through the screen than combat skill. However, if you are more action inclined you can take a different route and deal with less puzzles or adventure elements and just concentrate more on the fighting. The main path through the game, however, will include all of these elements, so it’s best to polish up all your skills as you never know what you might encounter in the shadowy corners of the game!

The game areas:

The game spans a number of different areas. From the castle grounds, the hero or heroes must navigate through the inner castle to reach the exit and freedom. But there are many different places to explore. I won’t give away too much; but you may be able to find secret passages or unlock special doors, which lead to vast underground caverns, forgotten graveyards and perhaps places which are far more sinister. In total there are over 100 screens to explore... If you can find them all!

The Hazards:

As well as the nasties chasing you around, there are many objects and hazards in each room or area that can help, cause hindrance, pain, or even loss of life. You must learn how to pass, dodge or just plain avoid them. Below are just a few examples: 

Crusher: Designed to press body parts out of chicken nuggets (stolen from the local fast food drive thru!) for the Frankenstein monster, when a fresh cadaver part isn’t available. This lethal hazard stomps careless characters into the floor causing instant death. 

Van Der Graaf Generator: Dracula is looking to mass produce Frankenstein monsters (cheap recession proof labour, you see!) and they will be used to spark these monstrosities into life. Some are triggered from elsewhere; others are auto-triggered by someone stood under them. They produce a bolt of electricity that can cause massive damage to a character. Beware!

Cutter: Used for sawing Dracula’s coffins into shape, but can also be very deadly to the unfortunate character who steps onto one of these!

Sinking Sand: Avoid at all costs! The poor soul who unsuspectingly walks onto this will get a terrible surprise; they will get sucked down to contemplate their stupidity as they slowly drown!

Vampiron (TM) Tablets: Dracula has ploughed a substantial amount of cash into sun block research and the fruit of such labour has been this product. It’s like a concentrated, internally digested, high power sun block cream, created by highly paid demented scientists. Such a pill was created to allow vampires to go out in the daytime, but if another character was to gulp it down, it may just have extreme effects on ordinary people… Who knows!

Force Field: Cannot be passed and will even cause damage if touched in some cases. However, there is usually a switch somewhere close to turn them off.

‘It’ Block: When a character walks over this block, he then becomes ‘it’ and all the computer controlled characters will go looking for this unfortunate character. Mind where you step!

Puncho Block: This block can be continuous, triggered from elsewhere or self triggered. In any case, if a character is stupid enough to stand in front of it when a punch has been swung then… well… I hope you like seeing stars or are particularly fond of lying on the floor, looking up and wondering about what just happened!

Slime: Unlike water, which isn’t too dangerous (unless you live in a big city and go for a swim in the local river!), slime is a waste product from the castle. I’m not even going to start to describe what’s in it. Suffice to say, perhaps it’s not the best mouth wash alternative in the world!

Spell Block: This block oozes a certain magical aura which can be dangerous to the touch. In fact, such high concentrations of magic are contained within its mysterious shroud, that it could even will you to turn back to a more primal creature! A sudden shock (like falling or a good smack) usually clears the senses and restores you back, though! 

The Characters:

‘Long Chop’ Lee:* Legendary King of Kung Fu. Needs no introduction. Probably the most suitable fighter of the bunch. (Special Attack: Flying Roundhouse). 

Ike Biteson: Infamous ear biting boxer. Seems to have spent more time in Jail/Court than in the ring. (Special Attack: Bite Opponent). 

Mr.BIG: Hilariously large British wrestler. Don’t let him fall on you! (Special attack: Spinning Fairy and Belly Ponce).

Drucilla the Witch: This woman is an old country witch. More black mass than white candles, though and watch out for the claws! (Special attack: Night Club Stiletto Attack and Succubus Kiss).

Dracula:* Probably the most famous scary character of all time. Be careful, as he has had several centuries to practice his fighting skills! (Special Attack: Demon Possession Bite).

Zamo: Zamo had his glory days when he was hired to destroy Li. He failed miserably and decided on a more solemn career, so he went to work for Dracula as a chef. Seeing Li again has made him very angry and he still has some old scores to settle. (Special Attack: Open mouthed Chicken Punch).

Frankenstein:* The grand daddy of what is now called “plastic surgery”. Taller than the other characters and, for goodness sake, don’t get in range of that punch! (Special Attack: Leaping Salmon Manoeuvre).

Bones: Not a member of the original Star Trek team, but almost as old! (Special Attack: Bone Clobber).

Fu Manchu: An old Chinese villain who is a master of deception and cunning. Not bad at Kung Fu either! (Special Attack: Flying Matrix Kick).

Mad Axeman: This nut will attack any hero who look like they need their neck trimming… That’s generally all of them. (Special attack: Executioners chop).

Pinyata the Yeti: This female Yeti is out of her comfort zone and very distressed here. She has had her young children kidnapped by Dracula and he has somehow twisted her into thinking that it’s the hero characters who have taken them. So she’s in no mood to reason with anyone! (Special Attack: Woodland Uppercut).

Mummy:* Called from the grave by Dracula because of his fierce warrior king reputation many millennia ago. However time changes things and this guy is a bit rusty and about 4000 years past his prime. He is still immensely powerful though. (Special Attack: Wrath of Ra and Spell from the book of the dead).

The Code:

It’s not until you really look at what can be done with this game that you can appreciate the depth of it all. I have included several different game genres and extra detail to each one. This has resulted in a very extensive game code, but one which has been well maintained throughout its creation. No game has perfect code, but after the demo release, I have also allowed for over 6 months of user feedback code maintenance and error fixing in the DC game roadmap. I am confident that I can maintain the code to a high standard throughout the life cycle of the game.

The Development Kit:

As far as I’m aware, no one has tried to create a retro beat ‘em up/platform adventure/ puzzle game on any computer, ever! And even if there was one, or if you find that this game is not quite your thing, then the many years of honing the game engine -with the forward thinking applied to allow for end user editing of every single feature that the code framework allows- means that the end user can create their own game, which can be vastly different to my design in many ways. With the DC1 development kit, you can design many different genres of game, like arcade adventures, puzzler, platform games or a beat ‘em up. (All these are elements that Dracula’s Castle has in game.) Anything I can create for the game can be harnessed by the end user with the DC1 Development Kit. In fact, if you do create a game, (there will be a competition for user projects later) you could end up famous or winning some great prizes because of it! 

The Facts:

Let’s look at the cold hard facts and get a glimpse of what the DC1 system has under the bonnet:

Game Types:

3 game types are available. Adventure, Death match and Capture the Key:

• Adventure mode allows players to navigate back and forth between screens to complete the story.

• Death match is a free for all fight between all the game characters on one screen. Whoever lasts the longest wins.

• Capture the Key is similar to Capture the Flag games, however, a character must find a key on the screen and use it to exit. The team who does so wins. 

Characters:

• 12 different fully editable 64x64 pixel size characters are available in the game. All can be AI or user controlled by joystick/joypad.

• 120 different character states (e.g. running, climbing, attacking, etc.) per character.

• 32 animation frames available per character state, each with individual speed control.

• 7 different attack moves available for each character per direction. Screens:

• 1000 different (1024x768 pixels resolution in 16 or 32 bpp) screens available in the game (25 each for DM and CTK, up to 950 for Adventure Game mode).

• 4096 screen blocks (64x64 pixel size) available for building screens.

• 60 Available animation frames per screen block. Each with individual speed control.

• 1000 of the screen blocks can be alpha blended (e.g. for water) or additive alpha blended (e.g. for lighting).

• Blocks can be in front or behind the characters.

• 16 Different types of block animation (collision animated, remotely triggered, etc).

AI:

• Advanced and well thought out mature 80+ stage FSM AI system. Designed to deal with any user made variation in game dynamics. Just needs to be ‘switched on’ for any character that requires it (Advanced scanning system to be unlocked in full release).

What do I need you to do?

I have coded the best possible framework for the game content that I will create. The coding side of this project is pretty much complete, with just the full game AI unlocking (for more advanced AI situation resolving like being able to deal with dangerous or tricky screen blocks) to be completed and any minor bug fixes that may crop up after the demo release or code maintenance as required.
However, the actual game content is generally at a fairly early stage- as can be seen in the demo. As the game has expanded in the complexity of its features, (earlier goals have been expanded upon significantly and the workload has increased exponentially) I can no longer maintain the level of commitment needed for this grand project, whilst at a part time hobbyist level. Game dynamics have to be tuned, characters need to gel properly into the game and attack moves need to be balanced so as not to give any unfair advantages. Screens also need to be engineered to give more of a challenge whilst still remaining fun to play. Levels need to flow, continuing the theme for that particular area. Puzzles need to be devious, whilst not requiring the player to have to totally tune into my wavelength, etc.
What I’m saying is, basically there are so many different parts of the game that I now have to tweak, adjust or just work on, that I can only show you a basic glimpse of what the game will be providing in its finished state. I have much to do and aside from the points I have just mentioned, here is a rough outline of what’s needed to complete the project:

• Finish designing, drawing, animating and tweaking the strengths, flaws, speeds and general dynamics of the 4 demo characters. As well as creating from scratch and doing the same for the extra 8 full game characters! (As an example, if I set an average of just 3 frames of animation per character state for every character that needs animating, then that’s (3x120x8) 2880 frames of animation that I need to draw just for the characters! Then I have to adjust frame change speeds, attack timings, energies and other such variables to make it good enough to use.)

• Finish drawing and designing all the game screen blocks. This includes any animations, animation types, block type setting and all the other block values that need to be set. I then need to test them (to see what ‘works’ in the game) and incorporate them in the screen design.

• Design all the screens for the game, checking there are no flaws (screens cannot be completed, missing items, logic flaws, continuity flaws, etc). This is no small task in itself despite having developed my own level and block editor!

• Any other thing that needs finishing, from game music tweaking to titlescreen changes. I’m sure there will be something that I have overlooked!

All this takes time! That’s something that I do not have anymore. But with successful funding, I can carry on working to put all my efforts into the completion of this very individual- in more than one sense- game.

With your help I can complete my game in full and finally release a game that has always had its roots in the 80’s. Think of it as a great game that was lost for years and has only now surfaced! Relatively untouched by the design processes and ideas of more contemporary titles- due to its actual concept, but borrowing one or two great ideas that have come to pass since, (namely the DM and CTK game modes) I will try my best to re-light that early game atmosphere that so many people are trying to harness in the retro game industry.
I will pledge that I will try my very best to make it a great treat for all the fans of retro games. And if that’s still not good enough, then YOU can put it right by creating what YOU had envisaged! I have gone to great lengths to include all the necessary tools to create any variety of different characters or screens that you may want to use in your own game. Everything I have put in the DC1 engine can be harnessed in the Developer Kit.

I have put every ounce of effort and creative thinking that I possess into this project. I hope I have made clear how much work this game has had ploughed into it and more importantly, will have added to it with your help, and I would just like to thank you for any help that you can contribute to this ultimate retro cause! Thank you very much for taking the time to read this far and please try the demo for yourself!

FAQ

  • What platform is the game for?

    The game is for Windows and should work fine on Windows 98/Me/2000/XP/Vista and W7. You should also have at least one game controller plugged in to play. The demo can accept up to 4 controllers, and the full release will take up to 12!

  • Why should I fund this project?

    If you would like to see the full version of the game then this is the only way it will ever happen! I am in full time employment (sometimes doing 14 hour days) and will not be able to finish the game without being able to work on it full time. (Which is where your backing is needed). To get this far, working in the few hours that I do get to myself, it has taken 13 years. To finish this project without the funding may take a comparable amount of time to that! Hence if I am sucessfully funded I can release the game in a reasonable time frame.

    Last updated: Sun, Nov 3 2013 5:47 am EST
  • Will the game be available for IOS/Android/Linux?

    The game is written exclusively for the Windows platform and the controls needed for up to 12 players just dont exist or are not practically viable for the current generation of Ipads and Android Tablets.
    That's not to say definitely no, but just definitely not for this project funding.

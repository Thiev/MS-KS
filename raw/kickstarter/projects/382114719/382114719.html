Starfighter: Eclipse:

We are looking to create an adult visual novel based on the webcomic Starfighter. Visual novels are interactive games in which the player can choose different outcomes to a narrative, sort of like a visual choose-your-own-adventure, but this time, with all the romance, drama, and sci-fi action of the Starfighter universe! You’ll be able to interact with familiar characters (and some new ones, too!), but don’t worry if you’re not caught up on the comic -- this visual novel will be made for both long-time followers and newcomers!

STARFIGHTER ECLIPSE, oh yes, let’s do this.

Maybe you’ve heard of visual novels! I had heard about them online -- mostly about how they were awesome, had sexy art, but were either not available in English or required me to jump through some technical hoops to get working on my computer. They seemed out of my reach for a long while, BUT EVEN SO, I still always wanted to play one!

When I finally got to try a few of them out, I realized visual novels have the whole package: music, art, narrative, and ROMANCE OF YOUR CHOICE (this is kind of the ultimate for me and all I desire in this world). I have longed to make one for a while and now, with Date Nighto’s awesome VNengine, I finally have the chance: what I need is your help!

Here’s what I want to do: make the game easy to pick up and play. You won’t have to be a gamer to know how to navigate (heh) the visual novel and you won’t need any high-tech system requirements: you can play right in your browser on datenighto.com.

I would also like to make the visual novel accessible to both long-time followers of the comic and those who are just being introduced to the world! I want to include something for everyone; nods to the fans and a nice welcome to new players.

And of course, I’m aiming to bring all the personal immersion and interaction of the visual novel format to you combined with the graphic style, drama, and romance of Starfighter!

(To give you an idea of how the visual novel will look - you can play a demo of what I'm envisioning for the visual novel here! This will only work in modern browsers like Chrome or IE11.) 

The basics of the game will be as follows: you start out as a Fighter (yes, a brand new one) who joins a crew for a dangerous reconnaissance mission. You are introduced to the world and your new Navigator. The regular cast of Starfighter characters will appear, although this story won’t specifically be set in webcomic timeline (think of it more as a side mission of the current series). There will be lots of character development and new, exclusive art.

We hope to have the game done in early Spring of 2015. I know that seems like a long time, but there’s a ton of hard work that will go into the game and we want to make it really special for you! (Plus, you’ll always have the weekly Starfighter pages to keep you company!)   

Starfighter is a webcomic (you can read it here, NSFW) that focuses on character interaction, drama, sci-fi action, and sexual tension: it contains adult material! (And should only be viewed by adults!) It has been in creation since ‘08 and is known for it’s definitive art style, main characters, and explicit content (SO I HEAR!) This is the world the visual novel will be based on, though as I’ve said, having read the comic won’t be a requirement!

Even though Starfighter is very much an amalgamation of my interests and a nod to genres that have influenced me, I have always strived to put my own personal voice into the work, and I intend to carry on this tradition in the Starfighter visual novel. I hope to make a story that’s refreshing, intense, and most of all, unique.

It’s my dream to use the Starfighter visual novel to expand on the Starfighter universe and bring it to you! I am very excited to work on this project -- LET’S MAKE IT HAPPEN TOGETHER!

I’m the creator of Starfighter, HamletMachine, (hello!) and I’ll be doing all the lead artwork, story overview, and art directing.

Date Nighto will be heading up the engineering effort. Made up of Conrad Kreyling and Lindsay Woods, they'll be responsible for shipping the game on datenighto.com, and implementing it in their own, exclusive htmlVN, a visual novel engine that works right in your web browser.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Creating a visual novel is a BIG project and there can always be delays - either we make a mistake with a scene and need different artwork or need to rework the user interface - whichever it is, we hope to go into this as prepared as possible to avoid such instances, but I understand they are not always predictable.

Thankfully, the game will be a digital product, so it will be much easier to deliver (as you can play it in your browser!) but delays and technical difficulties are the biggest risks and challenges we face at the moment.

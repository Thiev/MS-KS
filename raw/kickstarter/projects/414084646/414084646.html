Holomon is an upcoming hunt-and-battle, location-based,Â cross-platform game in which the user can hunt for various monsters called Holomon. Holomon can be captured in generic "World Areas" based on real locations around the world. Additionally (and importantly!), users will also be able to use their mobile devices to capture exclusive Holomon that are specific to their location in registered "Local Areas." Afterwards, captured Holomon can be used to fight against other players in an exciting arena style, real-time, 5-on-5 battle. To help you get an idea of the game, Holomon is somewhatÂ similar to Pokemon, except...

  • Location-based: Individual Holomon are location-based - what you catch is dependent upon where you are in real life! Our vision for the game is that hundreds of location-based Holomon will be attached to regions, cities, and even buildings. These Holomon will tie into unique aspects of the local culture and history.
  • You Make the Game:Â In order for the game to truly offer a unique experience for users in varying locations, we need your input. We want this to be a game for the people, by the people. So send us your ideas for characters! If you donate, you can buy a spot for any grade of Holomon, and we'll make sure it makes it into the game.
  • Reality: Similarly,Â Holomon creatures are based on real/mythological animals, people, and other creatures that can usually Â be attached to a single location/area.
  • Deep Combat:Â Holomon's 5-on-5 battle system is deep. While it is simple enough for a child to pick up and play, the real-time aspect of the combat lends itself to skilled, practiced players. The best players will become experts at managing all five players at once.
  • Wide Net:Â Holomon, although kid-friendly, appeals to an audience of all ages.
  • Multiplayer:Â Holomon will be targeted towards creating a massively multiplayer atmosphere. Interacting with other players through battling and trading as you assemble the ultimate team will be our focus. There will be no single player story mode.
  • Availability: Don't worry about buying a specific handheld device to play- our game will be available on any browser, Facebook, and the iPhone upon release. We hope to expand to Android devices as soon as possible.

We at Tierra Games believe that Holomon has the potential to be an extremely popular game that is played by people of all ages and skill levels. We have worked hard over the last year and a half to make it a reality. While Holomon will not have a story-driven single player campaign due to the game's focus on multiplayer battles, there is a story behind the game. A short summary of the back-story will help illustrate the nature of a Holomon:

The video above alludes to a time in the future when the world has been scarred and destroyed by global wars that lasted decades. Nuclear warfare has left the Earth barren of the beauties of nature. Beautiful grasslands and forests, as well as most of the world's animal species, are among the victims of the colossal war that took place.Â 

Several years after peace had been established, a group of scientists convened to create a plan that could artificially restore the Earth to its former beauty. This plan to revive the Earth came to be known as Project Phoenix. Â By creating an advanced network of satellites that projected holographic light matter down to earth, much of nature's beauty could be visually reproduced on Earth's surface. Drawing on all the electronic records of the past, the scientists created a Master Holobase containing complex algorithms that accurately replicate the behavior of all wildlife. Using the information from the Master Holobase, the satellite network projected a continuous, biologically accurate natural environment to the Earth's surface. Project Phoenix was a success!

Unfortunately, a few years after the implementation of Project Phoenix, several of the leading scientists behind the project disappeared. At the same time, the Master Holobase became corrupted.Â Plants and animals alike that were being projected began to exhibit strange behavior and even special powers. Furthermore, animals and figures that were never included in the initial build of the Master Holobase began to appear as projections on Earth. There were even reports of long-dead historical figures being seen. All of these anomalies became known as Holomon.

With the Master Holobase going haywire and the main scientists behind the project missing, special devices called HoloPads were given to certain members of the public that allowed the holographic anomalies to be captured. By accurately calibrating a Holomon's electronic signal, the Holomon could be stored in the HoloPad. As more and more of the public were enlisted to help gather the anomalies, Holomon catching became a popular sport. Eventually, people found that they could train their Holomon to use their special powers to battle with one another. Hence, the practice of Holomon battles began.

It is in this setting that the game Holomon takes place. This is just a short summary of the story behind Holomon. If the initial game is a success, we would love to expand the game to make a full single-player campaign! However, unless the funds raised in our Kickstarter.com campaign greatly exceed our goal, we will continue to focus our efforts on the Hunt and Battle aspects of the game.

A basic part of the game will be hunting for Holomon. There are two ways to do this:

  • Hunt in Location-based AreasÂ using the GPS on your Mobile Device: Which Holomon you run into will depend on your real geographic location. You can hunt in these areas through an app on your mobile device. The strongest Holomon will be location-based.
  • Hunt in World Areas using your computer: Because some areas might be a little difficult to travel to, we decided to make world areas (locations outside the US and the UK) available to everyone on their computer. You can also hunt in World Areas on your mobile device.Â 

Each area - either Location-based or World - will have a set of 8-20 Holomon that are (in almost all cases) exclusive to that area. For instance, if you hunt in the Central America World Area, you will find a different set of Holomon than in the South America World Area. Similarly, the set of Holomon in the Houston, Texas area won't resemble that of the Las Vegas, Nevada area.Â 

As you travel the area in your HoloCraft, you will encounter individual Holomon within the area's set. Which Holomon you run into will depend upon your luck. Rarer Holomon (Gold and Diamond Grade) tend to be stronger than more common Holomon (Bronze and Silver Grade). In order to catch a Holomon, you will have to calibrate its electronic signal by lining up the blue beams on your HoloPad. Catching a Bronze and Silver Grade Holomon is relatively easy, as the energy complexity is low and the calibrators move rather slowly. However, you may find it rather difficult to successfully snag a Gold or Diamond Holomon.

In game you can purchase a Calibrator that will stabilize the beams and make it easier to catch the rarer Holomon you run into. More on this later, but as a reminder, those who contribute just $10 get a FREE ACCESS PASS that comes with 50 calibrators! You'll have no problem catching even the most rare, powerful Holomon with a stash like that. Be sure to check out the rewards.

Location-based Areas:

So what do we mean by "Location-based" areas? Our goalÂ is that everywhere you go, there is a unique Holomon for you to find. By logging into your account on the HolomonÂ app on your mobile device, you will be able to enter an area that contains Holomon that are exclusive to your geographic location. For instance, if you are in Las Vegas, you might find an escaped Area 51 Alien, or a powerful magician. If you are in Seattle, Washington you may run into a vampire. If you happen to take a vacation to the Floridian coast, you may come across the dreaded St. Augustine Monster (don't know what that is? Someone from Florida might!)

In addition to the individual location-based Holomon, there are plenty of broadly location-based Holomon that are attached to larger regions of the United States (Northeast, South, West, and Midwest). This way, no matter where you are, you benefit from having the app to be able to find Location-based Holomon.

Suppose you live in Orlando, Florida. Because you are in the Southern part of the Untied States, you would be able to find any of the Holomon associated with that base area. However, because you live in the Orlando location-based area as well, you will also be able to find the rare T-Rex Holomon, escaped from a nearby amusement park. The T-Rex Holomon can only be found in the Orlando "focus" area.Â 

Or suppose you live in Provo, Utah. Because you are in the west you will be able to catch all the Holomon in the Western Region. You also happen to live close to Timpview High School, which has a Thunderbird mascot. By chance, someone has submitted a Thunderbird Holomon that has been attached to that high school. So now when you are in range of the high school focus area, you will have the opportunity to catch this geographically rare Holomon (in addition to the Western base area Holomon). Pretty cool, huh?Â 

As you can see, a location-based Holomon can either be very related to the geographic area it is associated with (for instance, the Thunderbird example), or it can be rather unrelated (the T-Rex example). Additionally, you can see that there are highly specific Location-based areas, as well as broad Location-based areas.

Location-based hunting will be available on the iPhone first, with the Android version following. If we raise more funds than our goal, we'll get someone on the Android version ASAP so that both versions release at the same time.

A final note: while the video only mentions that Location-based Holomon will be available in the United States, we've decided to open it up to the UK and Australia as well! If you're Australian or English, consider submitting a Holomon that would be exclusive in your country!

As a small group, we did our homework and worked hard to study local characteristics and histories of regions across the US. Together, we have created more than 150 Holomon. Each one has a unique appearance and a unique set of moves or attacks. Right now, we have finished about 70+ location-based Holomon, including the base areas. Now that we have a base, we want to ask for your help while also giving you a special opportunity to make your mark on this game.

As part of our reward system, we are giving you the option to submit a design for a new Holomon. Send us a drawing, picture, or photo that we can use as a base for the design of a new Holomon, along with a name and suggested playing style (damage dealer, tank, healer, etc.), and we will do our very best to capture your vision. In addition to choosing the look of your Holomon, you can also choose its availability: do you want it to be available to all computer players via a World Area? Or would you rather have your Holomon be exclusive to your state, city, or even your local highschool? The choice is yours!

What can your character be based on? The answer is...anything (legal)! Your Holomon can literally be based on anything, as long as it 1) doesn't get us into legal trouble, and 2) it isn't offensive to any people and/or organizations. Holomon is meant to be approachable and accessible by everyone - from kids to adults. Feel free to design a character that would be popular with either group, but that is approachable by all.

After you submit your Holomon design to us, along with a name and suggested playing style, we will work hard to create the character in a timely manner. The benefits include:

  • You will get a copy of your Holomon to play with in-game and it will appear in whatever area you recommended for others to catch.Â 
  • On our website, HolomonTheGame.com, we will put your name and Holomon on our "Contributors" page.Â 
  • You will be given credit for submitting your Holomon at the end of each battle that involves your Holomon
  • You get to leave a lasting mark on Holomon!

Our vision for Holomon is that this is a game by the people, for the people. We want there to be hundreds - if not thousands - of unique Holomon for users to find, catch, and battle with. We hope that you will help us make this a reality.

The HolomonÂ battle system is meant to be accessible for newcomers, but sufficiently deep and difficult so that a level of expertise is possible.Â Battles will pit your best team of Holomon (consisting of 5 individual Holomon) against your opponent's best team. Combat will not beÂ one-at-a-time. Instead, all ten Holomon will be on the field at once, allowing for a chaotic, fast-paced experience.

All moves are time-based. In other words, for a Holomon to execute a move, it will have to charge it's move after being selected. Most moves take 15 seconds to charge, but more powerful moves can take longer. It will take some practice and experience with your Holomon to get used to efficiently commanding your team's moves.

Battles will be playable on any web-browser.

We have been working on Holomon for almost two years. In that time, we have brought the game to the final stages of production:

  • Artwork - 90% complete: We're just tweaking and changing a few things as we go at this point (note: this does not include Holomon to be created as they are submitted by Kickstarter donors).Â 
  • Coding - 90% complete: We're debugging and tying up loose ends. Also in this category are balancing issues, where we're still sorting out some things.
  • Animations - 40% complete: still some work to do here. If you saw the video above, you probably noticed that some of the moves have a "ball of light" place holder animation, as well as some other place holders.
  • Website - 90% complete: again, just tying up some loose ends
  • Location-based App - 100% complete and approved by Apple...butÂ we want to make it better.Â 

The final point to be made here is that, assuming we receive many submissions for new Holomon (we hope for at least 300), it will take time to make these available in-game. We have to create stats, create/assign moves, test those moves, and make sure that no Holomon is too strong. We've figured out how to do this efficiently and effectively, but it will still take time. In less than six months, we should have all of the submitted Holomon in the game, assuming we get some amount that is less than 500.

So that's where we are; we're very close to the finish line here.Â

If you decide to donate enough to submit your own Holomon, guidelines for sending are as follows:

What do we need from you?

  • An Illustration- We will need some something to base the design of the Holomon off of. This might include a picture off of Google; a photo of the person or object that the Holomon will be based off of; a drawing that you do yourself.
  • A description of how your Holomon will differ (if at all) from the graphic that you send us. If you want a specific pose or costume for your character, you can let us know!
  • Battle style recommendations- Examples include: Damage dealer, damage over time dealer, defender, tank, buffer, healer, etc.
  • A name for your Holomon
  • Suggestions for titles and functions of special moves/attacks you would like your Holomon to be able to execute.Â 
  • A location to make your Holomon available in-game- There are two options here. You can either place your Holomon in a World Area in the game, or you can place your Holomon in a Location-based area. The former option will make your Holomon available to anyone who plays the game, no matter where they are (assuming they're lucky enough to run into your Holomon). The latter option will make your Holomon more exclusive.Â 

What type of Holomon can I submit?

Just about anything- Any object, plant, person, mythological creature, made up being, stick figure, planet, etc. Be creative! We will do our very best to exceed your expectations. However, there are a few limitations:

  • Because our game is kid-friendly, we cannot accept Holomon that are offensive or inappropriate. That means they need to be modestly dressed, and not making any offensive signs or poses.
  • We cannot accept Holomon that are legally owned by other business entities. For instance, unless Disney changes their mind, Mickey Mouse will not be an available Holomon. However, we can use a character owned by someone else as a base for a Holomon. If you send us a picture of Mickey Mouse, and say that you want a Holomon created that looks similar to that, we could certainly do that. Realize that we will have to ensure that the final product of the Holomon is distinct from the character. For an example, the Holomon "The King," or "Rapunzel" below)
  • The description above regarding legally owned characters or objects also includes people. For instance, unless LeBron James gives us the nod (don't count on it), he will not be featured in-game. However, we could absolutely make a basketball player Holomon that is location-based in Miami.Â 
  • If you want us to do a character that is based on you, we will need your permission to capture your likeness in the game! We'll provide the documentation for this.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Of course, with any project, there is a certain amount of risk. However, our game is so close to completion; we feel rather confident about our prospects. The animations need some work, there are some character balancing issues (in reference to the battle system), a bit more work on the app, and that's it!

Because our game will focus on constantly offering new characters, it will be a challenge to make sure that each Holomon is balanced - that no single Holomon ruins the game by being too powerful. However, we don't believe this will be a problem. In the worst case scenario, the users will discover (and report) that a Holomon is too powerful. We will respond quickly by investigating and taking necessary steps to ensure that balance is restored to the game.

Some of you may wonder how we will be able to pump out so many new Holomon submitted by donors by December (the estimated delivery date of all submitted Holomon). We are confident that we will be able to produce a minimum of 350 new Holomon by December. If there are between 350 and 600 Holomon donors, it is likely that our efforts will stretch into January and February. The good news is that we'll be able to deliver new Holomon as they are done. Hence, it is likely that most of the new Holomon will be delivered and available in-game by/before December.

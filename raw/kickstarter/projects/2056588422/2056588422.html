Hand of Fate brings card gaming to video game life. An infinitely replayable series of quests; build your deck, then try to defeat it!

You can still get Hand of Fate!

Our Kickstarter is over, and Hand of Fate is getting made - but you can still get access to the Alpha, Beta, and pre-order the release version at the Hand of Fate page!

A sublime blend of tabletop card gaming and video game magic, inspired by tarot and fantasy, developed by Defiant Development in Brisbane, Australia. Released DRM-free for PC, Mac, and Linux.

In a cabin at the end of the world, the game of life and death is played. Draw your cards, play your hand, and discover your fate.

Hand of Fate is a card based roguelike, in which the player builds a collection of cards into a deck, which is then used to deal out the dungeon floors through which they adventure. Upon entering a combat, all of the cards the player has collected fly into their character's hands as fully modeled 3D assets, and combat begins.

Key Features

  • Tabletop card game brought to vivid life.
  • A unique take on roguelike gameplay.
  • Visceral action-RPG combat.
  • Game changing gear and items make every playthrough unique.
  • Hundreds of encounters, items, armour, weapons, artifacts, and mysteries to unlock.
  • Unique deck building mechanics let you customise your own adventure, as you seek to defeat the Jack, Queen and King of each suit.
  • Discover the mysteries of the world of Hand of Fate, as you complete your collection.
Hand of Fate uses a deck building mechanic that allows players to choose both the types of loot they will gain and even the type of adventure they will embark upon.

Most roguelike games use randomness to increase game difficulty, Hand of Fate uses Deck Building mechanics so the player can completely customise and control their game experience, which is then randomly distributed as the dungeon level they delve into.

Got a favourite combo of weapon and ability? Stack the deck with those to increase your odds.

Need to defeat the Jack of Skulls? Swap in holy weapons and abilities to do extra damage to undead.
Scales, Skulls, Dust and Dread, Four Suits, Four Fates, Four Prices Paid - Ancient Proverb

Jack of Skulls. King of Dust. Jack of Dread. At the end of each dungeon waits a member of the court, ready to end your quest where you stand. Defeat them, and youre one step closer to facing the game's ultimate challenge. Fail, and your quest ends.

The twelve cards of the court provide the structure and framework for the player's progression through the game.

A host of equipment waits to be discovered in the Hand of Fate world. The player will come across a variety of weapons, artifacts, armour and gauntlets to assist in their travels.

The weapons range from brute force apparatus to magical devices of destruction. Artifacts provide the player with special abilities that can be triggered during combat, while magical weapons have their own intrinsic powers. Armour ranges from pure defensive, to magically enhancing your abilities and attacks.

The guiding principle for Hand of Fate is that every piece of gear should be a game changer. There are no "Sword + 5% damage" instead there are weapons that become more powerful the weaker you get, shields that shoot fire when you block, artifacts that create a whirlwind of destruction, and many more. Discovering unique combinations of weapons, armour, and abilities is one of the keys to mastering Hand of Fate.

FAQ

  • What is the minimum spec?

    This is still being determined (we're still optimising!) but if you've got a SM2 card you should be fine. The game has been developed in Unity 3D, and we come from a background in mobile, so we're pretty good at squeezing things onto platforms where the constraints are tight.

    Last updated: Wed, Nov 13 2013 1:40 am EST
  • Is the game coming for Linux?

    Yes, we've just announced Linux support! All rewards now include a Linux build. We can't update the reward text (because you're not allowed to change rewards that people have already pledged to) but rest assured you'll get a Linux copy with any appropriate reward tier.

    Last updated: Wed, Nov 13 2013 8:26 pm EST
  • Do I get a Steam key with my pledge?

    If the game is Greenlit (and it's looking good - we're 20% of the way to the top 100, in the first 48 hours on Greenlight) then you'll receive a Steam key with any appropriate pledge level.

    Last updated: Wed, Nov 13 2013 8:26 pm EST
  • Is the game Free To Play / Freemium?

    No, it's not. You get the whole game, with any stretch goal cards, for the purchase price. We have no intention of implementing consumables, or rare cards, or booster packs. We plan to ship expansion packs of additional cards after release, with new themes, encounters, and opponents, but those will sell at a set price.

    Last updated: Thu, Nov 14 2013 10:56 pm EST
  • When will the game be finished?

    April, 2014 is our current release date. If we hit our stretch goals, some of the stretch content may come on board a little later.

    Last updated: Thu, Nov 14 2013 10:56 pm EST
  • What's the currency conversion for USD?

    $1USD is pretty much equal to $1AUD, so just think about the prices as if they were USD and you're close enough! You should have a little change left over (An Aussie dollar is only worth 93c Amercian, as of the start of the Kickstarter) but it's pretty close.

    * Our currency conversions are a rough guide, and may change over the course of the Kickstarter.

    Last updated: Thu, Nov 14 2013 10:56 pm EST

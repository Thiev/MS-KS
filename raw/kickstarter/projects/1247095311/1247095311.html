Fun, addictive combat

The primary combat mechanic in DoE is inspired by two games we loved: Dr. Mario and Tetris. Your main offense is throwing capsules at the enemies. Each capsule has two halves which can be one of four colors representing the Four Elements (Fire, Water, Earth, and Air). Your enemies are also color-tinted to correspond to one of the Four Elements. To defeat enemies you must connect four of the same color, and then BOOM!

Don't get lost! (Don't worry, you can't get lost. Dungeon of Elements has a pretty slick UI that is easy to navigate.)

In Dungeon of Elements, you explore all three continents of the world of Primordiax. As you travel through Mitralon, Ta-Raekhet, and Katayvan, the branching storyline takes you through 45 different environments, including dungeons, ruins, temples, crypts, and even the lair of an ancient dragon.

Experimental crafting that is intriguing and engaging.

Crafting is a big part of Dungeon of Elements. The basic process is simple, but with over 150 recipes to discover, it is quite deep. How it works:

1) Take any two ingredients and put them in your cauldron. Ingredients include elemental essences, weapons, armors, etc.

2) Click the CRAFT button.

3) If your two ingredients successfully make something, you learn/unlock that recipe. From that point on, you can craft it again by simply clicking the recipe in your list.

4) If you made something, that product may very well be one of the ingredients in yet another "higher tier" recipe.

Breathtaking gear that looks amazing and deadly.

Dungeon of Elements features an enormous compendium of armor, weapons, shields, offhands, and other gear.

Weapons - Click on your weapon then click on an enemy and instantly defeat it. Basic weapons affect a single enemy, but more powerful weapons are area effect. Different weapons have different cooldown timers which determine how often they can be used.

Boots - The better your boots, the faster you move. This is represented by a lower base speed at which capsules travel. This gives you more time to plan your moves.

Shields & Offhand items - As time passes in a dungeon, capsules start to move more rapidly. Click on your offhand item to slow things down for a bit. More powerful offhand items slow things down longer or can be used more often.

Special Capsules - Click on one of your special capsule slots to make that your "next capsule." Special capsules can be as simple as the exact element you need, bombs that explode and take out multiple enemies, or something even more spectacular.

Suit Armor - Purely cosmetic, but that matters a lot, right?

Pets so cute and cuddly you'll have to collect every one! (WARNING: Some are neither cute, nor cuddly. Do Not Taunt.)

Do you love pets? Do you love collecting? Dungeon of Elements currently has 10 different pets - some cute, some cuddly, some scary. We may add more depending on feedback we receive from the community.

Beasts... get in my book!

Including a bestiary in our games has become a bit of a tradition for us. As you defeat enemies throughout the game there is a chance they will drop a scroll. When you collect the scroll, it adds that creature to your Bestiary. From your Bestiary you can zoom in on the 3d model for that creature, spin it around, experiment with its animations, and generally get a closer look at it.

Boss? Schmoss. I'm the one with the sword.

There are currently 12 Boss Battles in Dungeon of Elements. Periodically through the world you will encounter a boss at the end of a series of dungeons. Unlike other enemies, Bosses actually have a health bar and you will need to make numerous matches that connect to them.

20 years of players can't be wrong. This world is awesome...

Dungeon of Elements takes place in the world of Primordiax. This is a game universe shared by all of Frogdice's games: Threshold RPG, Coin 'n Carry, and Tower of Elements. The more of our games you play, the more you learn about the world and over 10,000 years of lore we have created for it.

Some of that lore has evolved out of actual things players have done in our games. Threshold RPG is a text based RPG (or MUD) that launched in 1996. Threshold has nearly 20 years of player generated history. We have carried that history forward and it continues to impact the evolution of our game world to this day.

We love to include things our players have done in the evolving lore of the Primordiax game universe. One small example of that is this Dungeon of Elements boss, The Cubicle:

Fire, Water, Earth, and Air - elements of creation or tools of despair. Alchemists wield them with talent and skill, to strengthen the mighty or heal the ill. The practice of alchemy was respected and grand, with Guilds that prospered throughout the land.

In the time of the Sundering, kingdoms fell, and Alchemy Guilds collapsed as well. Quacks and charlatans peddled their wares, posing as alchemists at markets and fairs. The practice of alchemy was no longer trusted, so their labs were abandoned and cauldrons rusted.

King Rand rose to power, against the Void threat. He unified the continents putting all in his debt. The Void were pushed back and cities grew, the people prospered with hope renewed.

Trade resumed and guilds returned. But alchemists still were mistrusted and spurned. One Alchemist Guild seeks to change this view, and towards that end they've recruited you.

Ruins and dungeons, tombs and mines, you'll explore them all and bring back what you find. In your cauldron, mix the plunder you brought. Unlock knowledge too long forgot. Restore the Guild to power and glory. Begin the adventure: this is your story.

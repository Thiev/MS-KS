A gaming experience that has the story, look, and feel of an RPG, with the jumping and puzzle solving mechanics of a 3D platformer.

Forbidden Sanctity is a 3D platforming game in which you play as 3 different characters who all have different abilities. In this fantasy adventure you travel vast lands on, above, and beneath the earth meeting new characters, solving puzzles, and fighting evil! This is a Nintendo Wii U title. The Wii U version contains exclusive content and features, however there will also be a PC release. For those who are not fully convinced of the Wii U, Forbidden Sanctity will be a reason to own a Wii U, just to play this game with the Gamepad.

Forbidden Sanctity is a game of mixed genres, an RPG adventure game and a 3D platformer! To more clearly explain the gameplay since none is shown, I will explain how you play. In the game you control Malachi, a young Hemite. You will play in the 3rd person perspective. At first you only have control of Malachi but as you progress through the story you will unlock more characters they will be added to your party. As you unlock more characters you are able to switch between them to utilize their different abilities! Certain parts of the game will favor the RPG genre as you will be able to buy, forge, and collect new weapons while leveling up your character also. Combat will not be turn based, but live action. You have the choice to initiate certain battles, but if you get too close to an enemy of a much higher level, you might be left with no choice but to fight! Once in combat you rely on skill to win the battle, not turn based strategy. Other areas of the game are heavily influenced by the 3D platform genre! The jumping mechanics will be different for each type of character, the Skarcos will be able to jump and glide, the Hemites can double jump, and the Groutus have the shortest jump. Each character will have different abilities to solve different types of puzzles! The Groutus have special technology that the other tribes cant use, also being smaller helps fit into small places! A Groutus can sneak through a crack in the wall, and open a door from the other side to get the others through! Another example might be a large gap across a ledge and only a Skarcos can glide across, then from the other side of the ledge, you can knock down a tree so that you can get any character across! There will be a huge focus on the narrative in the game but you will not be restricted and forced to play on its timeline, as you can freely explore areas and take on sidequests!

In the beginning, there were three races, or tribes lived together in the same region, all occupying the same surface. The Hemites were lead by King Thedin and they were great warriors. They almost seemed as beasts of the earth. They have large lumps protruding from their brows. Their canine teeth are large and sharp, resembling that of a jaguar. They also have thick fore arm hair that can be hardened in self defense to protect from bites and even swords on occasion. The Skarcos were nicknamed children of the sky as they were born with body features unnatural to the Hemites. The Skarcos were born with thin, flexible flaps of skin connecting their arms to their torso. They also developed long and unusual looking ears that protruded out so far off their head that they flopped down over themselves, or stand straight up pointing to the sky. The Skarcos were weak in stature in comparison to Hemites, however they were much more agile, easily moving through the air. They are also a very perceptive tribe. The third tribe was called the Groutus. They were were smaller than both of the other tribes. From birth the Groutus children have a natural instinct that urges digging beneath the earth. This tribe was also the most intelligent and quick witted, always researching and learning. They spent most of their time indoors or underground in shallow tunnels, avoiding all sunlight. The Groutus were lead by the earth Queen, Shanei. As it is recorded, the King of the Skarcos, Edian, waged war on the Groutus, causing the Medallion of Peace to become broken. The medallion contained magical properties of old, and existed as an almighty power to defeat any evil that opposed the one wearing the medallion. The medallions power was only potent if all three tribes were at peace. This war became known as the Great Judgement. After three years of fighting, the tribes created a treaty. In agreement with each other, the Skarcos retreated to build a city in the clouds never to be seen again. The Groutus tribe dug deep beneath the surface of the land in an attempt to colonize the depths, never to be heard from again. The warriors of the Hemite tribe remained on the land to live as they had always known. As each tribe split and went their own way, a piece of the medallion was kept by each leader. Each leader then appointed a family to become Sages. These Sages were then labeled as protectors of the peace, charged with only one duty, to protect the Peace Medallion Shard. Centuries have passed since this great event. History has become myth, and these myths are now told to the tribes as if they were only fables. There is an ultimate darkness arising. An unknown tribe from the North, far beyond the reaches of Hitral, is moving forces towards the borders intending to destroy all life and start anew. The force is led by something so unstoppable that the only hope in existence, is the truth of the ancient myths. Shadow Beasts lead these vicious tribes in an assault against the Hemite Tribes. These beasts are no longer alive. Nor are they dead. They are caught between life and death, fallen to evil. They are hooded creatures with glowing eyes. One eye is green and the other red. These symbolize the life they once had and the side they now serve. Their cloaks are dark and tattered, remnants of an old empire, ages ago. This is the first sign of the old prophecies that tell of a hidden evil awakening once more, coming true. A young Hemite, named Malachi, who was always intrigued about the ancient legends, will stumble across some events which lead to his travel across the world of Hitral in order to unite the mythical tribes once again. He then starts a journey to delve beneath the earth and find the lost Goutus tribe and retrieve the missing Medallion shard. From there he would hopefully find a way to gather the Skarcos shard, if such a thing even exsisted...

As natural life goes, light repels darkness. Evil will always try to vanquish the Good. There is a deep evil that exists in the seams of the world. Hitral is a home to all the tribes and its many creatures, but a devastating evil also exists. Before the time of the Great Judgement War, this evil was banished and fled voluntarily as it was defeated when the tribes originally obtained peace. The evil power was diminished and fled from the power of the Medallion. All that was left of this evil force formed a new region in Hitral. It was deemed unreachable and only believed to be inside the fabric of Despair Region. However some evil remained, and it was this very evil that whispered the temptations into the minds of the Kings and Queens during the Peace Period, as it is now called. King Edian was the first to fall to this persuasion. This is a testament to the evil's power as the Skarcos are naturally quick witted and perceptive to lies. He then attacked and waged war and the evil began to resurface. The dark one who convinced Edian was called Lodren. As the war ended and the peace treaty came into existence, all traces of Lodren and this evil shrunk back into the seams of Despair. He warned the Kings and Queens that day however. Although peace was achieved and the shards held their power still, if the tribes spent time apart from the others, the power of the shards will diminish, slowly losing their potency, and the evil shall rise again.

Mabyn (forever youth) - Groutus

Mabyn is a beautiful Groutus girl. She is always joyful and playful no matter the situation, if you need someone to pick you up, it would be her! She has been raised and taught to respect others, including individuals from other tribes. Her name name means forever youth translated from the original Welsh language. She is a valiant and important member of the party. She is a very intelligent creature, as knowledge seems to come natural to the Groutus. She carries technology and equipment that the other tribes have never seen before. She is always polite and treads carefully when needed, while at points her overflowing joy can cause her to be a little reckless.

Malachi (my messenger) - Hemite

Malachi is the main character of the game. He is a Hemite raised under strict authority. His father is the leader of his particular clan. His mother and brother were captured by a rival clan when he was very young and ever since the incident, his father has gone hard on him for just about every action he has taken. He has been under the impression that the other tribes do not exist and that the old wars were just legend, nothing more than a myth. He perception changes a little after the Dark Ones from the Far Lands arrive. As all prophecies seem to be true, he must fight everything he has been taught and begin the adventure to discover and reunite all 3 tribes against this seemingly unstoppable evil. Despite his father's attitude, he keeps a positive outlook on life, even if there is no positive thing to be found. His temper can be raised easily and he has an incredibly protective nature due to his past family experiences. He is the leader and protector of the party.

Hemites also have an ancient power passed down from tribe to tribe. Hemite Elders of old mastered the power of controlling special stones called "Shifting Stones" in which the Hemite who held the stone, could then communicate with animals and creatures in their native tongue. Each Hemite warrior would go through this process after a series of trials were passed. Hemites then pick an animal to communicate with and then bond. If the bond is mutual, the Hemite gains the ability to transform into the animal. The bonded animal will be absorbed into the Hemite and live inside of him or her. The animal will be able to see out, use it's senses, and also communicate with it's Hemite host. Even after a bond has happened, as long as a Hemite is in possession of a Shifting Stone, they can still communicate with any animal or creature. Many great Hemite warriors have picked Lions, Bears, massive reptiles, and huge beasts of the fields, but our hero and main character Malachi, has chosen a badger.

Jabin (perceptive) - Skarcos

Jabin is a Skarcos from a semi-average home. His parents brought him up on respect and he has been taught the legends and stories of the other tribes, but he is not so sure he believes everything. He is a critical thinker and very perceptive, which is also the meaning of his name in Hebrew. He analyzes and over thinks most everything, but he is always first to answer correctly. He is full of quick wit and great at problem solving. He was never the most athletic Skarcos however, but his brain overcame his other shortcomings.

Don't worry TreeFall Studios is now a licensed Nintendo Developer.

This game was developed specifically for the Nintendo Wii U. There are many gameplay possibilities and things we can utilize with the Wii U and it's gamepad controller that are not possible with any other system. In this game we plan to take advantage of the Gampad touch screen and it's other unique elements. The map screen of each location will be conveniently placed on the Touch Screen for better visibility on the actual TV. Also weapons, potions, items, and abilities can be equipped with a simple touch on the Gamepad touch screen. We plan to integrate the microphone capability as well. For activities such as plucking a dandelion, then physically blowing on the Nintendo Wii U Gamepad will cause the dandelion to be blown away, sending seeds into the air. We believe it is the little details that count most of the time to make a great game. Many other controller features will be used and revealed in time. We plan to launch a MiiVerse community specifically destined for the many aspects of the game. The community will be a great place to give help, seek advice, and talk about side-quests, forging weapons, exploring the worlds, and advancing through the story. We plan to take advantage of many of Nintendo's new exciting control, social, and online features.

There has been a lot of time invested in thinking of applications and uses of the Wii U Gamepad. The controller itself really is the centerpiece and high point of the Wii U console. So many different things can be achieved or innovated using this very special controller. We plan to do our best to go above and beyond in our utilization of the Gamepad's unique capabilities. We have many ideas in store ready to be tested! We plan on breaking down the touch screen into multiple sections in which you can continue with gameplay, without pausing, and you can use the swipe of your finger to switch and equip items, scan the map of the surrounding area, read quest information, and switch the members of your current party and player. We plan to also make the game completely playable on the Gamepad without needing the TV, however this mode will sacrifice key touch control features mentioned above. In the previous section we mentioned taking advantage of things such as the Gamepad's microphone. In certain instances of the game this feature alone might have the capacity to change the entire experience based on immersing the player into the gameplay by adding depth, not gimmicks. This is a core RPG style of game, and silly maneuvers are not what the Gamepad is about.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

TreeFall Studios is now expanding and changing everything. Currently, all of the games we have released have been products of my partner and my spare time. We both hold jobs and many situations have arose of the course of developing Journey of a Special Average Balloon, The Gem Collector, and Night Detective. Our development period for Night Detective was severely increased as different challenges arose in our personal lives. Each Kickstarter project that we have created in the past have all had small funding goals which did not provide money for us to use during the development, we just used them to get the necessary equipment needed to make the games, and then they would be finished as soon as we had enough time to get together between work, and family events. However this project is a game on a much larger scale. This title will be comparable to AAA titles and other large studios. TreeFall has expanded it's team from myself, Eli, and Joseph my partner, to containing 4 full time employees. We have added a new artist, and a new 3d modeler/level designer. We also have a couple more contributors who will work with us on our games, just not full time employees. With this project, we have set a funding goal that will provide us with enough compensation to ensure we will be able to work on this game everyday, full-time with no interruptions, for an estimated development period of around 6 months to a 18 months. We have the skills combined with the time on this project to create a stellar Wii U exclusive version, and release a standard PC version alongside of our Wii U title. There are risks that come with this project that are similar to the risks and challenges we faced with Night Detective and our other projects. One way to overcome is through contracts. Joseph and I have hit many, many bumps along the way, and sometimes things just might not line up in the greatest way and he could be forced to quit the company for the his lack of free time for example. After this Kickstarter project is funded, if successful, I will have a legal document drafted in which all members of the team will sign. This will state the payment each member has received after the Kickstarter Campaign and by signing, they commit to the full development period and cycle of this game, Forbidden Sanctity. If they can no longer commit and work full time for whatever reason, they will have agreed to provide all the funds, or partial funds(depending on circumstances) back to the company in order to hire another member to continue in that employees place. We hope that if those specific challenges arise, we will be able to overcome them by the foundation we laid in stone before the start of the major development cycle. Other challenges might arise, but we fully believe we have the experience now, as it grows and with each project we create, that we will be able to come together, brainstorm, and find a way to persevere through each challenge no matter what.

Disc Golf Unchained brings the excitement of throwing the perfect shot to the palm of your hand!

What is Disc Golf Unchained?

Disc Golf Unchained arose directly from our passion for the sport of disc golf. Disc golf is a remarkably fast growing recreational activity, and to some (including us), a serious sport. The sport is played by people of all ages, and no one is excluded from the game due to a lack of expensive equipment or high-priced club memberships. Disc golf is a sport for anyone and everyone! Disc Golf Unchained looks to capture the excitement and experience of throwing the perfect shot and bring it to the palm of your hand. We attempted to create realistic flight physics and course environments that, along with a complex player progression system, offer a fun and engaging game anyone can enjoy, just like actual disc golf. 

The game is slated for release on Android and iOS sometime in 2014, and we have developed an extremely intuitive touch interface for the game on these platforms. Although we intend to initially release Disc Golf Unchained on mobile devices, advancements in human interface technology and online networks for next-gen consoles have us thinking about the full potential of disc golf in the virtual world. Imagine throwing a round with or against your friends at your beautiful local course, but from the comfort of your respective living rooms! The prospects for Disc Golf Unchained are very exciting.

   

Flight Physics/Discs-  Flight was, without a doubt, one of the primary objectives for the development of Disc Golf Unchained. The main goal with the Flight Engine was to produce believable flight from any disc you desire to throw. Many disc properties are taken into account in the game: stability, distance (speed/glide), mold type, weight, plastic type, and condition just to name a few. Throw types including backhand, sidearm and overhand create those familiar flight paths that you know and love. Assemble your real-life bag or experiment with new combinations of properties to try a completely new disc. The attention to detail in the Flight Engine pays off by allowing for a seemingly unlimited number of disc and throw combinations to suit any style of play.

Course Engine/Editor-  The Course Engine tests the limits of beauty and realism in a disc golf video game. Courses are dynamically created, taking into account every aspect of the environment, from the landscape and vegetation to the weather. Be wary though, adjusting the conditions may make that open, easy course into a bear! Soon you will be able to harness the power of the course engine to create your own courses with the Disc Golf Unchained Course Editor. Mold the terrain, place your teepads and baskets (with multiple options for each hole if you so desire), or add some vegetation for some sweet tree love. Nothing is quite like knowing a course like the back of your hand, but it is especially sweet when you designed it. The Course Engine and Course Editor aim to support a variety of environments and provide the tools to accurately model realistic disc golf courses. In the future, you may even see some of your local courses available to play in Disc Golf Unchained!

Gameplay Progression-  We didn't want to stop at just having a pretty disc flight simulator, although Disc Golf Unchained could be used as such. We wanted to create (and play!) a great video game. An immersive leveling system will allow you to upgrade base attributes and learn new skills, much like advancing in the sport in real life. As you progress through Disc Golf Unchained, you get to unlock and experience exciting new courses and hole configurations that promise hours of engaging gameplay. Throughout the game, you will build and tweak an impressive stack of your favorite discs that will light up the chains!

Music- We have a fantastic band out of Madison, WI, providing the soundtrack for Disc Golf Unchained. Red Rose puts down some of the funkiest, spaciest grooves out there, and the music fits perfectly into the game. You may find yourself sucked into, not only the beautiful environments in the game, but also the trippy loops Red Rose provides. If you would like to hear more of their music or check on their tour schedule, take a look at the Red Rose website.

Why Disc Golf Unchained Will Succeed

There is a large group of people that have been waiting for a game like Unchained. Both disc golf and mobile games have exploded in popularity recently with the rise of championship-level courses and smartphones, especially among young people. The number of disc golf courses has more than doubled in the last ten years. Likewise, the number of mobile gamers has topped 100 million in the United States alone. The game will speak for itself when folks are exposed to it. We need to steadily build our audience, and then all it takes is getting the right exposure to explode in popularity.

Disc Golf Unchained will act as a bridge to bring disc golf to the digital world. There is a hole in the market for realistic disc golf video games, and there are very few disc golf video games in general. Unchained was developed by disc golfers to really capture the essence of the sport and bring it into the digital realm.  The game will hit on more aspects of disc golf than any that has preceded it, while still being accessible to non-disc golfers. We really hope that Disc Golf Unchained will expose our favorite sport to people who wouldn't otherwise have come across it.

Mike Batka runs Glide Disc Golf, a successful disc golf retail shop in Madison, WI. We had the opportunity to sit down and talk with him about his thoughts on Disc Golf Unchained.

Please take a moment to check out his website. He does a great job promoting the sport and is really involved in the local disc golf community. If you are ever in Madison, stop by and check out his shop across the street from Hiestand Disc Golf Course.

How Backing the Project Will Help

Disc Golf Unchained is still in development. The initial release of the game will be on the Android and iOS. To effectively develop the game on these platforms, we need to upgrade and add to our collection of software, hardware, licensing, and equipment. With these additional resources, we can continue to improve the release version of the game and make sure that it is available on anyone's preferred mobile device . Upgrading our systems in a timely manner will ensure that we can develop Disc Golf Unchained for a variety of devices as quickly as possible. Kickstarter funding would allow us to perform these necessary upgrades.

We both devote as much time as we can to developing Disc Golf Unchained, but being independent developers, we also need to support ourselves and our families. For Local Route Labs to produce high quality media at an efficient rate, we need to be able to give more time to our projects. We are working to turn Local Route Labs into our full-time job, but until then we are limited in the amount of time we can spend developing the game. It is not to say that the development of Disc Golf Unchained is coming along slowly by any means. We continue to add to the project every day, and piece by piece, Disc Golf Unchained is coming together. The biggest question is not if, but when the game will be finished.

The Kickstarter funding will also allow us to market our game properly.  There are many events in both the disc golf and video game communities that would be perfect for displaying Disc Golf Unchained. Traveling and setting up displays at disc golf tournaments and events, as well as video game expos and conventions, is expensive, but it is one of the best ways to get a project out to the public.

FAQ

  • What backing level includes a copy of Disc Golf Unchained?

    The main content of Disc Golf Unchained will be available for free. That means that you will be able to download and play the game free of charge. There will be numerous courses and discs included with the free download, and you will be able to spend hours playing and developing your profile without paying a dime. Disc Golf Unchained will also offer many opportunities to purchase unique add-ons, discs and courses in the in-game pro shop. How much you spend is completely up to you, but you will never be required to spend your hard earned money to simply play the game. A great example of paid content that will be available is the DGU Course Editor.

    Last updated: Thu, Nov 7 2013 2:30 pm EST
  • What types of throws will be available in the game?

    Disc Golf Unchained will include almost any type of throw you can pull off in real life, from flicks and tomahawks to backhands and even rollers. We want you to have the option to get out of sticky situations with a well-placed flick anny or a tomahawk over some thick bushes. However you choose to throw, the disc will act accordingly.

    Last updated: Wed, Nov 6 2013 10:50 pm EST
  • What types of courses are going to be available, and will any real ones show up in-game?

    We have already developed quite a few course styles, with terrain ranging from grassy fields to mountains and glaciers. We want to focus on making as realistic courses as possible, but some more "exotic" courses may become available down the line. Our course engine is capable of producing just about any landscape you can think of, which allows us to create courses based on the ones we play in real life. Adam and I have both played tons of courses spread across the US, and we have some real-life courses in mind to include in the game.

    Last updated: Wed, Nov 6 2013 10:50 pm EST
  • What if I don't see my favorite course in the game?

    The Disc Golf Unchained Course Editor will allow you to build and play any course you can dream up. You may choose to make a course that you play every day, just to plan some new lines or a course that is too cool to exist in real life. The opportunities to create and play new courses are limitless with the DGU Course Editor.

    Last updated: Wed, Nov 6 2013 11:04 pm EST
  • I saw the video of the functional prototype in action, but some of the features leave something to be desired. Why should I be excited about this game?

    The functional prototype is just that, a prototype. We wanted to show off some video of early development of Disc Golf Unchained. A lot of what you see in the video will be improved or outright replaced. Aspects of the game, such as the throw mechanic, menus, and landscapes are currently being or already have been upgraded The functional prototype pulls a bit from early development and combines it with what we are working on right now. Just know that we have a very solid framework to build around, and we are doing everything we can to make sure it lives up to your expectations. Please bear with us as we progress through development and feel free to drop us a line and let us know what you want to see in the game.

    Last updated: Wed, Nov 6 2013 11:04 pm EST

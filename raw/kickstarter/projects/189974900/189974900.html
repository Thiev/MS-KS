Batonk! is the sound a robot makes when it runs into something. (and a delightful video game we want to make for you!)

Batonk! is a "robot sport" where players compete to control adorable (and slightly snarky) robots on the field of glory. Imagine something like soccer, but with with 4 goals, 4 coaches, not enough players, lots of yelling – and the occasional spark-shooting, circuit-frying, hair-pulling robo-shutdown. It's more fun than 6 barrels of robo-monkeys.


A free app turns your smartphone or tablet into a robot-bossing machine, and the core game runs on your TV (connected to OUYA or PC), shared by all players. It was so much fun as a human-powered prototype that it was an Official Selection of IndieCade 2013 and Come Out & Play 2013, and got such a great reception that here we are, setting off on an epic self-publishing journey. We need your help. Please. 

The "sport" of Batonk! plays out as a fast-paced multiplayer digital card game in which you use your touchscreen devices to issue orders to the robots on the field – but your opponents are issuing their own orders to the same robots in real-time! Protect your goals! Command the robots towards your opponents'! Form uneasy alliances and break them in the next breath! Somewhere in the weird realm between soccer, Uno, and Super Smash Bros, all the madness unfolds.

The robots, called Tunkers, dumbly follow the command until they get a new command or else score a goal. If they hit obstacles, they turn around and keep moving. If they score a goal, they'll show off or do tricks. They'll sometimes get confused or misbehave depending on how many conflicting commands they get from players. They'll cheer you on or taunt you.

Ever since we invented the game, we've poured every spare minute we have into working on it or thinking about it, and we've come up with countless ways to turn this core game mechanic into many different games. Score the most goals to win! Have the fewest goals scored on you to win! Score goals only with the red robot! Keep the blue one out of goals! There are a lot of options that keep the game fresh.

Our goal now is to bring that freshness to the digital world, to a whole new big huge audience. Our experience with the live action Batonk! already tells us this game is for all ages, all genders... everyone!  It takes no time at all to learn how to play and is easy enough that even the self-identified "non-gamers" can pick it up and have a blast. 

We are seeking immediate assistance to make Batonk! our full-time jobs for the next couple of months, until the game is ready to release to the public.

PROGRESS: We already have the core game system working and talking to a variety of Android and iOS tablet/mobile devices. We're playing it digitally and still taking the big human prototype on tour to refine the fun. 

If we reach our goal, we can finish the art, add support for more devices, polish up the gameplay and user experience, hire our favorite composer, and bring the game to OUYA and PC.

We've opted in to OUYA's Free-the-Games drive, and if we get at least 300 backers, we'll unlock enough matching funds to put out a version of the game that we really think would be a crown jewel of the console's great catalogue of couch-based multiplayer extravaganzas. 

We want to do more, though. If the crowdfunding tiki gods smile upon us, we'll add more consoles to the list. We had a lot of good meetings at IndieCade with the console majors and have plans in place to expand once we get the core game built out. 

True fact: The game doesn't discriminate between operating systems. So in our grand vision, it doesn't matter who makes your smart device or what console your friend has. If you're at your buddy's you can play at her place with your phone. If she's at your place and you have a different console, she can use her phone with yours. 

Long story short, nothing will be wasted. Every platform has its own unique features and special challenges. Our plan lets us focus on those challenges and take advantage of new features with every platform we expand to.

We're GOING to make this thing. We love it, and we know you will too. Your help lets us give it our undivided attention and do it much, much sooner. Crowdfunding is fundamentally changing the way that small teams and upstart developers can secure the kind of time and money that a polished professional game needs. Every single one of our meetings ended like this: "The game seems great. We can't wait for it to be on our console. Let us know when you're funded." 

For our game – which is a unique experience with broad appeal – crowdfunding is how we're going to prove there's demand and support for the game that justifies the finishing funds, PR spend, and general publisher support a brand-new game demands in the modern marketplace. On the positive side, we believe in our little game. We love its irreverent sense of humor and all the little touches and smatterings of weirdness that we're planning to cram in there. 

We come up with some weird stuff over here sometimes. One of those ideas is a sort of a sudden death overtime that involves a Pork Rift opening up and flooding the field with interdimensional pigs. Just typing that sentence we can hear our old AAA game producers uncapping their red pens to strike that bit of insanity from the feature list. But with crowdfunding we're our own producers. You can support our madness and play the kind of game that unfettered creative control can truly unleash. And that's freaking awesome. You (and your friends, and their friends...) have the power to help us open the Pork Rift.

(Don't be afraid. It's all going to be okay.)

And finally, we believe in Kickstarter.  In the magical ability it has to let the crowd decide what they want to have, and see, and play.  That's why we've decided to participate in the Kicking It Forward program.  When Batonk! becomes profitable, we promise that 5% of those profits will be used to back other Kickstarter projects.  Learn more about Kicking It Forward here: http://kickingitforward.org.

We're Psychic Bunny. 

We're a multidisciplinary creative company based in downtown Los Angeles. For the past ten years we've been making films, animations, games, and other sundries that delight and surprise. We've made games for Las Vegas casinos, cruise ships, military training, classrooms, and mobile devices. Our interactive team has multiple veterans from the console and AAA space, and we're incredibly passionate about the power of games to achieve all kinds of ends that are beneficial to humanity. 

This time around, what we're after is simple: a darn fine time had by all. We know we've got the chops to make that happen, but if you want a little more history: Batonk! was an Official Selection of IndieCade 2013 and was just selected to be part of the Come Out & Play Festival this November. Our first mobile game (and first Kickstarted game!) FREEQ released to excellent reviews this April and was also an Official Selection of IndieCade 2013. The game made another splash last month when we released an update to FREEQ that made the game completely accessible to the blind and vision-impaired community. Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

When we kickstarted FREEQ, we took too long to deliver the game. Part of that was because we tried to do too much of it ourselves to conserve budget, but the big problem was the game wasn't fully written or designed when we started the campaign. Everything from writer's block to hardware limitations to vanishing freelance engineers sprang from this oversight. We persevered and delivered a game that we are proud of, but many, many moons after we had promised... and we feel bad about that.

Today, we're older and wiser and determined to deliver on time. We're starting out with a complete design document, a working software prototype, a known game engine (Unity), and concept art already in place. We have an office and a staff this time around whose full-time job is making games and shipping titles.

Of course there will be new challenges.

Exposure worries us. Getting the word out about our stuff has never been a natural talent of our studio. We've built in some money to enlist the aid of those wiser than us this time around. Plus, we'll have you, our new best friends who hopefully love the game as much as we do.

We know that the live action game is fun. Translating this fun to screens and devices is our core challenge, but the good news is we already know it works, at least in our tests. We just have to keep our eyes on that prize and make the best game we can for your living room! And luckily, we have a great group of fans of the game who are standing by to playtest the experience and get it feeling just right.

Apart from that, the biggest challenge is always balancing budget and over-promising. That's why we're being so practical about the platforms. When we promised Android support for FREEQ, we failed to account for the significant effort it would take. That hurt the quality of the Android release and we had to go a little over budget – and far behind schedule – to remedy that. This time around, we're budgeting (and scheduling) accordingly. We want EVERYONE to be able to play this game, eventually. Right now, the most important thing is delivering a great experience, one platform at a time.

As with every project we do, we're here to learn from our past successes and mistakes and make something new and awesome. With your help, we know we're going to be able to do that!

FAQ

  • Will there be online multiplayer?

    Not at first, no. We're trying to keep the game to a manageable scope, and a server architecture is a big thing to bite off. We're also pretty sure that the local version is the best version. So for now, local multiplayer only. However, we will definitely be revisiting this issue for later versions, to see if there is a way to make it work, and make it fun.

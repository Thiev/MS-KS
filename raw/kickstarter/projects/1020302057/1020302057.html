A guild-centric Medieval MMORPG combining the best elements of Ultima Online, Sim-City, Minecraft, and Civilization.

The Game's Back-Story

    The universe is known to be ruled over by six gods. The neutral gods of the elements lie on the neutral plane, with Fire opposite Water, and Air opposite Earth. Above that neutral plane exists the god of Life, caretaker of the universe, and spark of all life. Below the neutral plane exists the god of Death, who keeps the natural balance, for without death, there can be no life.  

    Many Eons in the past, the god of Life approached the Elemental gods with a proposition.  

    "I would beg your indulgence in an experiment. While I understand the necessity of death to balance life, I feel that the methods of my counterpart are unnecessarily harsh and destructive. Disease and decay, the pain and suffering of aging, all seems very cruel. I wish to create a world without my counterpart's influence, and make the experience of death one of peace and happiness, and the process of aging one where wisdom is gained without loss of physical function. I invite you to help me, but we must not let my counterpart know of this plan!"

    The elemental gods, having plenty of time on their hands, agreed to the proposal with the condition that each of them be allowed to create intelligent races to represent each of them. The god of Life agreed, and further proposed that a common race be created by all of them as a symbol of unity between them all. 

    Thus the world was created and populated with six different races that existed in peace and harmony. For many centuries, the people of this world lived and thrived without a concept of brutal death, disease, decay, or even war. Until...

The Conflict

    The God of Death inevitably discovered this world, and is immediately angered by his exclusion from its creation. From his home world, where he enjoys the constant devoted adoration of his minions, he devises a plan. 

    Summoning up all of his power, he touched the center of the world, unleashing a torrent of death upon it. Volcanoes erupted all over the world, from the sea floor to the highest mountain tops. The land folded upon itself, swallowing whole cities, and burying them below many tons of rock and dirt. Tsunamis demolished coastal cities and washed them out to sea.

    The "Gods of Creation" took immediate notice of this destruction, and took action to save their people, even if their worldly possessions could not be saved. One by one they wrapped their people in temporal bubbles to protect them in stasis from the destruction. These bubbles were buried below the masses of rock and earth, and slowly started to rise back to the surface.

    This attack passed, and those who survived thought the worst was over.

    They were wrong.

    The world of Vermund was now under siege.

KEY GAME CONCEPTS

Graphics

  • 3D using the BigWorld game engine
  • Player-contributed graphical content via "Workshop" submission and voting system.

 Combat  

  • No such thing as a "safe zone" unless the players organize to make one (e.g.: Cities).  And even then, the cities will be under attack.
  • Weapon Queue
    - Weapon breakage will occur!  Better have a backup!
  • No button-mashing mess of abilities.
    - Just how many ways are there to fire an arrow from a bow?
    - We are not going to break down all of the various sword moves you can do, or otherwise make combat unnecessarily complex.
  • VERY limited use of buffs/debuffs.  No complex debuff patterns to maintain.
    - We feel these are contrived methods to complicate things and introduce player skills in to combat, where character skills should prevail.
  • Fewer hits, and hits matter more.
    - Most combat will be attacks that miss, are blocked/parried, etc... 
    - Being hit MATTERS!
    - Example: No longer need 100+ arrows to bring down a single mob.
    - Example: A swarm of 100 mobs requiring 1 arrow each to kill, rather than 1 mob requiring 100 arrows.
    - Armor MATTERS!  (Most damage will be to armor, not to the player)
    - Less need for healers, more need for armor repairing.
  • Realistic Damage consequences!
    - If you're hit in the leg, you won't be able to walk or run as well.
    - If you're hit in the arm, you will not swing your sword so well.

Characters

  • Logical Inventory System 
    - The Bulk of an item MATTERS
    - The WEIGHT of an item MATTERS
    - The QUANTITY of items MATTERS
    - Seriously, you won't be able to carry huge/heavy items... there WILL be a need for carts, horses, pack animals, etc...
    - Discourages carrying your entire life with you everywhere.
  • No Levels 
    - Entirely skill-based character development.  Practice whichever skills you want without restrictions.
  • Skill Tree/Hierarchy 
    - Skill Classes contain Disciplines, which contain Skills, which may contain Specializations.
  • No class restrictions on skill use.  No character classes at all, entirely skill-based.
  • 4 Standard and 2 Custom Races 
    - Humans: Neutral/common, balanced
    - Elves: Life, balanced
    - Orcs: Fire, Chaotic Stability
    - Dwarves: Earth, Ordered Stability
    - Kao: Air, Chaotic Change
    - Seri: Water, Ordered Change
  • Character Fatigue & Resting 
  • - Three different levels of fatigue (short, medium, long term)
  • Character Hunger & Thirst
  • Non-combat characters/roles
  • Simple and standardized sensible attribute system.
    - Nothing contrived to make things more complex.

Social

  • City Governments 
    - Guild-based politics, elections, etc...
  • City Building 
    - Guild elected to control a town, runs the town, and determines a city's growth.
  • No NPCs
    - The world is entirely populated by the players.
  • True to life crime system (Criminal Lists;Heresay system) 
    - Truly crowd-sourced troll/jerk/"anti-social person" control.
  • Robust guild system
    - More than just a group association.
    - Guilds, and the type of guild you belong to, will be used to determine who can own various houses, etc...
    - Example: To claim a blacksmith shop requires a guild of at least 20 members, with 75% of those members having a metalworking skill (includes blacksmithing, tinkering, glass making, and smelting skills)  of at least 40.  All of these values are set by the city government upon building the new blacksmith shop.

The World

  • Large seamless world
  • Single-server world (no "shards" or "realms") 
  • Logical Spawning of mobs
    - Mob spawns can be destroyed
    - Players have a real impact on the world
    - Spawns left alone too long will spread and new spawn areas will become established.
  • Logical Spawning of new characters 

Items

  • Prevalence of mundane items
  • Item breakage 
    - Crafters need a sink for items so they can do business in replacing them.
  • Logical Crafting
    - Items don't take 2 seconds to craft, or 10 seconds to craft.  They take hours, sometimes DAYS, to craft.
    - Crafting while offline (done by your character, depending on character's stamina/fatigue).
  • Items Persist in the world (not destroyed upon leaving your inventory)

Some Nifty Stuff

  • We will avoid showing numbers whenever we can.
    - Can YOU tell me what your dexterity is, or the durability VALUE of your car is?
  • Specialized skills for those who are merchants.
    - Scholar, appraisal skills.
  • Non-combat fun skills such as the use of various musical instruments, and a skill at "conducting" an orchestra.
    - I very badly want to make it so a "composer" can organize a group of musicians to play his composition in a concert in-game.
  • Speed of movement, speed of horses, etc... will all be stat-based, not based entirely on the TYPE of horse you ride.  There will be no standard speed of movement.  (Depends not just on stats, but encumbrance as well.)
    - Yes this means you can train your horses, and not all horses have the same potential or stats!
  • Architecture skill, for developing new houses/buildings, and being able to draw their plans.  (Plans/blueprints will be required for each new building, and will be consumed upon use...)
  • I have a strong foundation in Economics.  The economy of this game will be unlike any other you have seen.
    - Example: All decisions about the economy will be based on supply and demand considerations.
  • Mining will require the digging of an actual mine, and will actually entail DIGGING IN TO THE WORLD!
    - Construct a mine entrance, start digging!
    - I foresee entire towns being constructed around mine entrances.
    - None of this "respawning node" stuff!
  • All economics and politics are local.
    - Auction houses are not linked!  All will be local.
  • Growing forests, clearing of forests, dynamic plant life, farming, etc!  The world will be DYNAMIC in realistic ways!

DEVELOPMENT HISTORY

    As stated in the video, I have been working on this concept off and on for about 14 years.  I have had multiple "teams", mostly of other "ideas" people (who honestly didn't contribute much... they just wanted to be a part of it and relied on other people to bring success to the project).

    I have had literally DOZENS of people show interest in helping to complete this project, but almost all of them have over-promised and under-delivered.

    When the project first started, it was called "The Real World Project".  The name of the planet, "Vermund", is actually derived from the Latin "Veri Mundi", which loosely translated means "Real World".

    I spent a huge portion of the past 6 years or so working with 3D engines, experimenting with them, attempting to build worlds, etc.  I have worked with Ogre3D, its .NET counterpart Axiom, Truevision3D (which I built a GUI system for called "EEGUI", now open source).  I even purchased an indie license for BigWorld (The engine we will be using)... but never even got a chance to try it because of some tragic life events a few years ago.  I have all of my design documents on a private Wiki site, and have been building the game's design for years on that site.

TECHNOLOGY

    Game Engine: BigWorld  -  http://www.bigworldtech.com/

    Landscape Generator: L3DT  -  http://www.bundysoft.com/L3DT/

    Distribution will be done via direct download from the Vermund website, and we will attempt to get the game on Steam for content distribution there as well, via the Steam "Greenlight" program.

    Via Steam, we can also sponsor a workshop, and hook in to the steam community and achievement system.

    I firmly believe that with player-contributions to content creation, we can pare down the development of the game itself to where most of the game can be "done" without much of the content created by me and the team.

REVENUE STREAM

The main revenue stream will be a monthly fee paid by the players.  I feel there is a certain sense of ownership and responsibility for players that are paying at least a token amount per month.  There WILL be a free trial period, of course.  I am also very open to free to play accounts with restrictions (such as being unable to join a guild, which would be very limiting considering how important guilds will be).

That said, the monthly fee will be nowhere near what it is for AAA games like Everquest Next, World of Warcraft, etc...

I figure a small $5 per month payment should be sufficient to cover costs and turn a small profit.  Imagine having only 10,000 players at $5 per month.  That's $50,000 per month!  Enough to hire a few people to maintain and manage the game, and really flesh it out as time goes on.

The game client will ALWAYS be free to download.  I do not believe in charging for a game client when you are also paying a monthly subscription fee.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

1) Ramp-up time to get to full production may be slow. This will mainly be due to all of the set-up of the servers, client systems, software, etc... Finding an office rental space that is within budget, hiring the appropriate people if needed (I won't be hiring just the first cheap artists to come along... ), etc...

2) We are paving some new ground here, and I am honestly somewhat concerned about getting ALL of the parts of this project done on time. With the proper funding, though, I will be very free to concentrate on this project full time, and really knock out a bunch of work. I just worry sometimes that I will encounter hurdles that will take more time than anticipated to overcome. For example: The Workshop client is going to be necessary to really get the community involved, and to flesh out the game, and for the team to create our own content to put in the game... but we may not deliver that to the community at large until after we get the main game client and server done. Then again, we may do that particular project first to jump start content creation and create buzz for the game! It all depends on how quickly things fall in to place.

3) Approval to get on to steam may take a while, and it's out of my control.

4) I am not sure how well BigWorld will handle all of what I want to do with the dynamic world plans we have. For example: will BigWorld allow for all of our foliage to be dynamic, or will it require those items to be "baked" in to the world? Will such a design entail too much bandwidth?

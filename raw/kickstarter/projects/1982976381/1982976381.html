Muti is a first-person horror experience. A terrifying journey into the darkest magics of the dark continent: Africa.

Muti is a horror game based in South Africa which explores the country's oldest dark magics. The game is played from the perspective of Zola, a young African woman searching for her brother.

As Zola's search continuesÂ  she crosses paths with the newly formed Occult Crime Unit in Johannesburg and finds herself drawn into the heart of an ancient insidious cult. This is a journey which will force he to face her own darkness as she learns about her family's past.Â 

Longer video proposal. (12 minutes)

Zola is searching for her brother who disappeared shortly after to returning to South Africa. Her search will take through the opulent suburbs of northern Johannesburg as well as the impoverished townships that surround it.

She must summon all her courage, finding both her faith and her beliefs tested, as she reaches further into the darkness of her tormented past and deeper into the forgotten world that frightens her most.

She must survive the corruption and the temptation of a dark African secret, to find her brother and save herself.

Muti is a survival horror game incorporating a mix of puzzles and light action. Playable in either first or third person. Each character in the game has their own distinct personality-driven A.I. that changes how they react to situations, environments and other characters. This adds to the puzzle element, as players must learn how to work with the characters in order to progress through the game. Audio and animation cues are built into the environment to maximize the cinematic effect and give clues to what may happen next and suggest ( possibly) appropriate actions.

Compliments of the Soweto Art Gallery. These are Copyright of the artists that work with the Soweto Art Gallery.

For Muti we are blending Africa art styles with a horror theme to create a fresh distinct look and feel.

We are involving local African artists who will be working with us throughout the project; assisting us with the style and providing reference artworks on canvas. Working to enhance and capture the mood and the colour intensity that is very African.

The game engine is built in Unity 4. We are using various art and creative tools; such as Photoshop, Blender, Rhino and a few smaller UV unwrapping utilities. Additionally, we have built Parley, our quest and dialog management solution that helps tie game events, dialog and story together.

To make Muti as much a cinematic and story experience we are building audio and game systems that work together to great the appropriate ambiance as you play. Also the A.I. of both the player characters and the monsters is build to interact and understand the environment. This will combine to create a more immersive final experience.

Despite been born in Africa, Zola finds herself a stranger to both the land and its ways as she returns to her place of birth.

Â Zola's brother, Khaya, disappears on a research trip to South Africa only a few weeks before the death of their mother. Feeling lost and alone, Zola sets off to her land of birth to find Khaya; wanting to inform him of their mothers passing as much as needing the connection of family during such a difficult time.

As a child Zola often saw terrible specters at night, leaving her with a crippling fear of the dark, despite that fear she is determined as she sets off on her journey. Unaware that she will be plunged deep into the dark truth of her heritage as she seeks her brother.

DLC Character

Khaya is Zola's driving force throughout the game.Â 

The torch Zola 'wields' once belonged to him, given to him by their father. After an incident where she was left alone in the dark, Khaya gave it to her as a token of his belief in her overcoming her fears.

Finding him is primarily the only reason she forces herself through the story and essentially the only thing that maintains her sanity throughout the game. That is, until, she begins to unravel more of the mysteries behind Khaya's disappearance and starts learning things about her brother and his actions that begin to test her belief in him and her own courage.

Khaya has always been Zola's pillar of strength, with his disappearance Zola must find her own strength. She must finally believe what Khaya has always said to her, "My little sister, you feel weak, but you are the strong one. You will see one day, when needed most, you will find your true strength"

Stretch goal character

Mandla is a young modern African male. Newly assigned to an established South African Occult Crime Unit. A unit that investigates crimes supposedly linked to supernatural events or committed out of a belief in the supernatural.

He isnât a true believer in the traditions he was brought up in. Living in the urban jungle that is Johannesburg has opened his eyes to what he deems the 'real world'. Assigned to the occult force isnât what he regards as a negative, as his traditions still hold sway in his mind, but he feels to some extent that the money towards the unit could be better spent elsewhere.

Due to his past experiences he has developed a cynical nature, and spends most of his time working or alone, dividing himself further from his born culture.

Trained to deal with confrontations, Mandla handles his fear and can, generally, hold his own in a fight situation.

On the down side his cynical nature means he doesnât befriend many people along the way, and his social interactions can be clumsy and aloof, creating difficulty in befriending others, which means you deal with many situations alone and must through your investigatory skill move forward through the story.

Mandla lives for his work, it is only through his job to protect does he ever feel a connection with others. Due to his strong convictions and his incorruptible nature he is the strong moral guide through the corrupted darkness.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Aside from meeting a high standard of quality that we demand of ourselves and as a team we can be proud of, these we feel are our other challenges:

In South Africa, the Games industry is very young but emerging. Growing partially on the backs of the advertising, animation and movie infrastructure, it has yet to truly find it's own footing.

With the South African consumer market being so small, we must build a game for the international market that meets its standards and publishing requirements.

It is a challenge to tell an engaging South African story in such a way that our international (and of course our local) players can relate and enjoy the experience, while obtaining insights into our local cultures and our world perspective.

The subject matter of Muti is a sensitive one, with the belief in it rooted deeply into our culture and its people. We run the risk of carelessly offending local beliefs, therefore, we're approaching the subject matter as respectfully as possible.

Game Infirmary is an in depth guide book into First and Third-Person Shooters. This book is for both beginners and veterans.

The book I am working on includes everything missing from the guides. It does not focus on any one game but on the entire genre of 3D shooters. I delve deeply into aspects of gaming that will open the player's eyes and ultimately make him a better player. It is intended to give a better understanding of software, hardware, and features these games offer to improve how you play and to be less frustrating.Gamers have their own language. They speak in terms that people outside the gaming community do not understand. This book covers that and the rest of the gaming culture. The history of the games is spread throughout the book that offers an interesting view on how the games progressed through the years.

There are currently many video guide books out in the market. These books are usually dedicated to helping you through a specific game. The guides are great for the game of choice but they are missing quite a few details.One of the items I see that is missing is the guides assume the player has a good grasp for gaming in general. There many routines in games that I do automatically that lesser experienced players may not be able to do without prior knowledge. The guides do not break down the mechanics of the games giving the player a deeper understanding of how the game works. The final items left out are bugs, glitches, and cheating that are discussed in my book. These items are omitted because the publishers do not want to make their games look bad to the game buying public.

I graduated from Widener University with a bachelor of Science in Management Information Systems (MIS). I am an Eagle Scout with two palms. My hobbies consist of video gaming, table top gaming, collecting comics and fencing. I have been gaming since I was 4 1/2 years old when my parents bought me my first gaming console. I have been playing video games for over twenty years. There aren't too many games that I have not played during that time. You name it and it's a safe bet I played it.. Currently my Xbox Live account gamertag is WORLDEATER which has been active for ten years. I have a gamer score over 190,000.

Here is me doing doing well playing Crysis 3 multilplayer during the beta.

Here is a video of me doing a guide for a hard achievement in Deadpool.

This has not been an easy project to do because this book is to be an all encompassing genre of game playing. First of all I have the knowledge based on my game playing experience of more than twenty years. This also includes my degree in Management Information Systems (MIS) with courses in hardware, database management and coding. I have taken courses in computer animation as well. I had to put in the time and energy to do the research to find out how a lot of this is done. I had to research the gaming history, coding and how the game engines are designed to get the information just to name a few. I also had to fact check to ensure the information is correct. This is a nice sized book written in layman's terms so that anybody can read and understand it. I finally had to go in and add visuals to further explain the topics written. It was a monumental task to cover each and every single gaming topic. It took quite a bit of time to get this done. There is at least twenty-one chapters covering just about everything in the genre of games. I hope this book gets funded so people can apply my knowledge to improve their game play.

This is not the final naming or order for the chapters. this is to show what content you will expect to see in the book.

These are chapters that I will be writing in the very near future and the list is bound to grow. Let me know if there is anything in particular you may like to see in the book that I did not include.

The book will also include a complete glossary for all the terms used in the book as well as quite a few that aren't.

As of this moment the book is ninety percent written and completed. I will be adding a few odd and end topics that I think need to included. At present I am doing research on cloud computing and on the nuances of the Xbox one and Playstation 4 for a new chapter.

I guarantee that after being funded this book will be completed in a six to nine month time frame. I say this because quite a few projects after being funded are not completed and fall through. They either need more money, more time, or both. I will not let this happen which is why I am making this guarantee.

I am having an artist drawing forty to fifty action pictures so the reader can get a better visual of what is being described. I will be doing further fact checking to insure the accuracy of each topic discussed. It is in my plans to have an editor proof read the text to ensure that it is grammatically correct and easy for the reader to understand. Once this is all done to my satisfaction the book will be published and distributed.

Once this project is funded with its minimum goal, I will allocate the funds accordingly. I have already prepared a budget to cover all expenses with a cushion for any problems that may crop up. In combination with having the money and the budget in place, I will have no excuses for not completing this project in the six to nine month time frame previously stated. This project is definitely the real deal.

I will be happy to entertain all comments on here or on the forums at www.GameInfirmary.com. They will be held in high regard and I will use the best and most popular to make the book better. The other ideas will be taken into consideration. If your idea is not used please do not be offended because as the Kickstarter changes your ideas may still be used at a later time since this is an evolving process. I want to work with you because in reality this book is your book.

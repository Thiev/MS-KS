2D Action-Adventure, nonlinear, Metroidvania style beat'em up with player driven dialogues and weapon customization.

Help Buck discover what happened to his love while encountering the strange and dangerous inhabitants of Cordova Desert, a dangerous wasteland filled with remnants of forgotten towns, lost technology and fearsome enemies tearing each other apart for survival.

Buck is a 2D action-adventure game in the spirit of modern 2D side-scrollers but with new and unique mechanics such as weapon customization, player driven dialogues, none-linear story progression and many other features that aim to move the 2D side-scrolling genre to newer and more exciting territories.

  • Explore a hand drawn,post apocalyptic world brought to life, filled with interesting characters to both converse with and fight against.
  • Step into the shoes of Buck; the most skilled fighter and mechanic in all of Cordova, as he out talks and fights his way through crazy bandits, local colonists and very dangerous individuals.
  • There's almost nothing Buck can't fix or build. Use his knowledge to upgrade your arsenal, improve your weapons' reload speed, damage output, ammo capacity and much more.
  • Buck knows how to handle a fight, use his fists and weapons together to string aerial combos and leave his unlucky opponents helpless.

Buck is an able fighter and an expert mechanic who lives in the outer reaches of Cordova Desert. His step father; an old vulture named Grit, taught him how to solve any problem, mechanical or otherwise. It's a dangerous life in the wasteland and Grit has made sure his adopted son could take care of himself no matter what.

After years of living in nearly complete solitude a female canine appeared with a motorcycle in need of repair. Buck knew that he could not pass the chance and risk never seeing her again, ignoring Grit's warning, the two fell in love.

Our story begins three months after Buck and the girl met, Buck woke up in the middle of the night alone. Jodie had disappeared without a trace, leaving her motorcycle behind.

Grit tried to calm him down but Buck wouldn't listen.

He set out into the wasteland to discover what happened to Jodie.What he will find will change him forever.

Exploration:

Exploration plays a major part in Buck. We want you to take the time and map out your surroundings, discover new locations and characters, all while completing missions in any order you choose.

Health:

Buck does NOT regenerate health automatically, you'll have to purchase food from shops or traveling salesmen before heading out to a mission. Your inventory space is very limited, so decide carefully on what to take with you.

Combat:

Buck's combat is divided into two main categories:

Melee combat

Punching is the quickest way to get your enemies up into the air where they are completely helpless. The melee approach will serve as your basic damage output but if you punch for too long you will leave yourself vulnerable to ranged attacks from far away enemies.

Ranged combat

Your strongest damage output will be with your Ranged weapons.You can deal massive damage andknock backseveral opponents with the shotgun, but it has a limited range and a slow draw/reload speed.

Remember that you can run out of bullets, so make sure you use your weapons efficiently.

Dialogues:

In order to advance the story Buck will have to talk with many influential, dangerous and colorful characters. The dialogue tree will allow you to choose a couple of answers for certain conversations.

Paying close attention could get you extra rewards and even additional missions.

Weapon Upgrades through crafting:

Buck can craft items and ammunition alongside various upgrades to his arsenal, but in order to do that you'll need to get your hands on scraps and materials.

Scraps serve both as currency and a vital crafting material. You earn scraps completing missions, looting enemies and selling items. Every item you craft will cost scraps, so make sure you don't run out. Otherwise you wont be able to purchase anything.

Materials serve as building blocks for all of your potential weapon upgrades. These materials are rare and will only be available to you in certain areas and special vendors like Nibbler the Salvager.

Inventory:

Buck can carry up toeightitems in his bag, this will include healing items (food) mission items, currency (scraps) and crafting materials. This means that you will always have to make sure you are carrying exactly what you need.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

This game has really taken a lot of effort and dedication. After an entire year of very hard work with a very small and constrained budget we finally made it to Kickstarter. It's exhausting to think of the amount of effort it took us just to get here. Now all we need is one big final push to make it to the finish line with a complete game.

Revealing a project that is so close to our hearts is a terrifying process and we wouldn't have been here without the amazing support of our amazing community.

We've done all that we can to ensure that we have the most efficient, cost-effective and fastest tools in place. The basic framework and infrastructure are all ready to go and all that's left for us to do is to add the content and the actual "fun" part of the game to the mix.

The risk was always there, we knew that we only had the budget to bring the game to Kickstarter and then hope for a miracle.

I've put my heart and soul into this game and fought tooth and nail for it to be where it is today and I am very proud of our small team and what we managed to achieve in such a short time. I believe in this game with all my heart and I hope that you will too.

Thank you for visiting our Kickstarter page.

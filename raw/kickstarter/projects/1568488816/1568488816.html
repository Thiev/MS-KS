OK here's the key challenges we'll have to overcome once we are fully funded:

1. The game has to be accepted by Apple, and there's the smallest chance that they won't.
2. Completing all your rewards on time. We'll do our best, and there's a decent chance we'll hit this since we've a lot of experience already. A kart, for example, takes 4 weeks to make. It's also the reason why we've restricted this number as we know we can fulfill this amount.
3. The game won't be finished in time. We're aiming for February, with your rewards added between February and April as an update. We've got the funding to finish the game, and it's all been planned, but this will be your only chance to guarantee something personal to you gets in there.
4. All the complicated technical challenges have been overcome including the kart physics, and as you can see from the video it feels great!
5. One of the founders die. Yes it might happen. But don't worry, we've got a good team behind us to pick up the slack. If we both die then, well, there's a chance we might not get the game out.

In terms of the steps once we have the money:

1. We'll start working on any karts and branding immediately.
2. Helmets will begin work in February.
3. The game will launch in February in one or two countries to test the game. Once we're satisfied it's wonderful we'll launch it shortly afterwards around the world. Ideally, with some real world prizes like DRIFT Cameras up for grabs.
4. We'll update regularly - probably every 2 weeks and start importing your rewards into the game.

We just want to get over the line and give you guys a great chance to get some personal touches into the game, and keep developing it in the first few months when it goes live.

THE GAME

Monkey Mania is an intuitive and addictive game for iOS revolving around its main character, the Monkey Master. The Monkey Master is your typical top of the line corporate executive, who never does any of the work but gets all the credit, money and the glory by exploiting those beneath him for his personal means. 

After having their attempts at getting a promotion and to climb the corporate ladder stifled one time too many, the career seeking corporate monkeys have had enough and form an uprising to get rid of him. 

Cornered at the top of the Golden Banana Inc headquarters, your job is to command your team of loyal Security Gorillas around your headquarters and knock away any incoming corporate ladders, and to hold off the corporate monkeys from infiltrating until your personal helicopter has arrived to whisk you away.

THIS SEEMS A LITTLE FAMILIAR...

That would be because this is a follow up/relaunch of Monkey Master, which was first launched in January 2013 and received discreet success in terms of its number of users and quality of reviews, thanks to its captivating graphics and characters that paid homage to Angry Birds, its ability to challenge your hand-eye coordination and reflexes in a vein similar to Traffic Rush, with the smooth controls of an instant classic like Flow. 

HOW THE GAME WORKS

As the Monkey Master, your job is to command your team of Security Gorillas around your headquarters by dragging them around in different directions to kick down any incoming corporate ladders that you see appearing at different parts of your building. 

You control them by sliding them in any direction, up or down, right or left, while making sure you don't collide with any of the other Gorillas along the way. 

HOW IT STARTED

It all started with a book that we wrote to kill time during our previous employment. The book was loosely inspired by the office where we used to work, but within a month we had come to realize that what we actually had in our hands was a corporate world domination handbook. 

However, we felt that this had the potential to be something so much more than an eBook, so we started thinking about an app for iPhone. In the beginning various ideas were bounced around, including a corporate quiz app, which was abandoned before it began.

In March 2012 we began the game with nothing but an idea. We had an image in our heads of what the Monkey Master, Security Gorillas, and Corporate Monkeys should look like, and a basic idea of how the game should work. We had never created any game previously and neither of us had a clue what we were doing!

Armed with some personal savings, we started looking for a designer that would bring the characters living in our minds to life. With some beginners luck we found someone who did a great job, but as we were going to discover throughout the entire process, it wasn't going to be smooth sailing.

But that's another story. After a couple of months of detailed collaboration we had our precious designs and animations. It was then time to find some developers. After scouting various sources with our limited knowledge at the time, we stumbled upon a group of developers that seemed very promising initially, but there was another lesson we were going to learn along the way, particularly about how definitions of quality could vary from person to person.

After a few months of back and forth, and having to go to great lengths to force the quality we wanted out of them, we had the foundations of a good game. However, to get it anywhere near close to the level we wanted we had worn out our welcome with the development company, and had to search elsewhere to finish it off.

We had depleted our already limited resources quite significantly by this time. However, as time went on in the process, we became a lot better at    finding the right talent and knowing which places to look for it (some obvious and some not so obvious). 

Finding it hard to believe?

We won't go into details about the process of getting the trailer done, but the best we could get with our knowledge at the beginning of the process was this....

After a 9 month process which started as just a basic idea, we had the game ready that we wanted to release. In January, Monkey Master was launched.

OKAY, SO WHAT'S KICKSTARTER FOR EXACTLY?

After the game's release was when the feedback started to come in, from both players and professional reviewers alike. The general consensus was that while it was a fun game to play once you were a few levels in, it took a little longer than it should have to get there. They felt this game had the makings of a blockbuster, but there were things missing from it that were going to take it to that level.

By the end of the review process and statistics we had collected from the first few weeks of the game's release, we knew exactly what needed to be done. However, our pockets were now completely empty.

If we met our target pledge we'd like to implement the following changes/additions:

1. Game Speed

The general consensus from reviewers was that the game was highly addictive and a lot of fun. HOWEVER, it took a few minutes of them playing to really get into it. We initially wanted to start it off slow, to give the user a chance to get into it gradually, but we underestimated the amount of time a player wanted to spend getting a rhythm going. Should we get the funding we will start it off at a much faster pace.

2. More Enemies

'Okay, it was fun for a little while. But after that what else happens? It's the same old thing!'... was a common expression from a lot of players. Taking this into account, we spent a lot of time afterwards reflecting on how we could make the game more challenging as you went along, and different ideas were bounced around. 

Eventually, the Executive Monkeys and Bombastic Baboons (character profiles found further down) were born! If we meet our goal then these colorful characters will be brought in to provide new challenges in the game and make your life as the Monkey Master that much harder!

3. Level Removal

We are hardcore fans of old school video games, which was the initial inspiration behind our game. The structure of games like Sonic the Hedgehog, Super Mario and Donkey Kong was that there were clearly marked, separate levels, and there was always an end to the game at some point.

This was the plan for Monkey Master, and our belief in this was confirmed when our beta testers insisted they liked it as well, since it gave them a clear indication of where they were in the game and a chance for a quick breather in the middle of all the frantic action. However, when we compared it to action games like Temple Run 2 and Subway Surfers, our opinion began to change. 

While our game didn't strictly follow the type of gameplay style they used, we could see after the release of Monkey Master and playing it over and over again that it was actually quite annoying having the flow constantly ruined.

In Monkey Mania there would be no level breaks, and the game will flow a lot better. There will no longer be an ultimate level to reach in the game. The challenge will now be closer to that of a game similar to Jetpack Joyride or Temple Run where you survive longer/obtain as many coins as possible.

4. Graphics Overhaul

If there's actually one thing about the game we are still quite proud of to this day, it'd be the graphics. For all intensive purposes we feel they work quite well as they are. However, as we plan to add all these new items, characters and scenarios, we feel it provides us with an opportunity to make them that extra bit better. Does an Ice Cream Sundae need a cherry at the top to taste good? Not really, but it makes it that much better, and that's what we feel we could do with an increased budget on graphics...

5. Free Game 

Our business model for the game and constant inspiration throughout was Angry Birds. And why not? It was one of the best selling games on iOS. As you may have noticed with that and a few other games, they let you play a few levels for free to see if you like it or not, and then let you purchase the full game for a fee. 

As we got to understand who our main audience was, we felt that making the entire game free would suit their needs more. Using an inApp coin currency to enable them to buy more things to spice up the game would work better, which will be covered more in the next point. 

What we mean by inApp currency is that in the store section of the game where all special weapons, scenarios, outfits etc are placed, you will be able to unlock them by paying a certain amount of 'Gold Coins'. Gold Coins will be obtained every time you play the game as long as you score more than 0 points before you get a game over. So you can amass a small fortune just by playing the game a lot. Alternatively, you will be able to buy stacks of them for real money should you wish. 

 What should you ultimately take from this point? Free game if you want it to be (Note: We will not at any point set the game up to be one of those games where it's technically free but you need to purchase special items to get further. Although special weapons can help you rack up more points easily, it will still be possible to score well without them.) 

6. More cool items to jazz it up! 

Have you had a look at any of the above images, or played the game and wondered how the Monkey Master would look if he was dressed as Napoleon? Or how the Security Gorillas would look if they were dressed like ballerinas? We did... so compiled a list of cool new outfits for the characters to wear, that could be bought with inGame currency. 

But we're not finished there... With one of our contributor packages you can collaborate with us and pick out your own outfits that you'd like to see them in. On the condition that we don't get a call from Charlie Chaplin's people at 3 a.m. with a lawsuit because the Monkey Master's new outfit looked kinda familiar... 

New special weapons have also been devised, which are operated in exciting ways and score you a number of different point multipliers when used. Each provides a uniquely different comical effect. Find yourself getting bored of the New York backdrop we used for the original game? New ones are in the planning, including Las Vegas and Tokyo. But the fun doesn't have to stop there. With one of the collaborator packs you can help create one with your name on it! 

7. VS Mode 

To make the game more interesting to play. We will enable you to challenge your friends to a game. How it would work is that while you play you will be able to simultaneously see your friend's current score, so you can see if you're behind or ahead of them. WARNING: DO NOT PLAY VS MODE IF YOU SUCK AT THE GAME. 

8. Social Media Sharing 

While it is possible to share your score on the current version of the game, we found that it is not really enough to encourage people to share their scores, and subsequently help spread awareness about the game (even though we copied the Temple Run model to a tee, so not our fault!). 

Now that we will be implementing a Gold coin currency, we will be offering a small number of coins each time you post your score on Facebook or Twitter. Not to mention that there will also be new, cooler awards on offer for reaching certain milestones. 

A favorite question a lot of people have for us is 'How will you market it?' The best way is and always will be word of mouth, so we believe that this simple tweak to the Social Media functions will help spread the game further and wider than ever before! 

9. iPad and iPhone 5 Compatibility 

While you can play the current game on both devices, the graphics in all are still those which were tailor made for the iPhone 4. This is for 2 reasons. The first is that the development of the game was quite far underway before the iPhone 5 had been released, and by the time it was released we were already a lot closer to the end of development than the beginning. 

There was no iPad version because we simply didn't have the money! Rest assured that if we meet the pledge goal then an iPad version will be there and waiting for you :D 

All versions of the game will be updated to be compatible with the upcoming release of iOS 7. 

10. New help menu 

One regular complaint we received was that it took a few tries for players to understand how the game was supposed to be played. We updated it a while back to improve it and it worked to an extent. However, a live action version that does it in the middle of a user's first game works a lot more effectively, and we would look to implement something similar in Monkey Mania.

THE CHARACTERS

Monkey Master

The CEO of the Golden Banana Inc Headquarters, he got his start in business as a humble intern and climbed to the top of the corporate world by stabbing people in the back and cheating on taxes. Next to his career as a professional backstabber, he often travels around the world as a motivational speaker. He is currently working on his book, "The Monkey Master's Guide to succeeding in the Corporate World" where he reveals the secrets of his success. 

Likes: Gold, Martinis, Cigars, Private Jets, Eau de L'argent

Dislikes: Bananas, Meetings, Brainstorming Sessions, Sylvester Stallone   

Security Gorillas  

The last stand in the Monkey Master's defence, these guys are loyal to the end. While their overuse of bananaroids made them the strongest primates in town, it didn't leave them too smart. That means acting as the Monkey Master's bodyguards is the last good paying job around, and they will destroy anyone who tries to take that from them. They do not have any sense of strategy whatsoever and the Monkey Master keeps them loyal by giving them bananas and protein shakes. 

Like: The Gym, Protein, Justin Bieber, Arnold Schwarzenegger 

Dislike: Books, Junk Food, Ted Mosby (the architect of How I Met Your Mother)

Corporate Monkeys 

Hard working to the end, these guys were typical straight A students from the moment they entered elementary school. Trained to work long hours for minimal rewards, their dream is to take the place of the Monkey Master and they are ready to do anything needed to taste a glimpse of his power. They live in cubicles, where motivational posters are often found hanging nearby to keep them focused on working hard. After a lifetime of internships and slave jobs, they rise up against the Monkey Master to take his place. 

Like: The Boiler Room, Wall Street, Clubbing, Motivational Self Help Books 

Dislike: The Monkey Master, Avril Lavigne, Fun 

Executive Monkeys

These are monkeys that made it to the VIP class. Thanks to their network of contacts acquired through their attendance at top universities and country clubs, they reached a decent level in the corporate pyramid. Actually, they are just a step behind the Monkey Master, and they serve as his executives in the various branches of the Golden Banana Corporation. Opportunistic by nature, when the corporate monkeys started their rebellion, the Executives decided to follow suit to gain as much as possible from the upheaval with minimal risk. 

Like: Golf, Country Clubs, French Wines, Modern Art, Fast Cars

Dislike: Cheap beer, Football, Cheap cars, Metallica

Bombastic Baboons

These are mercenaries/war consultants that got hired by the Executive Monkeys to do the dirty work during the riots. Descendants of the inventors of gun powder, they share a passion for everything that explodes. The Monkey Master used to hire them to get rid of his enemies, but right now they sense a change in the winds and decided to team up with the rebellious monkeys. 

Like: Bombs, Secret societies, Vin Diesel, Heavy Metal, Lemmy Kilmister of the Motorhead

Dislike: Ballet, Water, Comics, Windsurfing

THE TEAM

Dimitri Verikio

During the handful of remaining hours of each day when he's not asking the mirror who the prettiest or most charismatic of them all is (turns out it was the mirror after all), Dimi is honing his specialty: finding the best artists on the market. His eye for scouting and securing talent is almost as good as Hugh Heffner's. When it comes to working with developers, Alistair has the cooler head on his shoulders when things turn sour from time to time, and reminds Dimi that the 80's were now over 30 years ago in his attempts to bring him up to speed with modern times.

Oh boy! Now there's a problem we'd love to have! Well rest assured, if we were ever in a position where we were fortunate enough to exceed our original targets then there are a couple more bonus ones that we would like to implement:

We are aware that a large chunk of smartphone users are Android users. In an ideal world we'd like to make the game for this platform too, so that far more people could enjoy the game. This was something that was never previously feasible due to our financial constraints, but if we could raise the amount then the dream could become reality.

£21,000 - New Fully Animated Trailer

While we liked what we did with the still image comic style that we used for the original Monkey Master trailer, we'd be lying if we said it wasn't due in part to financial constraints we had placed on us.

We have a strong idea of what would make a great trailer for any kind of relaunch. So if you like what we've done so far with one arm tied behind our backs, and want to see how we'd do if we were armed with a larger budget (still not a Hollywood sized one by any means), then this is your opportunity.

£?????? - To be decided

If we can see our wildest dreams are coming true then we will update what we'd do with any extra revenue, but rest assured it would be done to pull off something out of this world.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

In our experience with developing apps, we are aware of the most common pitfalls that can occur during the process. The biggest issues are time issues.

By this we mean how long it takes for designers to design, programmers to program etc. A lot will be dependent on the types of packages ordered via the Kickstarter campaign and the time needs of our designers. There is a realistic possibility that after all the design requirements are presented to suitable designers and animators they may say it will take them 3 months to complete as opposed to 2 for example.

However, that is one reason we have asked for the specific pledge amount required, because it will enable us to hire people who are the best at what they do, and that means quicker turnarounds. Could we do the project with a little less money if our lives depended on it? Probably, but we have been down that road this last year and it is an absolute nightmare.

You can get good results out of less talented people, but the back and forth process of trying to reach your ultimate goal can get dragged out far longer than it should have been, whereas with talented people you give them a brief then a couple of things they should change afterward and you have a finished product of the highest quality.

Since we would be using financial contributions outside of our own personal incomes this time around, we feel a much stronger obligation to providing the quickest turnaround time possible. After all, why finish something in a year that you could do in 3-4 months?

The other biggest issue would normally be with the programming side of the game itself, but again, we have done this dance before, and know what we are realistically looking at. More importantly is that we already have most of the code in place from the previous game, so all alterations and additions to it will take a lot less time than for someone who was starting it from scratch.

Me and Alistair being able to create the finished product of the Monkey Master game, as well as the trailer, with our meager budget was a miracle akin to Jesus turning water to wine. So armed with a great budget this time around we are certain of our ability to truly astound you and deliver on the promise that the original game showed.

Mission Statement

We are a syndicate of elite gamers with a passion for the gaming industry. We have spent countless hours and insane amounts of money on our hobby. We have witnessed the gaming industry grow from a whelp to a giant, but with this growth has come an increase in gaming costs. With inflated game prices, they have become more of an investment.

That is why we elite gamers have formed The Syndicate. Our mission is to put YOU, the gamer, back in control! No more spending money and hoping the game is good! No more trusting reviews that are sponsored by gaming companies, and in turn being manipulated into buying a bad game.

The Syndicate is sponsored by YOU, the Gamers, which keeps our reviews honest and on your side. Our advertisement is minimal and only promotes games we think are a good investment. Our goal is to change the way the gaming industry views the people who finance their organizations and to make them produce quality games for the prices they are selling them at!

Join us and become an Elite Player. United we can change gaming forever!

Sample Review

Black Ops II

GRAPHICS

Treyach has been slowly upgrading their IW game engine, which is the main reason all the games have similar features, and an equally similar feel. However, Black Ops II ran on IW 4.0, which is slightly different than the previous engines. My colleagues at Syndicate agree that this is the best looking Call of Duty game to date…as it should be! I was very impressed with the dust and rain elements in the early missions, and it definitely set the game apart from the others in the series. While it wasn’t perfect, nobody will be disappointed in them.

SOUND

As with most video games, I wear my headset while playing Black Ops II. After all, I don’t want to upset my girlfriend as her show is drowned out by the “rat-a-tat-tat” of the guns I am using to blast my enemies into oblivion! I do, however, wish there was a way to avoid “jaw dropping” news while I was in the middle of destroying everything on the screen.

PLOT/STORY

The single player story was extremely impressive. One of the biggest changes is that, for the first time in the series, some of the missions occur in the present. This is great because you get to alternate past/present missions throughout a majority of the game. A second addition was the “strike force missions”. These missions occur on a limited basis throughout the single player story mode. Once you have a decent understanding of the controls of the game, these missions will be relatively easy to conquer. In these missions, you will find yourself shifting in and out of various groups of vehicles/drones to fend off or attack certain targets. What is great about these missions is that they can directly affect the ending to the game. This is Treyach’s first crack at a non-linear plot, so, you will have choices to make which can lead to multiple story endings. So, as you finish the game, make sure to discuss the different endings with your friends!

At the start, you take control of David Mason (Alex Mason’s son). You and your partner (Harper) are heading to a top-secret location where an aging Frank Woods is being kept. You are searching for a man by the name of Raul Menendez and are well aware that Frank has information on him. The first missions tap into the back-story where Alex, Frank, and Hudson, first meet Menendez and how he came to despise the “west”. In this first mission, Alex and Hudson rescue Frank from near death, and while trying to escape the surrounding area we find out that Frank was being held captive (and tortured) by Menendez. Later on, Alex and the team meet up with Menendez, and Frank absolutely loses his mind! He tosses a grenade at Menendez and Hudson as a knee-jerk reaction. Alex is able to deflect the grenade, however, and it bounces in the room where Menendez’s sister is. Menendez breaks free of Hudson’s grip and lunges into the room, and despite trying to save his sister, they both perish from the grenade blast (or so everyone thinks). While Menendez didn’t plan on surviving the blast, he did, and this is where he turns his hatred towards Alex, Frank, Hudson, and the “west”. As we head back to the “present”, you play the role of David Mason, and it becomes your job to hunt down Menendez and prevent him from bringing the world to its knees.

MULTIPLAYER

As with every other “CoD” game, we have a new way to unlock weapons, perks, and, kill streaks. As you level, you gain tokens, and these tokens are used to unlock one weapon, perk, or kill streak. Everything is level restricted, so, no more unlocking, or trying to get “ghost”, right away. They also added a “pick ten” system. This simply means that each player can only have 10 tokens worth of gear in their custom classes. Another addition is the “wild card” system. This allows the player to either, add more perks, or, us two primary weapons, at the cost of tokens. This means there are, seemingly infinite, ways to set up your class as you see fit!

CONTROLS

The controls in Black Ops II are pretty standard. There are 4 stick layouts and 5 button layouts. Just once, I’d like someone to come out with a game where you could pick your own layout, but that is another article for another time.

RE-PLAYABILTY

This is where Black Ops II becomes worth the $59.99 (if it hasn’t already). Once the story mode is complete, you are free to spend countless hours gaming with your friends in an array of online match types. Whether you prefer zombies (which they added in a few new game modes), or you prefer to show your dominance pwning other players, Black Ops II has what you crave, and you will NOT be disappointed…unless, of course, you come across me on the opposite team in the game lobby!

Remember…run and gun is where it’s at!

Sample News Article

Battlefield 4 Thoughts So Far
In an age where everyone seems to be trying as best as they can to show which graphics are the shiniest it’s hard to determine which games are so deserving with so many amazing looking games out there. So the big question is this. Can that game drop an entire to scale skyscraper in its multiplayer mode do to player actions? I thought so.
Battlefield 4 is looking to be one the best FPS this year. Of course we all knew that, so what are our thoughts on this game now?

One of the biggest improvements looking for Battlefield 4 is its single player previews and push towards having a better story mode. The story this time around will be based around a Communist Chinese being the enemy in this scenario. I’d like to see a less scripted campaign that doesn’t feel like something pasted from Call of Duty that’s only meant for the players to just rush through without care just to get to the multiplayer. Though being realistic there probably won’t be any choices you get to make in the campaign that let you chose different outcomes that would make the single player mode extremely interesting. I believe it would be a turn for the better to give us more characters that I can actually say more than just an ‘oh, dang’ when they end up dramatically dying a third of the way through the campaign and you switch to your new private for the next mission. They seem to have a good start by creating memorable and gritty scenes with the ‘Fishing in Baku’ trailer and awesome local changes and dramatic scenes. So far it’s looking pretty good for a modern military shooter campaign; let’s hope it will be as memorable as it looks.
Now for the part that everyone buys the game for, the multiplayer. It looks and feels like Battlefield 4, just a lot more of everything really. More frame-rate, more pixels, more blue and red dotes on your map, more big falling buildings [read above], more cool tactics of destroying random stuff like having a tank fall through the main road into the underground tunnel area, more weapon upgrades, just a lot of nice new things that will add onto the experience and just make it feel better. My thoughts so far on the multiplayer is that it is looking fantastic with great familiar gameplay and many new tactics to be added to returning hardcore players while putting out enough new areas on the multiplayer maps to keep you wanting to return to find the best camping spots to flank your enemies positions. I hope the maps are just as grand and large as it seems they are making them out to be to added thousands of ways to proceed with each new battle between matches so that there is no definite camping spots on the map that are superior to all other ‘lesser’ camping spots and no areas that say a squad of tanks can just patrol to keep the other team at bay the entire match. Battlefield is known for its battlefields so l can’t wait to see what other new maps will be offered soon as well.
As for gameplay combat it is as familiar as ever, which is a good thing. As mentioned there are a few new toys to play with so it would be nice to see what other guns and gadgets will be reviled, nice add-ons like the option to spot out many targets at once, reverse stab in oncoming enemy, and calling in air/battleship strikes from your literal phone (which in context sounds awesome but also could end of being a major annoyance and just plain cheap and unfair). I would like more than anything to see everything balanced which is why I’m happy to hear the Battlefield 4 will be having a beta to test for glitches and unfair weapon and utilities advantages. The bold thing about DICE having a beta for this game too is how we all know that it will already sell millions of copies and a beta will could possibly loss you customers if they are dissatisfied or just plain get bored of the new game through the beta where it feels to similar than this really goes to show how much detail DICE is willing to put into this games multiplayer to make it as enjoyable and stable as possible. As of right now the only drawback has at times seemed too familiar, which isn’t a huge big deal, but more just fact that there was day one DLC announced… I mean really? But still the multiplayer is looking to be just as fun and time consuming as ever.
With more news coming up in the future I hope all of you are looking forward to Battlefield 4 and stay tuned in for more news and more updates on the game and all that DICE and Frostbite 3 have to offer for this latest installment which is looking to be a lot more grand in size than is predecessors but also remaining familiar as well.


Until then, ciao

Explore new systems, mine resources, manufacture goods to satisfy consumer demands, manage your trading fleet, monopolize the galaxy!

Since the beginning of time, humans have enriched their lives by trading goods and services with each other. The further these goods travelled, the more prized they were, and so how to get these commodities from one distant point to another would be the problem that would lead to ingenious transport solutions throughout the centuries. From the earliest galleys through to modern container ships, these vessels, and the organisation behind them, have powered world trade, and enriched our lives for millenia.

Now, at the beginning of the 22nd Century, the human home planet is choked with over-population, suffering environmental damage, and experiencing a shortage of raw-materials. Attempts to reduce the population and economise on consumption have failed. It seems the only way out is to find new worlds to return the desperately needed resources, and perhaps, to ultimately to give a new space upon which humans can live.

Rising to this challenge are a group of rival business men and women, keen to turn the situation to their advantage and profit. Each come from varied backgrounds – innovators, farmers, shipping experts, manufacturers and traders. By developing an integrated business that can explore, mine, farm, refine, manufacture and transport commodities to demanding populations, the opportunity to improve the lot of humankind, and more importantly, to generate vast wealth for themselves, are the prime motivations for the tycoons of INTERSTELLAR INCORPORATED.

Game Summary

Interstellar Incorporated is a real-time business strategy game in which you head up a large enterprise exploring, exploiting and developing star systems across the galaxy.

Drawing on elements of previous games like Elite, Civilization, SimCity, Railroad Tycoon, the X series and the Anno series, the game also bring new and original elements into the world of business strategy gaming and simulation.

Features include:

  • Hundreds of astronomically realistic solar systems containing gas, ice, rocky and habitable worlds, awaiting your exploration, randomly generated each game.
  • Build mines and farms to extract over 30 different natural resources.
  • Build refineries and factories to turn your raw materials into 30 different finished goods.
  • Develop new colonies into thriving civilisations by supplying them with the goods they need to grow.
  • Operate 16 ship designs, from heavy ore freighters to tankers and container vessels, to transport your goods between the stars.
  • Research over 50 different scifi-inspired technologies to improve your ability to extract, manufacture, transport and sell.
  • Play one of 9 different tycoons, each with different strengths and weakness, and each having unique abilities, requiring different game-play styles.

Meet The Team

Ben Ranson, Project Lead / Developer – Ben is a professional software developer with a bachelors degree in Aeronautical Engineering from the University of Bristol. For a decade he also ran a successful computer system building business in the United States. When not working, he writes science-fiction novels and plays video games (a hobby he’s had since the beginning of arcade games in the 1970s). In heading this project, he brings his extensive experience in software design, development and testing along with business and management acumen to his twin passions of scifi and gaming.

Tim Jokl, 3D Modelling Designer - Tim attended art school before landing his first job as a games designer and story-boarder. Later he went to film school (University of the Arts London) where hewas awarded with a 1st class bachelors degree. He joined the BBC and produced rich digital media and games for dozens of well-known brands. He is a journalist and blogger with a keen interest in urban design and technology. His love of sci-fi started early with a boyhood collection of Stewart Cowley spacecraft books.

The Memory Age, Music – Delivering the exclusive 2 hours plus ambient/chillout soundtrack for the game, The Memory Age possesses a Diploma in Music Technology from Huddersfield Technical College and has been producing electronic music for over 20 years.

Deng Yi-Jia Liu, Tycoon Portraits Artist - Yi-Jia is a professional concept artist living in Montreal, Canada. He graduated from Université du Québec en Abitibi-Témiscamingue in 2011 with a Bachelor Degree of Creation and New Media in Artistic Production Program. After graduation, he worked as contract concept artist for Meduzart and Behaviour, and later as a full time concept artist at Side City Studio on a casino video game.

Lasse Perälä, City/Planet Landscapes Artist – Lasse is a self-taught concept artist hailing from Finland who specializes in epic sci-fi and fantasy environments.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

This is our first professional game release, and so one would think what we're doing here is full of risk. But you have seen the demo videos, and they speak for themselves - an exciting, playable game is clearly in the works. Most of the hurdles in learning the tools of game development have been overcome, and our extensive professional experience in related areas has paid dividends.

That said, we are still some way away from a fully professional, balanced game that is fun to play and great to look at, and challenges will be in getting the game to the high standard that we (and you!) strive for in gaming.

We are confident, however, that with active help from dedicated gamers like yourselves in the test stages that the game mechanics and look will be refined. After all, this game is for you, and without your feedback and support, we know that delivering the goods simply wouldn't be achievable.

FAQ

  • When will the game be released?

    The final version of the game is expected to be released in the first quarter of 2015. This is assuming we meet our Kickstarter funding goal, or if not, securing other funds to allow us to continue fulltime development. Alpha and early-access versions of the game are expected to be available on Steam in the last quarter of 2014.

    Last updated: Tue, May 6 2014 3:25 am EDT
  • What platforms will the game be available on?

    Initially the game will be available for PC Windows only. Soon after we anticipate release on Mac (and Linux if there is sufficient interest), and, depending on sales success there will be tablet-optimised versions for Windows 8, iOS and Android.

    *** If you're keen to play the game on a non-Windows OS, please send us a note, or add a comment, so we can gauge interest. ***

    Last updated: Wed, May 7 2014 5:37 am EDT
  • How do I get on the alpha/beta testing list?

    The basic donation level includes a final copy of the game when released along with access to the final beta test program several weeks before launch. An upgraded donation level includes to the early alpha test programs. The alpha test programs are expected to take place in Sep/Oct 2014.

    Last updated: Tue, May 6 2014 3:25 am EDT
  • Will there be any combat in the game?

    No. The reason is that this is a business simulation, just as SimCity is. The challenge will come in keeping your company expanding without going bankrupt, and competing against other AI-driven corporations. We set out from the beginning to ensure that this wouldn’t be yet another strategy game that leads to combat or war.

    Last updated: Tue, May 6 2014 3:25 am EDT

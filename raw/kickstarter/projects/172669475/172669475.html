Have you ever had an idea that you knew would make a game better? That's exactly what strange paradise is all about.

What is Strange Paradise?

Simply put, Strange Paradise is a game just as much as it is a community experiment. Your ideas and our ideas put together to make a masterpiece for everybody to enjoy. A game by you for you. 

Strange Paradise, right now, is better called an idea rather than a game because it truly can't become a reality without the support of others. This is because the whole point of this project is to be a game that is driven by community ideas, whether it be a game mechanic, a character, a location, a game mode, a hairstyle, a color, a song, a puzzle, anything. The idea for this is that 50% of the content comes from us and 50% from the community, although if this becomes popular and there are an incredible amount of submissions our part will most likely shrink, and if we don't get many submissions, the opposite.

The Way We Will Make This Work

With all of the social media out there it is so easy for people to communicate and share ideas. We will have a Facebook, Twitter, Youtube and of course a Kickstarter account that will make it possible for people to contact us. Send messages, pictures, songs, videos, anything to help get your idea to us. We will be constantly be posting updates and even send out requests for something we need at certain points.

You will be given credit for your contribution no matter how much it is, whether you donated a dollar, changed a hair color, or designed a whole world. Of course you will be given the amount of credit associated with how much you contribute. If someone donates $10'000 and somebody else gives an idea for a single NPC, expect the person that donated $10'000 to get a little more recognition. You will be contacted about whatever your idea is (if we wish to use it) to discuss what changes we may have to make and to make sure what we do with it is alright; It's your idea still and we will not do anything to it that you do not approve of.

Another good thing is that you don't necessarily have to donate money to be a part or help this game. As it's mentioned above, you could pledge absolutely no money (although you won't get any of the awesome rewards!!) and still get a lot of recognition simply by sharing ideas. We understand that some people just don't have the opportunity to pledge money and that's fine, we don't expect everyone that has interest in this to pledge money or even have an idea, although it would be nice.

How much is our game?

First thing people care about is price. what will the game cost, you may be asking. Well it's not right to make people pay for ideas that aren't completely mine, so... Absolutely nothing!

There will be no hidden fees at all. No monthly fees, Special editions, advertisements, in game purchases, hats, anything! Now you may be saying "WHAT?!? You don't want any money? there has to be a catch!". There is and there isn't. I have two ideas on how to try and make money off of this:

  • 1st: Although I boasted no advertisements, I have an idea for a system more like sponsor rather than advertisements. For a fee someone could have their product or name put into the game. Nothing big, just something like a decoration as seen in other games. Nothing that would take from the experience.
  • 2nd: A very unnecessary paid beta. This in no way is needed for the game and will not give them something you do not get other than maybe a "thank you!" for their support. It would be a beta of the game and it's mechanics. It will just be a chunk from the final game. This would be released around a month prior to the final game's launch. It's just there for people who want to support the game further and are maybe too impatient to wait the extra time to try it out.

None of these are set in stone. We may not have any of these when everything is finished, and if we don't the game will still be 100% free. And I promise, anything put into the game to help hake money will not take away from the core experience and all money made will go towards either more content for this game or a second game in the future.

The demo will only contain original ideas that came from us. This isn't because we think our ideas are better than yours, but rather because we don't want to make money off of your ideas. That's why the game will be completely free in the end. We also feel like the sponsor idea will work well too because we still aren't making money from your ideas, or ruining the core game experience for others.

Where's the money going?

The core $13,000 is exactly what we need to get everything for this game. It's enough to get all the base hardware and software; enough to pay for every backer reward and every fee we need to pay to make this perfectly (The info graph above is a visual representation of this). As we pass our goal and work towards our stretch goals this will result in better hardware, the possibility of finding more developers/artists, advertising, and a little extra to be put aside in case of new assets.

The Gameplay/Mechanics

Now that the idea around this game has been cleared up it's time to talk about the actual game and gameplay (so far).

The game will be a two dimensional adventure game for the most part, and after much consideration it will run off of the GameMaker engine. We want this game to be a game friendly enough for everyone but maybe a little more directed towards teenagers and young adults, so there may be vulgar and foul references. It will mainly have a cartoon look however during the game it may change.

There is going to be little to no dialog and the game will rely mostly on narration to put together the story. Although the game, as the name implies, is going to be strange, and because of a lot of different people contributing, random, it will still have a story that will be heavily worked on just as hard as everything else put into this game.

And last but not least, what will it be on? Well as of right now PC, Mac, and Probably Linux. If any possibility comes up for another platform and  one is heavily requested we will try to make it happen.

We are also working to get this game greenlit for steam but at the moment we are encountering issues. As soon as we get everything sorted out we will be posting a link to our greenlight page.

The Story of Strange Paradise so far

To make it short and sweet, Strange Paradise is the story of a boy who for the most part doesn't feel like he fits in. One day he goes on a walk and ends up in a place filled with randomness and strangeness which makes him feel like he finally found a place where he can fit in. He then goes on the search to find people like him.

It will be divided into acts, each portraying a different setting and realization of the character. The amount of acts we have planned (as of now) is 5. Our first funding goal after the $15'000 will be $25'000 at which will result in another act.

And Finally some stretch goals!

If this project makes it to the $13,000 we're asking for, the game will simply be made. but here are some nice stretch goals of how we will go further if you guys help us out!

 A Prelude Chapter!

If we make it to $15,000, we will have a prelude chapter to explain the beginning of the game instead of a cinematic.

2 More Acts!

 If we reach the goal of 20,000, two new acts will be added in. This means a longer game and more content, so more content from the community as well. But it will also means a slightly longer development.

A Professional Narrator!

If we nearly double our initial goal and get $25,000, we will get a professional narrator to narrate the story instead of one of us.

 New Developer!

If we over double the asked $13,000, a new person will definitely be hired to work on the game, either an artist or programmer to help speed up the development. If we can find/afford new people before this, we will try to get them, this is just when we will definitely find some new help.

Improved Cinematics!

 If we reach $35,000 we will get new artists to help make our cinematics much more pleasing. This along with the narrator will cause the story to be told a lot better than originally planned.

 2 Bonus Acts!

If we make it to $40,000 we will include two bonus acts that will be playable after completion of the game. They will make up a new, mini story that takes place after the games ending.

 A Lore Graphic Novel.

If we smash our initial funding goal and reach $50,000, there will be a graphic novel to with lore that will tell an extended story. This will be a side project of the game and be released with the game. It will include settings, stories, characters, and objects created by everybody; just as the game does. This will be available for free digitally but will cost money if a physical copy is requested. 

When/if we notice we are reaching stretch goals we will talk about what would be appropriate to go with this project. It may even be an idea from the community.

Thank you for reading and please, don't forget to pledge! We will be keeping people updated!

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

There are two main risks involved with this idea:

The first being that people are going to be skeptical and criticize the idea and think that the point of it is for me to make someone else's game and take credit. This is not true at all but I hope and believe that this will be the minority of the people that have things to say about this project. I will only take credit for what I did in this game: the idea behind the game, and the around 50% of the game that I hope to make. Other than this I will most likely be developing most of it as well. Everybody else who contributes whether it be money or an idea or both will be given some form of credit, although the more that one person contributes, the more credit they get as explained before.

The other is that I have never developed a game before but it has always been my dream and I understand how it works. The main thing to be concerned about is that it may take time for this game to finish. This is also the main reason for the use of GameMaker studio. I am very new to coding and to make my own engine or even to use another may make the time in takes to make increase at an exponential rate. GameMaker is a tool that makes it easier and can actually yield great results. The best example of this is a game called Hotline Miami; made in GameMaker, it's (in my opinion) a great game and has also won many awards. So please, no hate towards the thing that will make this project easier.

If you have any concerns about this project don't be afraid to ask or give your own opinion.

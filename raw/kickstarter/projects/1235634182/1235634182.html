The King's men are missing! Rescue them to help The King fight enemies and traverse obstacles in this chess-inspired puzzle platformer!

Try the game! Download the demo here: King's Men Demo

View the game on Steam Greenlight Concepts: King's Men on Steam

King's Men is a puzzle platforming game, featuring silly human characters that resemble iconic chess pieces. As The King, the player must rescue their men from the enemy! Once a character has been rescued, they are available to use as long as they stay alive. The player can easily switch between rescued characters to fight enemies, solve puzzles, or sacrifice themselves for the King. If a rescued character dies, they might be found again at a later point. Just as in real chess, if at any point the King dies, the game will be over, and the enemy will provide an insult with one of several unkind phrases.

Each character will have strengths and weaknesses, loosely inspired by the way their chess counterpart behaves on the chess board, as well as any historical significance associated with the chess piece's name. The Pawn will move slightly faster, but have a weaker attack. The Bishop will find unconventional and strategic ways to deal with enemies. The Queen will have an area of effect attack and quickly come to the aid of other characters.

I've always been a fan of games in which challenges can be solved differently based on the tools you have. In King's Men, there will be multiple ways to overcome puzzles and obstacles. It's possible the Knight, who is a powerful combatant, isn't available because she died or fell off the map during a previous fight. In this case, you could move some blocks around to safely pass an enemy, or risk losing a different character by attacking the enemy. There will be points in the game where the players will have a choice between platforming, solving puzzles, or combat, and will be able to choose based on their currently unlocked characters and personal taste. The demo has one such point towards the end, where the player can choose between a dastardly puzzle or a difficult series of platforms.

The game will be available for Windows, Mac, and Linux.

Continue reading to find out more about the planned gameplay components of King's Men! Keep in mind that some details about gameplay and design are subject to change as development progresses!

The King: Once a great warrior, The King's love for his land soon outshone his love for combat. He advises the kingdom and his men with wisdom and a warm heart. He's seen better days, and hobbles about. The King can attack with his staff for light damage, or block attacks by pulling up a magical shield. The shield can be released to briefly enlarge its size before disappearing - this action also pushes enemies away a short distance.

The Pawn: A loyal and eager devotee to The King. The Pawn is ready to do anything for The King, even if he doesn't understand why. He can move a little faster than the other King's Men. The Pawn has a fast sword attack for light damage, but lacks a secondary attack. The Pawn can't take as many hits as some of the bulkier characters.

The Rook: Fighting is so messy.As an archer accustomed to fighting from castle turrets, The Rook's main attack uses range in an attempt to keep her small health pool safe. If things get a little too close for comfort, she can try to stab her enemies by grasping an arrow.

The Knight: The Knight wears a fearsome horse-head helmet. A slower but more potent melee combatant than The Pawn, The Knight is the go-to character for fighting your way through. Her primary and secondary hammer attacks work together in succession. Her armor provides more protection, but also slows down her attacks.

The Bishop:The Bishop is quite unusual - why does he wear that bizarre hat? As The King's intellectual and legal advisor, he possesses great knowledge of the kingdom. The Bishop's primary ability temporarily freezes moving platforms and puzzle objects. He is also equipped with a short-range teleport allowing him to skip certain puzzles or groups of enemies entirely. These powerful abilities have long cooldowns. His puny arms lack the strength to damage enemies.

The Queen: The Queen is accustomed to handling the various affairs of The King, and as such, has a developed a certain diplomatic efficiency to aid her beloved King. However, she is no stranger to luxury and possesses many jewels and trinkets to assist her. The Queen's primary attack uses magic to damage all nearby enemies for a moderate amount. Her secondary ability, with limited charges, places a gemstone on the ground that can be used by other King's Men to restore a small amount of health.

The Kingdom is filled with lots of movable objects, switches, levers, hazards, spikes, and moving platforms. The King doesn't even remember why he asked for all these traps, but for right now he doesn't have time to ask questions - he has to save his men!

Currently Completed:

Level 0: Tutorial level. This is the level that's currently present in the demo download and development images of King's Men! Black and white checkerboard with lots of planters. The King is dreaming about his days on the battlefield and solving puzzles! This level is comprised of floating platforms and staircases in the sky. Clouds float by and banners blow calmly in the breeze.

Planned Levels:

Level 1: Throne room and great hall. Stone tiles, columns, and high walls are accented by boldly colored banners, crests, and sigils. The King awakes and realizes his men have been taken hostage by the enemy! This level will give the player a sense of scale for the castle, and introduce the Pawn and Rook.

Level 2: Interior greenhouse and courtyard. The glass ceiling keeps this area of the castle warm and humid despite the winter chill outside. Lots of various plants and vegetation, with some organic terrain dispersed throughout. This level will feature tighter corridors and multi-layered puzzles, and introduce the Knight and Bishop.

Level 3: The King makes his way to the library! Bookshelves and columns create puzzle barriers and hiding spots among the large corridors. Parts of the library are damaged from the enemy invasion of the castle. This level will feature very complex puzzles (it's a library after all) and introduce the Queen.

Level 4: The King realizes that the castle is filled with enemies, and decides to head underground through the basement and dungeons. Dark and wet, this level will not be as pretty as the rest of the castle. The King and his men must push onward to escape the castle and exact revenge on the enemy! This level will feature larger groups of enemies, a maze-like layout with dead ends.

Level 5: Outside the castle, it's the freezing winter. The King and his men must brave the frozen roads outside the castle walls en route to the enemy castle! As the King works his way up the mountainside, there will be lots of jumping puzzles, slippery surfaces, and dangerous heights. Unlike the tutorial level, this exterior will feature solid ground and terrain, interspersed with architectural elements like paved floors, stairs, and statues, obscured by the snow.

Level 6: The enemy King's castle! Filled with enemies and dastardly puzzles to hinder your advancement. Lots of sinister "decorations" adorn the walls of the enemy's keep, and peril lurks around every corner! The King and his men must triumph against the enemy!

Each level will be filled with puzzle elements, props, statues, explorable areas, and hidden rooms.

The main puzzle elements are magical stained glass cubes, which can be used to bridge gaps, access elevated areas, activate switch plates, or to hide behind in combat.

Pink Blocks:The fleeting block. Once touched, the pink block will begin fading. After about 10 seconds, the cube will disappear and return to its starting location. Act quickly!

Blue Blocks: The "lock block". Blue glass cubes can only be moved when their corresponding switch plate is being pressed down by a character, enemy, or another cube. As soon as the switch plate is released, the block will freeze position and become immobile - even in midair!

Green Blocks: The "simple" block. Green blocks can be moved about indefinitely, enabling them to be used in layered puzzles or strategic fights. Green blocks will disappear and reset to their starting location if their switch plate is activated.

Golden Blocks: The royal block. These can only be moved by the King. Similar to the blue block, this block will freeze position as soon as the King becomes inactive. Its position will reset if anything trips it switch plate. Be careful! All golden blocks will reactivate and respond to gravity when The King becomes active again.

Moving Platforms: May move back and forth, up and down, rotate, or tilt downward like a trapdoor!

Spikes: Result in instant death upon contact. Alternate forms may include lava, poisonous gas, quicksand, etc. The King really picked a geographically diverse and dangerous spot to live!

Decor:The Kingdom isn't all doom and gloom. In fact, each level will be populated with props, explorable areas, and hidden rooms. These items will change throughout the different areas of the Kingdom, giving each level its own style and theme.

Rescue Nodes: At first, the characters a player rescues will be "assigned" in a specific order, so that the player has a chance to unlock and play as each of the King's Men. Afterwards, the more difficult levels will be populated with random rescue nodes, which award the player with a random, currently locked character. If all of the King's Men are currently unlocked, the player will sometimes be able to backtrack to the rescue node at a later point if needed!

Exploration:Each level beyond the tutorial will have explorable areas. These might be fun areas with something extra to see, an alternate path to the end, extra rescue nodes, or they may feature dead-ends and impossible puzzles.

Hidden Rooms: Each level will feature hidden locations which reward the player with a visual storyline, humorous objects, insights about the game's development, or objects/outfit changes for the King's Men to wear. These may be at the end of a very long side-path, hidden behind a hanging banner, or underneath that suspicious looking statue. Keep an eye out for the hidden room in the tutorial level!

I've always dreamed of being a game developer. Inspired by games like The Legend of Zelda series, I've always been a fan of titles that allowed me to combine problem solving, reflexes, and knowledge of the game world to my advantage. I studied and obtained a degree in 3D art from Pratt Institute in Brooklyn, NY. After graduating, I've spent the past few years learning Unity and creating prototypes to further my knowledge of game design, as well as game art. Now that the depth of my programming knowledge is developing, I'm ready to complete and share my first ever fully fledged game from start to finish.

King's Men is an an exciting and ambitious project! With valuable input from supporters and early play testers, I hope to develop King's Men into a beautiful game that's incredibly fun to play.

King's Men has been in development since September 2013! I've been working in my free time and squeezing in extra development time whenever possible. I've spent most of this time focusing on gameplay mechanics to make sure the game is enjoyable to play. Now that a large portion of the "engine" is complete, I'm free to add art, additional levels, and more specific programming - such as the other characters and their abilities.

What's Completed?

Programming: Currently, the main game "engine", programmed in Unity and PlayMaker, is approximately 85% complete. Character movement, character and enemy health, physics, switching between characters, etc, are all completed. Checkpoints, character death, character rescue, essential GUI and menu items are complete. Enemy AI is in-progress.

Art: Look and feel, basic level concepts, as well as the tutorial level. The King, and his animations, are nearly complete. 3D textures and color palettes are approximately 30% complete.

What needs to be completed?

Programming: Fine-tuning the GUI, character-specific attack sequences, level-specific programming (trapdoors, hidden rooms, etc) , and the remainder of the enemy AI need to be finished.

Art: The remaining 5 characters need to be modeled in 3D, and animated for appropriate sequences (walking, attacking, etc). Level assets beyond the tutorial level need to be created and levels need to be assembled. Fine tuning of in-game elements, such as attack effects, puzzle elements, rendering effects, etc, need to be completed.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

I understand and accept the risks associated with being a new game developer. At any time, a wrench could be thrown into the gears and I may not know how to solve the issue immediately. Because of this, as with any game in development, certain release checkpoints could potentially be delayed or altered. I am always learning new things, and every mistake, bug, or hiccup helps me resolve future issues faster! As such, I will continue to push myself to overcome obstacles in development and research solutions when needed. The game is designed with this in mind - I have avoided adding in game mechanics and concepts that would be too hefty for a single developer to tackle.

My friends often tell me that I am dedicated and driven. I usually just refer to it as "computer face", those frequent development sessions where hours go by and I am too focused to notice. Like many game developers, I refuse to quit when an issue presents itself. I am faithful that my tenacity and patience will ensure the game's completion and release!

FAQ

  • How do I access the menu in the demo?

    Press the ESC or DEL key once you've loaded up the tutorial level. From here you can access settings and detailed information about the game!

    Last updated: Fri, May 2 2014 2:02 pm EDT
  • I'm stuck on a puzzle - help!

    The trickier puzzles rely on blue puzzle cubes, which don't respond to pushes, pulls, or gravity unless their switchplate is being pressed. Try to use other cubes to keep these switchplates pressed down, or properly time their release to freeze the blue cubes in place.

    In case you get lost, every cube has a way to reset itself. Press ESC or DEL to access the menu, and click the "how to play" menu option for a series of graphics which will help describe the function of each cube!

    Last updated: Fri, May 2 2014 2:02 pm EDT
  • How do I switch characters?

    The only unlocked character in the demo is The King. In later versions, the player will be able to unlock additional characters and switch between them.

    To sample the actual switching mechanism in the demo, you may press "1" to switch to the King (even though he is already active) to see the effect and animation.

    Last updated: Fri, May 2 2014 2:02 pm EDT

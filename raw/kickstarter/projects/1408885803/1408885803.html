Action-packed, 2-level, 3D game with traps, springs & exploding game squares for Mac, PC, or mobile.

Chess is arguably the most popular board game of all time. But after 2,000 years it is getting a bit tired so we decided to give it a twist or rather a new level—literally a new level. ChessTrap is traditional chess with a two-story board. Pieces move between boards through hidden trap doors and spring loaded squares. Oh, and the game is in full 3D!

Whether you're a grandmaster, casual player or beginner you'll love ChessTrap's new challenges.

Not knowing where the hidden traps are keeps play exciting and fun no matter your skill level. If you are a master chess player who likes to plan 12 moves ahead, the trap positions can be revealed and made stationary giving you back doors all over the board and two levels of possibilities. 

We thought the programming would be easy since computer chess engines have been around for over 30 years. It wasn't. We programmed for months to create a master level computer that can calculate the best possible moves on two stacked chess boards. This proved to be quite tricky, but the results are amazing. We even modified PGN (Chess' portable games notation) so games can be exported and shared.

But we did not stop with the board. We had fun with the pieces as well. Out of the box, or rather the mobile app store/marketplace of your choice, you get a choice two sets of pieces; the traditional black and white, and a goofy novelty set.

Quite regularly you'll have the option to download additional sets—dinosaurs, fish, cats vs. dogs, whatever. Our wish list is long already, but we would still love to hear your idea and who knows, maybe we will make it. We are also programming holiday themed games with unique boards and have several already in the works.

The really cool part about our novelty sets is the person you are playing across the cloud sees the set that you are playing on their board even if they don't own the pieces—your zombies against their fluffy bunnies. After all, what is the point of having a cool novelty set if nobody can see it but you.

PS: Rovio, Lego and Lucas LTD don't currently endorse or license to ChessTrap (but they should) and they own the Angry Bird, Lego and Star Wars trademarks respectively. Since we are handing out credits, the music from our video is royalty free and from Pond5.com.

Chesstrap can be played in three modes: against the computer, pass-n-play on one device, and with a friend or stranger across the cloud.

Other features include:

  • Chat
  • Video chat (depending on your device)
  • Multiple trap frequency settings
  • Multiple computer (AI) difficulty settings
  • Multiple preset viewing angles
  • Full zoom, angle adjustment and rotation with pinch and swipe gestures
  • Replay past games (modified PGN)
  • Load and watch others' games (modified PGN)

Platforms planned:

  • iOS (iPod, iPhone 4 and 5, iPad) - near completion!
  • Android mobile and tablet (with Kickstarter funding)
  • Blackberry (we know there is a user out there, somewhere)
  • Mac (with Kickstarter funding)
  • PC (with Kickstarter funding)

We have been playing ChessTrap in a testing environment on our iPads for a few weeks now. We are loving it and we are not true "chess players." Honestly, we never would have believed chess could be so much fun.

Our developers however are hardcore chess enthusiast and are even more excited than we are to release ChessTrap. When your developers are excited to release a product just so they can play it, you know you are onto something. 

We have started saying if you love chess, you will love ChessTrap. And if you don't love chess, you will still love Chesstrap.

ChessTrap is a great way to teach and keep children interested in chess. We built in a help feature that reveals all the possible moves a piece may make so you don't even need to know the rules to start playing, just learn as you go. 

The 3D and novelty pieces disguise the fact that children are playing a game that actually requires thinking and that has been proven to increase IQ—no kidding, read this or you can google the benefits of chess playing—About 786,000 results in 0.25 seconds can't be wrong. If you are parents like us, you will want to put this app on the kiddos' iPods.

ChessTrap is not going to create world peace or clean up the environment, but it is fun and will bring hours of enjoyment and social interaction to many around the world. So in its own small way, ChessTrap will make the world a better place—how can you not back that?

At Hughmanity we are a world-wide team of part-timers with full-time gigs to pay the bills. We are based in France, China, India and US and are dedicated to creating fun, thoughtful products. ChessTrap is Hughmanity's first 3D-based game (although our lead programmers have programmed several) and we learned a lot during the process. Won't you support us to make it a reality? You'll be justly rewarded and based on your pledge you can have multiple versions of the game, COOL SWAG (see above) or even an exclusive version built just for you.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Software development is a tricky thing. With every software project you plan a budget based on estimated hours, but once you get going, new technical challenges crop up, or parts of the project take longer than expected—we have had our fair share of surprises, but that is to be expected when you are doing things that nobody has done before. 

The main challenge for artificial chess development is the programming of the chess engine. As if programming a chess engine was not hard enough, ours had to work two boards of play at once, which as far as we know, had never been done before we built ours. If we weren't 100% complete with the programming of our chess engine (several arduous months that we are happy to have behind us), we would not be as confident in completing our project. 

With the chess engine is completed, connected to the game engine we and our alpha testing group have been playing ChessTrap on our iPads for several weeks. We feel that ChessTrap is all but ready to be ported to Android, Mac and PC. Our funding goal of $19K should be enough to finish up and launch so you all can play too. However, we have been around long enough to know that things outside of our control could affect this budget, so feel free to push us above and beyond our goal—really, we don't mind at all. 

We have been project managing and developing software for many years and unless there are major changes to the current Mac and PC operating systems, we are confident we can deliver ChessTrap as the fun, exciting and challenging game we outlined in this campaign and with your help and support, we will.

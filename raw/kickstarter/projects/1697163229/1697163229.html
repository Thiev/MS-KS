Insectobot Tactics is a simple multiplayer strategy game. Choose your insectobots, place them on the map, last one standing wins!


The game involves two players, each with their own board. Using a team of robotic insects (insectobots), each player attempts to eliminate the opposing player's bugs by firing on the enemy grid.

Release Platforms

This game is going to be released on iPhone, Android, online, and a downloadable desktop version. All variations of the game will save your progress online, and all platforms will be able to play together.Â 

Choosing your team

At the start of every game, both you and your opponent will get to select a specific number of insectobots based on the size of the game. Every insectobot has 4 attributes to consider while deciding on your roster:


  • Movement determines the cost of moving the insectobot during the turn.
  • Attack gives the attack type, the cost of using the attack, and the size of the attack.Â 
  • Visibility shows how big of an area will be shown to the enemy when you attack with the insectobot.
  • Size is how many hits that insectobot can take before being destroyed, and how many squares it takes up on the map.


The Roster

There are currently 18 insectobots planned for the game, with the possibility of 3 additional bugs based on rewards. The three current attack types are as follows:

  • Standard attack: This is instantaneous damage to the selected square. A miss with a standard attack will have no effect.
  • Mine: A mine is like a standard attack if it hits, but if it misses it will stick around on the enemy map for a set number of turns. The enemy cannot see mines, and if an enemy moves over a mine, that insectobot will receive damage. The points used to lay a mine is usually greater than a standard attack, and only one mine can be placed in a square at a time.
  • EMP / Trap: This type of attack prevents the enemy from moving if it hits. The size of an EMP attack is usually far larger than a standard attack, but it does no damage to the enemy. If an EMP insectobot is the last bot alive, it's attack type will switch to a one square standard attack.Â 


A Standard Turn

All examples in this section have only one friendly and one enemy insectobot for simplicity, but during a standard game you will have 4-6 bots under your command.Â 

After your insectobots are chosen, you will get to decide where you want to place them on the map. When all insectobots are placed, you can then take your first turn. Both players start the game with half the action points available.

Then you can go through your bots and decide what to do with each. After selecting an insectobot, you will see the stats of the selected bot on the bottom right.

Movement is often defined by a fraction, and determines how far an insectobot can move per action point spent. If a bot has a movement value of 1/2, that says that they can move 2 spaces per action point. Movement is always based around the head of the insectobot, so a movement value of 2 will be two squares in all directions from the head. Rotating an insectobot doesn't count towards moving, and action points always round up, so for a 1/2 movement bot, moving 1 space, or even just rotating them will still use a full action point.

Visibility specifies how much detail is shown to the enemy when attacking with that insectobot. If you attack more than once with the same insectobot, the smaller visibility area of the two attacks will be shown to the enemy.

Each turn, players will be able to fire upon the enemy and move their bugs around the grid. To keep things fair, both teams will simultaneously move, and then both teams will simultaneously attack. This avoids giving either team aÂ first-turn advantage. These actions are limited, however, by the number of points available per turn.Â 

How much information can you risk giving your opponent? Should you attack and risk giving away your location, or move to avoid attacks? Is it better to take all attacking bugs, or bugs with high movement, or mine-laying bugs? The premise is simple, but a well planned strategy can be far more effective at eliminating the opponent.

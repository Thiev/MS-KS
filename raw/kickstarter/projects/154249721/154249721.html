Babycastles is an effort to provide a social home for the game development culture that has long been part of New York City.

Babycastles (http://babycastles.com), in conjunction with Showpaper (http://showpaper.org), is opening a pop-up video game, art, and music venue next to Grand Central in Manhattan, at 217 East 42nd Street, from October 8th through December 29th this year.

The primary goal of Babycastles is to establish independently developed video games as a viable form of social culture for New York City and its youth.

We have existed since 2005 as a student work exhibition in Columbia University's Collision Art Party, to an arcade installation at a Brooklyn record release party in 2007, to a NYC game developer exhibition in the back of the music venue Death By Audio in 2008, and finally into our permanent home in the basement of DIY art and music space Silent Barn in Queens, NY, where we have hosted nearly a year of monthly arcade exhibitions since November 2009 (including "Games That Will Make You Cry", curated by NYU-Poly student Arthur Ward Jr, and "God Bless Babycastles - Christian Games" curated by game designer Ivan Safrin).

At a Manhattan location, we're aiming to strongly influence the public perception of video games and the culture surrounding this art form. There are diverse explorations of play, statement, format, enjoyment, and development of games, often unpublished and barely acknowledged even by most game enthusiasts. By presenting six consecutive 2-week exhibitions of independent video games at the center of New York, Babycastles is looking forward to showcasing international game development to a wide audience of game enthusiasts, students, press, curators, children, and especially to an audience that may never otherwise encounter the variety of activity in video game culture.

It will be an incredible arcade in Times Square!

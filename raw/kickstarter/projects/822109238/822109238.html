Experience a true odyssey through a movie in which you play as the main character. For movie-lovers and game-lovers alike.

UPDATE 1: Ever wondered about the history of Turkey Island?

3001: A Turkey Odyssey is a game movie–a world we created on PC and iOS for adults and also teenagers 13+. What we love about it is that it plays exactly like a movie, due to its rich cinematography and complex plot, incorporating elements of suspense, desire and dark humor.

In the midst of key moments within the movie, each critical scene transforms into an action-oriented, arcade-style game level, in which you play as the main character, Turki Wilde. The main difference between the Turkey Odyssey experience and say, other games that also use cinematic elements is that our game levels really follow the rapid changes in mood throughout the film. This means that each game level is completely different from the next in terms of content and genre, staying faithful to the scene in which it’s embedded.

After centuries of apocalyptic wars, violent climate change, and radioactive waste spillage thanks to you humans, animals had to adapt to their dangerous environment. It was a race through the evolutionary ladder, and us turkeys came out on top! We became a LOT smarter, probably because we were sick and tired of being made fun of for being the dumbest birds on earth.

That’s right! We won’t be laughed at no more! Using our big brains and muscular wings, a few of us pioneers broke out of the poultry factory–that filthy prison you expect us to call home–and colonized Staten Island (though it had already been deserted by humans long ago for being uncool).

We put our foot down and renamed it Turkey Island, and now, the industry is booming and the land is fertile. You people just couldn’t see its epic potential. Now, we got schools, we got our own berry farms, heck, we even got our own government.

Other birds, they’re always begging to immigrate to Turkey Island. But before setting foot on our land, they gotta take an oath saying that turkeys are the smartest, most elegant birds in the world, without breaking eye-contact or laughing. Few have passed this test. Anyway, I’ll save you the historical lecture. The movie’s really about my teenage nephew, Turki Wilde.

Turki Wilde goes to high school everyday, along with other pheasants–some more popular than him, others not so much.

One day, his life is turned upside down, flipped inside out, and spontaneously combusted, just for the hell of it. All in a matter of minutes, and all because of love. At first sight.

When Turki Wilde meets Lola Love, all hell breaks loose. He and his male friends, the young lads, they get into a cockfight! One on one combat just like we did in the old days. That’s where you, my friend, have to step in and help Turki fight those other son-of-a-turkeys, because let me tell you Lola is one sexy chick!

This is Turki Wilde’s story, but it’s also your story. If you’ve ever been in love, if you’ve ever secretly rebelled against the status quo, if curiosity has ever taken you over the edge, you might find something deeply resonating in 3001: A Turkey Odyssey. After all, this adventure, crazy as it is, is really a slice of life with all its glorious complexities, with elements of:

  • Narrative immersion: The content­–the objective, the features–of each game level depends on what the main character is doing, and what’s going on in the movie. In each game level, you play as the main character of the movie.
  • Diversity of game genre: We can’t assign one particular genre to the gameplay of Turkey Odyssey, because just as any movie undergoes turbulent changes in content and mood from scene to scene, the game levels you’ll face along the way will be equally diverse. We’re promising at least 10 action-oriented, arcade-style game levels in different genres: fighting, music, platform, shooter and more.
  • One story: The beauty of movies lies in their inevitability–their commitment to telling one story, the best story. This is why the gameplay of Turkey Odyssey does not influence the story. But why would you want to play any particular game level again after playing through the entire movie?
  • Replayability: Each game level takes about a minute to play, is easy to pick up, but hard to master. Similar to something like Fruit Ninja, the carefully crafted addictiveness of our game levels stems from the constant feeling that you could have done better. You’ll want to go back to certain moments in the odyssey even after completing the journey–to beat your own best, to compete with friends, to savor the moment.

What we LOVE about 3001: A Turkey Odyssey is the wonderful story and the movie-like visual storytelling surrounding the game levels. Writing the screenplay and backstory, as well as drawing the extensive storyboards took pure passion and dedication, but making the imagination come alive on the screen takes even more. It takes funds. Even creating the trailer you see in our Kickstarter video meant that someone had to sacrifice Chipotle for a month. Here’s why:

  • Keeping art beautiful: Each finalized image takes a long journey from the storyboard to the sketchpad, and finally onto the computer, where it is digitally painted. Keeping up our current visual style for the rest of the movie requires additional illustrators. Otherwise our head illustrator will put in so many hours that he’ll start thinking he’s a turkey.
  • Keeping animation smooth: We were only able to self-fund the animation for the beginning of the movie, and even that was a vein-popping endeavor. But most frustratingly, it took months. Creating around 40 minutes worth of story through motion graphics, occasional frame-by-frame animation and visual effects is a massive undertaking that requires additional animators.
  • Keeping sound appropriate: If you take the soundtrack out of the shower scene in Psycho, what was legendary becomes moot. Sound is crucial. To create a spectacular game movie that caresses all the emotional cords, we need additional musicians. We don’t want our turkeys sounding like cows.
  • Keeping stomachs from eating themselves: Currently, our team of concept artists, illustrator, writer, game designers, programmers and musician are working either for free or on a shoe-string budget, neglecting our other duties, classes, wives. Adequate funding means we’ll get to keep doing what we love full-time, without having to eat nothing but ramen noodles.

We’re extremely excited about the potential Kickstarter will unlock, as we will be using every bit of funding towards keeping the artistic integrity of the script intact and building upon it, enhancing every little detail in the movie, the game levels, the transitions between movie and game levels, to make the absolute best experience we can make.

Once upon a time, few movie-lovers who wanted to stretch out those sweet, climactic movie moments and experience them first-hand, got together with some game-lovers who savored the challenge of games, and the result was the start of 3001: A Turkey Odyssey. We have the focused passion to go on.

So far, we've completed the entire screenplay, designed almost all of the characters, developed approximately a third of the game levels, composed the first version of the soundtrack, and created the beginning of the movie. We need your help to finish, and deliver the best experience possible.

We've clearly fallen in love with this project, and want you to love it the same way we came to love it–by becoming a part of the creative process! Throughout the campaign, as well as the developmental process, we’ll be unleashing fun surveys and polls that shape the finer details within the world of Turkey Island. If you share the love of games and movies, let’s build the vision together!

Project Lead: Zaeem Golbaghi

Zaeem has a master’s degree in physics from NYU (the character of Jake may or may not be based on him). Besides being a physics nerd, he is also an avid gamer, analyzing the psychology behind casual games and what makes them click with players. He's the leader of the wolf pack, the glue that holds us together. Zaeem is the person who sacrificed Chipotle for a month.

Art Director / Screenwriter: Calypso Guo

Calypso watches 10 movies a week and happily took on the daunting role of being the artistic force behind the movie aspect of 3001: A Turkey Odyssey. Drawing inspiration from thousands of films watched and knowledge from NYU Tisch, her unique style weaves the laugh-out-loud humor of Pixar and the mature themes of an R-rated movie, all to make you feel uncomfortable.

Head Game Developer: Babak Samari

Babak spits out games like a vending machine, having created games like Spread Out 3D. In addition to a masters degree in computer engineering, he's a vision and graphics postgraduate at McGill University. Most importantly, he has had an ongoing, passionate love affair with games of all kinds ever since he was old enough to crawl.

Concept Artist: Rasoul Ahmadi

Rasoul has flowing hair like Jesus and all goodness and beauty sprouts from his fingertips. Rasoul is our concept artist. He wields the pen like a mighty sword, but instead of slaying, it creates life. Rasoul has extensive experience in character design, illustration and graphic art. Rasoul can never be overworked, for he is magic.

Game Designer: Emran Shadafza

Emran is a true Renaissance man. Besides being a programming wiz, Emran is the fusion of the artistic eye and the engineering brain made flesh, and therefore has a say on absolutely everything. There’s an ongoing rumor that Emran never sleeps–a desperate attempt at explaining how Emran manages to complete so many tasks so quickly.

Sound Designer: Alireza Karduni

Alireza describes himself as an art machine. He opens his mouth, and music comes out, and he is the sound designer of 3001: A Turkey Odyssey. Alireza’s superpower is analyzing everything to the core, almost to the point of paranoia, making him the perfect guy to collaborate with our composers to create the best sound.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

The project was started over a year ago by three passionate and persistent individuals with a clear direction in mind, though they were three separate directions. We solved the massive problem of forming a collective vision, and this cohesive vision has attracted and will continue to attract many skilled artists and engineers along our journey.

Currently, we envisioned the story down to its minutest details, designed all of the game levels along the way, as well as developed four game levels. After working together for nearly a year, the team has a clear understanding of its capabilities and potential. Now the uncertainties are almost entirely a matter of enhancement, striving towards perfection, not struggling with core components. Based on our past history, we know that we have set a completely manageable deadline and will deliver on time.

We had the experience of tackling numerous odd problems on this project, and we are fully prepared for the future. Many of us have sacrificed and risked a lot for this project, and that only makes us more committed and hell-bent on reaching the full potential of game movie, creating the best possible 3001: A Turkey Odyssey.

Shadow Era was the very first full-scale mobile trading card game, that also featured a free-to-play game model hailed by TouchArcade as "a fantastic representation of what freemium games should be."

Since its release, Shadow Era's community has continued to grow, attracting more than 4 million registered players and one of the largest and most active official forums of any trading card game. The game currently features 350 cards, available to play on iOS, Android, PC, Mac and even in a web browser. There is also a premium quality physical version of the game, using the exact same rules found in the digital version.

But what makes Shadow Era so special?

  • Dual Resource System - Each turn you can choose to sacrifice one card to generate a resource, used for casting cards. Heroes also generate 1 shadow energy per turn to use their special abilities, which can drastically turn the tide of a battle.
  • Fast Paced - The game is designed to play out in 5 or 10 minutes. You get to choose who you will attack, either going straight for their hero or picking off their allies first.
  • No Interrupts! - We did away with the annoying interrupt mechanic that makes it necessary to constantly pass on your opponents turn. In its place there are plenty of traps, turnabouts and surprises to keep things interesting.
  • Highly Balanced - We spent a lot of effort to make this one of the most balanced card games you can find. You'll see a wide variety of decks being used by all levels of ranked players.
  • Great Community - We love our community, and it shows! Whether you want to discuss strategies, join guilds, or just chat about the overpowered Firesnake, you'll be welcomed on our official forums!

But don't take our word for it! Play the game yourself right now or visit our official forums for more info! You can also download the full rulebook.

The setting for Shadow Era is in the medieval world of Balor. In Call of the Crystals, the inhabitants discovered magical shadow crystals with great power, but using them led to the appearance of strange creatures that began to overrun the land.

In Dark Prophecies, more was learned about the nature of the crystals and the invading Shadows.

Shattered Fates continues the story of the battle for Balor. For those interested, here's some background lore:

As the moon set, another petty squabble erupted among what remained of the Shadow's forces. If he'd been asleep, the noise would have likely awoken the tactician were he able to sleep to begin with. Arthyle looked down over the map again carefully while he waited for one of the tribal leaders to step in and reign in their soldiers, his temper kept back only by a sheer force of will.

It had been weeks since the battle at Fort Rothem. Weeks since Gunther had led the human armies up the side of Mount Balor itself to assault the impressive building named for their former king. The advantage had belonged to the Shadow, and Arthyle held no doubt that given a few more hours the Human armies would have suffered too many losses to continue the attack. With the knowledge to be gained from the Conduit, it even appeared the war was coming to its end.

Then something unpredictable had happened.

Somehow Loest had interfered with the device, and triggered an explosion no one could have foreseen. What numbers of Shadow's forces that weren't destroyed by the blast itself lost their footing and resolve. It was a matter of minutes before the sounds of retreat were signaled and Arthyle was forced to watch the army he'd helped to lead, helped to guide, slip into the shadows once more. Now the infighting was becoming more than he could tolerate.

Yet he knew there was still hope. The Conduit was not the only weapon the Aldmor had left behind for him to acquire.

Slowly Arthyle's decrepit lips curled into a cruel smile, as he drew a sheet of parchment and scribbled a series of orders down for his personal aide. The ravagers were cruel, but intelligent and he knew his request would be fulfilled to the letter. It was time to gather the leaders of the various shadow worlds together and call forth their warriors to battle once more. There was work to be done.

  • What is the Elite Membership?

    The Elite Membership is a monthly plan with many benefits.

    For starters, you'll get an epic avatar. An example of this is the CotC preorder customers, who have a star next to their name throughout the game and a gold border around their avatar during battles. The Elite Membership will be visually distinct.

    Next, you'll also get a free event ticket every day. This means you can join a tournament and not have to pay the entry fee of 100 shadow crystals. You can also save up your event tickets, to use them all on the weekend for example. This is a great way to be active in tournaments and get a good chance to win prizes.

    Last updated: Mon, Nov 25 2013 5:41 am EST
  • When will the physical items ship?

    Once the digital Shattered Fates set is out on the live servers, it will take several months of final balance checking to ensure that the set can be locked down and printed. But we have vastly improved our production workflow over the first two physical sets, so the time between digital and physical should be reduced. Currently our goal is to have the physical Shattered Fates cards shipping Summer 2014.

    Please note that this Kickstarter is for digital items only. Only the higher tiers $500 and above have physical bonuses.

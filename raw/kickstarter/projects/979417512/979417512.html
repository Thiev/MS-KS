TIME FOR ZOMBIES TO OVERRUN THE FARM…

Niftymancer combines a lane defense with a match three game (think “Plants versus Zombies” meets “Triple Town”), tied together with an RPG-style setting with quests, magic items, and boss fights.  The whimsical, sinister, and curious creatures of Niftymancer give life to the creative work of artist/cartoonist JUSTIN HILLGROVE, of impsandmonsters.com fame!

Niftymancer is being initially released on the web in flash, and (with your help!) on iPad.

The game casts you as a misunderstood Necromancer, trying to do your experiments in peace.  However, the mean, wicked forest creatures and fairies are “upset” that you’re summoning ghosts and zombies in their forest, and are out to smash your laboratory.  It draws a lot of inspiration from dark fantasies like “Coraline” and “The Nightmare Before Christmas.”

…BUT ONLY WITH YOUR HELP!

We’ve taken Niftymancer from spark of imagination to prototype, but there are a lot of expenses associated with building the art, worlds, levels, music, and iOS version of the game.  Our minimum target gives us enough to cover the basics and realize the game; we’d like to do that and carry it further to be truly fantastic.  You can make that possible!

Your support is deeply appreciated, and we have some cool rewards in the form of special art from Justin Hillgrove and the art team, as well as special powers, pets, and benefits with which you may smite those troublesome pixies.

Read on, fellow practitioner of the Dark Arts…

We’re taking what you expect from a fantasy game and flipping it on its head.  And giving it a dark, creepy, fun soul.

Niftymancer creates a great “mid-core” game by combining fast, fun mechanics (what some might call “casual”, although that’s a bad word in some quarters) with compelling, in-depth, RPG-style world map and setting. We want the basic gameplay to be addictive and fun, and the progression map to actually tie things together with a light fantasy RPG, enhanced by multiplayer elements that let you help out your friends, brag, and collaborate on quests.

  • Available on the web and (hopefully) the iPad.
  • Not some obnoxious farm game.You play a Necromancer destroying the pixie’s forest.  Three cheers for wanton destruction!
  • Cute, but in a creepy kind of way.  The style of Niftymancer is heavily influenced by Tim Burton, Coraline, and other dark fantasies.
  • Engaging lane defense.  Summoning your zombies and building your laboratory is strategic, compelling, and fast paced.
  • Fun fantasy narrative.  You explore your story as the Necromancer makes his or her way deeper into the world.
  • Help your friends.  Brag to your friends.  You can send your pets out to help your buddies as they try to solve difficult gameboards or defeat boss monsters.
  • Quest system.  The game world is tied together with questing, story, and exploration.
  • The art of Justin Hillgrove!
  • The music of Midnight Syndicate!

This project isn’t in the “dream on” phase.  We’re leveraging a strong multiplayer server backend, core technology, and a fun game prototype.We’ve been playing with the core gameplay to the point that we know it’s fun, and we have a vision we want to take to the world.

We need your help to bring something fresh and new and wicked to the game space.  Your assistance makes it possible for us to build the creatures, design the levels, animate the world, create the music, and add more exciting RPG game systems.

“WHO ARE YOU GUYS?More importantly, are you some dude in his basement or are you experienced game developers?”

The Niftymancer team has an extensive bench of game developer talent spanning more than a decade of Massively Multiplayer, Social and Action games.

Thomas Sitch spent ten years making MMOs over such titles as Shadowbane, Pirates of the Burning Sea, and Wizard-101, before striking out on his own to be an independent game developer.  He brings a wide range of skill in engineering, design, and leadership to the team.

Justin Hillgrove and his work have been rapidly gaining fame and notoriety in the Pacific Northwest and beyond.  Justin’s work (documented at www.impsandmonsters.com), ranges from the silly to the truly magical.

Morgan Lavigne is a programmer with a long list of social games under his belt, including Collapse! Blast and Bayou Blast.  When not hammering out code into pretty shapes, Morgan terrorizes the greater Seattle area with his band, Steel Tigers of Death.

Robb Sanchez is a veteran artist animator, with over 15 years in the game industry.  An avid surfer and mountain biker, Robb also teaches digital media at the college level. 

Lindsey Krassin graduated from the Art Institute of Seattle in 2006 with a degree in Media Art and Animation and subsequently worked for six years in the video game industry as a character animator. She then made the leap to children's book artist, animating and illustrating two digital titles: Alice in Wonderland and Hansel and Gretel, before returning to the game industry and taking a faculty position at the Art Institute.

Joowon Kim graduated from Guildhall at SMU with Masters in Interactive Technology in Digital Game Development specializing in Art Creation and went on to spent two years doing MMO development at KingsIsle Entertainment. Her undergraduate study in computer science helped her work flawlessly with programmers, and she also enjoys brainstorming new game ideas with other creative people. When not wrapped in game development, she enjoys yoga, running, and concept sketching.

Beth Hammer is a Seattle based Graphic Designer and Illustrator, with an undying love of zombies and kids cartoons.

Lucy Bellwood is a freelance illustrator and cartoonist operating out of Periscope Studio in Portland, OR. When not glued to her drawing board, she can be found sailing traditional tall ships up and down the West coast and dancing the Blues. You can follow her adventures online at lucybellwood.com.

Milo Hayden is a composer and sound designer from Portland, OR. It's been his life-long dream to work on a video game. He is an avid fan of monster sounds.

STRETCH GOALS

We’ve budgeted Niftymancer for the minimum we need (after deducting Amazon fees, Kickstarter fees, and pledge awards) to realize the vision and core spirit of the game.  That said, there are a lot of awesome things we want to be able to do, and we hope that with your support and the support of the community, we can expand the game’s release into many of these cool areas.

$50,000 – Boss Fights & the Realm of Flame
We have an excellent boss fight system that lets friends collaborate with each other to take down the big bad adversaries, and if we hit the 50k mark, that system will get the coding and art it needs!  Also, the fire elementals, fire mephits, and fyre-fae will also get the art they need, along with cool fire-based loot!

$60,000 – More content and additional Midnight Syndicate Music
This stretch goal lets us get a lot of additional magic items and customization options and, most importantly, additional musical tracks composed by Midnight Syndicate!

$70,000 – Support from fans at this level will be incredibly exciting, because it will give us the coding and licensing budget we need to get Niftymancer out on the iPad!

$80,000 – This is an awesome content goal, because it gives us the Frozen Realm, yeti pets, frost-fae, and ice themed magic items.  Levels here involve freeing ghosts from icy prisons and the occasional snowball fight.

$90,000 – Reaching this goal gives us the support to add additional Midnight Syndicate music (to give full diversity to the elemental realms), and also unlocks the Halls of the Cavern King and its subterranean denizens, sverfneblin, and earth elementals.

$100,000 – The cloud realm, ruled by the sky-fae monarch, Peralda, Queen of the Sylphs!  This is a gorgeous place of floating castles, misty islands, and other lovely things just waiting to be despoiled by a Necromancer in need of (very important) spell components.

We live in an extraordinary time, when a few people with a dream can tap the energy and support of people like you!We feel deeply honored to have this opportunity, and to create something fun and extraordinary.

Your support makes it possible to bring a dream like this to life; not some marketing driven carbon copy cranked out by one of the big publishers, but a dream realized through hard work, passion, and a little luck… and, of course, YOU!

Thank you!

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

We’ve worked to mitigate the technological risk by leveraging Trilabrys core technology, but all game projects entail a large amount of work. In general, software is difficult to finish, and unexpected hurdles or technical difficulties may arise, even for industry veterans.

Enough of the game framework is in place for us to be confident that the game will ship this year, but there is also the possibility that we may slip from our August target date.

There are challenges in doing the iOS port, and in other Apple compatibility and performance issues. Here, as with the rest of the software, there could be delays, and we will also be at the mercy of the App Store (for the iOS version, not the web).

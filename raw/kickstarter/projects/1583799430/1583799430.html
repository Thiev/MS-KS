Train and nurture your Dino from birth, then race it through lands that time forgot in this prehistoric hit of a game

Us "Wrensters" (the team behind MOOPS) are creating the Jurrasic-tastic game DINO RACING - an ultra-high quality game that will rival the best cartoon racing games currently available in the style of Mario Kart.  

This game will combine elements of Tamagotchi principles, as your racing Dino will effectively be raced and nurtured by you.  This TRexiffic game creates an original and immersive experience, not easy to stop (bit like a Brontosaurus).

DINO RACING is a "Race game" like no other.

Dinos are saddled up, with cavemen or cave women (player avatars) mounted on top as "Dinojocks" guiding their Dinos at high speed through hazardous prehistoric courses, trying to make the finish line first and pick up some useful bits and pieces along the way. The game has a slapstick feel to it, making it fun to play and hard to ignore (like dinosaurs). "Let's get ready to Rubble" ...

The Twist

You've seen a bunch of racing games out there trying to grab your attention but what makes DINO RACING so addictive, is that your beasts have to be fed and trained to perform well. If they are not fit, they won't win a race. In fact, they might not even be well enough to get out of their dino-beds in the morning. So, it's up to you....... If you don't earn some "Dino-scran" and take them down the "Fossil Gym" for some "Raptor Reps" or "Very cross training" they just won't get you anywhere (only well trained "Dinos will succeed in the harder races).  

There are many amazing prehistorical worlds to race in (Forest - Desert - Ice and Cave for instance ) and each one will have its own specific obstacles and dangers. 

Game Dynamics

Racing

Each user will become a "Dinojock" controlling a caveman or cavewoman (player avatar) The player will attempt to complete a dangerous prehistoric circuit against other players (up to four in live 'head to head' mode) or race against the devilishly hard CPU controlled players.  Bump into other players, slow them down, whilst navigating obstacles and hazards along the way. Dangerous creatures and Jurassic perils are littered about the course, to be avoided and interacted with, to hamper other players progress.  

Early test movement

"Tongue flapping"

A further dynamic is using the Dino's tongue which acts as a super-quick, sticky, retractable device, whipping items into the Dino’s mouth like a hyper-active lizard; such as ‘pickups’, food and some of the local wildlife (if they weren't wild before, they will be furious afterwards). The tongue can be a more productive tool also, hurling objects into the path of other players to slow them down or make them extinct completely. But, even in these B.C. times, accidents can happen, and these might include "accidentally" giving caveman spectators in the crowd a good tongue-flapping. Depending on how healthy (or not) this "Dino-head" is, this could prove nutritional and increase chances of victory.

Dinosaur Interaction

Outside race times, there is the Dinogym where Dinojocks can nurture, train and play with their Dino. If looked after, the healthier and happier the Dino will become, helping it to grow quickly and compete in the progressively harder Dino races.

Eggs

Dinos start as eggs. These are hatched by tapping and rubbing the egg (on the mobile screen) until it cracks open to reveal a baby Dino ("aaahhhhh"). These little reptilian miracles will need to be looked after thoroughly in order for them to grow up and join the race. They will need food to help them grow and some training to build up their ability ratings. This will have to be bought by "Iguanos" earned in racing a grown up Dino. Initially, there are traditional Dinos to choose from for racing in the game, each having their own unique abilities. The player can also select Dinos that they have hatched and nurtured themselves. These can have different abilities depending on breeding and can be given unique names. Additional Dino eggs are earned by collecting "special items" in the Dino world. For example, collecting all the blue mushrooms around a track in the game will give the player special bonus items such as a new egg to crack open giving the player a new Dino to race with. These special items will be hidden in places all over the race track for the player to discover whilst racing, giving a replay value to the tracks.

Dino Abilities:

The Dinos have capabilities which increase or decrease, depending on how well they are looked after; including being fed on a regular basis, maintaining good health through visits to the Dinogym,  improving reflexes and strength. 

Dino attributes that can be improved in the gym are:

  • •Jump Height = How high the Dino can jump.
  • •Speed = How fast the Dino can travel over land.
  • •Acceleration = How fast the Dino takes to reach maximum speed over land.
  • •Strength = How much (hit points) before the Dino collapses (food replenishes Health).
The player can put any of their Dinos into the Dino Gym to train, once they have been hatched and have participated in their first race. The player will need to spend 'Iguanos' (currency) to further improve the abilities of each Dino, before participating in race events, such as the Bronze, Silver or Gold Mosquito races. These can be earned during a race. The Dinojocks are charged a fee based on how much training is required to level up the dinosaur and qualify it for participation in progressively harder Dino races.

Traditional Dinos:   (to be used while baby Dino grows up)

  • Bipedal Carnivore: Tyrannosaurus Rex. Velociraptor. Spinosaurus. Dilohosaurus, Garudimimus. Baryonyx. Carnotaurs.
  • Quadrupedal Herbivore: Triceratops. Brachiosaurus. Stegosaurus. Ankylosaurus.Diplodocus. Garudimimus. Baryonyx. Carnotaurs.

Ex Dinos:

Dinos will become extinct (again) in the game if they are not looked after, becoming fossils in the process. Players can view the fossils of all the creatures they have hatched and cared for even after death.

Game Worlds:

Each themed game world will contain a number of levels which are selectable and are difficulty based. Each world will have its own paleon-tastic look with unique hazards, features and challenges. The initial stages will feature the following:

  • •Volcanic
  • •Underground Caves
  • •Jungle
  • •Ice Tundra

THE TEAM

Andrew Bishop - Headhonchosaurus of DINO RACING.

"The Bish" has worked in the UK animation industry for over 20 years, producing topnotch work for many leading companies including BBC, ITV, Channel 4, New Line Cinema and Disney. He has worked on many feature films too; including James Bond 'Golden Eye', 'The Wind in the Willows' and 'Lost in Space'. Andrew has started some of London’s best known effects houses; AMGFX (now called the Hive) and Darkside at Molinare (where he won industry awards for animated commercials) and most recently Darkside Animation and WREN Animation Studios where he continues to direct graphics and special fx animation for Broadcast, Advertising and Corporate clients.

The "Wrensters" at WREN are an excitable and knowledgeable bunch of animators, texture artists, concept and storyboard artists, art directors, modellers, and effects producers with over 20 years of industry experience straddling 3 continents for companies such as Electronic Arts and 2KGames.

PEGI GAME AGE RATING

The game is intended for children and teens (and any grown ups with a baby dino still inside them) containing some acceptable cartoon violence. As much as we would love to have our Dinos munch live cattle and bite the heads off cavemen, this would need a higher PEGI rating when it goes on sale on the iTunes store and this would limit our target audience. We expect the game would probably receive a PEGI rating 7 based on the current content level.

http://www.pegi.info/en/index/

SUCCESS

So far the Dino Racing game has been developed using sweat equity. We have all given our time for free or very little Iguanos.  If the Kickstarter campaign succeeds we will be able to dedicate ourselves full-time to completing the game.

If for some reason we don't reach the target funding by the end of the campaign we won't get any of it. You keep any money you pledged and we have to find another way to complete the game.

Over-funding

Even more could be done if extra funding is available, things like multiplayer, more levels and an accelerated timescale for release on other platforms. More details and video updates will be released if it seems possible that the original funding goal will be surpassed.

What about PC, Mac & Linux?

Dino Racing has been planned from the start to be a multiplatform game, with iOS and Android being the lead platforms. Once the iOS and Android versions are complete work will begin on creating specially tailored versions for other platforms.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

The Wren Animation team have been developing games, films and animation for over 20 years, having worked for the likes of the BBC, Channel 4 and Sky making commercials, special fx and feature films. We released an app called Moops in Summer 2012 to critical acclaim using the same team we are using to create DINO RACING.

We have the skills, we have refined them we continually hone them to be among the best working in the UK today. What we have learnt is that every project comes with challenges and that makes us concerned that when DINO RACING becomes the massive hit we know it will be, there will be another Triassic period with the Dinos returning with a vengeance.

We have been in the industry for a long time but to say we have everything covered would be a lie but we believe that the key to success is to anticipate the pitfalls and develop tactics to cope with them. We will take the same care that we have on all our past projects to ensure we develop a brilliant and immersive experience.

So, to ensure success we have been creating plans in pre-production to tackle any issues we may face from the UI, Mapping, Command System and general game mechanics. We are fully confident we can succeed and will develop an incredible game.

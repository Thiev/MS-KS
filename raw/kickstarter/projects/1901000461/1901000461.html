GRIDLOCK TACTICS is a grid and turn-based multiplayer tactical RPG that features several unique factions in a biopunk universe.

COMMUNIQU DE PRESSE EN FRANAIS ICI

Gridlock Tactics (working title) is a turn-by-turn RPG inspired by PlayStation era tactical games such as Final Fantasy Tactics, Disgaea and Front Mission. It also add moderns elements that can be found in more recent titles such as XCOM and Shadowrun Returns.

All actions, including movements and attacks, have range and radius based on a grid. This way, it is easier to visualize and carefully plan your moves.

If you are not familiar with the genre, think about a game of Chess in which you can assign special abilities to each of your characters. Also add terrain elevations, multiple playable factions, online multiplayer, stunning 3D environments and an extensive upgrade system to customize your team and you get a glimpse of what Gridlock Tactics is.

This turn-based goodness takes place in a biopunk universe in which:

  • Companies gained so much power that they dissolved the government and became independent states.
  • Science reached a point where it is easier and less troublesome to create humans in labs than having a child the old-fashioned way.
  • Essential resource supplies are dangerously low.
  • There are no laws to speak of.

Well, you get the idea. That's a nice little setting for turn-by-turn warfare :D

In Gridlock, there are several groups of humans, cyborgs, robots, creatures and aliens that all share the same values and visions. We call them Factions. Each of them operates in a really different matter. You could compare Factions to Races in Blizzards StarCraft. They all play differently.

This concept is really important and to put things into perspective, here are three factions planned for Gridlock Tactics:

THE FREE PEOPLE

Background

With the growing influence of big corporations and mutants clans, some pure humans decided to create a decentralized organization to defend whats left of them. There are several clans spread across the world that live in old buildings, caves and under viaducts.

The Research Lab

Humans create their own weaponry with the large amount of always-accessible and always-expanding sources of information found on the free open web. To upgrade the Free People's weapons and other objects, the player must research new technologies in a lab. To get new weapons, they must be bought from specialists on the black market.

Classes!

Humans can learn new things and thats what makes them stronger. The extensive class system makes it possible for one soldier to shoot sniper and being able to cure his friends. Another one may learn to hack doors while being really good at using a closed-range weapon. With the Free People, experience is the key. The more you fight, the stronger your fighters are until they reach their full potential.

TITAN CORP

Background

Titan Corp administrators have two main goals in mind: increasing profits and reaching new markets. To do that, they need loyal employees. To attract them, they promise to give all workers a place to live, military protection and health care. In return, they ask everyone to perform tasks that stand in line with the company vision.

Cyborgs... YEAH!

Titan Corp doesn't like employees that perform poorly. People who are not good enough for the company are turned into cyborgs and sent to the battlefield. Specific parts of their bodies are replaced by robotic add-ons to make them more efficient at fighting and a special chip is implanted into their brain to control their thoughts.

The Market

Titan Corp buys different kinds of weapons and parts from other big companies. The administrators use these parts to assemble new cyborgs assigned to specific jobs. By buying ultra-specialized products, cyborgs have better accuracy and raw power than humans. However, being controlled by a chip and relying on robotic parts to operate makes Titan Corp less versatile than the Free People.

THE DEZ EXPERIMENTS

Background

The Dez Experiments are mutants created in a biological laboratory owned by Dr Dez. The highly skilled doctor created those in order to protect himself and the result of his extensive studies. Some of the hybrids are created and assigned to maintenance tasks while others are sent outside to collect additional DNA sources.

DNA combinaisons

To create mutants, Dr Dez combines multiple sources of DNAs. This is a trial and error process, so some creatures may die straight after birth while others may prove being really successful at their job. Dr. Dez have several contacts to buy DNA sources from, but some sources are so rare that they cannot be bought and have to be harvested directly on the field.

Biological abilities

Mutants are not humans. They have biological special abilities not requiring any kind of external energy to be performed. Some may jump higher and move further than humans while others may see through the fog of war. They are not as versatile as the Free People and not as tough as the Titan Corp employees, but they are faster and sneakier.

Music is composed by Kim Derome, a music composer from Laval, Quebec (Canada). You can listen to other tracks she made onher website.

For the two tracks she had to create for Gridlock, Kim was influenced by video game soundtrackssuch as Deus Ex and Shadowrun Returns, but especially by the Mass Effect soundtracks. Thesound she was looking for was mainly orchestral, but with a touch of electro/sci-fi colors so itcould give something epic and biopunk.

COMBATS

  • Each combat last for approximately 15 minutes.
  • Turns are based on individual units (some units and actions are faster than others).
  • One turn equals to one movement and one action, similar to how Square Enixs Final Fantasy Tactics works.
  • Some abilities cannot be performed instantly and therefore, may be cancelled by an enemy action.
  • While not in the video, we plan on adding a fog of war and visibility cone system. Some units will have the ability to see further than others.

UPGRADES

At the end of a combat, you earn XP and money. The way you spend those rewards depends on the factions you choose to play. There are no real-money upgrades. Once you buy a faction (or use the free one), you have access to everything.

GAME MODES

Two main game modes exist in Gridlock: Asynchronous and Dynamic.

In Async mode, theres no time limit per turn, ideal for playing on the go. A player can play multiple Asynchronous games simultaneously.

In dynamic mode, theres a time limit, ideal for playing without having to wait for the other player. It is not possible to play against more than one player when in dynamic mode.

We also plan to include a hardcore mode in which your characters dies*permanently* if you do not revive them under a set amount of turns.

COMPETITIONS

Once a player has fully upgraded one faction, the faction becomes master. At this point, a competitive mode is unlocked. This is where we will put the majority of our efforts for balancing. There will be a ladder system and official competitions that will lead to real life prizes.

PLATFORMS

We plan to support as many platforms as possible, including computers, consoles, tablets and smartphones. The game as it is now runs smoothly on Windows, Mac, Linux and high-end tablets (iPad 4, Surface 2, Nexus 7, etc.). See the stretch goals to see how much we need to port the game to all platforms.

We want players to be able to play against each other regardless of the system they use. Since the game is turn-by-turn and does not require ultra-low latency, cross-platform matches are possible on all platforms were targeting.

RELEASE SCHEDULE

We are currently developing the game prototype. We plan on releasing Gridlock before the holidays of 2014. Pre-Alpha is planned for July and beta for early November of next year.

WHY MULTIPLAYER?

We would all like to include a single player offline mode to Gridlock. In fact, it's already spread in the stretch goals. Hiring a narrative designer, drawing a whole world and setting up an engaging story are quite expensive. Let's put it this way: We start with developing a solid multiplayer and we will see what will happen next.

WHY SHOULD WE TRUST PARABOLE?

At Parabole, weall have a well-established career. Many of us already shipped games to the market and know what it takes to reach this milestone. Moreover, we invested more than 20K $ in the project and we are planning on investing 20K $ more, mostly coming from the company profits and loans. To put it simply, if this project gets funded and falls, we all fall with it. Needless to say that we will work 20 hours a day before it happens.

WHY KICKSTARTER?

The game production costs is way higher than a 2D platformer or first person survival game. It requires a team of 10 people for about a year. A successful Kickstarter campaign will help us pushing the production forward. If we raise a substantial amount of money, we could even be able to self-publish it. If the campaign is successful but we don't have enough to get the game out there by ourselves, publishers and/or external investors will see the appeal and credibility of the game. The game will then have the necessary support to get released;and you will be able to play endless hours of multiplayer biopunk awesomeness.

WHY A PUBLISHER?

This is not really by choice. We realize that we need a lot of money and experience to ship this game. Publishers have money and know a lot about marketing a game, which is really important for a multiplayer title. To have this kind of expertise in-house would cost several hundred thousand dollars. Unless we raise an unbelievable amount of money here, we will have to deal with a publisher or some external investors.

WHY FREE-TO-PLAY?

The way we see it, there are only benefits in the model we're pushing here. You can buy the full game the old fashioned way if you want to, but you can also pay less to play only the faction that you like. For example, if Blizzard did the same with StarCraft, the Terran race would be free to play for everyone and you could pay 30$ to play the Zerg. In fact, they are slowly adopting this model since you can invite a friend to play the Terran for free if you want to.

Time and money:

Unless we raise an impressive amount of money, we may need to do business with a publisher and/or external investor(s) to push this game to the market. While we are confident that publishers will be opened to the idea, signing deals with those can take some time, and in our case, time is crucial.

If we do not raise enough to self-publish, here's our plan to get our game out there:

First of all, we will be attending both Montreal International Game Summit in November 2013 and Game Connection Europe in December 2013 to show the project to all possible partners. Since we know how to establish strategical partnerships and we have a business credibility, we think publishers will be more than opened to discuss a possible partnership for Gridlock, especially if the Kickstarter is on its way to success.

In other words: The only thing that might happen, if everything goes really bad, is to delay the launch of the game.

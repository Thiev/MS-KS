Make your own American or Canadian Football Video Game! OPEN SOURCE! No programming language required!

QUICK NOTE: As the page states below, the Gridiron GamePak is a software development set that allows you to create your own football game for PC. This is not a game. I repeat; THIS IS NOT A GAME :-) , this is a football game creation system. This is also not just an editor where you just edit things. The Gridiron GamePak is much better than that. With the Gridiron GamePak you can make a football game completely from scratch or by building off of the included examples in the Pak. 
 
All graphics you see on this page are just placeholders within the Pak used for demonstration purposes; you can take them out and add your own graphics. You can add your own 2D or 3D models to the game, create your own A.I., animations, sounds and more, with no programming knowledge required. If you can drap & drop, point & click and can follow simple instructional guides, you will love the Gridiron GamePak.
 
Please read all information on this page carefully, and if you have any questions please don't hesitate to contact me, as I would be more than happy to answer them.
 
Thank you for stopping by, and be sure to pledge. The initial Gridiron GamePak will only be released if we reach the pledge goal!
 
 
Best regards,

The Gridiron GamePak is a collection of EASY-TO-USE, drag-and-drop, point-and-click software starter sets for PC that allow you to create (not just edit) your own American (or Canadian) Football game; fully CUSTOMIZABLE, fully OPEN SOURCE,  and with absolutely NO difficult language programming required! Every play, whistle-to-whistle will be your own creation made the way YOU want it to be, the way you've always dreamed. You can create any league you want, with any number of teams you want, with no restrictions. You will be creating YOUR game. This is YOUR moment.

There was a time not long ago, that the Football video gaming genre was exciting. Each year, we would wait with baited breath to hear the details about the next big football game. Competition was fierce, and the hours of fun were incredible. It seemed like the dream would never end. But then it did.

In 2004—the greatest season that video game football has ever seen—it all came crashing down. Exclusive licensing and the resulting efforts killed the competitive balance, halted innovation, sent many of our favorite football franchises into early retirement, and turned our once seemingly indestructible dream into a nightmare. 8 years later, the nightmare hasn't stopped, and it keeps getting worse.

It's time for a change.

What if I told you that right now you have the power to change the course of football video gaming history? What if I told you that you can make your own football game and you didn't have to be a programmer, spend a fortune in resources, or have any special equipment? And what if I told you that all you have to do to see this change happen is pledge a few dollars? What would you do?

Hi, my name is D.M. Noyé; I'm an avid football gamer, and a well accomplished and experienced professional of various endeavors (see bio). Like you, I understand that football gaming is just not what it used to be. It used to be great.

To return to greatness, we need a change; but in order to see change occur WE must be that change... and we WILL be.

Instead of complaining year after year for one company to finally get it right only for us to be let down each time, or hoping for past legendary companies to return and save the day; the reality is that the only way for us to gain our pride back as football gamers is to stop fighting each other, start supporting each other, roll up our sleeves, and produce our own football games ourselves.

But How?

While succesfully making it through several stages of development for an indie football video game for XBOX 360/PC...

 

... I made future plans to give football gamers a way to develop their own football games. The goal was to create something that everyone could get involved in. Something everyone could afford. Something versatile. Something that everyone would find EASY TO LEARN. Something OPEN SOURCE. But more directly; something that would allow us to make our own football games so that we wouldn't have to continue to rely on the big companies to do what we want. Enter the Gridiron GamePak.

Allow me to ask you the following questions:

Do you want a customizable Real-Time Physics Engine?

Do you want progressive lighting?

Do you want progressive and variable weather?

Do you want the option to create in 2D or 3D?

Do you want interactive sidelines?

Do you want customizable AI?

Do you want to be able to create your own plays and playbooks?

Do you want in-game saves?

Do you want custom button-mapping?

Do you want tiered playcalling?

Do you want a fully-editable franchise mode?

Do you want custom stadium sounds?

Do you want 24 hour, 7 day a week development support?

Do you want to be able to sell your game ROYALTY-FREE?

If the answer to any of those questions is 'yes', then the Gridiron GamePak is what you've been waiting for! The Gridiron GamePak makes all of these features—and more—possible. And best of all, it's totally expandable allowing you to easily create your own features and add your own unique personality and style to your game.

Please look to the right column of this page. Notice that the pledge goal is only $25,000. It is a small amount, but it will ensure that the first version of the Gridiron GamePak is released to the public. That said, we need to deliver a crushing, finishing hit to our football gaming issue once and for all, and to do that it's going to require more resources.

I can do quite a bit on my own, but no man is an island. I have strong working relationships with some of the most talented indie developers around, but they won't walk away from their own projects and work with us for free. Because of time and valuable human capital, product development is not a cheap proposition especially when you want to produce the highest-quality product with all the bells and whistles we crave. 

The following information explains what reaching each goal level will allow us to implement into the Gridiron GamePak:

$25,000 Level (LOCKED) = Release of the initial Gridiron GamePak and all of its tutorial guides.

$50,000 Level (LOCKED) = All of the above, and the finalization development and release of the finished 2D Real-Time Physics Engine Set, plus a pack-in animation set that the whole community can use, and a Play-By-Play Commentary set.

$100,000 Level (LOCKED) = All of the above, and the finalization development and release of the finished 3D Engine Set, and the development and release of the finished Franchise Mode Set, and the development and release of the finished 3D Real-Time Physics Engine Set

$250,000 Level and beyond (LOCKED) = All of the above, and the development and release of various stadiums/fields, development and release of 3D player models, development and release of an Online Play Set, integration with mobile devices, integration with consoles (present and upcoming), and Pak's spanning multiple development platforms so that our base of indie football video game developers grows larger and larger, stronger and stronger, and more diversified:

Remember: The more you pledge beyond the goal, the stronger this gets. The ball is truly in your territory.

Because the games we'll all be creating can be developed as shared projects amongst the communities, what better way to advance the project and promote the spirit of community cooperation than to have a support base that is 100% by the gamers for the gamers? Kickstarter is the place to be.    

Simply put:

“There is no force so powerful as an idea whose time has come.”

- Everett Dirksen     

                  ... The time is now. Buckle your chinstrap.

FAQ

  • What are the minimum PC requirements?

    Windows XP/Vista/7, at least 128mb ram, 1 gig of hard drive space, 500 Mhz processor, and DirectX 9.

    In other words, if you have a computer made in at least the past decade or so, you should be all set to get started.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Is the Gridiron GamePak a game?

    No. As the information on the page states under "So what IS the Gridiron GamePak", this is an open source software development set that allows you to create your own football game for PC. All graphics seen on this page are placeholders. You can easily add your own 2D and 3D models and graphics.

    This is not just an editor, this is a total creation system.

    Last updated: Thu, Sep 6 2012 6:05 am EDT
  • Will it be hard to make my football game?

    Not at all. In fact, it will be much easier than you think. You won't be required to learn C, C++ or any other type of difficult programming language. The entire system is point, click, drag and drop with very minimal typing, and with the tutorial guides and 24/7 support you'll be off and running within minutes!

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • How do I make something using point & click, drag & drop, like A.I. for example?

    The video shows a bit of that. But for more, please take a look under the heading "So what is the Gridiron GamePak?" on this page. There you will find a free A.I. Quick Doc download with a step-by-step explanation that includes pictures, example editable code, and an example results video.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:14 pm EDT
  • How many files sets are in the Gridiron GamePak?

    As of this typing, there are currently 25 sets; a few are in Alpha, some are in Beta, all of them work well. However, in order to reach finalization, funding is necessary. The number of sets may increase or decrease slightly, depending on project need.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:10 pm EDT
  • How do I get access to the Alpha and Beta programs?

    Everyone who pledges to at least $25 level will automatically receive the Team Selection Program Beta. Each tester will be able to submit testing data to me directly. The data will be extremely helpful for the development of the final software sets.

    Pledging at higher levels will allow you to gain access to other early testing programs. Please see the pledge information on the page for more details.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can me and my friends work together on a football game?

    Yes! That's the beauty of it. You can build your own team of developers and create the game you want!

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • If I make my own football game, can I sell it?

    You sure can. And best of all, it's royalty-free. That means you get to keep 100% of the money from the sales of your game!

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can I make an NFL, CFL, NCAA, AFL, USFL, UFL game?

    You can make any kind of football game you want. Just keep in mind that fan-made products released as free novelties are fine, but if you intend to SELL a game that carries licensed properties you will get yourself into some legal hot water.

    The Gridiron GamePak is not intended to promote, encourage, or provide indie developers with the means to infringe on any existing copyrights, trademarks, or patents and any such enterprise is engaged in with the sole discretion and responsibility of the end-user.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 4:08 pm EDT
  • Will I be able to add play-by-play commentary to my game?

    Yes. I plan to develop a template and tutorial guide for this as well. Your pledges will help this get added to the Pak.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can I create my own plays?

    Yes! Go nuts!

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can I create my own playbooks?

    Yes, and you can save and load routes and assignments. Your pledges will help this set reach completion.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can I have progressive lighting and weather?

    Yes, and you can control how it advances down to the very millisecond.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Are there snow and rain effects?

    Yes, very nice ones. They are included in the Pak.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can I have custom stadium sounds?

    Yes. A new Pak set will be developed for this with your pledging support.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can I import and export files?

    Yes. You can import and export: .wav, .mp3, .jpg, .bmp, .wmv, .mpg, and .obj

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Can I use a gamepad/controller to play my game?

    You sure can. Currently, the kit supports the XBOX 360 controller.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • Will I be able to make a game as good as Madden or 2K Football?

    Your dedication to work on your game will determine how good it can be. Whether the game will be as good or better than games like Madden or 2K Football is a totally subjective thing, but if you put in the time and effort and put quality first, you will give yourself the opportunity to make a fun and exciting game worth being proud of, and worth showing off to the public.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • What if I just want to make tech demo's to show proof of concept?

    That's perfectly fine. I understand that everyone may not want to make a full game, and for that purpose the GGPAK is set up in a way to be 100% scalable to your needs and goals. So if tech demo's are your goal, then you'll find it a very accessible task with this software set.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT
  • What if the Gridiron GamePak doesn't reach full funding?

    Then we'll all have to prepare for 8 more years of football gaming misery with no choice in the matter.

    Last updated: Fri, Sep 14 2012 3:08 pm EDT

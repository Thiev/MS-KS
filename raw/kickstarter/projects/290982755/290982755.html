Take out the zombies in this homage to the arcade light gun games from the 1990s. Inspired by Area 51 & T2: The Arcade Game

Some of my fondest memories come from the arcades of my youth, especially the light gun games I experienced there: Operation Wolf, Area 51, and Terminator 2: The Arcade Game.

At home I played Hogan’s Alley and Duck Hunt on my Nintendo Entertainment System.  As an adult I enjoyed this type of game again through the fantastic Nintendo Wii port of Ghost Squad.  

This is what Never Surrender aims to recreate, the fun, frantic, on-rails shooting experience that these games pioneered!


The game will be played with a mouse or trackpad.  While that might seem to go against the inspiration, I want to be realistic.  By standardizing a mouse for input for the gun crosshair, I can make the game accessible to all players across multiple platforms.  Part of the appeal of the light gun games was how accessible they were to all types of gamers, there was little need for tutorials and "how to play" walkthroughs.  Almost anyone was able to understand the premise and then pick up the gun and shoot the targets on the screen.

The three platforms that will be supported if the project is funded (not including stretch goals) are Windows, Mac OS X, and Ouya.

My 2 goals for Never Surrender are to:

  • Create a quick, arcade inspired, pick-up and play game accessible to all types of players.
  • Remind old-school gamers of the on-rails shooter genre and introduce this type of game to those who may not have experienced it before.
 

Who am I?

My name is Nathan Gilmore and I have been a working in the Information Technology field for over twenty years.  I am also a gamer and have spent the last two years learning the Unity3D game engine. I am ready to put these new skills along with my industry experience to use in creating a commercially viable game.  I hold two Master’s Degrees, one in IT Management and an MBA.  I bring up my education so you, my investors, know that I am familiar with business models and that in my professional life I manage extensively funded projects on a daily basis.

Rewards for backers:

The budget:

I consider this a business proposal and as such I am treating it that way. My costs are known and identified.  I know how many hours per week I can devote and how much work I can accomplish per hour. 

The budget for this game is low compared to other video game Kickstarters, but there are solid financial reasons for that.  First of all, I do not need living expenses.  Additionally, I do not need to buy any hardware.  Every dollar goes directly into the game.

Second, I am not an artist, I am a project manager and programmer and this game can be made on a smaller budget because I’m not going to re-invent the wheel when it comes to creating all of the art I need. I have identified and priced out 100% of the art assets that I need. These assets will give me about 85% of the set pieces for the game.  I am confident that I can create the remaining 15% myself.  As part of my preparation to build Never Surrender, I took the Jonathan Williamson Master Modeling Workshop in  January of 2013 and, as a result, I am capable of building the models that are not available.

While I will be coding the GUI, I have an agreement in place with a professional 2D artist to create GUI elements for this project. Licensing, plugins, sound, music, animation files are also identified and are within budget.

Further, the programming budget is low but the reason is that I am doing it myself.  The game programming piece of this project is something I can comfortably do in house, however, I do have a small amount reserved to purchase consulting hours from a Unity3D veteran programmer.  This individual has given me a great rate.

Given the information above, I have come up with a timeline of six months for this project and have added one additional month for possible delays. In this timeline, Never Surrender will be released in seven months, in July of 2014!

Stretch Goals:

If the project is successfully funded, any additional funds will be put towards the following:

Story:

Many light gun games did not have a lot of narrative, but I value story and, therefore, have a narrative arc written for Never Surrender.  The gamer will control a female Air Force pilot, Judy.  At the start of the game, she and her nephew William are returning from a hunting trip in northern Canada. Their plane is landing at Chicago O`Hare airport.

While they are disembarking all hell breaks loose as zombies swarm the plane. Aunt Judy grabs an Air Marshal’s gun to start blasting away. Eventually she and William make it to a light aircraft and escape. Game play begins when they run low on fuel and have to land in a farmer's field in Iowa.

Looking out across the field, they see a swarm of zombies coming towards the plane.  In the distance, a large farmhouse looms and they begin to fight their way to that oasis.

The game will take place in two major locations.

  • First, gamers will fight in the field toward the house. This is not a straight path and players will play through buildings, barns, and grain silos before they can make it to the farmhouse.
  • Second, gamers will fight inside the house as they clear out the undead so that Judy can access the radio to contact her Air Force base.

F.A.Q.

  • What about Wii U or PS4?  Could you make a real light gun game with their systems? I would love to see Never Surrender on those platforms and I have thought about how it would work, the control schemes etc.  However I am not going to over promise.  Once Never Surrender is finished and has been released to the Kickstarter backers, I will investigate other platforms.  I am focused on what I know I can achieve with this budget and schedule, I will look at additional platforms and alternative control schemes after that.
  • What about Xbox One?  It will be free for all Unity users soon! I'm aware of the Microsoft and Unity partnership. As with the and Wii U and PS4, I do not want to get ahead of myself and promise what I cannot guarantee.  A Kinect powered version of Never Surrender would be really slick, but I need to focus on Ouya, Mac OS X and Windows first.
  • If you are buying most of the art, how will you form a cohesive style? I am not going for a cartoon style game which is much more sensitive to different art style changes.  Additionally, while I am not an artist, I am more than proficient in modifying art so it looks like it goes together.  Most graphics will only need minor shader and texture coloring to fit the style.  Never Surrender will look great and play wonderfully.
  • When will the beta be out? -  I have written and documented all milestones for each week in order to stay on schedule.  The first beta would be released at the end of May, 2014.
  • Will you provide gamepad support? - The game feels and plays much more smoothly and naturally using a mouse.  However, I will include the ability to use a gamepad analog stick under game options.


Ouya!

I am participating in the Ouya Free the Games Fund. Ouya has a touchpad built into the stock controller. This will allow for very fast and accurate input that would not be possible using joysticks. If Never Surrender successfully hits the funding goal, Ouya will kick in additional funds that will allow me to create a version of Never Surrender for Ouya and bring a great on-rails shooting game to this platform. See their information below for further details.

Double Our Fun(ds)!

We're participating in OUYA's #FreeTheGames Fund, created by OUYA to support developers making new and creative console games. OUYA will match your pledge dollar-for-dollar up to your goal amount (and a max of $250,000), so help us get there and make a great game for everyone! To be eligible for this match, we commit that this game will be an OUYA exclusive for a period of time. To learn more about OUYA, go to www.OUYA.tv.

Risks:

Game development is a risky business in which many issues can arise. What I want to emphasize to you, potential investors, is that I have done the research up front to identify and minimize these risks.

I plan to run for political office someday and the Internet is a permanent record. Therefore, I do not want anything negative to come back and haunt me during a campaign. I will communicate to my backers on a regular basis during the course of development so they are kept in the loop on the state of the game.

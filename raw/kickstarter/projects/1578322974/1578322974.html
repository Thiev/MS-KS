The Story

Hello and welcome to my Kickstarter campaign for The Virtual Trillium Trail. A few years ago I founded a company called Virtual Field Trips, to bring this new type of computer game to market.

The purpose of The Virtual Trillium Trial (www.virtualtrilliumtrail.com) is to allow you to freely go and explore, and discover, an infinitely beautiful, expansive, natural world. Here you will have complete freedom of perception, freedom of movement, and freedom of inquiry.  Your free perception and your free choice are at the heart of the game’s design. You will not be controlled, framed, tracked, or profiled in this experience. It is as rich and as beautiful as the real world surrounding you, but open to inquiry and learning, and free of distracting man-made structures. You will be able to see this planet in its original state, and explore and learn to the limit of your intellectual curiosity.

Push yourself, you may be surprised, to find that you are infinite too.

The Virtual Trillium Trail is a game that is both fun and beautiful, and ideal for young children. Where you can go and explore as long as you like. Where you are free to go off-trail and into the woods to learn about any of the flowers, plants, and trees that spark your curiosity. Just go and find the little Fact Cards, or find any of the Forest Sprites that glitter and glow, which tell stories about the real wild world around us. Learn about the forest, ecology, watersheds, photosynthesis, and much, much more.  Or become a baby deer, a fawn, and run through the woods, or play in the stream, or jump down the waterfalls, or turn into a red tailed hawk and fly over the forest canopy to land on cliffs overlooking the pond, and see it all, for as long as you wish.

My name is, Maria C. R. Harrington.  I am a professor of computer science, an information scientist and an artist, and I believe that everyone wants to understand the infinite beauty and wonder found in nature.  I believe that all of us need beauty, truth, and freedom in our lives. I believe that everyone needs to experience the awe and wonder found on our planet Earth, and wants to understand the natural world.

The Virtual Trillium Trail, is only available on the PC ~ hardware-bound. But, if you want to help to bring it to the Web, for all to access, then please help with a pledge now!

Thank you very much!

~ Maria C. R. Harrington, Ph.D,

For teachers and parents

The Virtual Trillium Trail is a new type of computer game, one that crosses the boundaries into geo-spatial visualization and virtual reality.  It is an immersive learning simulation, and virtual environment of a real world field trip to a North American, deciduous forest and wildflower reserve. The content is based on the fourth grade science and ecology curriculum aligned with the Pennsylvania Department of Education Ecology standards, and informal learning from real world field trips offered by the Audubon Society of Western PA.  There are about 20 concepts, 20 plants and about 1,000 facts.

The original prototype software was part of a Ph.D. dissertation on intrinsic learning in simulations and virtual environments where it showed about 40% learning gains, student enjoyment, and parental approval.

Teachers can deploy and use the software in many ways. Ideal for the fourth grade student, it is age appropriate for children from second grade up to seventh grade. The teachers can act as a guide, while the virtual world is projected on to a screen for the entire class to see, or the software can be installed in a PC computer lab where the students can explore independently at their own pace. Empirical tests show learning gains close to 40% in the independent, free and open exploration mode in only one hour. If structure is desired, teachers can give students a map and assign a treasure hunt to find facts and concepts. Moreover, it can be used before and or after a real field trip, to more than double learning in the real field trip, thus providing a higher economic value from real field trip.

At home, parents can choose to purchase it for the same cost as other non-educational, entertainment video and computer games, but know that their children will be entertained and also learn. Look for a new computer in 2012 with the 1 GB dedicated graphics card (GPU) such as a NVIDIA or AMD, and the 4 GB RAM, and which will run this game quite well. Other options are to upgrade older computers with adequate graphics cards and RAM necessary to run the application.

The Virtual Trillium Trail is a product of Virtual Field Trips, LLC. It is an immersive learning simulation and virtual environment built on high-end computer game technologies, positioned to become a new type of virtual reality game for learning in school or at home. Not your typical computer game, but a realistic virtual world infused with educational content, where the learner can explore anywhere, and inquire on anything of interest, to the breadth and depth of their desire.

What it is you are trying to do?

Make it playable online! It was developed for the PC and released to market last year, (PCs with the high-end graphics cards).  However, neither schools nor elementary aged children have such machines.  Yes, I could “port” it, or really re-develop it for the Mac, and then again for the iPad, which they have, but then I would lose some of the power and beauty found in the photorealistic renderings of wonderful natural places, those types of places that inspire a feeling of awe and wonder, curiosity, and an intrinsic desire to explore and learn. Virtual Field Trips is about creating aesthetic and emotionally engaging experiences for independent explorers and learners. I am trying to raise funds, to deliver this high quality game to schools, and to children who do not have PCs with high end graphics cards.  They have told me that they want it in a Web browser over the Internet.

How the funds will be used?

$50,000 will be used to fund the online version of The Virtual Trillium Trail  v.2.0, and to pilot test the software delivered online by September 20, 2012.

  • Get the game online on the web and playable in a web browser.
  • Help to build the user accounts web site for Virtual Field Trips, LLC.
  • Help to improve the game.  New and better trees. A more elegant way of dealing with the “edge of the world.” A better GUI and introduction to the help menu.
  • More plants and animals.
  • Fund some testing time for the Alpha and Beta testers.
  • Fund some time for student virtual field trips.
  • This should kickstart the project; get it out there into people’s hands.

Any amount raised above the target amount will go into product development and quality.

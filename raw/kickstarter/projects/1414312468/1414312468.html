DUEL OF HEROES PROJECT

           Duel of Heroes: Chronicles of the Lost Planet, is a free to play online trading card game, combining elements of MMORPG, fantasy and strategy to create a rich and immersing gaming experience for everyone. 

Duel of Heroes MAIN GOAL

To be the first game that combines the elements of TCG games along with the elements of MMORPG in an open world with a real RPG interface as in retro - JRPG, so it is not just snapshots along with some dialogues that don’t give the correct feel of MMORPG, it is a complete 2D game world in which you have your own character, move, chat and interact with all the world features as NPCs, Quests, Heroes, Bosses, trading and PvP with others.

And for the first time ever Duel of Heroes introduces Clan War mechanics in a trading card game in which you use only your Hero and only the best of your cards, to fight alongside with your friends in Clan vs Clan War to become the Castle King claiming dominion over several territories of Allaxia’s world.

Our Aim:

  • Introducing a unique game experience based on fantasy created world, specially designed for Duel of Heroes.
  • Offering an easy to play addicting game that suits both the casual and the hardcore gamers.
  • Featuring new systems that has not been offered before in any trading card game and fusing them correctly within the game as Guild Wars, Card trading and Capturing bosses.
  • Making the game play customizable, to the extent of feeling like each gamer has created his\her own game.

And as our goals stretched we can achieve an ultimate goal of:

  • Introducing cross platform TCG online gaming through Facebook, iOS and android offering unbreakable gaming experience anytime everywhere.

DoH features unique sets of cards with dazzling graphics and unique architecture. The uniqueness of DoH lies in its innovative game play and dueling mechanics that are designed thoroughly to provide players with the freedom to choose and customize their own in-game experience. The game will be designed for all ages and all backgrounds, for the expert gamer and ones still starting their gaming life. One more amazing aspect of DoH is that it is based on a - rich fantasy - manga background story  created exclusively by the game creators to enrich the experience, so you get double the fun playing the game and uncovering the story behind it.

We intend to make a game that has many unique game features which are not supported in any other TCG.

DoH game mechanics are carefully designed and developed to make all the great features of the game count. They are also designed to make the game easy to play but not so easy to master. Remember, you will need to use your skills!

The following is a breakdown of the game mechanics:

  • Heroes not only guide and control your army; they also have the ability to attack as well as raise and lower the morals of the attacking or defending characters and creatures.
  • Our Unique Heroes have the ability to evolve during the battle by drawing the correct cards and using them to upgrade your hero to gain more power than ever.
  • Heroes are your own personification on the battlefield, they have attack and health attributes and once their health drops to zero your hero is destroyed and you automatically lose the battle.
  • Heroes stand at the back line of the battlefield and can only be attacked once your side of the battlefield is clear of all characters and creatures.
  • Each card will have a command points label determining how many command points are needed to be able to play the card.
  • The higher the class of the card the more command points it will need in order to play it.
  • Monster cards will have attack and defence attributes.
  • Each player will start with 5 cards at hand and a Hero of level 1 on the battlefield.
  • Normally, each turn the player will get to draw 1 card from their deck except for special situations where you will draw more than 1 card or will not be able to draw at all.
  • The player can play as many cards from their hand at one turn as long as they have enough command points.
  • The player will get command points each turn determined by the turn number that he is in, example: on the first turn the player gets 1 command point and on the second turn the player gets 2 command points...etc.
  • Command points, if not used, get accumulated till your next turn.
  • The Deck or "your army" will consist of 40 cards of your choice with at least 1 hero card of level 1.
  • Players can fuse two cards into a new one outside of the battlefield to strengthen their deck.
  • Magic cards are put face down until the player chooses to activate them.

The Battlefield: [#UPDATED]

Because you will spend most of your time on a DoH battlefield, we have made sure to put together a battlefield structure that will make things easier for you to focus on battle tactics and game strategy. We have also made the battlefield dynamic and eye pleasing.

The Structure:

  • The Deck: The 40-card deck you start with, contains at least 1 hero card of level 1.
  • Hero Card: The hero card is your beating heart on the field. Heroes will strengthen your troops, use magic and execute your strategies in battle.
  • Creatures Cards: There are 5 of them; they are the front battle line. As you know by now, there are 8 types of attributes for creature cards, each with a different interaction with Heroes, Magic cards, other creature cards as well as the battle environment.
  • Magic Cards: These cards mainly support your hero and creatures, and will be a large part of your personal strategy.
  • Graveyard: Where your fallen creatures and used magic cards are sent. You can bring back some of your fallen troops back using special magic and techniques.
  • Hand Pool: These are the cards that are drawn into your hand. They are drawn at random, but keep in mind, luck is not the only thing that wins battles.
  • Player Avatar: Your avatar image, which can be chosen from a variety according to your class, gender, and personal preference.
  • Health Bar: Your life points which can only be attacked when you have no troops in the field, or through special techniques. Your health points are directly linked to your hero’s health points, so be careful and protect him/her well.
  • Command Bar: Where you can switch to different game phases.
  • Command Points Circle: This is to show how much accumulated command points you have over the consecutive turns within the duel.

Resources system:

Each turn you will get “X” resources where “X” is the turn number, and the unused resources will be carried over to the next turn. This results in not having issues with resources as the game uses the automatic resource system (ARS). With the resource carrying over system, we have carefully adjusted the overall card pool’s casting cost to maintain the balance. DoH will have a higher resource curve that varies according to the turn count, so it is totally different from other dTCGs. This will enable you to include many high cost cards in your deck rather than including just limited number of high cost cards against several low cost cards.This will also give players the chance to cast Bigger and Stronger creatures and magical spells that can change the tide of the battle, making battles fierce.

Heroes :- [#UPDATED]


-There are ten types of heroes. Each hero can evolve to their ultimate form at level 5:

SABER MASTER

They are agile and powerful melee warriors. The royal guardians of Allaxia.  Saber Masters are one of the three knight classes along with Lancer and Archer.

ARCHER

Archers are proficient with projectile weapons and can survive longer due to their large range attack.  Archers are one of the three knight classes along with Lancer and Saber Master. 

LANCER 

Lancers are very agile and skilled with long-range melee weapons, such as spears, lances, etc. Lancers are one of the three knight classes along with Saber Master and Archer.

CASTER 

Casters are the most skilled magicians of the world.  Casters use the magic of Allaxia, in order to protect the kingdom and the whole planet. 

ADEPT 

Adepts are a  support type magicians that use their abilities in order to enhance the powers of their comrades and help them recover from their injuries.

ASSASSIN 

They work in the shadows and rarely show their faces.  Their special ability is "Presence Concealment", which allows them to remain undetected. Offset by their stealth is their weak combat skills. Due to this; assassins usually attack from the back and target individuals rather than group fight. 

BERSERKER

They have high brutal strength but are unable to control their powers fully which sometimes may result in losing their sanity and losing control over themselves giving chaos.

AVENGER

They are the manifestation of evil of humans that where betrayed, treated with injustice and abused. Fueled by their anger their motive is to take revenge.

VAMPIRE 

They gain super strength by sucking the blood out of other creatures, they have tendencies to attack people in the night and suck their blood, while they have weakness towards the sun light. 

SUMMONER

They have the ability to summon creatures to their aid in battle; they use their summoning abilities in order to take advantage over their enemies by surrounding them. 

- Each of the heroes will have their own effects that will increase as they evolve. 

Creatures:-  [#UPDATED]


DoH has 8 main attributes to indicate the origin of each card:

Duel of Heroes has a vast fantasy world which is rich from different attributes, to the different regions and territories of the world. This allows an offering of hundreds of creatures from different origins.

Fire

Fire monsters tend to focus on inflicting effect damage on the opponents Hero's health points and stacking up attack points when they are on the field together. 

Water 

Water is a very versatile Attribute and because of their versatility, Water monsters can be used in a number of strategic ways, possessing many different abilities. 

Earth 

Earth monsters are powerful cards focused on both brute strength and rock-solid defense, they rely less upon special effects to win the day, but they often enhance their combat abilities. 

Thunder

Thunder monsters represent the anger of the gods; they have the ability to paralyze any monster in front of them for a whole turn on the turn the thunder card is summoned. 

Dark

Dark cards often focus on strong creatures with outstanding special effects that can turn the tide of battle, most effects are related to dead corpses and the Graveyard, by using them as a resource in some way. 

Light

Most light cards focus on overwhelming offensive power, but some focus on positively reinforcing the hero instead of directly damaging the opponent, by increasing their Health Points or drawing extra cards from Deck. 

Wind

Wind monsters are often flying monsters, they often make use of their aerial advantage over other creatures in order to launch attacks from above and surprise their opponents. 

Metal

Metal monsters represent the power of the underground, they are made of steel. Be aware of their traps as they have the ability to reflect a portion of your attack to your monsters.

Heroes evolution:

Hero Evolution is not a new concept, but it is addressed with a different approach in DoH where you can have your hero level up through 5 different phases and Heroes do participate in the battles themselves.  In order to evolve your hero, you need to draw the higher level version of the hero and have enough resource “Command Points” to cast it.  The level up is not just simple from level 1 to level 5 directly in other TCG’s, but there will be up to 5 levels for each of current 10 heroes where you need to evolve them step by step, through each battle.

This will give the player a strategic choice in the deck they will use where it is not a necessity to level up your hero and you can just use a level 1 Hero and strengthen your deck using creature or magical cards. Also you can use different heroes in the same deck to make use of their different effects and powers, you can exchange your current hero on the battlefield with another hero OF THE SAME LEVEL from your hand without having to pay command points. While you can play a hero that is one level higher than the current one, but you are still required to pay the command points as normal.

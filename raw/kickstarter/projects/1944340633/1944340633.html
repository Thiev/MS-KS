Basic Information

Tales Of Tameria: Dawning Light is a 2d game that mixes some RPG, Simulation and Adventure game elements. The games world was developed in a light fantasy novel I am currently writing/creating.

The Game follows The path of your character which can be either an Elf, Dwarf or Human. You will also have a chance to choose a back-story for your character, different races have different choices for their backstory  Some of these backstory choices will play a part in the game later on. Once you have made your choices for a race and backstory an in game cutscene (currently an alpha scene) will play and you'll begin your game on an old road that leads to a tavern. The tavern will play a major role at the beginning of the game, this is where you will accept quests and talk to the more story important characters. The end goal of the game is to have a fort/village that you have built up from scratch.

The subtitle also holds substance. Dawning Light, the dawn of a game, world and character. It is the first in a series of games that are being thought upon. This first entry is more about life, the choices that it presents to us, the decisions we ultimately have to make and the hardships that can come from the choices made. To go into what all this means for the game would be to ruin the experience for people that may decide to play this title. Have faith though that the decisions made in game will range from affecting how the world areas can look, how other characters will treat you and in other ways. For example, a man needs food to feed himself and you have enough food to spare, do you help him or let him starve? Knowing that if you allow him to starve it would mean his death. That choice and consequence would be down to you. These are the kinds of things you'll be faced with in this game. However, the next game that takes place in Tameria could be an entirely different style which could be based more on story than life.

Further Information

The game takes place in the land of Avaskir, one of three countries that will in the future come to be known as "The Three Great Nations". However this is before that time, this is during the period where the first king has established his right to rule. You are no one of importance, a new and unsure traveler to the lands of Avaskir. Your journey will start as a humble beginning and gradually change to a more prominent role in due time. You will do quests for locals to build up trust and gain a reputation for yourself so you can advance. Eventually you'll depart and find your own place in the world. For whatever reasons you will build a house, that will eventually turn into a village and then a fort. However, what waits for you in between and after this is another story.

The choices you make from gender, race and other various things in the game will have an impact in the world of Tameria..

What will the money that funds the project be used for?

The money that is raised will be used to fund a soundtrack, to an extent (A certain number of tracks). We emailed a professional based website that can do music for the game, the only problem is they are pricey. I have already gone over budget with the current sprites/tilesets/music currently in the game and unfortunately I can't afford to put anymore of my own money into the project.The reason why music is important is because it is a big part of the game. As this is a 2d game it would lean more towards music for immersion. The best way to do this is to hire a professional that can do the job.

If stretch goals are met the money would also be used to hire graphic artists to create more appealing menus (I'm not an artist, hence the current menus look) and possibly add further animations to the sprite sheets so more depth can be placed on actions the player does. A simple example would be the player cutting wood with an axe (This would only happen if enough money is left over).

Possibly hire some programmers if enough money is left over to create systems such as a battle system. At the moment no real battle system has been planned, there's one in the game I just haven't made it functional. However depending on the route that the game takes it may not have need for such a system. Although I have my own idea for one that I may do myself, however this would take up production time and extend the length of time needed for the game to be completed. I would rather hire someone to make a real time battle system.

Stretch Goals

The main goal is to reach £1000 for the purpose of hiring a professional to compose a soundtrack and possibly the things listed above. However if the project reaches further these stretch goals will be able to go ahead too

£1200 - Hire artists to add additional animations to the sprite sheets. Such as wood cutting, smithing and such.

£1400 - Hire additional programmers to make systems to increase production time

£1800 - Hire artists to create more indepth graphics and art for menus.

£2000 - Replace the entire soundtrack with a more unique and powerful soundtrack. (This would allow me to release the entire sound track without the license issues I have with the current in-game music).

When Will The Game Be Released?

Good question. The game is planned for a 2015 release. This however could change and either be pushed forward or backwards. Currently the features are being made, added, tweaked and in their final stages are either kept or cut from the game. Once this stage is complete the rest of the game should progress and at a steady rate. However, please keep in mind that 2015 is a placeholder release year and as stated above could very well be sooner or later than 2015.

Final Thoughts (Jerry, Jerry, Jerry!)

Please keep in mind when backing this project that this game is still early in development but there is an early build we are working on. The game is not designed to be an epic RPG, it's more about life, the choices we make and the consequences of our decisions. This is a simple idea for a simple game that we wanted to bring to life.

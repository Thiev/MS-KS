The Great Brain Adventure is a single player mobile game with puzzle, adventure and social gaming elements.

The Great Brain Adventure is a mobile game for Apple and Android. It is a single player game with puzzle (trivia based), adventure and social gaming aspects. It is driven by a heartwarming story and takes place in a colorful, funny, and a strangely familiar fictional world incrementally revealed to the player.

The game is an attempt by the developer to combine great entertainment and education. Entertainment by great game play, funny stories, lively characters, and fun music. Education is achieved by profound lessons and trivia based play of varying difficulty on various topics.

The game is also an attempt by the developer to try to address issues in the gaming industry such as games lacking in female role models or the lack of "minorities". The game's greatest message is the importance of family and team work.

The game's estimated release date is approximately 4-5 months after the successful raising of funds.

The best way to describe the game play is that it is a combination of the adventure elements seen in Candy Crush, the story and character development seen in Wizard of Oz, and the puzzle elements of games like QuizUp, Are You Smarter Than A 5th Grader, Who Wants To Be A Millionaire and other trivia based games.

Here is a list of key features that should be expected in the game when released:

  • Emotional Fighting System

    Both hero and villain are designed to express multiple emotions during stage battles.

  • Dynamic and Interactive

    Every single part of the game has some varying level of interactivity.

  • Fully Developed Stories

    One of the appeal of the game is its rich story full of drama, comedy and out of this world ridiculousness and each character has some story to tell.

  • Digital Comic

    A digital comic for the 4 intros and the 5 endings is part of the game to help tell the story.

  • Secret Ending
    There are four endings per main hero. But there's even a secret ending reserved for the most dedicated players!

  • Expansive World

    16 worlds to visit and 11 stages for each!

  • Brain Melting Experience

    While the game is designed to be fun and it also meant to be challenging. The game has multiple modes designed to either entertain or fully test the players intelligence.

  • Exciting Sounds and Humming Inducing Music
    The game is packed with awesome sound effects and music so good that you'll find yourself humming the music of the game.

  • Trophy - Brain Ranking System
    The game has a trophy system that ranks the player brain against another player's brain based on literally how smart they are.

  • Collect Stars
    There are 2112 stars to find. The more you have, the higher your rank is.

  • Facebook Integration
    If you have friends playing the game, the player will be able to see where they might be in the game - like a race of sorts.

  • Memorable Characters
    4 heroes to play and over 32 characters to meet and beat!
  • Player Statistics
    So you know where you are doing well and where you need to improve.
  • Mail and Gift
    A built in mail system allows the player to send gifts to friends to help them on their journey.
  • Power-Ups and Market Place
    Optional power-ups of 6 kinds can be earned or bought from a market place.

POSSIBLE FEATURES / STRETCH GOALS: The features below are items that I hope to be able to add if the budget permits. If I am able to exceed the pledge goal significantly, I should be able to add more features like the ones below!

  • Multiplayer Mode
    This is a feature that I am exploring at the moment as a way to make the game even more fun and immersing. In the future, I would like the player to be able to challenge any random person anywhere in the world who is playing the game. This means custom avatars and a possible revised points system to be able to determine who is truly the smartest in the world.

  • Custom Characters
    While the game is being developed as a single player game, I would like to be able to make possible that the player is able to make their own custom character to play in the game.
  • Play As The Villain
    Sometimes the villains are way cooler than the heroes. I think it's because they're bad. Another feature I would like the player to be able to do is play the villain.
  • Advanced Mail and Gifting System
    The current mail and gift system is quite simple. In the next version, I would like to be able for any player to send any player power-ups that they have in excess or don't want through the game's mail system.     I think this is the ultimate way to help out a friend who may have a trigger happy finger for power-ups.
  • Versions beyond Apple and Android
    I would really want everyone to be able to play this game. So if possible, I hope I can release a Windows Mobile version and maybe even release it on consoles or for various PC OSs.

Hi everyone! I've loved video games since my first try at Super Mario Brothers back in the glorious 80s. Since then, I've always thought that it would be a cool job to someday be involved in making a video game. But one decision lead to another and as most stories go, we end up doing something different from what we dreamed of doing when we were young. So one day, I told myself that I have to try and decided to stop everything so I can focus on making my own game. Shortly after, I founded an independent mobile game studio called "Sneaky Bruno Games" and decided that The Great Brain Adventure should be its first project. The Great Brain Adventure comes from a list of 8 game concepts I have created. I chose it simply because of the great potential that I see in its story, characters, and planned features.

So for 8 months now, I have been immersed in game development. I spent thousands on books, online seminars, interviewed game developers and picked the brain of basically anyone who was in the know so that I can properly research my project. I spent even more on artwork by hiring experienced artists. When I couldn't find the help I needed, I even decided to learn the tools myself stopping only when the limits of what I can do is reached. I then took what I learned and created a 135 page game blueprint and wrote a 38 page business plan.

So I hope that what you have seen has gotten you excited and inspired to help me release my first game of many that I would love to make for all to play and enjoy. Thank you all in advance!

-Patrick Gatdula (Founder of Sneaky Bruno Games)

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Risk 1:
Having the game able to run on a variety of mobile devices.

Remedy:
I plan to hire the most experienced programmers to ensure that the game is coded properly this means that it is optimized so that it can run on old or new devices which vary in hardware power. Additionally, extensive beta testing is part of the budget and schedule to ensure that the game functions well on as many devices as possible.

Risk 2:
I listed a lot of features and these are my minimum goals as an effort to make it appealing to gamers. But there is always something gamers want and I might anger them if I miss it.

Remedy:
Being active on message boards, email, and social media pages like our Facebook, Twitter and Instagram accounts to get the feedback I need to improve on the game as an effort to address everyones concerns or at the very least deliver on my promises.

Risk 3:
Not making the planned release date on time.

Remedy:
Ive been planning for over 8 months to ensure this does not happen. I gave more than enough time to ensure that programmers are given the time they need to create the game properly. There is even enough slack for beta testing and a strong chance to even release ahead of time. Plus, a lot of the tedious and time consuming parts of the game are complete such as the art so meeting the planned release should be possible!

Risk 5:
Not getting the game on distribution services such as Google Play and the super picky Apple Store.

Remedy:
The programmers I plan to hire have solid experience when it comes to handling Googles and even Apples requirements. So we have already planned ahead of time to ensure that the game follows these guidelines to the dot making it less likely for Apple or Google to deny us.

Risk 6:
Getting physical rewards shipped out correctly.

Remedy:
Ive worked as a project manager before from working for ad agencies and Ive had a lot of fulfillment experiences. I have set up a supply chain so everything is nicely organized and ready to go.

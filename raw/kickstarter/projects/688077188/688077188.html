The St. Christopher's private school, known across the UK for its prestigious staff, its high test results, its generous scholarships and its breathtaking architecture, has been seized by its student body. Roughly two hundred teenagers (and even a few primary schoolers), led by the charismatic and headstrong Roger Spencer, have taken their own campus by storm, staging a lock-in protest against severe new financial rulings set forth nation-wide by the Ministry of Education. Barricaded within the building and facing pressure from the police, the government and the media, the young St. Christopher's crusaders are going to find out how quickly things can go from bad to worse...

The St. Christopher's School Lockdown (working title, referred to as St. Chris from here on out) is a point-and-click adventure game, reminiscent in style of the Sierra and LucasArts glory days, and will be released initially for PCs, Macs and Linux, and if the demand calls for it (and our finances allow it), ported to mobile platforms.

This is an interactive story on a grand scale of seven progressive episodes. It is heavily inspired by recent events in both the United Kingdom (01, 02, 03) and in Chile (01, 02, 03), involving massive student demonstrations, rallies and riots. We invite you to do a little bit of reading into it, as it's a fascinating subject, and will help give a great deal of insight into the game's setting and political climate.

Now, let's talk gaming. Right from the get-go, we want to promise you:
-- NO meaningless fetch quests!
-- NO pixel hunts!
-- NO juggling eight hundred inventory items and combining them until you hit upon that one perfect puzzle solution by sheer accident!
-- NO deadly boring "find the key for this locked door" puzzles!
-- NO mazes!

St. Chris is a product of love, put together by folks who know that at the heart of the best video games lies, first and foremost, a damn good story. We might remember The Secret of Monkey Island for its ingenious puzzles involving rubber chickens with pulleys in the middle, but the reason it holds a special place in our hearts is due to its utterly memorable characters, its vivid setting, and pitch-perfect writing. And we hope to live up to the standards set by adventure game icons such as Al Lowe, Jane Jensen, Tim Schafer, Ron Gilbert, and Corey and Lori Ann Cole.

If you missed the link up above, we want to invite you to go ahead and visit our WEB SITE. (DISCLAIMER: As we are constantly updating the web site right now, please excuse any temporary technical malfunctions. Trust us, we are on it.)

Our goal is to release St. Chris in seven installments. In each of the first six chapters, you will play as a different character, each caught within the protest for their own personal reasons. From a runaway suffering from bipolar disorder to a ten-year-old who manages his own pawn shop, you will be forced to make difficult moral decisions, further their personal agenda, and try to make the best of a bad situation! Decisions will carry on from one episode to the next-- a minor action you might have taken as one character in the second episode might have dramatic consequences for the character in the fifth one, for instance. As for who you will be playing as in the seventh chapter? Well, we don't want to spoil the fun, do we?

(Let us state here-- our current goal will only cover the demo and the pilot episode! See the Goals and Timeline section, as well as the Stretch Goals section down below!)

-- A plot inspired by very current and ongoing political events!
-- Hand-drawn characters and moody environments in a comic book art style!
-- Build relationships between characters! Forge and break alliances! Find love! Spread gossip!
-- Puzzles! Dialogue puzzles! Mini-game puzzles! Gadget puzzles! Computer hacking puzzles! Even a puzzle where you have to play drunken charades in the dark! Yes, really!
-- Teenage hijinx!
-- Optional side quests and achievements!
-- An in-world collectible voodoo doll trading game, featuring a stat-based battle system!
-- Roleplaying elements, including stats, reputation and a morality gauge!
-- Puppies!
-- Hours upon hours of gameplay-- we're shooting for something in the realm of 40+ hours! (Although not PER episode, of course; come on, we're not THAT crazy.)
-- Exclamation points!!!

This is an ambitious project. However, if we meet our funding goal, that would allow us to devote 100% of our time to the development of this game, and to spend the resources on the highest quality software and assets to make it a truly polished and elegant product.

Now, let us state that the funds we are currently asking are only going to cover the playable demo and the pilot episode. That is as much as we can promise with the amount we are currently requesting, and all subsequent episodes will continue to be financed by donations, sales of the first episode, and personal investments from the staff! As much as we wish we could promise you that our current goal would fund all seven episodes, that would be unrealistic.

The donations we are asking for will go into development of the in-game artwork, hand-drawn animations and environments required to put together a playable demo and the pilot chapter. We'll also be able to pay our composer to record a professional, original soundtrack, we'll be able to afford royalty-free sound effects, fonts and extra graphical assets. Any donation, no matter how small, could end up making a world of difference. Our aim is to release a playable demo in Q2 of 2014, and the full first episode in Q4 of 2014, with each consecutive episode release coming out every four or five months.

However... We like to be optimistic, so, continue onwards to the next section!

LANEY BERRY -- As creator, character and sprite artist and lead designer, The St. Christopher's School Lockdown is basically Laney Berry's brainchild. Whether it be character concept art, dialogue scripting or puzzle design, Laney is in charge of making sure it all fits together into one seamless product. Laney is a detail-oriented multitasker, and she holds a degree in Visual Communications from the Savannah College of Art and Design. She is originally from the United Kingdom.

ALEJANDRA ROJAS -- Alejandra Rojas is a published author, environment and game designer from Chile, currently based in the US, and has independently developed several of her own casual puzzle and arcade games. She really enjoys digitally evoking imaginary worlds. She very much believes in the narrative of this project and the potential transition of a great story into even greater game mechanics. She is in charge of backgrounds, visual assets, and the scripting required by the game engine.

GREG CASSEL -- Greg Cassel is an essayist, nature photographer, multimedia artist and fantasy world designer. He has a BFA in Film and New Media Art, and is absolutely nuts about blazing new trails for interactive digital media. He’s also somewhat crazy about social theory and activism, but let’s not get into that. He’s our creative content editor and test target for voodoo curses.

KARA QUEEN -- Kara Queen is a jack of all trades who is helping to round out the team by offering with her skills in art, marketing, dialogue/script supervising, media production, and by giving rounds of high fives for moral support. Another graduate of the Savannah College of Art in Design, Kara currently resides in Seattle with ambitions to draw (a lot!) and make independent comics (hopefully a lot!). She thrives on visual art as a whole, good storytelling, black coffee, and she can name every single one of the original Pokemon.

DERIK STILLER -- Derik Stiller grew up with the music of the 8 and 16-bit video game eras, and embraced its aesthetic and philosophies. As composer, he will contribute atmospheric backing tracks in the style of classic PC adventure and role-playing games, as well as thematic musical elements. Derik plays multiple instruments, including keyboards, bass and drums, and will be adding his performances to the soundtrack recordings.

JON BERRY -- As co-writer and overall video game enthusiast, Jon Berry pipes in to help deliver punchy and often redundant dialogue. Battle-hardened from innumerable play-throughs of the old-school Sierra point-and-click collections, Jon has become a severe and unforgiving critic of the genre. With his degree in Film and Television from the Savannah College of Art and Design, Jon's background in script-writing comes into play tenfold.

OKOSAN -- As team mascot, Okosan enjoys eating salami, taking long walks in the park, sleeping on piles of dirty clothes, and caring for his human, Laney.

We are massive believers in the independent game scene, and we genuinely wish we could currently do more to support it, but it's rough when we're in a similar boat ourselves! That is why KickingItForward.org is such a genius idea, and we are proud to announce that we will be donating 5% of our overall profits from the sales of our finished game towards other burgeoning Kickstarter projects.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

When it comes to any independent game project, there are always cartloads of risks. However, we promise you that this game WILL be completed. The basic concept has been fermenting within the darkest recesses of Laney Berry's mind throughout most of her university career, and it just wouldn't let go until she decided to make it a reality (despite her father's best efforts to have her get a "real job" in something like dentistry, accounting or upholstery).

The biggest risk is certainly the fact that this is our first major game. Our current release timeline is only an estimate, especially beyond the first installment. However, our co-creator Alejandra Rojas is a published author in South America and Europe, with five books to her name, and she is gleefully putting her professional reputation on the line here. The rest of us aren't quite as accomplished yet, but we are acutely aware that our creative industry reputations depend on this project. So, while we recognize that there might be delays, we assure you that we will work absurdly hard to avoid them. If we reach our minimum goal, production of the demo and full pilot episode will become our full-time job. And, fingers crossed, if we meet any of the stretch goals for further episodes, new features and other goodies, we will be working EXCLUSIVELY on releasing them to you as soon as we possibly can.

Other obvious potential risks involve situations we have no control over-- the possibility of severe illnesses, family emergencies, meteor impacts... In that respect, we can simply promise to keep you all in the loop in regards to any potential delays. Pinky swear.

FAQ

  • Why British pounds, instead of dollars? It says right there that you are located in Savannah, GA.

    Yes! We are currently based in Savannah, but, alas, I am not an American citizen. In other words, I don't have a social security number, which is required to set up an Amazon Payments account for a dollar-based Kickstarter, so instead, I am using my British bank account for this, which means British currency.

    We are aware that it is somewhat harder for British campaigns than it is for ones based in the USA, but we still have faith that we can pull this off regardless!

    Last updated: Fri, Jun 28 2013 3:46 pm EDT
  • £30 seems like quite the pricy sum for the full game. What year do you think this is, 1996?

    YES. I mean, no. What you have to understand is that, while we do consider each "release" to be one episode, each "episode" is going to be a stand-alone game on its own terms, offering roughly ten hours of gameplay, individual minigames, its own protagonist, quests and side quests, soundtrack, etc. In the words of team member Greg Cassel: "First of all, you're crazy. And second of all, it's like a double-game!"

    So, multiply that by seven, and you will realize why we are asking for the amount we are. £9000 is, in fact, not much at all when it comes to making even ONE episode-- really! Check out our stretch goals! You have to factor in the software purchases, the license for the various art and sound assets, the time we will be spending working on this exclusively instead of at a paid full-time job... It adds up, and quickly!

    Also, keep in mind that £30 also gets you an "in" into the developers' inner circle, where you will be able to interact with us, offer input and advice, ask us "Are we there yet?" on a regular basis, etc, as well as a shout-out in the game credits, web site, etc.

    I hope this clarifies our thought process a little bit, and if you have any further questions, do drop us a line! We are always very happy to hear from you!

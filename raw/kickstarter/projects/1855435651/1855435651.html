A Video Game Reviewers Paradise​, Protected from loss of income due to Copyright Infringement Claims.. backed by Swedish Laws

This project has been floating around my head for years, i loved watching reviewers on the Web, didnt matter what it was at that time i just loved the way people put their own spin on explaining and showing off a product, if you think about it there is a art to it.. to do it right and keep you coming back for more, i want to to give more back to the reviewers that every one really enjoys including my self, the aim of this project is to put more more money in the hands of reviewers that really go the extra mile and try to not only extend a little out to the community but also have us hooked on their work

Most if the Backing i will get will go to paying a company i am in contact with that will be building the website for me from the ground up, after all the Non-Digital rewards are put together and shipped out i will use the remaining on server rent to make sure that the server is kept online a while to allow it to grow as much as it can, i am open to any comments you may have, at the bottom of the page you will see my twitter name, you can follow me and tweet me, i have it linked to my phone as well as kickstarter so will be keeping a close eye on what is going on and will be able to answer any questions any one may have.

The Hosting company i will be using is located in Sweden that way it is protected by Swedish Laws, no one will be able to bring down videos with out a fight.. they wont take money out of any of the uploaders on our website and if it really comes down to it.. it will be removed but we wont bend right away we will fight for people to make reviews of a game. that is what i am aiming for, if there any claims the owner of the video review will not lose any income during the time that the claim is being taken care of and disputed on between My self, the reviewer and the Company or group filing the claim with my ISP

The way i plan on doing that is simple, At the beginning of each month all the videos uploaded will give 100% of their income to paying the server's rent, after the server is fully paid off for the month the reviewers will take 80% from each video watched for the rest of the month and the website will keep the other 20%, i think that 80% after the server is fully paid for is a great way to give back to the reviewers, this will allow them to have more cash flow to invest in games to make reviews for, be able to buy audio tracks, sound affects they need or been wanting to get but were not able on limited income they were making from other video steaming websites, on top of that there is one reviewer i watch all the time, in one of his recent vlog posts he had a great idea that if we can all agree on would stop all of the Copyright infringement claims floating around if all else fails.. we will have to do some thing like what AngryJoe talked about in one of his videos, if a reviewer uses a 5, 10, 15~ clip of a song and they complain about it to the ISP i will be using and they come to me and tell me remove the content or you will be suspended i will not make any one remove any thing, i wont let them take your income, this is some thing i feel is totally wrong, playing a clip of a song in a video of a review that is gaining a lot of views and promoting not only the game.. but that sound clip as well, in the end a company will try and flex their "Guns" to just push others around because they can, one way i aim to make this into a quick fix if i am not able to calm the claim reports down is going to be included in our own video player for the site, since you would have to edit the video in a 3rd party program on your PC and then re-upload it onto your account with any edits and corrections, i will add a few editing tools that will allow you to cover up a sound clip that can't be used or some sort of logo a company may be claiming, the way i want to try and do this is with multiple layers in the video player that would allow content creators to completely block out of view any thing has claims on it, also for sound i want to work a way to be able to isolate a part of the video to play a different sound clip, some thing that wont kill the reviewer's vision when they were working on the video, that way they wouldn't have to have to fix and re-upload if they didn't want to

This project can be as big as every one working on it wants to make it.. that includes uploaders/reviewers and how interactive the community is with each other and the way we all experience it, i my self am ready to take this project as high as it needs to get and i am in no way going to let it stop ever if i can help it

I think that with the help of every one who enjoys the reviewers they watch wouldn't want them to go away if they can't keep gaining income like they were before, and why not help them make more income to not only give you a better show, but give them that extra money for the hard work they put into each video

One thing i would like to add is like i said before i feel that this project can grow into some thing big, not only big time reviewers will be allowed on the website.. at the beginning yes because like a house the site needs a strong foundation to sit on, after the site has grown to a level i think its time.. i will have video events for new reviewers to try their luck to be accepted into the community as a fully time up-loader, every few months we will have a event like this, maybe 3 to 5 times a year to bring in fresh blood into the uploaders community, i don't plan on ever making uploading a thing for every one as that would kill this whole idea, the idea of this project is to bring all the best reviewers on the web into one place where there the only videos you will find is ones that will be interesting to the gaming community, by doing so, more videos that will get views.. less clunky videos that just taking up space because they are not watched.. just costing the owner(s) extra money forcing them to give a much smaller % to the person who does all the hard work bribing in a large crowd... speaking about some thing else before i end this, if this works out the way i see it in my mind we can even have a annual gathering every one who is apart of the community can come together face to face and enjoy live shows and so much more, my question to you now is.... did you pack your sunglasses?? because the light glowing off this finished project is going to blind you its that epic.. Any way as a last thing i want to do before ending.. i want to reach out to to reviewers all over the web, i want to talk to you about your thoughts about what you just read.. what are you thoughts or commands i really would like to know

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

First of all is gaining trust... i have to gain trust from not only people helping me fund the project, but also the reviewers that i won't try any thing shifty or any thing like that, this is why when they sign up to be a uploader to the site and i review their Application i will give them a contract protecting them from any loss of income on my end, and will do my best to protect their income from claims filed by company's, sadly that cant always be helped some company's will not stop till they win, but what i can do is keep in contact with all uploaders to the site, keeping them updated on any thing that is happening having to do with updates to the websites and when they be gaining their 80% each month, i have two ways i want to do this, not only if they log into their private uploader's HUB that will give them stats on each of their videos, witch ones have been watched and how many times this month, who is sharing their videos and to where, who is giving the video props, on this same page they will see a Number in Red that will be going down as video are being watched when it gets to 0.00 USD$ it will turn green and will set the split to its normal 80/20, i am also going to have Android and iPhone app that uploaders can download for free off their HUB page that when logged into it on the phone, it will give them a push notice when they are gaining income from their videos for the current month, i want them to know how much is needed to cover the cost of the server for the month when ever they want, i will try to keep it updating the info every 15 minutes on the HUB, thou on the mobile Apps they will know the moment it hits the Green.

Talking about what else is in the contract, it will be written in plain text and easy to understand, it will state that they are the owners of their videos, they upload videos to my service for monetary gain and such should be respected, if a problem does happen as nothing is perfect i will get in contact with every one and explain the problem and keep them updated till it is fixed, this sort of thing does not need to be in the eyes of the public unless it has a big impact on the service, i will try to fix any bugs within my system asap and return any lost funds to the correct reviewer.. after all that is why they will be able to see how many videos are have been watched in the current month, if such a bug in the system were to happen and they are not seeing on their HUB that they income is growing the server will log when the income stopped being updated for that reviewer or all if its a big hiccup in the system, once it logs when the income stopped updating the view counter will split at the moment in witch the server can estimate the error started, and next to each video counter another one will start counting the number of views that they are gaining per video while not updating their account income, so they will know just how much their income should be adjusted to as soon as we find and fix the bug, i find that this is a simple way to treat a error like this in a calm way, i will do all in my power to make sure that they received what they are earned, after all this is their job, i wouldn't want my boss to come to me and tell me that my paycheck will be short this month.. so nor would they like that, i aim to have that never happen but have ways around it if it does occur, i wont share more of what the contract covers but it protects viewers more then it will protect me and they will be able to read and see that, i also invite them to bring a lawyer to read it for them beginning to end

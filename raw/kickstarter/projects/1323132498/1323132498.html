Derived from a Global Game Jam, we are creating Tetrapulse with fast-paced, lighthearted,cooperative multiplayer for friends andstrangers alike. Here's the scoop:

Gameplay:

Your planet is under attack by alien robo-bug invaders, but you and your friends have the power to stop them!

Destroy your enemies by shooting powerful energy out of your arm. The catch: The energy is also your own life force!

The Heartstone is your source of life and your ultimate tool. Grabbing onto it revitalizes your character.

When grabbing onto the Heartstone players move as a single unit. Work together to keep moving!

Use the Heartstone to activate your planet's defense beacons.

Activating all beacons in a single region will enable the planet's force field, effectively ridding the area of enemies.

Features

  • Your health and ammo are the same resource. Every shot you take depletes your energy.
  • Collective movementthat encourages cooperative play.
  • Identical views shared by each of thetwo to four playerswhetherlocalornetworked multiplayer.
  • Several different enemy types.
  • A variety of characters to play as and power ups to chose from.
  • Infinite replayability with use ofprocedurally generated levels.
  • Competitive online leaderboards and stats.
  • Developed in the Unity engine forPC(Windows),Mac, andLinux.

The Amiable is made up of five misfits that happened to find each other at DePaul University in Chicago. With our combined powers in break dancing, fashion design, acoustic guitar, and pole vaulting we banded together to make video games.

(from left to right)

Ricky Roberson: Ricky is the glue that holds our games together. He enjoys working in the grey area between art and programming. No one knows why he hangs out with us.

Peter Sheff:The most amiable of the bunch, Peter has a passion for making environment art and hand painted textures. You can often find him couch surfing at one of our apartments.

David Laskey: The only thing David loves more than programming is to work with his friends and meet awesome people wherever life takes him. In his spare time, he likes playing guitar, knitting, eating toast, and many other hobbies for which he doesn't have time.

David Finseth: Giving up the theatrical stage, David has committed to what was once a back-stage love affair with animation. When not bringing the game to life, he is heading our business venture with his entrepreneurial spirit.

While we've been developing Tetrapulse for about 7 months now, there is still a good amount of work to be done to make it a complete and polished game. We saw Kickstarter as a great way to get connected with awesome supporters and raise the money to finish the game as we envisioned.

With the your financial support, we will be able to add features that would otherwise be impossible for us to complete.We've been working out of our apartments and pockets with the hopes of bringing Tetrapulse to people all around the world, and can't wait to play the game with all of you!

Where are the Funds Going?

  • Networked Multiplayer

We want to get you connected with your friends. It's not always easy to get four people in one location to play a game. Whether you want to play with a friend down the street or across the country, being able to hook up online will make playing Tetrapulse more fun. We foresee both public and private lobbies that support the ability to join with multiple local players. We'll be using 20%-25% of our funding towards developing this online networked multiplayer.

  • Sound Designer

Another 20% of the funds will be put towards paying a wonderfully talented sound designer, Logan Wright. He's already created a few tracks for our trailer and we can't wait to see what he comes up with next!

Obviously a lot of risks go into a game development project. Hundreds of hours of work still need to be put into the game in order to bring you the best experience we can deliver. A big task that will need to be tackled will be getting fluid networked play into our game. FAQ

  • Will Tetrapulse have online multiplayer, or merely local (and LAN)?

    Tetrapulse will have online, local AND LAN multiplayer. We really want to make it easy to get into the game and play with friends and strangers alike.

    Last updated: Sun, Sep 15 2013 11:51 am EDT
  • Will your game be DRM free?

    Our goal with Tetrapulse is to reach as many users as possible. That means we'd like to be on as many forms of digital distribution that we can. I know plenty of people who aren't fans of Steam, but it is an attractive platform for us seeing as how many users we could reach.

    That being said, we're looking to be set up on Humble Store at the very least so if people desire a DRM free copy they could do so. We'd love to be on GOG, Desura, or any other platform where we manage to gain access. DRM is definitely not something we're looking to implement unless required by the specific platform.

    Last updated: Sun, Sep 15 2013 7:57 pm EDT
  • Have you thought of porting your Tetrapulse project on Ouya?

    We've talked about porting the game to platforms such as OUYA, but it isn't officially in our roadmap. Tetrapulse feels great to play on the couch with friends but may not run fast enough on OUYA. Again, it's something we've considered and are looking into.

    Last updated: Sun, Sep 15 2013 11:51 am EDT
  • I run a youtube/podcast/news site and would love to cover your game. How can we get in touch with you?

    We would love to be apart of your youtube/podcast/news site! Please contact us at info@theamiable.com so we can set something up.

    Last updated: Sun, Sep 15 2013 11:51 am EDT
  • Do you guys need another sound designer or composer?

    While we appreciate the offer, we've already found a very talented sound designer and composer to work with.

    Last updated: Sun, Sep 15 2013 7:57 pm EDT

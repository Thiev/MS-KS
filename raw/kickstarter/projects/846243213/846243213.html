Fyurien Studios is proud to start development work on our ambitious new action role playing real time strategy game The Legendary Defense of RivenStar (TLDoR). The game represents a significant shift in the way the player interacts with the game world. 

Traditional elements such as Action RPG and Fantasy RTS are combined with a dynamically changing, open world to create truly Legendary gameplay.

TLDoR looks to create a deeply immersing game world thru multipart, multi-themed stories and art design. While at the same time providing game play systems that are both easy to learn, yet in the end-game, hard to master. At Fyurien Studios we have a determined and sometimes scary dedication to game development, world building and storytelling. 

But we need your help to see our game become a reality. So, will you help us fight the hordes and begin… The Legendary Defense of RivenStar 

The Legendary Defense of RivenStar tells the story of the amazing world of RivenStar. It has, to the dismay of all, been conquered and corrupted by the forces of evil. 

Legend became lore, lore became myth and as time passed few remembered where the darkness came from. Hordes of monsters flood the plains and forests. In the deep caverns of the world massive creatures dwell in waiting. RivenStar is a dark and dangerous place, where peril is the norm and life struggles against the tide.

Your defense of RivenStar begins using a classic action RPG character. Build them up with experience and grow in strength, skill and equipment. Very quickly you realize that the world is both massive and open to you to explore. 

Not long into your adventure you are tasked with rebuilding the last remaining beacon of civilization. A torn down, battered stronghold from which you will caretake your heroes, build your city and grow your army. Real time strategy will require you to manage resources and defenses. Build armies and level up your heroes. 

It all starts and ends with your new found city. But be alert and weary of the calm, for as you try to defend RivenStar and your home city, hordes and monsters will fight to regain lost territory. But should you prevail thru will and courage, you will gain ground and free all manners of conquered cities and strongholds. 

Thru this legendary struggle you will be victorious and the world of RivenStar will be yours once again.

We are excited to share you with and in-depth look at the various parts of the game. Read on and learn more, but before you do, have a look at our quick walk-through of the game. Make sure to click HD in the video ;)

One aspect of the game that was a must from our original concept was to have a very large open world, but also one that would change with your actions. We wanted the game world to be a living, breathing part of the game that may be one way today, and completely different tomorrow. There are no loading zones, no invisible walls. 

Your actions change whole territories and influence how you play the game. 

We also wanted an element of a chess board on the game map, so that you don’t just mindlessly attack cities, strongholds and castles one after the other. We’ve never liked the wash, rinse, repeat type of game play. 

There has to be a reason and a strategy on a macro level for why you would attack one place over another. The order in which you attack and your timing is equally important. No great adventure and strategy game ever sent you directly to the end. No, you have to plan your attack. No simple hack and slash here. Game world strategy is a must.

Introducing the Legendary Defense System

As you begin your quest of reclaiming RivenStar you start your game by attacking, with your main Player Character (PC), and liberating small settlements and outposts. All ending in the liberation of your main kingdom and future home. 

From here you will move into an RTS game made and build up a minor group of fighters. This is done by gathering resource and building the required buildings. These fighters, will fall into the basic archetypes of Soldier, Archers & Mages. There are no set rules to how you build your initial army. Have all Mages, or mix Archers with Soldiers. 

It all depends on who, what or where you’ll be fighting next. These characters can then in turn be leveled up as well. You then set out to take on larger conquests, attacking full strongholds and fortresses. You slowly begin to move your way thru the map, eventually freeing up entire zones. 

And so continues the defense as you free up more and more parts of the map. Growing your army, your main character and your epic monsters (more on that a bit later).

Dynamically Generated Content

The game world, while keeping true to the narrative is both static and dynamic. It is static in that the main story line and gameplay follows your travels thru the open game world and is preset. Every time you play the game, the story dependent part of the world is the same. 

However, on the outskirts of the world map is the wilderness leading into the great unknown sea. It is this wilderness that is dynamically generated every time you start a new game. This is also the most dangerous part of the game. And, in true RPG fashion, it is the most rewarding. This is where you will find epic loot and more importantly, epic monsters.

When we say Traditional Action RPG, we mean hack and slash and cast, and run and jump and get blown up and blow things up, top down, hordes and area spells and chaos galore. When we say Role Playing Game, we mean stats and skills, leveling and growing your customized character. 

Your character will be a high fantasy archetype following in the footsteps of great lore like that of Lord of the Rings and other amazing novels. You will start the game with basic equipment and progress to find better loot, discover and learn new and amazing skills and grow in strength and destruction. 

There are in total 50 levels for your main character to go thru. Each level opens up new abilities and new skills.

As for the action. This is a real time action game, there is no pause, and there certainly is no luck involved. You are as good in the game world as you are with the mouse and keyboard in the real world. 

At times you will have to click carefully and strategize your moves and actions. Other times it will be an all-out fight with hordes of monsters coming from all directions. The controls are very easy to learn as is the game. 

How well you play it in the end, is really up to how much you, as the player level up. Your character can only do so much. As you play the game, you, the player, will, like it or not, level up as well.

At the core gameplay of TLDoR, are two very important elements. First, is the Action RPG mentioned above. And second, and equally important is the RTS gameplay. You will be able to seamlessly switch from ARPG to RTS and back. This will allow you to manage your kingdom just as you would in a traditional RTS sense. 

First and foremost you must create resource gatherers, or workers. Resources include, stone, wood, food, gems and metals. Your workers are then sent out by you to begin working at building up your stock. How fast resources are gathered depends on the equipment and levels of your workers. 

In time you will have enough stock to start building your town, city, castle and defenses. As time passes you will be able to upgrade every buildable structure from crude wood, to rough stone, to polished masonry, to iron structures and epic fortresses. 

Within the RTS game mode, you can also hire on your army. Train them, heal them and house them until the time they are needed and ready. Your army falls into three categories. 

  • Men at Arms – Consists of Soldiers, Archers and Mages. On a basic level you are able to improve their equipment, their training and basic skills. They are meant to serve as pawns and provide you with a force of numbers, and not necessarily specialized skill.

   • Hero at Arms – Are more advanced NPC’s that will fight shoulder to shoulder with you. They consist of classes like Knights, Assassins, Wizards and Warriors. They are able to wear special made armors, magical accessories and unique weapons. They can grow in level and strength much like your main character and develop into formidable allies. They are analogous to the main pieces on a chess board. Take care not to lose any of these characters, they are very important and take some time to develop.

  • Colossus at Arms – This is your end game NPC. It is huge, rare and incredibly powerful. You are only given one of them to play with per game. They consist of Dragons, Demons, Elemental Giants and the such. You must first defeat the monster before you can capture it. 

Once captured, each monster reverts back to its infantile state. So for example, should you defeat and capture an Ice Dragon, it will, upon being captured, turn into a dragons egg. You return it back to your city, build a dragon den and nurture it in to its full size. You are then able to use your dragon in combat. Destruction and mayhem await.

We’ve made sure to keep the RTS feature in line with traditional and standard RTS game play. There won’t be any surprises here. Just a full and complex real time strategy game.

RivenStar is full of monsters and bosses for you to battle. Some are part of the main quest and fall into the game narrative. Others are random and in the wilderness, waiting for you to capture them. There are in total 50 major bosses to fight and capture.

Each boss fight is part of a zone or area you happen to be in. Once arrived at the main boss, for example a Fire Elemental, you will be engaged in a long drawn out fight. There are important things to consider, like in this example, the type of element you are fighting against. 

As such you and your allies should prepare accordingly. Other such aspects of boss fights can be a flying boss, or one that is only affected by certain weapons or magic. Each boss will have their own tactics and methods of attack and defense.

But should you be up to the challenge and win your fight, you will be rewarded.  You will find gold and rare loot.  You and your allies will be given untold amounts of experience to grow in strength.  But most importantly you will be given the opportunity to capture the boss to have and to keep.  

Once captured, you can nurture and grow it up to epic proportions.  You can use the monster very early on in fights, though it may be a bit weak.  But with time and care on your part it will grow to be the strongest member of your army.

Part of building up an army is going to war with said army. You can attack in the open field, lay siege to towns and even bring down entire castles. The control method for your army is as follows. 

You have complete and direct control of your main player character, as well as your Colossus at Arms. You are given generalized control of your Hero’s at Arms, in that you can control movement and general attacks. However some elements will be missing. 

As for your Men at Arms, you have very generalized control. You instruct them to either attack or defend, a player character or location. Some basic elements, but as a whole they serve as your pawns in the game.

Each point of interest that you are attacking, for example a town, will have key objectives. Most involve destroying a town or castle fixture. Others may include holding an area for a specified amount of time from on-coming attackers. Still other objectives can be very specific like setting something ablaze or freezing it cold. 

Strategy and preparation are all very important components of the raid. However, once all of the key objectives are completed, you have successfully conquered and as such freed that location. You can then move on to your next adventure.

·Full Physics Engine, Destructible Environment. There is nothing more satisfying then blowing things up, destroying city structures and causing general mayhem where once was order and peace.   When you release a fireball at an unwitting goblin, not only will he be set ablaze, but he will be blow across the map, only to bounce and crash thru the environment.   

We plan on putting in a full physics engine and destructible environment for one reason and one reason only.   It’s like bubble wrap popping, it serves no purpose, but darn if it’s not satisfying. 

·300+ Hours of Single Player Game Play.  As you being your epic journey you will undoubtedly want to fight thru each part of the game to see it completion.  But then the end, as the cliché goes, is only the beginning.  With varying character classes to choose from and over 50 Colossus at Arms to capture, there is no telling how long it will take to complete it all.

·Beautifully Realized Game World.  We want you to remember the game as much for the gameplay as for its beauty and esthetics. 

·Complex Sound System. No game is complete without the proper sound track to go along with it.  Be it the wind blowing across the glacial ice with cracking icebergs below your feet, or the battle cries of a rushing horde, you will hear it in its full glory.

If the brains of a game is the logic built into the many thousands of lines of code. Then the heart and soul is undoubtedly the art and sound respectively. We recognize that a game that does not function is not a game worth making. 

So we put our biggest emphasis on great programming and great gameplay. But once that’s set, once you know the code is solid and does what you need. What’s left?

Art & Sound is what. It is the beauty of the game world that really draws you in. It’s the ability for our creative team to visualize and image a world, and then bring it to life. Its not unlike being able to share a dream with someone. Imagine waking up in the morning having just had a dream of a beautiful place with amazing colors and landscapes. 

Imagine now being able to share that with your friends. That’s what game design is really about. We can literally dream something one night, and the next day make it real in our game.

Sound then helps fully immerse you in the beauty of the game world.  It starts with proper ambiance setting the mood and feel of the game world.  Are you in a dark abandoned cemetery, or wondering within a beautiful magical forest.  The sound design needs to be able to help paint, as it were, the world you are in, but instead of using shapes and textures, using sounds and music.

We will let Carmen our Senior Artist tell you a bit about the art style she is going towards.

“I think the most important part about any art style is consistency in style.  Actually its important that the art communicate the theme and idea very clearly.  If you are for example in a haunted cemetery or town, everything from the lighting, to the fog, props and structures need to convince you that it is haunted.  

I believe there is a delicate balance between creating immersion and over doing it.  And while art in general is different things to different people I still think we are able to strike a nice balance and make a beautiful game.”

Rather then write about it, we’ll let Robert tell you about the sound design of The Legendary Defense of RivenStar.

THANKS AGAIN EVERYONE!

Wild Season is a Farming Simulation Game, inspired by the likes of Harvest Moon, Animal Crossing, Rune Factory and Thousand Arms. Although it shares many of the same concepts and mechanics, Wild Season differentiates itself in its approach to these same ideas.

The game revolves around the main character (You), suddenly coming into posession of a farm in a far away town, and the story of how you repair, build and expand it. However along the way you will experience the many distractions, including friends, enemies, love, heartbreak, marriage, winning, losing, discover some mysteries, and perhaps even discover the meaning of true evil.

What makes Wild Season Different from other similar games?

  • Character driven story-telling approach. Each character has a history, internal conflicts, issues and distinct personalities.
  • A more mature undertone: Unlike other games of this genre, we want characters to be deep, compelling and relatable. As such we have decided to take a slightly more mature approach with our storytelling, with characters having to deal with a range of issues, ranging from drinking problems to crisis of concience.
  • Its not just about making friends: Its not about achieving the highest level of friendship with everyone in the town to achieve rewards. If you just dont like a particular character you can make them your enemy!
  • Off-Duty Activities: Whilst running and mantaining the farm is important, we are also focusing strongly on activities available to the player when not on the farm, including activities (like dating, mystery solving, spying, learning to cook), jobs, entertainment & more social options. (with farm automation options later in the game to make it easier for those who get bored with the same routine after a few years).
  • Contextual relationship building: NPC's will notice when you come out for drinks in the evening, if you're antisocial, or when you barge into their houses and rooms at odd hours off the night (and when you do it too much!), or begin to stalk them.
  • Political System: Join the town council and help shape the direction of your town.
  • Use of technology. Use evolving technologies (tv/ cellphones, etc) to help manage and automate your farms & relationships.
  • Dog & Horse racing. Breed Dogs & Horses for races (yes more than one).

 The main character (You) lives in the big city! However due to some unfortunate circumstances you have lost your home and most of your possessions to the mafia. On your way home you pass by a realtor, and you see an amazing deal on an old farm not too far away. With no home and little chance to find somewhere else in the city you see this as a fresh start! An opportunity to get away from it all. You empty out your bank account, sell what remaining possessions you need to, and you buy the farm! 

The sales associate gives you keys and directions, and before you know it you're home, packing what you have left into your car (before the Mafia throw you out!), and you drive out to the farm!

However its not all smiles and roses, getting to the farm is not as easy as it seems, and when you arrive, the townsfolk are friendly but distant, the town seems to hide a secret, there is a dark feeling coming from the forest...

What to expect from our stretch goals

Developing a game is very much like a car. Although all cars get you from point A to point B, the experience between a coupe and a sports car is very different. The same can be applied to game development. Whilst with our current goal we feel we can make a great Wild Season. However engines can always be better & more detailed, stories can have more depth, & characters more stories. In other words, surpassing our initial funding goal does not necessarily mean there will be more features, but rather we will make the existing features even bigger and better!

We have structured our development process so that we can scale it up based on how much support we receive for the game. We have additional bachelors, bachelorettes, story lines, crops, animals, and different approaches for our engines, that require more time and programmers. We want to make the best possible game that we can and we want to put as much towards that as we can.

We're also looking at physical deliverables for backers, as well as expanding our publishing reach into more platforms such as the PS Vita, Xbox One,  PS4 & nintendo ecosystems and as part of our stretch plans. 

We will be publishing our stretch goals over the coming days in one of the updates.

Why Kickstarter?

Wild Season has been a labour of love of the team over the past few months. To this point I have been personally funding the development of the game and its assets, with a little help from my dad. Unfortunately my funds are running out, and I can no longer afford to support my passionate team and their families. We are confident that we can make a truly awesome game, and want to continue working on this full time, with no distractions. We hope with the support of kickstarter we can do this.

Wild Season is being developed by Quickfire Games, a small but passionate & dedicated team of game developer veterans based out of the Philippines.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

The challenge is to make a truly awesome, exciting and memorable experience, however as with any software projects, there are any number of issues that could lead to delays. Despite that, combined with our extensive experience in development, we have done our best to plan for any issues that come up.

Everyone on the team has had experience in development. Almost everyone in the team has come from a combination of software and game development backgrounds. We know what its like to ship products, and we have an excellent idea of what can cause problems and delays. The trick is to stay organized and keep going. (and inform your backers of course)

Come what may we are truly passionate about developing this. And we really hope you can help us along our dream to make a great studio by continuing to produce great games, that we're passionate about!

Explore secret chambers. Discover hidden tombs. And unravel an ancient Riddle entombed by the ancient Egyptians, in real-time 3D!

Riddle of the Sphinx is an epic Egyptian adventure game that takes the player through these compelling archaeological ruins and history of ancient Egypt while uncovering Biblical evidence answering age-old mysteries surrounding Giza Sphinx and Great Pyramid. Players of all ages have access to amazing life-like real-time 3D environments in this family-friendly archaeological adventure. (continued below)

Game Designer's Intro to Riddle..

Riddle of the Sphinx Screenshots

Major levels of the game involve exploring real-life archaeological finds, then taking those discoveries to the next level. Players assume their role as close friend and confidant of famous archaeologist Sir Gil Blythe Geoffreys as he excavates the site of the Sphinx. The tale begins as Sir Geoffreys realizes he has unwittingly unleashed an ancient curse while translating a sacred scroll. Sir Geoffreys dies, and the scroll mysteriously disappears.

The players task is to locate the missing scroll, finish Sir Geoffreys translation, and solve the time-worn puzzle before . . . well, we wont spoil the intricately woven plot.

But there's more! Riddle of the Sphinx is also unique is that we have woven real archaeological finds into the gameplay that have yet to be completed. You can be the first to complete these discoveries and solve the Riddle (see the Timeline video for a list of actual archaeological work in and around Giza).

Family-Friendly Adventure Game

What was most endearing to many of our fans is the family-friendly story, intellectual challenge, educational bonus, and Christian nature of Riddle of the Sphinx. We have so many testimonials of this, it was hard to choose one that reflected it well. Out of all of them, this one stood out the most...

Just how BIG is Riddle of the Sphinx?

Riddle of the Sphinx is HUGE! It really just keeps opening up into more and more environments and an incredible amount of exploration.

  • Explore around and IN the Great Sphinx
  • Explore INSIDE the Great Pyramid, including the existing Grand Gallery, King's Chamber, and Queen's Chamber.
  • These environments are exacting replicas of the real thing.
  • Over 50 million sq.ft. of detailed 3D environments
  • Over 100,000 sq.ft. of truly unlimited exploration

How accurate are the Sphinx & Great Pyramid models?

Riddle of the Sphinx was created after almost six years of detailed research, 3D modeling, and sculpting the perfect Egyptian adventure (for us, at least). The Sphinx and Great Pyramidwere modeled to exacting detail (inside and out). About the only item we did NOT include is the graffiti defacing the Pyramid's walls.

But there is MUCH more to just exploring the Sphinx and Great Pyramid in it's wonder and awe. We went further and turned all of the speculation of secret chambers and hidden tombs into an immersive reality and megalithic archaeological adventure game.

This is your opportunity to take your virtual tour of the last of the "Seven Wonders of the Ancient World".

How does Riddle 3D compare with the original?

Subtle dust, dynamic fire and smoke, hi-def real-time rendering, and more await you as you discover a whole new experience. Imagine sacred artifacts and rare treasures fully accessible for full examination. Imagine every detail at your fingertips!

ROTS 3D Features:

  • Unlimited exploration into almost every crevice of the myriad of detailed chambers and lush environments of ROTS.
  • 360 Explore & Point-n-Click Simulation modes: you can choose to explore freely or have the visual continuity of point-n-click.
  • Realistic Torches & Fire with smoke plumes
  • Realistic Water ripples, flowing rivers, and underwater scenes
  • Realistic Physics effects such as picking up objects, moving furniture and vases, opening doors, etc.
  • Dynamic Time of Day and Weather. Explore the Giza Plateau and other outside areas in all times of day or night.
  • Kick up dust that has been settling for millenia: Feel and see the dust kicked up in these ancient chambers. Of course, this can be disabled if youre an allergic archeologist :)
  • 3D Sound. With 3D sound, the sound will pan to match your direction.
  • 15th Anniversary Soundtrack! As an added bonus, Jeff Tobler, original ROTS soundtrack composer, will be composing new musical textures more organic in feel than the originally orchestrated style. You will be able to toggle between the original, the new, or no music.

The Genesis of Riddle of the Sphinx

It all started over 15 years ago, with Karen and I, and a dream. Inspired by MYST(congrats to Cyan's successful Kickstarter campaign for Obduction) and armed with the powerful 3d tools and beautiful rendering engine of Strata 3D software, we thought it would be so intriguing to be able to explore the Sphinx and Great Pyramid without any restrictions. We got excited about the possibility of discovering the oft-speculated hidden chambers, secret passageways, and lost treasures that many say exists deep within these enigmatic monuments.

After many years of building the a virtual Giza Plateau (and more fictional areas), Riddle of the Sphinx was finally unveiled and the response has been amazing! ROTS has sold way over half a million copies to date.

FAQ

  • Which platforms will ROTS real-time 3D support?

    We are definitely porting to Mac OS X, Windows, and Linux. ROTS 3D will either be ported to iOS (iPad most likely) and possibly Andriod tablets shortly afterward, or at the same time as the other platforms if it's feasible with our delivery date.

    Last updated: Tue, Apr 22 2014 10:02 pm EDT
  • What game engine is ROTS 3D using?

    We are very excited to be using Unity3d engine for our immersive real-time 3d port of Riddle of the Sphinx. It's beautiful as you can see in the demo video (above).

    Last updated: Tue, Apr 22 2014 10:09 pm EDT
  • What are the minimum system requirements for ROTS 3D?

    While we don't have those minimums just yet, we can tell you that ROTS real-time 3D should run on the lower-end machines just fine, as well as being very beautiful on the hi-end computers. We are optimizing every model and texture, including lighting, etc. But even so, Unity3D is an excellent engine and handles polygons and particles very efficiently!

    Last updated: Tue, Apr 22 2014 10:09 pm EDT
  • Will you provide a "point-n-click" option for navigation?

    We have had much interest in a point-n-click option from those who get seasick from real-time 3d navigation, and this is a planned feature of the game. You will have the option of freely exploring as you'd expect from a first-person 3d game, as well as a more fluid, controlled point-n-click exploration to both avoid any dizziness or seasickness, and to mimic the original experience.

    Last updated: Tue, Apr 22 2014 10:09 pm EDT
  • What ages are appropriate for playing Riddle of the Sphinx?

    ALL ages! This was one of the most amazing aspects of ROTS when it was released and we began hearing from so many who loved the game. Well, we heard from father son teams, where the son was young enough to be sitting on dad's lap. and retirees and housebound players were immensely enjoying the game.

    Riddle of the Sphinx is intentionally geared to be family friendly, and accessible to young and old alike. Some of the younger children may need help solving some of the puzzles, but the hint guide can help with that, too. Although what a treat for that child to have mom or dad with them as they explore these rich environments... together!

    Last updated: Thu, Apr 24 2014 9:06 am EDT
  • If only Episode I is fully funded, what happens to those who've pledged for the full game?

    This was a great question posed by one of our backers. The short answer is that all full game pledges will be honored. In other words, all who pledge for full game access WILL be able to play the full game even if we only fund for Episode I.

    So, if you can afford it, please consider pledging for any of the rewards that include the full game ($25 and above). It will help us get that much closer to completing the game and you that much closer to PLAYING the game :)

    The longer answer is that it may take us longer to complete the game only funding Episode I, but we are confident that with all of your help and/or even with pre-orders after the KS campaign, we will be able to complete the full real-time version sooner than later and get it in your hands. (Thanks KR for your question!)

    Last updated: Thu, Apr 24 2014 9:06 am EDT
  • Will Riddle of the Sphinx be DRM protected?

    The good news for honest people everywhere, is that ROTS 3D will NOT be DRM protected. This means a paying (or successfully pledging) user may make unlimited copies of the software for their own personal use. Making copies for friends or family, or to sell or giveaway is and will be strictly prohibited. Awesome news, huh?!

    Last updated: Sun, Apr 27 2014 9:26 am EDT
  • What does 'ROTS' stand for?

    ROTS became a favorite among the original supporters as the acronym for Riddle Of The Sphinx. It is more than just an acronym. Riddle of the Sphinx (ROTS) is a crusty, dusty, old tomb exploration game with mummies, and all sorts of ancient relics.

    Last updated: Sun, Apr 27 2014 9:26 am EDT
  • What's the difference between ROTS, ROTS HD, ROTS 3D, and ROTS 3?

    We understand how confusing this might be for those who are unfamiliar with the iterations and designations underway. Here's a short breakdown:

    ROTS - the original, point-n-click (still frames with limited animations) game released in 1999.

    ROTS HD - an optimized, hi-res version of the original point-n-click game (in the works and to be announced.)

    ROTS 3D - what this Kickstarter campaign is all about. A total remake of the original, but this time in real-time 3D. It's almost a whole new game while keeping the familiarity of the original intact.

    ROTS 3 - the third in the ROTS Franchise. Currently this game is name "Oracle Bones" and depending upon the success of this campaign, we plan on going on to release this and another game in development.

    Last updated: Sun, Apr 27 2014 9:26 am EDT

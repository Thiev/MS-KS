Combat of Road Rash, graphics/destruction of Split/Second, upgrading like MotorStorm, with a crazy speeding Zombie between your legs!

Running Rancid is a ¾ view racing game, which puts the player in control of a hungry ravenous zombie. With use of a specifically developed drug, these are no longer your run-of-the-mill ordinary zombies. These zombies can really move. Being that they have much more speed and are almost car like, these ‘special’ zombies were given a much more suiting name, Car-casses. With an ever vigilant crew of med-canics at your side, you gain various Car-cass and weapon upgrades. You must race, batter and abuse your fellow competitors in order to claim top ranks in this underground world. Car-cass upgrades help increase a range of features, such as control and handling to things like speed and aggression. The most unique feature about this game is that for the first time in a racing game, you can race anywhere. Imagine racing down a street, now imagine racing through an office or an apartment building. Utilizing elevators as shortcuts, or potentially as setbacks! Hop over fences and over desks, closing doors or jumping from roof top to roof top. This style of game brings in a new dimension and element yet to be introduced into the gaming world. The unique features designed into this game would also introduce a never seen before multi-platform gaming setup. Imagine sitting on the bus on your way home after school or work and pulling out your cell phone to play a mini-game that can directly affect a person who is playing RR on a console. This is multi-play like it has never been done before. (MPGI – Multiple Platform Gaming Integration) The gameplay elements are setup so that you can be at any skill level and still be able to progress, allowing the player to level up and access new upgrades and features. There are small mini games for the younger or more inexperienced, maybe perhaps just a significant other who wants to be able to join in the fun. 

“Yep….Zombies! Who hell knows where, why or even how. Probably some obsessed nut with a zombie apocalypse fetish. What the hell should I care…right? Unless I can make money, of course. After all I didn’t make this company what it is because of my good looks! Hell…even those were paid for!” – Mikel Ross. A mysterious infection that seemed to act much like the Ebola virus at first, suddenly started to sprout up in random patches throughout the world. Only what was estimated to be a few thousand were infected initially but it was soon discovered that if a fluidic transfer was made, then the victim would soon also turn into a ravenous and mentally irrational state. Thus the quickly adopted term ‘Zombification’ was used. Sitting high up in the safe haven of his skyscraper, multi-billionaire entertainment mogul Mikel Ross looked down at the streets below. Watching the people as they casually corralled and led some of the zombie stragglers to makeshift holding pens till a local quarantine group could arrive to remove them. That’s when he suddenly realized the idea of a life time. He could provide the entertainment the people always craved but because of the moral issues he could never fully provide. He called up his lawyers and together they found the loop hole he needed. He claimed that they were no longer living and thus were not entitled to their personal rights. He also included that they are also not officially deceased thus forgoing any automatic ownership of their intellectual properties. Meaning he could do anything he wished with these left over half dead human zombies. At least as long as his lawyers could keep the justice system tied up from deciding on a proper outcome. Next he needed to find a way to make them more ‘entertaining’! Looking through the paper he noticed an article about a doctor who claimed he could ‘hyper accelerate cells’ in the body, giving them increased strength and speed. Perfect! Now all he needed was the ‘hook’. He leaned forward in his chair, looking towards the street again. He watches as someone rambunctiously leaps out from the crowd on the back of one of the zombies, steering it by twisting and contorting its head. His legs flailing in all directions, riding this zombie like a mechanical bull ride, all the while trying not to get his hands accidently bitten. “Brilliant!” he exclaims, as he slowly sinks back into his chair. Now he travels around in his customized armoured ‘mobile entertainment unit’, the MEU from city to city, holding zombie races. Offering huge compensation for the families of the donated carcasses, as well as valued credits and services for dedicated racers. Now let's get on with the show…. Ladies and Gentlemen, it is time for a little Running Rancid! Let the race begin!

This project is a 'proof of concept' project. Meaning that it is not being developed to be a complete self sustained game, but rather a test platform for much grandeur things to come. Don't get me wrong, this first version will be a fully playable demo, and we will be adding content continuously. But our main goal is to build this base to perfect the technical side, making it much easier to add future content.

We would like to make this a true free-to-play title. A complete free-to-play title. What I mean by that, is that if a player so chooses, they can earn (with much work) all of the main assets in the game. Most of the pay items, if any, will remain as ascetic, non game advancing options. This lends to the difficulty of adding the complete game as a reward. However Alpha(s) and Beta(s) access codes will plentiful and rewarding.

With that out of the way, on to the juicy insides! Our focus is mainly on the PS4 however, we are developing with the possibility of a PC version as well. This will be determined by many factors relating to the licencing from Sony. 80% of the concept has been conceptualized for this project, and are already in development.

Now we have to keep some things as a surprise, however we can share some details of a few of the features.

Handling and Control
The Zombies will have a tenancy to be attracted to each other, the Riders and even the crowd on the side lines. So controls will be like a tug of war until you can upgrade your equipment. Such as tricycle handle bars grafted to the Zombies head, or maybe a bucket first to slightly blind their senses. Eventually you could have a combination or two to tweak the control to your play style.

Environmental elements will have direct affect on players, water, sand, mud and so on. This can be neutralized by purchasing various upgrades or increasing your skill set, like strength to power through deeper water faster. We have toyed with the idea of seasonal effects as well.
 
Tracks
The first level we have chosen, has been a suburb street setting. The reason for this, is that it is familiar and well...right out side our door. This saves us a lot of time researching for textures, sounds and even the layouts. Allowing us to focus more on the actual mechanics of the game. Here are just a few of the titles of some future levels, just close your eyes and imagine how some of these could look.
  • City Office Building
  • Forest Swamp
  • Mountain Mine
  • Hospital / Asylum
  • City Sky-rise
There is only two I can give any detail on, at the moment. The others are so great, it would be like if you received twenty bucks in a chocolate egg that you unwrapped from a Christmas present!

Meat Fishing
  • Players sit just outside the races edge (maybe in lawn chairs). They then cast out a hooked line, just like fishing. The goal is to hook or snag a passing racer, in order to change the races potential outcome.
  • As they gain experience they can utilize upgrades, such as bigger more elaborate rods and hooks or more effective bait.
  • They can maneuver to an available position, then set up to cast. Once the racers go by, then can then move to another location. 
The Dogg Pound
  • Players are put in control of rancid dogs. They are released a few minutes later and then chase down the racers and try to take them out. They can bark and snarl, jump and bite. Once they get a good grip on a racer, they must hold the ‘bite’ button down. They then have to wiggle the joystick (or six axis, or cell phone) in order to pull the riders carcass down stalling them, possibly changing the races outcome.
  • Could have possible short cuts and dog sized pathways to allow them to catch up and head off the race leaders. Paths can be triggered when races pass a certain point.
  • Can start as a small dog like a Pomeranian and slowly gain upgrades to bigger dogs like a Mastiff.
  • Dogs could rely on visual scent trails. (like AVP)
  • Racers can deploy meat, if they have any equipped, in order to try and distract the dogs.

After researching quite a few previous Canadian based Kickstarter projects, I have gathered up some important information. Because the Canadian KS does not accept Amazon payments, many people have been searching for ways around the need of Credit Cards. Unfortunately for most, the answer was found quite late in their campaign. Luckily I have managed to gather up a few viable options for you.

First is obviously the standard Credit Card.

A second option is something my bank had recently sent me. It is a debit card that can apparently be used online as a Credit Card, which just uses your regular debit account. This is a link to TD's version, be sure to inquire with your own personal bank to see if they offer a similar product. (http://www.tdcanadatrust.com/products-services/banking/electronic-banking/access-card/access-card.jsp#what-is-it)

The last one I had come across is what is called a Virtual Prepaid Card, I have found a site that seems to do this very thing. (http://www.payoneer.com/VirtualCard.aspx)

I had also read rumours that Paypal may offer a similar product, but have yet to track down information or a link explaining the details.

Another option that has come up a few times is the use of Pre-Paid Credit Cards. These are sold almost anywhere these days however it is said that there is a service charge of some sort of a dollar or two, be sure to have enough for your desired pledge, you can use the left over for some penny candies at the store! *smiles* Here is a link to a thread from Double Fine's experience with these cards. A huge thanks to Double Fine for this information. (http://www.doublefine.com/forums/viewthread/5981)

If anyone else has any information about payment options, please post it in the comment section for others to see.

This project is but the mere tip of the hidden and much larger ice burg. This is a proof of concept stage, to work out the bugs and solve the complex platform integration issues that we anticipate having. Which is why we have chosen to focus on the PS4. With the mobile, PSVita and PS4 already designed to become integrated with each other, backed by the extraordinary support offered by Sony, this was our best option to achieve the technical goals we have set.

The first track was chosen based mainly on the fact that we have direct access to it. So our research for textures, sounds and even layout, is simply solved by stepping out side. Future tracks will be much more engaging and extraordinary. This allows to focus, again, more on the technical side rather than wasting much needed time on basic development.

Aside from Sony's over whelming support, we have some of the most experienced people in the industry living in our city who are always eager and willing to help serious indie developers. We also have access to wonderful groups like Game Camp Edmonton, to help support us every step of the way.

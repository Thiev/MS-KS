Your spaceship had crash landed in Roswell, NM in 1947 and you have been held captive ever since. Now, YOU must make an Area 51 Escape.

                               AREA 51 ESCAPE

Prepare yourself for the most exciting and immersive realistic, 3D role-playing game of all time.  It will be up to you to achieve an "Area 51 Escape".

In 1947, an alien spacecraft crash landed in the farmlands of Roswell, New Mexico.  The remains of the craft and its inhabitants have been brought to a secret military base in the Nevada desert called Area 51.  For many years, people have heard rumors of what really goes on there.  Some say it's just a testing ground for secret military aircrafts.  Others say that there are dead aliens and broken UFOs that scientists are reverse engineering.  The truth is that one extraterrestrial survived the crash over 60 years ago and has broken free from its confines.  Your job is to become the alien and find whatever means it takes to maneuver through the layers of levels and labyrinth of buildings in order to find your spaceship so that you may finally leave the earth and return to your home planet.

This 3D, virtual reality, third person video game has all the realism to make you feel that you are inside the highly mysterious and secure location called Area 51. But beware, the military will do everything it can to keep you and the stolen technology for themselves.

You are the sole surviving alien of the famous Roswell crash in 1947.  It's up to you to traverse the many underground levels and the above ground field of Area 51 to find your crashed spaceship.  Once you have found the spaceship, you must repair what is needed to finally escape the confines of the earth.  All this must be done without being re-captured by the government.

Your adventure begins at the lowest underground level of Area 51.  In a dark and secure room, you must find your way out, but that is only the beginning.  Once you have escaped the only location that you have known for years, you will need the "tools" that you had when you first crashed.  As you locate your personal items, you must avoid being captured while, at the same time, locate the exit to each level and re-take your ship.

Each level consists of many buildings, rooms, objects, and clues that you must gather so that you may use them all in your escape.  Although you are being tracked by every person in Area 51, you will learn that you have certain abilities that give you an advantage over humans.  These abilities begin to return to you as you "re-generate" after escaping your confines.

Once you have reached the outside main level of Area 51, you must locate the proper hangar which has stored your spacecraft for many years.  Inside which is your familiar craft in a state of disarray.  Scientists have been taking it apart to use the technology for themselves.  It's up to you to repair and reassemble your craft so that you may get home safely.

Do you have the knowledge, cunning, and patience to make an "Area 51 Escape"?

"Area 51 Escape" has been designed to appeal to many intellectual levels and various ages of video game players.  From sheer cunning to pure adventure, "Area 51 Escape" is the type of game that will appeal to everyone that wants excitement in their entertainment.

The closest that you will ever get to the actual site.

No one really knows what is truly in Area 51.  It has been a mystery for many years.  Only recently has the government of the Untied States officially acknowledged the existence of Area 51, however, that is where it ends.

With satellite images, a lot of research, and an enormous amount of imagination, I have been reconstructing the entire base of Area 51.  From the lowest levels underground to the very hangars that are said to hold top secret aircraft development, "Area 51 Escape" contains the most realistic and detailed depiction of Area 51 that has ever been created to date.

Players will be convinced that they are actually walking through the most secret and mysterious location in the United States.  But be careful, you might be compelled to travel to Nevada and check it out for yourself.

The most important aspect of creating "Area 51 Escape" is to have the players forget about the real life they are living and immerse themselves into the world that has been created for them.  The players will grow strong sympathy for the alien creature that they are portraying and have a deep desire to escape the clutches of the United States Military.

In my games, I try very hard to include everything that would actually be seen, heard, and experienced, as if the players were actually in the games themselves.  Inside Area 51, I made it a point to include all the hallways, laboratories, living quarters, closets, medical gear, and, yes, even bathrooms.  This is why "Area 51 Escape" is created in a 3-dimensional, first person environment.  By doing so, I can give the players the most realistic feel that is possible in a video game.

When the players are running around Area 51 trying to find components, escape their confines, and fix operational systems, I expect them to feel all the emotions and anxieties that they would truly feel as if they were actually being hunted down and captured.

Since I can't include the senses of smell or touch, I work very hard on making the visuals and the sounds as real and emotion provoking as possible.  Fear, anxiety, dread, triumph, and even hopelessness are just a few of the emotions that I plan to evoke from the players with detailed graphics, sounds, music, and animations.

It is not always necessary to have "blood and guts" in every adventure or role playing game for the player to have an emotional stake in enjoying it.  That is why I purposely keep gore to an extreme minimum.  Because you, as the alien, are a peaceful explorer, don't have harsh weapons on your person and, therefore, have no intentions on killing your captors.  In the same vein, the military, whom is trying to capture you once again, want to keep you alive and does not use lethal weaponry or violent attacks.  "Area 51 Escape" is a thought provoking, puzzle solving, and emotional video game that should be enjoyed by all ages.

Tom was born in Long Island, New York during a blizzard. After many years of drawing as a child, he graduated from Long Island University, C.W. Post Campus with a Bachelors of Fine Arts degree in Art. Tom had the rare fortune to be personally trained by two of the best illustrators in the comic book industry: Frank Giacoia (Marvel Comics, DC Comics) and Joe Giella (DC Comics, Mary Worth Comic Strip). During his college years and beyond, Tom was a free-lance graphic designer, illustrator and cartoonist. Tom had also held the position of cartooning teacher at Hofstra University on Long Island for two years.

At a young age, Tom was fascinated with computers and video games.  His first computer was an Atari 800XL that he bought when he was 13 years old.  From that point on, Tom tinkered with many programming languages and different computer platforms.  Much of the art that Tom created was enhanced by the computer. It wasn't long until he wanted to combine his knowledge of computers with his artwork and imagination.

During Tom's second year of teaching art at Meadow Park Elementary School (1996), he had his first book published by F&W Publications under the subsidiary name of North Light Books. Tom's book is titled How to Create Action, Fantasy and Adventure Comics and is about all the aspects of creating comic books as well as how to draw super heroes.  Tom had won first prize for his art at the 2003 Charlotte County Public Schools' Art Teacher Art Show at the Arts & Humanities Council of Port Charlotte.

When Tom purchased his first PDA (Personal Digital Assistant), the Apple, Newton, he was hooked!  He knew he wanted to create programs for that brand new type of handheld computer.  He began to play around with different languages but it wasn't until his first Apple, iPhone when he became serious.

In October of 2010, Tom started his small, one man, business called, Zephyr Multimedia, LLC. and began to develop education apps for the iOS system.  Just being satisfied that he was creating apps was not enough.  Tom wanted to incorporate all of his education with his art and imagination to begin creating video games.  He wanted to bring the excitement of Dungeons & Dragons in with the technology of handheld devices to create a new type of first person, three-dimensional, adventure/puzzle game for everyone to enjoy.

Tom's first game is titled, "DungeonMaze" and is currently available in the App Store.

The second game Tom created is titled, "Escape the Graveyard", which is also available in the App Store.

Tom has always had a fascination with the unknown.  This, along with his great interest in life on other planets, has fueled his imagination and desire to create a new and unique video game, but he wanted to wait until he had more experience in game development before he tried to tackle such a large project.

The idea came to Tom one afternoon when he was watching a UFO documentary.  He thought, "That it would be great if there was a surviving alien that crashed in Roswell, New Mexico.  But wouldn't it want to return to its home planet?  It would be a shame if humanity was so selfish that it kept a creature against its will simply because humans wanted to know more about it."

Realizing that this was a viable idea to base a video game upon, Tom began to do research on similar subjects.  The market has been saturated with cutesie little alien games that jump all over the screen, or quite the opposite, realistic aliens attacking humans who, in return, must destroy them in the most grotesque ways.  But nothing has been created to fill the empty void.  Because Tom hit upon a niche that has yet to be covered, he thought this would be a perfect avenue to take his ideas and create a new video game.

We are in desperate need of a good quality game that brings the adventure and thought provoking puzzles to all gamers regardless of their age.  "Blood and guts" is not always needed to make an exciting video game.  "Area 51 Escape" brings together stunningly real graphics with emotional music and great challenges to make a remarkable experience no matter how many times you play.

With five apps under his belt, Tom is ready to create the best video game that he possibly can.  It's time for "Area 51 Escape" to be launched.

I have always been one to go above and beyond in all the work that I do.  There are extended goals that I have in mind if the contributions go beyond that of which I am requesting.  These features are not required for a great game, however, they would enhance the game beyond the point of the players' expectations.  Many times, as a game is in development, ideas pop into the heads of those involved.  It would be wonderful to be able to say, "Okay, let's put that in the game!" without worrying about the financial burden that is usually attached.  I do not want to promise any particular features will be included in the game and be forced to omit them later simply because the funding might not permit it.  Having said that, I plan to include anything and everything that is in my power to make "Area 51 Escape" the most incredible role-playing adventure game that I possibly can.

I chose to include my project on Kickstarter.com because I want to be able to create the best possible video game that I can.  In the past, I have done all aspects of development myself.  With the help of Kickstarter.com and you, I will be able to include other talented people and services that I wouldn't normally be able to afford.  My plans include advertising, purchasing prefabricated objects to enhance the game, updating and purchasing new software and hardware to help develop the game, and hiring other talented people to bring components to the game such as music, 3D graphic art, and programming.

My previous video games are: "DungeonMaze" and "Escape the Graveyard".  These two games, along with two educational apps I have created ("P.E. Games" and "Simple Perspective"), were all developed by myself.  I took on many hats for my past projects. I must say that I am very proud of my previous apps, however, I want to continue to grow and improve.

I must admit that, due to lack of funds in the past, I have not been able to properly promote my previous developments.  Even though I have four apps available in the App Store (and one in the Apple App Store) at this time, sales have been slow and few.  With the help of Kickstarter.com and YOU, I will not only be able to properly promote this game, but be able to develop and distribute it in other formats for many more players to enjoy.

Thank you so much for your consideration to help my latest video game development titled, "Area 51 Escape".  With your help and the help of Kickstarter.com, "Area 51 Escape" will fill an empty niche in the world of video games for those players who enjoy realistic, role playing adventures.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

There are many risks involved in creating a video game, however, having already created two educational apps and two video games, I am extremely confident that I will be able to bring this project to completion.

The biggest challenge that I foresee is hiring others to help me in the development of "Area 51 Escape". All the previous projects that I have created, I have done solely by myself. It's not that I don't trust others to create wonderful work, it has to do with releasing some of the responsibility to others. I have been in total control of all my previous developments and now I must rely on others to help me make a much better game than I could possibly create on my own. I truly believe in the people that I plan to hire to help me and, once I delegate work out to them, I will be much more comfortable focusing on what I need to do to bring "Escape Area 51" to the world.

Since I have several apps under my belt, there are very few challenges that I haven't already experienced. I expect the development of "Area 51 Escape" to be very smooth. If problems do arise, I will deal with them as I have in the past, by discussing it with the experts around me and working out the kinks until the project is, once again, smooth as glass.

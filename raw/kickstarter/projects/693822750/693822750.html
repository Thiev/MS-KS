THANK YOU!

20 years ago Mike Stragey and Chris Tremmel (me) began working on a game for the Sega Genesis at Interplay Productions. The only requirement was that the game needed to be both fun AND gross. The idea was to appeal to the "Garbage Pail Kids" generation.Several concepts were pitched to the bosses Brian Fargo and Alan Pavlish. Some were incredibly funny, some were incredibly offensive. From this primordial design ooze, Boogerman was born!

The game took roughly a year to develop and was released on the Sega Genesis in November, 1994. Initial reviews praised it as "a total gas!" and "An instant classic!"

For the last 20 years, Mike and myself have been busy programming and designing games for studios like Virgin Interactive, Crystal Dynamics, Electronic Arts, Activision, Insomniac Games, and Way Forward. Credited titles include Boogerman,The Lost Vikings, Robocop VS Terminator, Clay Fighter, Gex 2, Gex 3, Walt Disney World Racing, LotR The Two Towers, Return of the King, The Third Age, Kung Fu Panda, Fuse, Shantae, Contra 4, Shantae: Risky's Revenge and more!

We have talked about doing a new Boogerman for years and now thanks to Kickstarter, the timing feels perfect! Mike and I are now 100% dedicated to this project and with your help and support we will be able to "re-boot" the franchise and bring a brand new Boogerman to the latest and greatest gaming consoles for all to enjoy.

Its a dark and stormy night at Professor Stinkbaums lab where a machine has been built to save the world by trans-existing all pollution to a far-away location in time and space dubbed Dimension X-Crement.

Eccentric millionaire Snotty Ragsdale has taken a job at the lab to investigate its safety and to find out exactly where the pollution is going. He has a bad feeling about Professor Stinkbaums latest contraption.

This night his suspicions will come true...

Join Boogerman on his journey to uncover the mysteries and evil that lurk within Dimension X-Crement!

Playing as Boogerman,it's up to you and your bodily functions to explore Dimension X-Crement, take on Booger Meister, his evil Henchmen (and woman), and his army of hideous creatures, including the Nose Goblins.

The game is broken up into 6 different worlds:

  • The Flatulent Swamps - Watch your step in this gassy bog. Word is, Hickboy does not take well to trespassin'. The signs read "Dayngur!" for a reason.
  • Boogerville - Voted Dimension X-Crement's number one place to live... If you're a Nose Goblin that is.
  • The Pits- I swear this place looks like the insides of some massive, disgusting creature.
  • Mucous Mountains - Look out for Butt-heads. The hills are alive, but snot with the sound of music.
  • The Nasal Caverns - Beware of post nasal drip.What exactly ARE they mining down there?
  • Pus Palace - The home of Booger Meister, Pus Palace is... well, Pus Palace is friggin' gross. Think about it.

Each world consists of 3 levels, plus 2 sewer "bonus" areas. In addition, each world contains it's own boss level, for a total of 18 full levels, 36 bonus areas, and 6 bosses.

Boogerman 20th Anniversary will feature the core mechanics from the original game, including:

  • Platforming - Sprint, Run, Jump, Slide, and Glide your way across the disgusting landscapes.
  • Gas Powered Attacks - It wouldn't be a Boogerman game without Burping and Farting!
  • Mucous Powered Attacks - Snot and Mucous make a triumphant return to Boogerman 20th Anniversary!
  • Power Ups - Milk Bottles andChiliPeppers are back to enhance your abilities!

The game will also include entirely new game mechanics and gadgets such as:

  • The Snot Whip
  • S.s.S.B.D Fart-copter
  • Swimming
  • Sliding
  • Wall Jumping
  • Mine-carting and more!

Other new elements include:

  • An Overhead Map - Get the lay of the land from the expansive overhead map. Linear world progression is a thing of the past in Boogerman 20th Anniversary.
  • New Collectable System - Multiple collectable types will now play a big part in how you unlock the levels and re-playability.
  • Dramatic Snotimal Rescue - Save the Snotimals, save the world.
  • An Expanded Story - Who IS Booger Meister and why is he so bent on taking over OUR world? Dive deeper into X-Crement than ever before!

Boogerman: A Pick and Flick Adventure is a 2D platform video game created by Interplay Entertainment and released for the Sega Mega Drive/Genesis in 1994 and later on the Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES) in 1995. The Genesis version was also released on the Wii Virtual Console in North America on November 24, 2008 and in Europe on December 12, 2008. Boogerman also made a cameo appearance as a playable character as the rival of Earthworm Jim in Interplay's ClayFighter 63.

The original game animation consisted of over 2000 hand drawn frames. Each frame of animation was drawn on animation paper, cleaned up, inked, scanned in, shrunk down, and colored before it went into the game.

The original Boogerman commercial aired in 1994, usually on MTV during episodes of Beavis and Butthead.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Never once has a game that we have worked on been considered "easy" to develop. The truth is, making games is hard. Mike and I will be relying on our experience from the last 20+ years to make this game happen. We have worked on and shipped games on numerous consoles including launch titles. We have worked at both large corporations and independent studios. We understand the process and the pressures that come along with tight deadlines and delivering quality games, and we welcome the new challenges of platforms like the PS4, PS Vita, and Wii U.

Another one of the biggest challenges in any game is creating quality assets and content on time. Over the years we have developed relationships with numerous talented developers. We are working with a number of incredibly talented artists and animators to ensure that we deliver the highest quality end product possible.

Taking feedback from the community is definitely not a new concept to us. However, involving the community in the design and development process is. We love the idea of pulling back the curtain and inviting you to come along on this exciting journey. While we are not 100% sure of exactly how all of this will work, we are approaching this with an incredibly open mindset. We are very open to trying different things and figuring out what works best for both parties. One thing we know for sure, we are looking forward to the process, and this is going to be incredibly fun!

After two years of love and devotion NovaLite Games proudly presents 'Tides Of Glory'.

A huge amount of work has gone into Tides Of Glory so far and with your help we will be able to give it the final push it needs to make it a truly amazing game.

Tides Of Glory is an action based MMO for mobile platforms; with fast paced, real time combat; and a detailed immersive world. You’ll need your reflexes to survive, but your wits to flourish as you play the economy and rise to power.

Tides of Glory’s fast paced action is driven by the shifting power between Trident’s three main nations. Their power is measured by their economy, and their economy is measured by the strength of their ports. Resources will vary from port to port and traders will need to develop trade routes between them to transport goods– buying equipment at the lowest price and selling it to the highest bidder. Not only will busy ports have a much stronger economy, but they will be able to use their available resources to upgrade their facilities as well – providing their denizens with the strongest ships and the best equipment. Players will need to trade goods, defend important shipments, smuggle sensitive items, and plunder their enemies in order to ensure their ports are more successful than their neighbours.

If a port has a weaker economy than those around it, it becomes vulnerable – allowing other nations to attack it and take it over – creating fierce PvP battles where the outcome will actually effect the balance of the world. To wipe out a nation all you need to do is take over all their ports – creating a worldwide strategy game, and allowing you to literally crush and cripple your opponents. 

You will create your own story and forge your own destiny, but on your journey to glory there will be many hurdles to overcome. Dynamic, player driven story-lines, introduced at regular intervals, will force the nations to unite or perish. The actions of each player will determine the outcome of a story. Will hordes of ghost ships destroy and plunder hundreds of ports or will the players rally together and vanquish the scourge that plagues the sea. When a lone ship unwittingly releases a sea monster, a pet of the Gods, will they kill it immediately or will it be allowed to destroy hundreds of ships before the players organise a fleet to destroy it.

  • Twitch based combat. Damage is in real time and dependent on your skill  – not the roll of a dice. Use the touch screen to manoeuvre your ship into position to unleash the perfect shot. While your skill and experience will affect how quickly you can move and reload, it is ultimately your own ability that decides if you live or die.
  • Dynamic player driven economy. Tides of Glory is a sandbox for players to sculpt into the game they want.Players will develop trade routes between ports based on their supply and demand. Players will be in charge of building all the ships and equipment in the game. The more you put into the game the more you’ll get out of it.
  • Player owned factions and ports. Create your own faction and choose a port as your base. Spend its income from trading and taxes on improving the facilities, making it safer and more appealing for players to choose as their home. Your faction members can build up its economy in return for special port bonuses, and apply a special faction colour profile to their ships, showing a united front as they sail into battle.
  • A genuine ‘Free2Play’ experience.Tides of Glory will be a free to play MMO with customisable content at the heart of its business model. Items that affects your abilities in game will only be attainable with in game currency - preventing large wallets from conquering the world, (it’ll be your enemies’ cannons that sink your ship, not their credit cards!)
  • Epic world events. The three nations in Tides of Glory are not the only danger to be found in the murky waters of the Trident, epic world events will bring you face to face with monsters of legend. Nations will have to work together to destroy the threat in game changing world events.
  • Caters for offline as well as online play. Tides of Glory will also cater for offline play. A number of instanced single player missions ensure you can always  move your story forward.

Backers will be given the chance to become in game characters, either just by name or with a hand painted in game avatar as well.

If you have read thus far and are a big fan of the MMO genre then really I have answered this already. ‘Tides of Glory’ has some really unique features – especially for a mobile based MMO. My entire design process has been dictated by my desire for change within the genre. My three main objectives were as follows:

  • The combat has to be engaging and exciting – I’m bored of playing the same old turn based combat over and over again. I’m not saying that I think those types of games are bad, just that they’ve been done to death. I’ve done my best to create a fun visceral experience, with fast paced combat that’s both deep and satisfying – easy to learn but difficult to master. I want people to be able to pick up the game, and be able to have fun playing it straight away.
  • The player must physically be able to change the world around them – I want players to be able to have a genuine impact on the world. I wanted a game where the balance of power is constantly shifting and evolving based players behaviour – in this respect I think it’s fair to say I’ve been highly influenced by games like Eve.
  • The Free2Play elements must be done fairly, and should not be intrusive - I wanted to create a free to play MMO that was focused on fun, and not profit margins. I have spent many unsuccessful moments trawling through the app markets for a free to play MMO that didn’t ask me to buy stuff every other screen, and make the game feel incomplete if I refused to purchase a pack of ‘gems’ or ‘credits.’ It seems that a lot of games made by bigger companies focus on farming money from the player in a number of cynical ‘pay to win’ situations in order to keep their profit margins high. I want to make a game that is many times more enjoyable without tugging at your wallet every 5 minutes.

Along with customisable content there will be a premium account offer as well to encourage a basic monthly spend. Premium accounts will range from £1 per month to £3 per month offering things like; monthly virtual currency for customising your stuff, a low experience buff getting you 20% extra experience from all your actions, extra ship slots (will always have at least 3 ship slots regardless of premium accounts) and numerous other benefits. I will also listen to the thoughts and concerns of any backers on this subject as there is a fine line between making a free to play financially viable and making it pay to win.

I have been able to spend two years working full time on Tides Of glory through funding received from family members. After carefully considering exactly what needed to be done and when it needs to be done by it became apparent that a little extra funding was needed to speed up the final stages and bring this game to the world in a polished state.

Currently the banks are unwilling to lend to many small businesses seeking cash injections and crowd funding is picking up the slack left by the bankers. Without you guys many awesome ideas would be lost.

I started programming professionally at Blade Interactive Studios in 2006. A year later I was made Head of Mobile and we released some of the best 2D and 3D pool and snooker games on the market. I was particularly proud of WCP2010 3D where we were allowed to really push the boat out in terms of graphics, making some of the shiniest balls ever seen on mobile at the time (apparently I became obsessed with polishing my balls).

‘Graphically the game looks very eye catching with deep soft colours and nice 3D graphics.'- Mobile Game FAQS

'The level of refinement and clarity do the game great justice, elevating it from yet another pool game into one of the current best examples of a digitised table, balls and cue' - Pocket Gamer

While I was working on these pool and snooker games, I was dreaming much grander ideas in my spare time. Ideas that would eventually turn into the game I’m asking you to fund today. I am extremely passionate about this project, so much so that I quit my comfortable job two years ago to concentrate on Tides of Glory full time.

This game has been my life for the last couple of years, and a large chunk of it has been completed– it just needs a little bit more work to get it up to a standard I can be truly proud of; which is why my financial goal is reasonably low compared to the majority of video game kickstarters.


During the KickStarter campaign I will be hosting a 24/7 feed (click the image above)– not just so you can watch me work (that would be too easy,) but so you can watch me work… dressed as a pirate. I’m going to don my captains hat and unsheathe my cutlass for the duration of KickStarter.

I’ll be dressed as a pirate from the moment I wake up, till the moment I fall back to sleep again. When I pick up my children, I’ll be dressed as a pirate. When go to the shops, I’ll be dressed as a pirate. If you go to a pub and you see a salty sea dog with an eye patch leering over at you, say hi, cause it’s probably me! You know that guy from ‘Dodgeball’ – Steve the Pirate – well that’s basically going to be me, so check back for regular updates on my pirate activities including pictures and videos.

What’s more my wife has said that if I manage to make 2/3 of my target goal in the first fortnight, she’ll join me by dressing as my salty sea wench. If I surpass my target, then my entire family will dress as pirates and go for a family day out. We’ll be sure to document everything so you can watch along at home.

Tides Of Glory is on twitter, facebook and reddit. Also, dont forget to check the live feed, I will look ridiculous!

We have an IRC channel where you can ask me questions if you would like to know more - #tidesofglory - http://irc.quakenet.org

Crowd funding is a fantastic opportunity for indie developers, it has given me, and many like me the freedom to work on innovative projects that would normally not be funded. As such, I feel it is my duty to continue to support the site as part of the Kicking It Forward campaign started by Brian Fargo of inXile entertainment. In other words, I will donate a portion of my company’s profits to other Kickstarter campaigns, ensuring that there is always hope for exciting and original projects like my own.

I have also always wanted to support the amazing efforts made into cancer research as well as help charities that support people already suffering from its devastating effects. My mother lost her fight with cancer just under 4 years ago at the age of 46. A minimum 2% of profits will be given to help both research and sufferers.

I intend to raise £15,000 from this kickstarter and I plan to spend it in the following way:

  • 40% - Art assets - My main artist is currently working for free and deserves all my thanks for his efforts made in the last few weeks, but apparently slavery is illegal so I really need to pay him. ^_^
  • 30% - Programming remaining features – As I mentioned before, the majority of the game has been completed, but there is still a reasonable amount of work to finish off and optimise.
  • 10% - Sound design –  It’s important we create the right atmospherics, making you leap embarrassingly to one side in a public area as a cannon ball flies passed your ship (headphones may make things a little too real).
  • 10% - User interface– Currently all UI elements couldn’t be described as anything other than ‘programmer art,’ Tides of Glory needs a user interface to match the quality of the rest of the game.
  • 10% - Backer rewards and additional costs– I think I’ve put together a great batch of rewards, and 5% of the raised funds will go towards paying for them.

An early alpha is planned for the end of May. If you pledge £15 or more you will get access to it, allowing you to give your own feedback and ideas.

The game will be made available to all Android users in August in the form of an Open Beta. Once this has finished the game will be ported to the iPhone as quickly as possible (1-2 months).

After release Tides Of Glory will receive regular updates to content and features. There are already many more features planned, but I want to keep them under wraps for now.

Thank-you for taking the time to read about this project. If you want to back it, but have more questions, please feel free to use the links given above.

Yours Sincerely,

Julien Heanley.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Delivering a game within a time frame is always very challenging and it only takes one or two issues to set things back. I was hoping to release the game around 4-6 months ago, but creating the tools to make it easier to scale the world quickly as more players join has been a big factor in the time scale increase.

The in-app billing model is taking a fairly large beating at the moment due to morally questionable methods, making parents and gamers very wary of all games using in-app billing and micro-transactions. Tides Of Glory will pride itself on the moral use of its in-app billing, ensuring children cannot rack up large bills for the parents to have slapped in their faces. This is, after all, a game targeted at all ages.

FAQ

  • What graphics engine are you using?

    I am using my own engine based off the cross platform functionality of the PowerVR SDK. I do all the rendering and model loading, but I use a modified version of their shell to make life easier to port to other platforms and I use their compressed .pvr texture format. PVR SDK is a free SDK by Imagination Technologies aimed at giving developers an easy API to get the most out of the PowerVR graphics chipset.

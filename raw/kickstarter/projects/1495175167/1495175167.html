Throne is a fantasy mix of Role Playing and Fighting video game genres, with vibrant playable characters directly influenced by you!


Throne is a Fighting Game..... mostly

Throne is a video game that mixes the better of two popular genres to create a unique gaming experience from familiar flavors. This game combines fast paced, action packed combat with free roaming, open world RPG elements, to bring together a fighting game that has depth, customization, skill trees, character development and just a little bit of fairy tale charm.

Another Fighting Game?  Really?

Growing up in the golden age of Massive Multiplayer games (MMOs) like Everquest and World of Warcraft, while at the same time being raised on the likes of Super Smash Bros. and Street Fighter, a young group of newish video game creators find themselves longing for a mix of those two very different game types.  Throne is a blend of those two video game styles, presented by likeable, entertaining, and often times arrogant player characters adventuring and battling in a vivid, colorful, story-driven world.  Concisely put, Throne is a fighting and Role Playing Game (RPG) that is casual, yet immersive, and one we want to play today ...so badly that we decided to make it ourselves.

Core Gameplay - Nuts and Bolts

Action based, competitive combat amongst friends is a long time staple of video games, but the interest in such gameplay is declining, and it’s no surprise with what little innovation which has occurred within the space the last several years running.  We love the close calls, skillful defeats, and epic comebacks fighting games bring, and want to stay close to that.  However, we also have a deep fondness for adventure, open ended worlds, and heroes that inspire us, entertain us, and (sometimes) make us smile.

At first glance, Throne might appear more like a fighting game than an adventure game, but its RPG components set it apart from the typical fighter.  Most of these RPG details can be readily seen in the depth of the character personalities themselves.  Throne will contain nine distinct, light-hearted character classes upon launch (concept art below), and while each character possesses distinct moves for their class, as a player moves up in skill, new moves, move combinations, gear, and titles can be procured.  Players may also heavily customize their heroes by purchasing in game clothing, pets, trinkets, and other accessories with in game currency (earned in game or purchased in the real world)

A New Twist on the (very) Tired Genres

As the mission statement of this project states, we happily grew up loving both fighting and RPG games, and we will always have fond memories of them, and have the utmost respect for how far they have come...

...But...

 ...we have played enough MMO/RPGs to cringe at the idea of facing more redundant "go fetch" and boring "medieval telephone" like quests.  After grabbing hundreds of small items for what seem to be the laziest of NPC's, or traveling back and forth on horseback to continuously deliver messages for hours, only to get some small sum of gold or token of appreciation, something has to change...

... as for fighting games, we believe game makers (and the people who play them) may be too focused on elaborate combo chains, resulting in the classic "Ryu vs Ryu" dilemma.  In other words, over time everyone ends up wanting to fight as the same, elite champion, and eventually fights his or her own mechanical twin.  Not the most emergent experience.  It seems the only readily available option we have is to cosmetically change between two or three basic color schemes, resulting in few meaningful (or fun) choices... 

What we WILL do is create one highly unique main quest for every character class.  Each class is very diverse on their own, with characters and campaigns following a unique path, yet each hero must reach the same destination..."Throne".  We aim for a simple quest system which is enthralling, yet attainable, and also optional.   No longer will you see huge gold exclamation marks above people spoon-feeding you your next mission.  We are headed back to the days where adventure was challenging... no one told you much, so you had to think to figure things out.  Side quests will come from mysterious sources such as notes on the wind.  Popular tavern songs will contain locational hints and clues.  Throne's musical score will inform you of whether or not you are progressing closer to your quest objective, or further away. 

  ...YET NO QUEST WILL BE ABSOLUTELY NECESSARY TO COMPLETE IF YOUR SOLE INTEREST IS SIMPLY WIPING THE FLOOR WITH YOUR ONLINE FRIENDS!

As such, you get to go as deep into your character building as you want to, and then try out the results in the world of Throne.  

Our opening alpha begins with a Fighter that has a broad sword, just like the first character you might have created as a kid.  We start with "Essence" which is a general tree that lets us increase our character's general stats such as health or speed.  Then we explore each class' own unique skill tree to progress in; an example, adding poison to the Rogue's already deadly knives, or berserker effects to the Warrior's furious shout.  We want each class to be compelling and ultimately an expression of yourself and how you think these classes should play, and we want you to be able to dress them in whatever way you like!

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Well with the risk therein-lies the challenges and we feel most of it is orientated around building a strong community around this game. We know how much play testing we can do, but ultimately when this game goes public, we will need player feedback to help us solidify the foundation. We want to encourage players to give feedback through in game rewards. We want this game to be a tournament game, but also have a very casual style for those who want it. This will be a delicate balance, but one we feel we can achieve with constant interaction with our community.

Additionally we need to be able to get the first 9 characters out as soon as possible the starting 3 are good but the first 9 will really demonstrate the uniqueness of our game and show off what we are really capable of. Although we know everybody will enjoy the first 3 they are designed to benchmark our balance once we have that we are going to really start playing with how far we can push trait building while still keeping a balanced multi-player fighting game that updates regularly. If we get the first 9 in and working we really think it will be obvious why Throne is a stand out amongst the rest of those titles from either genre.

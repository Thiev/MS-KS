Welcome to where Magic begins...

As the head Professor of Enchantment at the Royal Arcane Academy, very little in the world of magic remains a mystery to you. The Tangible Elements have been at your command for decades as have three of the four Intangible Elements. No one knows Void anymore though, so this area of lack never bothered you or your students.

Recently however, a sorceress claiming to be a Voidmaster has helped to propel you in to the Magical Dimension – a feat not achieved by anyone since the Light Crusade banished Void over a thousand years ago. Now you find yourself adrift in a world that you have only dreamed of. A world that mages, wizards, shaman, and sorcerers have told stories about for generations. A world that contains all magical knowledge and infinitely more cohesion with magic than you have ever experienced.

Will you be completely overwhelmed? Or will you solve your way through the ancient puzzles and explore the Islands of each Element? And will you be able to fulfill your promise to the Voidmaster and finally restore balance by unlocking Void's place in the world?

Prof. Dr. Caius Agrippa, the Phylactory, and Voidmaster Arnora Lamont

With a great focus on exploration and experimentation, Voidwalker is an experience about discovery and puzzle solving. Carve your own path through the world with dynamic puzzles aimed at giving you control on your own play-style.

  • Portal-like gameplay and puzzles (more magic, less physics, lots more paths!)
  • For Windows

  • Oculus Rift compatibility as well as Standard Mouse and Monitor configurations.

  • A free-flowing magic system, designed to give you complete control over your own spells.

  • Spellcrafting inspired by Ohm's law, Minecraft, Traditional magic circles, and a dash of Magicka!

  • Open-ended puzzles tailored to give you multiple pathways to progress. No per-determined “best” or “right” path means that you solve each puzzle according to your own play-style.

  • Immersive hand-crafted 3D environments based on a rich history and vast background.

  • Environment crafted by obsessive Elder Scrolls fans

  • Each Island is based on a unique element and reflects that element's characteristics in its design and puzzles.

  • Completely new language written specifically for the lore and runes of Voidwalker. Inspired by Hebrew, and Kanji.

An excerpt from the Rajafi Runic language from Voidwalker

Immersion

We've designed Voidwalker as an immersive First Person experience, and we do not want anything to hinder that. Cut scenes and cumbersome GUI (the User Interface) are a strict “no go” for our game design. The Spellcasting system, non-intrusive and easily wieldable, is the only GUI element that you will run in to. Your character would be doing these spells in his head, but unfortunately technology hasn't brought us psychic gaming yet!

Creativity

The puzzles and levels in Voidwalker all have multiple solutions and multiple paths. You will be able to solve your way through the Magical Dimension using whatever style of magic you can think to apply. If you do not want to unlock a door, can you blast it open with fire? Can you smash it with a boulder? Can you de-create it with Void? Maybe you will need that door later! You might create a key with Light in order to keep the door intact. Maybe an Air spell can force the tumblers open. Or maybe there is another path to the next room that would bypass the door completely. Keep your eyes and mind open to the possibilities...

A Sense of Wonderment

All eight of the environments that we have created for Voidwalker are meant to inspire a vast sense of wonderment. You are the first person to walk within the Magic Dimension in a thousand years! We want to instill in the player the same sense of monumental discovery that the character would be feeling at every turn.

Voidwalker's Magically based Creation Myth

FUN!

Since we have been working in the game industry, we have had one rule above all others: no matter what, our products have to be fun!

After all, this is what gaming is all about, right?

Magic is channeled in Voidwalker through the forms of eight basic elements and a combination of five “modifiers”. Each element can be shaped and influenced with two or more modifiers at a time, creating over 200 different possible spell combinations.

Fire, Earth, Air, Water

These are probably very familiar to players as they are the “usual” elements used in magic systems. They are called the “Tangible” elements because they are physical things and easier to understand and control.

Energy, Entropy, Light, Void

These are called the “Intangible” elements and mages who study them usually take much longer to gain mastery than mages of the Tangibles.

Energy is used to make things speed up either gradually or explosively, and is channeled to change other elements as needed. A common manifestation of pure Energy is song, and the Energy Priests and Priestesses are usually talented singers.

Entropy is a lack of (or decrease in) Energy, and can bring things (including yourself) to a halt – even in the middle of a long fall. Entropy Monks are solitary in nature and have mastered the arts of longevity.

Light is an illusionary magic based around creation. Weak Light mages can create apparitions of objects, fooling the eye (and other senses). It can create thoughts or make rumors seem real. True masters can create solid objects from nothing.

Void is another illusionary magic that un-creates (this is very different from destruction, which would be an Energy or Fire trait!). Weak Void mages can banish Light apparitions, or muffle their own movements so as to move without being seen or heard. Void can hide objects and banish thoughts. True masters can unmake physical objects.

Modifiers

Please watch our video for a great explanation as to how Power and Intensity come together to shape your initial spells! Our Programmer was inspired by Ohm's Law in that an output is shaped by the Amperage, Resistance, and Volts that are input. Instead of that, a basic Voidwalker spell is shaped by Power, Intensity (determines shape), and Aspect (element).

For example, a “Low Intensity, High Power, Fire” spell would manifest as a very large, widespread wave of fire.

Other modifiers include “Self”, “Insulate”, and “Join”. Without spoiling too much of the potential game, we can tell you that they do exactly what they sound like!

Examples include: “Entropy Self” will manifest as a slow-fall spell. “Earth, Join, Fire” will become a stream of lava.

We want you to experiment and see what happens! The more you play with the spells, the more you will be rewarded!

By now, the fame of this fantastic VR headset is widespread and well known. We have had our Oculus development kit for a number of months now and can promise you that it is truly able to live up to all of it's hype! It would be foolish for us to make this game with such a vast scope in it's magical vistas and settings and not include Oculus compatibility. (That being said - the Oculus will NOT be required to play Voidwalker - it will be optional).

Our level designer in particular was taken completely aback when she was able to put on our Oculus and walk around her initial island for Voidwalker. Since then, she has been incredibly enthusiastic in designing the world with the Oculus Rift in mind – from skies made of many moving layers and grand mountaintop views designed to take your breath away, to tiny crystalline details in caves and dungeons that might otherwise be overlooked on a traditional monitor.

We only hope to do as much justice to this groundbreaking technology as the Oculus will do for Voidwalker!

The Razer Hydra motion controllers are also being kept in mind when making Voidwalker, although they have been listed as a stretch goal considering all of the extra programming and integration time that it would take to include them. We would really love to be able to add these greatly innovative pieces of technology in Voidwalker as they are fantastic tools to keep the immersion levels high, and pair beautifully with the Oculus Rift!

Some selections from our character based concept art files and character descriptions.

Professor Doctor Caius Marcus Agrippa (Player Character).

Dr. Agrippa is the Head of the Enchanting Department at the Royal Arcane Academy where he has dedicated his life to researching all eight elements. He is a brilliant but mostly absorbed man who has spent a lifetime focused almost entirely on his research. By the time just prior to Voidwalker, Dr. Agrippa was about to give up all hope of ever fully understanding Void, due to the fact that the element was subject to a Crusade almost 1,000 years ago and all but wiped out. The *strictly professional* appearance of Arnora in his life however has revitalized his hopes in this subject. 

Caius most likely has three inner voices constantly monologuing about what is before him. As the writers describe: "Caius 1" wants to see, research, and do EVERYTHING, "Caius 2" is the quiet reserved person that he sees himself as, and "Caius 3" is constantly convinced that everything he attempts will go wrong in horrible, horrible ways.

 Voidmaster Arnora Lamont

Arnora is a very old Void practicioner who wants nothing in life more than to go to the Void region of the magical dimension. She started studying Void almost 400 years before Voidwalker, but like all other Void novices, realized that there was not very much to study and turned to Entropy - Void's sister element. From the Entropy Monks, she learned how to slow and stop everything around her - even her own aging process (a common expert Entropy ability). After her pilgrimage to the Entropy monastary in their realm of the magical dimension, she turned back to Void and puzzled out many of  their ancient techniques with the help of one of the last Voidstones in the entire world - it is her greatest treasure, and it says volumes that she lets Dr. Agrippa study it.

Arnora is an old wise woman, but time has made her incredibly caustic. She is highly protective of the fledgling order of Void mages that she is raising, and has very little time for social niceties or politics. Outwardly she will be at best politely aloof as she is usually calculating her next 5 moves. Most importantly - while everyone refers to her as "Voidmaster", she does not consider herself so, and will not until she's been to the Void realm.

Energy Priestess Rebecca Louise


Rebecca was an Energy Priestess many hundreds of years ago that traveled through the magical dimension before Dr. Agrippa. You will come across her footsteps (and her songs - Energy practitioners are all wonderful song crafters) multiple times throughout the game.

The Phylactery 

No one really knows how long this artifact has been in the magical dimension - it would tell you too long. Supposedly it's purpose is to help visitors navigate the main island hub with the vast library of all magical knowledge. While it *does* fulfill this purpose, it also finds great amusement in annoying and making fun of whomever it finds. Do not underestimate it's knowledge! It just might save your life...

This is the character that we will be casting a male voice for - and whom we would attempt to cast a well known voice actor for if we hit our $150,000 stretch goal! (He will be your constant companion throughout the game).

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Challenges are a natural part of any endeavor. We have weathered some already while maintaining this project on our own and are confident that we can see this through. Between the four of us at Super Tea Studios we have all the skills and experience we need to complete this project. We have been working together long enough to have formed a working team and are not averse to work long hours. A working day is never over until the job is done.

Our current goal is to complete Voidwalker by the end of Q1 of 2014. If we are successfully funded to the appropriate level, it will give us the ability to keep our 24/7 dedication to this project that we have, so far, maintained on our own. With that dedication we are confident that we can deliver a game that meets both a high standard as well as gaining the ability to procure outside work for those few aspects that we cannot provide ourselves while maintaining the quality that we want to impart on the final product.

As we have been maintaining this project so far, we have realized that our primary challenge is to procure a level of funding that would allow our team to remain fully dedicated to content development throughout the development time. This mainly consists of living expenses as well as some measure of infrastructure support, however the advantage of having a complete team is that we rely on outside efforts very little, and can thus control development and progress a lot more closely.

We are fully committed to Voidwalker, and to the best of our knowledge and ability we have taken active measures and precautions to account for unforeseen circumstances or events that might influence on time delivery of the game.

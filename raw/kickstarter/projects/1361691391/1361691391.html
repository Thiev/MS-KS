Customization is integral to Mongrel; you'll constantly improve and create new mongrels, never being limited to just one fighter. Â You'll have a fluctuating roster of mongrels honed to your favorite play styles.

Character customization is a 3-tiered system.Â The foundation of your mongrel's abilities are tied to its weight class and race. Each weight holds an innate set of attributes and each race adds its own unique attribute bonuses.

The second tier of customization is your mongrel's equipment. As you progress you'll build an inventory of armors and weapons both crafted and rewarded through arena winnings. Each piece of equipment will add to your mongrel's base attack, speed, and defense. Mixing and matching pieces will let you build a fighter that conforms to your play style.Â 

The last tier of customization is skill management. Each mongrel will have access to a universal set of skills that allow you to upgrade your favorite weapon style and other base attributes. Â A set amount of skill points are rewarded over the course of a mongrel's career, and a mongrel will never fully be able to master all skills.

Single player is your mongrel's training grounds and where you'll begin to master your play style. Game play will be thematic, and consist of arena matches against other mongrels, mutated beasts, and a combination of the two in an off-line version of The Pit. Winning regular matches will reward you with coin for armor and weapon upgrades while beast fights will unlock special armor sets and weapons.Â 

In the vein of gladiatorial fighting, Mongrel's multiplayer match making will consist of random game types. Multiplayer arenas will reward each player with a larger purse than any single player bouts and help in mastering your play style for entry into The Pit.Â

Currently we're only two, but we still have all technical and artistic bases covered. More importantly, we've fallen into place when it comes to the overall development process. Stephan's focus is on macro-development and the look of Mongrel. Blake focuses on micro-development and the feel of Mongrel.

After funding, our biggest challenge will be creating a fair and balanced person vs person system. Developing an intense action game that requires fluid input and time sensitive interaction is challenging on any platform. Person to person exchanges are going to make or break Mongrel, and a massive amount development time will go toward play testing those interactions. Getting a beta out early to backers will give us ample time to apply feedback and finalize multiplayer systems before the final release.

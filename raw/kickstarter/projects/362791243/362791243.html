A top-down action roguelike set on an alien planet.

'Brandon Must Die' is an action rogue-like that takes place on an alien planet.

When I first set out to make a game, I asked myself "What would I like to play?". The answer was: I've played hundreds of hours of 'The Binding of Isaac' and I really just want more of that! But... I'd like a new take on it, in a new environment. I still want everything that made the gameplay so fun, such as...

100s of items
Enhance every aspect of your character. Buff up your armor so you can take a lot of damage. Run faster or just get a jetpack and fly. Get a magic staff and summon thunderclouds. Shoot slow, giant, bouncy, freeze bullets, that spawn live grenades on impact. Build 'C.L.A.M.P.S' the tiny robot that just wants to bite everyone. Use special items that slow time, light everything on fire, throw meat cleavers, emit a force field, drop landmines, or place a sentry gun. 

 Most items will change the appearance of your character.

Permadeath
When you die, you lose all your items and start over. Although you can unlock items that can be found on other runs. A full playthrough of the game should take 1-2 hours.

High difficulty
There will be 'bullet hell' rooms that will make you go "HOW AM I SUPPOSED TO BEAT THAT!?".

Unlockable items and characters
When you first start playing there will only be a limited number of items to find. As you achieve feats like killing a certain boss without taking damage, or killing 100 slimes, you will unlock items that can be found in later runs. Beating the game under the right circumstances will also unlock new characters that start out with different stats and items.

Bosses
Each of the 12 levels will have at least 2 different bosses to randomly encounter.

Randomized dungeons
Each playthrough will create a unique map layout with random rooms.

Secret rooms
Doing specific things like exploding a grenade on a certain swamp will open up a doorway to a secret area. Inside you may find ultra-rare items. 

I started working on BMD! in February 2013 and I've put over 600 hours into it's design and development. I have enough content to fill 8 levels, but I plan to have 12 total. I'm hoping to release this project in June of 2014.

I want to raise these funds to pay for the audio aspects of the game since I cannot create those myself, these would include sound effects, music and voice over audio. Also for all of the various fees involved with self-publishing a game and for some marketing.

There are currently over 40 enemies already in the game. The final version will likely have over 60 total.

The Jungle

This is where your escape pod crashes and you begin your mission. This area contains exotic beasts, swamps and natives.

The Crypts
Holds the cursed ghosts of other crash victims, creepy creatures and undead remnants of the native creatures. There are also plenty of spike/arrow traps set to ward off the living.

The Canyon
A place of ethereal energies, where the native humanoids have harnessed the power of elemental magics. Native Rockmen lob bombs and Sphincters puke bile.

 The Urban zone
A war torn dystopia where various gangs fight for power. You'll find yourself caught in the middle as the 'Crag' gang, 'Blerg' gang, and Police battle each other. You can even take sides in the fight by adorning the right 'colors'.

Space Ship
Enter a giant ship and fight it's automated defense systems. Shielded laser bots, self-destructing suicide drones and auto turrets await you. There may even be some weird experiments floating in jars, break the jar with a stray bullet and who knows what could come out!

Moon Base
Fight the elite commandos that protect the rich and powerful living here. These troops will use grenades and heavy machine guns to flush you out. This will be where the final battle takes place.

 Music and sound design is being done by the talented Jack Powell.

A peaceful science vessel is attacked by a spoiled, alien teenager named Brandon driving his dad's luxury space cruiser. The scientist is forced to eject and crash land on an alien planet where she must fight her way through a wide range areas on a quest for vengeance.

I feel that this is a fairly low risk of completion. I already have a demo tested on Windows/OSX/Linux. The game is in a playable state with 6 levels, 100+ items, 40+ enemy types. This game is built with the Construct 2 engine, which has robust support and a large active community. I also have 15 years of experience developing software and have published products inn my career.

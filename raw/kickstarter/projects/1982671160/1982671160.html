Age of Arius is the first installment in the Remnants of Twilight series. It follows a young man known as Oboro Lazera, as he travels between six different worlds to secure the shattered sword pieces of Acacius. Age of Arius will be a 2.5D RPG that will be available for PC, Mac, and hopefully OUYA. Originally Age of Arius was created using the RPG Maker engine running off the Ruby Script, but we wanted to expand on it and make the game better with higher graphics, Voice Acting, stronger engine, and more features. 

  • 60 Hours of Gameplay(Not including Side Quest and Events)
  • Active Turned Based Battle System(ATB)
  • "Alchemic Historia": Collect materials from monsters throughout the game and use them to create awesome consumables and vanity items.
  • In-game Encyclopedia of all the monsters, Gods, and historic figures that appear in the game. (It's mostly to educate people about the gods in the game and the culture behind them)
  • Divine Resonance!: Converge with your Divine or Deviant to obtain tremendous powers and transform the character's appearance.
  • No random encounters! All enemies will appear on the screen and can be avoided.
  • Fully Voice Acted!(No shitty voice acting either <.<)
  • Original Soundtrack created by the wonderful people at Lucky Lion Studios!
  • Full Scale Talent System that allows you to build each character to your liking by gathering points through combat.
  • Olympic Stadium! Battle your way through hordes of NPCs to gain the respect of the people of Talarea and acquire the title of "King Of Olympius".
  • Skit System Small events throughout Talarea that will bring more depth to the story. (Mostly conversation that would occur in the book)
  • PC/Mac Exclusive(Hoping to expand to the OUYA)

     The Remnants of Twilight series begins with Age of Arius and follows a young man known as Oboro Lazera. Oboro is an abandoned child of Erdala who was adopted by the Lazera family when he was a mere child. As he got older he displayed unique abilities and was able to learn things at a extremely fast rate. As he went out into the world and saw all the corruption and sadness that the people of Erdala had succumbed to, he decided that he would follow the path that his parents did long ago. He would join the Vatican, a holy group of people that used forsaken magic to battle against a sinister group known as the Nebulus. When he turned seventeen, Oboro and his sister, Mereicym, were preparing to take their trip to the Vatican to enlist, but before they could make it there they were sucked into a dimensional rift that formed inside Oboro's room. They awoke in a new world known as Talarea and were separated from each other.

Follow Oboro and Mereciym as they search for each other in this new world, and at the same time learn more about themselves and the future that has been set out for them. If you feel like we've left out a ton of information keep looking for our updates as almost each one will be telling a story about the game and the many stories of the characters. We want you as the player to have an understanding of these characters and a feel for the world that they live in.

   Inspiration for this project came from so many different sources. As far as games a lot of the inspiration came from the Tales series, Disgaea, Legend of Dragoon, Golden Sun, Shin Megami Tensei series, etc. We at Dead Teddy Studios have played tons of different RPGs and we decided to use all of our knowledge and love for this genre to create a game that can go beyond what those titles could bring. The idea is to reinvent and bring back the old style of RPGs that we have all come to love and enjoy. It feels like to us that a lot of the new games have strayed away from the concept of giving us a better story and a better game and have just decided that they want to give us better graphics, but graphics won't make a bad game better. If you want a game to be great you need to have a great story, awesome features, and originality. This is what we are shooting for for this game and we will do anything to make that idea a reality.

We don't believe there will be any major complications with the development of the project, but we do believe that one of our biggest issues that will occur during development will most likely be our perfectionist attitudes. We will most likely go back or spend hours of our time on very small things to make it as perfect as possible, which will in turn hurt us on time. Also if the project is funded we will be working our hardiest to find a Pixel Artist that can bring our character and enemy sprites to life. Though we don't know how long this will take, it is one of our main priorities as of right now more than anything.

MechIES puts you in your own mobile mech to battle in online arenas against friends and foes. Play your Mech, your way, to victory!

Built for iOS, Android, Windows, Mac, and Linux. We will also be participating in Kicking It Forward!

MechIES (Mechanical Interactive Exo-Skeleton) is a game that places you in a completely customizable Mech to battle in an online arena against friends, foes, and the like. Set up your Mech your way, with interchangeable parts, gear, and weapons that all provide different benefits, abilities, and hindrances. Why play a Tank mech or Sniper mech when you can play as both? Don't get stuck playing one way. You customize your Mech to your play style. Combine different types of gear to create a completely unique Mech that is built to your unique play style and DOMINATE the Arenas.

After designing your Mech, queue up for different Arena types. Play in Death Matches against everyone or as a team. Battle over key points in a "King of the Hill" Arena. Demolish the other teams base before they demolish yours. Capture their flag and place it at your base to show your superiority. There are a large assortment of arena types to choose from and even more to be added in updates to keep the game constantly fresh and fun!

Enjoy a fun and unique art style that is eye catching and entertaining while you create massive explosions and absolute mayhem! These aren't your typical slow moving Mech. Battle at fast paced speeds with lethal attacks where mere moment decisions can change the tide of the battle!

*Mech Garage Slot - The Mech Garage Slot is a purchasable slot where you can build an additional Mech. You can use pieces from your other Mech or purchase new ones all together. These Garage Slots are unique because, upon death in an Arena, you can summon a different Mech from one of your other slots. This allows you to change Mechs to what is needed during a given battle. If at the correct Reward tier, you will receive these slots free!

*Paint Kit - Do you want to stand out from the crowd? Do you want your Mech to look unique and deadly? Paint Kits allow you to fully customize your Mech and give it different color schemes. These items are purchasable in game, but free to certain Backer tiers.

*Mech Skins - Want an even MORE unique looking Mech? Want people to struggle with figuring out what kind of Mech you are playing? Mech Skins do just that. Mech Skins give your Mech a completely unique look regardless of the gear equipped. This presents a challenge for those who know what certain pieces of gear look like when trying to read a player. Want your Mech to look like a dragon, or maybe something like Hello Kitty for the ladies! Some skins may even provide a unique ability that can only be used once per match.

A Few Questions That Need Answers

Will mobile players play against PC players?

Currently, we do not plan to allow the two to compete against each other. Mobile devices just aren't up to par with PCs to allow users to compete on equal grounds. This would make competing in Arenas very frustrating for mobile users when competing against PC users. To prevent this issue the Mobile device users and PC users will be divided onto separate servers. Possibly in the future, if mobile devices are up to par enough to handle it, we may create certain servers where both can compete.

Will the PC versions of the game be limited by the mobile versions?

We are aware of the limitations of mobile devices. The initial release of the game will likely be very similar on all devices. Through updates, differences may arise. For example, Arena matches of 10v10 or 20v20 may be added in updates to Windows, Mac, and Linux, but mobile devices may not see those types of large matches for a reasonable amount of time. This would be due to the mobile devices limitations. We want to ensure it runs smoothly on mobile devices to give you the best experience. So if it means making some smaller cut backs on the mobile versions for a time, so be it. As mobile devices continue to advance we will add content that can be handled. The vast majority of content such as weapons, armor, arena match types, and most maps will be the same across all versions. Performance hindered additions may not make it onto mobile versions until a later date. So short answer, no, PC versions will not be hindered content wise by mobile versions. PC versions will be what you expect from a PC game (grandiose and all), with mobile versions playing catch up through updates as mobile devices advance.

Am I going to be charged an arm and a leg through inApp purchases and the like just to play?

No. There will be purchases that can be made in game, but nothing that will directly impact the game in a big way. The bulk of the items that will be purchasable in game are almost all related to the look and design of your Mech. These are skins and paint kits. They don't have any impact on game-play. Other purchasable items would be Guild Charters (to allow you to start your own guild). You do not need to pay anything to join a guild, but to start one is a different story. It will not be expensive though. Part of the reason for charging for Guild Charters is to keep guilds real and serious rather than having any Joe Shmoe start a guild just cause. One of the few purchases that does have an impact on game-play is a Garage Slot. Garage Slots allow you to build more than 1 Mech (which you start off with). They also allow you to switch between your Mechs at special points in a match or upon death. You can see how it may alter game-play, but it in no way breaks it. If someone is not capable with one Mech, they are no more likely to be anymore capable with 5 Mechs. In any case, it still comes down to the player regardless of the number of Mechs someone has. So, none of the purchases will break game-play between those who buy and those who don't. Free players will be just as capable as those who buy items, but those items are there for those who really want to stand out.

Will I be able to sign in to the same account and use the same Mech on both mobile and on PCs?

This is something we have been considering. It is something we would like to have available at launch, but it will depend. We still need to explore this further because there are perks and issues with it. Time constraints may also make it difficult to have done by launch. Just know it is something we would like to have and plan to, at the very least, add in the future.

What happened to your previous project, Project: GEO (Global Endemic Online)?

For those of you whom are unfamiliar, we launched a Kickstarter trying to fund another project that was a large scale MMORPG. The largest issue we had with funding it, is that it requires a large amount of funds for a game that size. That we understand. Thus, we decided to work on the project (MechIES) that we were going to produce after GEO to 1) Allow us to build a community not only for MechIES, but for GEO and future projects as well. And 2) Through completion of MechIES, the profits made could be used to help expand our team and produce GEO. We didn't want to short change GEO by making cuts left and right, ultimately hurting the vision. Instead, we decided to take a proactive approach and not only further our experience and your faith in us, but to build another source of funds for GEO outside of Kickstarter. For those of you anxiously awaiting GEO, we just ask for a little patience. MechIES is a smaller project that can be done in far less time, but by completing MechIES we believe it will greatly enhance GEO across the board from building a community to building funds. In this way, after MechIES is complete, we can focus the majority of our time and resources directly into GEO (apart from MechIES updates of course). We very much appreciate our communities support and most certainly plan to reward those who stick around with us as we build and complete the projects.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Well with any project there can be obstacles to hurdle over. For instance difficult game bugs that impede game play can slip in and be very troublesome to diagnose resulting in delays. Although these obstacles can't be entirely predicted, it is expected in development and we provided a cushion for our development time frame to absorb such delays.

One hindrance we face is funding. Our current goal of $30,000 is the ground floor for production. That was after cutting out some initial content at launch that we had hoped to have (and plan to bring forth through updates). We had to do this because Kickstarter is an all or nothing system. We needed to bring the budget to an achievable goal to give it the best chance possible at success. If you were to add up all the points in the "Where do the funds go?" section you would see there is no real cushion. This gives us enough to produce the game of course, but as a skeleton of how we see it in the future. Any excess obstacles we face will undoubtedly come out of our own pockets. Of course if we exceed our goal, we end up adding some meat to this skeleton for launch.

If we are funded, you WILL get this game and we will do our absolute best to ensures it exceeds your expectations! You have my word and there is NO ifs, ands, or buts!

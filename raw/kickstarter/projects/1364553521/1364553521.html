Using simple actions you will fly your gunship around the battlefield, aim at targets with easy enemy selection and let loose the dogs of war with auto lock-on mechanisms. The missions we have planned for KILLSTORM will be many and varied. You will never enter the battlefield without an objective to accomplish. We have already implemented systems for many missions including:

  • Attacking enemy locations defended by static and mobile AI enemies
  • Defending a location against enemy attacks
  • Joining the battle and assisting friendly AI against enemy AI
  • Destroying static enemy targets
  • Escorting friendly AI across danger zones
  • Gathering intel by securing an area and landing to collect it
  • Following a path marked by beacons and destroying enemies along the way
  • Killing all enemies inside a given area

Each of these systems is sensational on its own, but when we combine them in missions the action is exhilarating.

KILLSTORM will also include both environmental and time of day changes. For the gear heads among you, night vision mode will give you the upper hand in the darkness. And for those nautically-inclined pilots, battling ships at sea will test your skills.

You can improve the performance of the helicopters in the game. Each helicopter has stats in Speed, Acceleration, Armour, Gun Accuracy and Missile area of effect. We've kept things very simple - so the more you play, the more you improve. Want a faster helicopter? Go fly more! Want to increase your accuracy? Go shoot more enemies!

KILLSTORM is being created by Nice Touch Games. The company was started in 2011 by Richard Bunn and David Green, two veteran AAA developers, who have worked at some of the biggest companies in the industry including EA, Criterion and Sony Computer Entertainment. Together they have published several indie games.

Richard (Creative) has been with the industry over 15 years and can list titles like Black and Burnout to his credit. A designer by trade he prides himself in the refined design detail of the titles he creates while driving David crazy with his incessant pursuit of a perfect look and feel.

David (Coder) has also been in the industry for the majority of his life, including a stint working on the real flight simulators used by fighter pilots today. An insomniac programmer he sits at a laptop for the best parts of his days and nights. For him sleep is merely a time to reflect on the next coding solution.

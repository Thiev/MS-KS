Terrestrial Domination is a space based Real-time strategy game for Window, Mac, and Linux

Terrestrial Domination is an RTS game that takes place in a solar system with planets orbiting around a star. When you start you build space stations around your home planet and mine asteroids for resources. You can also colonize or invade other planets, and build warships to destroy the other side space stations. Â

Planet Habitability System

Planet Habitability will be determined from a number of factors. Right now these factors include Temperature, Radiation, Volatility.

If the temperature of a planet becomes to hot or to cold then the planet's habitability will decrease, and the population of the planet will start dying. The temperature of a planet can change by its distance from a heat source like a star. Ships can also increase the temperature of a planet by heating its atmosphere, or adding greenhouse gases to the planet. Temperature can be decreased by doing the opposite.

If the radiation of a planet becomes to high a planet's habitability will decrease, and the population of the planet will start dying. If a ship shoots nuclear missiles at a planet then the radiation of the planet will start increasing. Ships can also disrupt the ozone layer, or magnetic field of a planet to increase the radiation of a planet. Once a planet has high radiation it will slowly dissipate over time, or can be cleaned up with radiation reducing technologies.

If the volatility of a planet becomes to high then a planet's habitability will decrease, and the population of the planet will start dying. Ships can create natural disasters to increase the planets volatility for limited periods of time. These disasters may include, mega hurricanes, volcanoes, earth quakes, pole shifts, and asteroid impacts.

Some of these problems may be overcome with technological upgrades, like subterranean cities and, shielded cities. But if the planets habitability is very low these methods will not be effective.

Loretron

Loretron is the homeworld of the humanoid race. Loretron is also the capital planet of The Humanoid Industrial Alliance.

The Humanoid Industrial Alliance

Most humanoid worlds have adopted a hierarchical form of government where a small class of elites rule over their massive serf like populations. The framework that holds these system worlds together is called The Humanoid Industrial Alliance. The Alliance also has a system of democratic institutions that are for the purpose making the masses think that they have a say in alliance policies. These institutions in reality have no real power, and are there just for show.

Non-Alliance Humanoid Worlds

Other humanoid worlds have more democratic forms of government and have elected not to join the alliance. These worlds try to stay out of the war with the reptoids. But are usually much weaker than alliance worlds, and are routinely manipulated to cooperating with the alliance wishes.

Humanoid Children

At birth humanoid children have their profession chosen for them. As humanoids growing to age they are programed in their chosen occupational instruction programs. Most humanoid children are allowed very little free time as it interferes with their training programs. Any children that are slow learners are severely disciplined. Any children that step out line or don't meet the expectations of their programing are sent to reeducation facilities. These children will usually be assigned a lower class rank, and more menial work duties.

Humanoid Elites

Humanoid elites come from a small group of selected bloodlines. The elites enjoy a much more lavish lifestyle then the lower classes. Most of the resources on alliance worlds ether go to the war effort, or are used to support the extravagant lifestyle of the elites. Many of the duties of the elites include attending regular drunken sex parties. Other duties also include attending mock democratic political positions, and ambassadorships.

Humanoid Ambassadors

The humanoid ambassadors are known across the universe for their dashing looks, and personal deviant charm. When interacting with other races, humanoid ambassadors may seem like friendly negotiators. These ambassadors also enjoy fine wine, and cocktail parties. But their true motives are much darker then they, at first, may seem.

Demous

Demous is the homeworld of the reptoid race. Demous is also the capital planet of The Reptoid Sovereign Territory.

The Reptoid Sovereign Territory

The Sovereign Territory consists of a hierarchy of regional, and local system lords. Local system lords must pay tribute to regional system lords. Regional system lords must pay tribute to the inner circle of the sovereign territory. System lord fleets also battle one another, in ritualistic competitions, for dominance over their territories. From time to time full fledged wars for dominance also break out across reptoid territories. The Sovereignty routinely wages war on other races, and takes over their worlds, making their inhabitants slaves.

The Humanoid Industrial Alliance is by far the biggest nemesis of The Sovereignty. Alliance forces strength are near par with reptoid forces. These two empires have been battling each other for dominance for many millennia.

Reptoid Adults

The reptoids are a boorish bunch of scoundrels. The appearance of a standard issue reptoid recruit can be striking to say the least. Reptoid recruits are very muscular, and highly genetically modified. These savages also like their liquor. It is very common for reptoids to drink on the job, and get in bar fights after they are off duty. Sometimes these fights will consume an entire ship, and all productive activities will cease for days while the fight is commencing. In rare circumstances nuclear/anti-matter bombs have been detonated in the middle of one of these fights killing all the occupants on board the ship.

Reptoid Spawnlings

The mating habits of a reptoid couple is quite disgusting, and I will not elaborate on this any more. All reptoid spawnlings are genetically modified before birth, and will grow to the age of 4 in a designated spawnling pool. Any spawnling that is weak in appearance or deformed is promptly thrown on the scrap heap for reprocessing of usable genetic material.

Disputed Territories

The disputed territories are solar systems that both the humanoid industrial alliance, and the reptoids claim as their own. These territories are where the majority of the fighting between the humanoids, and the reptoids take place. Over the centuries the economies of these systems have been desolated by the war. For the occupants that are left on these worlds life is hard and short. Many of these worlds are under constant bombardment by hostile forces.

Very few of the worlds in the disputed territories have democratic institutions, and most are ruled by military dictatorships. Slave worlds are also particularity common in these territories. Slaves are often needed for the mining colonies that support the war effort.

Other Races

There are many other races in the Universe, some of them are friendly, others more deviant.

Trade

Many worlds have adopted Bitcoins as a standard digital currency to trade with each other. A lot of worlds also use resource backed currencies, and fiat based currencies. Reptoid's often use bartering because of their barbaric nature. But most worlds won't trade in fiat based currencies because they can be easily manipulated.

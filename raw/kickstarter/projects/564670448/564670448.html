Pit your wits against other players in the wilds of space. For strategy lovers. No pay-to-win.

  • Massively multiplayer online (MMO) strategy game
  • Play from web browser, no download needed (PC, Mac, Android, iPhone, et al.)
  • Sci-fi / fantasy universe where every player gets the full feature set - absolutely no pay-to-win
  • Build industry, train military, conquer stars and planets
  • Form alliances, trade resources, share star systems
  • Galaxies run in real time over 3 to 6 weeks, then restart so everyone has equal strength again

Play Options

Players choose one of six different species with unique armies, warships, scanning devices, weapons and technologies. Each species has different advantages, so you have to adapt your strategy. Allies of different species complement one another and each can mitigate the other’s disadvantages.

Resource Production vs. Military Power

Players must balance military spending against industrial upgrades as you increase your power. Upgrading industry increases your future wealth, but you may lose it without military defenses. With a strong military, you can conquer the planets and industries of other players.

Military:  Space vs. Ground

Military power consists of warships and armies. Use your warships to control space around your stars and stop enemy attacks. Use your armies to invade enemy planets or defend your own.

Many players focus entirely on warships or armies. With an alliance of two players, one ally can focus on warships while the other focuses on armies. The result is a stronger combined military, though they must work together to be successful.

Technology

Players may research over 60 different technologies in the game. Technology affects all aspects of gameplay from terraforming your planets to building larger ships.

Advantages Of Our Token System

  • gameplay based on strategy - no pay to win  
  • price transparency - no hidden costs 
  • every player is invested - fewer idle players
  • use tokens how YOU choose - play multiple galaxies at once
  • use tokens when YOU choose - play every game or take a break and come back

Each galaxy in the Strange Universe will have an entrance fee based on how long it runs. A 3 week galaxy will cost 3 tokens to enter. A 5 week galaxy will cost 5 tokens. 1 token for every week of game play. Our token price at launch will be $1.90 per token, $7.60 for a month of game play. The per token price will drop as you buy larger packages.  

Rewarding For Players At All Levels

We have built the Strange Universe to be satisfying yet challenging for players at all levels and reward tactical brilliance while keeping a high level of danger across the board. Not everyone can get to the top and stay at the top, but everyone can have fun. Balance is everything.

There are countless paths to victory in the Strange Universe. Wit and cunning can trump resources and size. Players who have been knocked-down have options and can easily come blazing back for revenge.

Easy To Learn, Difficult To Master

With its depth of gameplay and lively community interactions, The Strange Universe is infinitely replayable. Some players will focus on technology, some on ships and armies, and some on industry. Some will try a more varied approach. Each has advantages.

We’ve built a game for strategy lovers. It’s endless fun and after years of play testing we haven’t come close to exhausting the possibilities.

Community

We're not just building a game. This is a community. Form alliances, trade resources and make new friends through our real-time, in-game chat system.

Victory Based On Ability

We won't sell victory. Our straightforward pay-to-play approach assures that the game is always a level battle ground. This makes strategy and diplomacy the deciding factors between players. The Strange Universe has a chess-like quality where thinking through a chain of future moves is how you succeed.

Galaxies run for a few weeks - usually 3 to 6.  When the next galaxy starts every player is on equal footing again.  No player has an untouchable lead.  

Style

We combine retro sci-fi and fantasy elements with a bit of strangeness added into the mix. Where else do you see Elves and Dragons fighting Robots, Martians and Demons?  

Players add a huge amount to the look and the feel of the game.  Every player can:

  • Name their own stars and planets
  • Name their own warships
  • Upload images for their player avatar
  • Upload images and personalize their profile page - where all trophies, medals, and accomplishments are displayed.

Complex Yet Easy To Use

Our graphical star map is a marvel compared to other web games. And you don't need Flash or any plugins to play - any browser will work. Smoothly zoom and pan around the universe. Explore other star systems and keep track of any fleets that your scanners have detected.

Our user interface keeps every option right at your fingertips in menus that are available from any screen.  Every menu option has a built in wizard to make sure you get the information you need.  

Carefully Balanced

The design of the game encourages powerful players to fight each other rather than attack smaller, weaker players.  A bounty system rewards anyone attacking higher ranked players. Higher technology costs slow empire growth and keep players balanced in range of power.

Sherman Adelson is a software engineer. He was mesmerized by computer games at an early age, so it’s no surprise he studied programming in school. His favorite game for years was Master of Orion, which was a big influence on Tales From the Strange Universe.

Jason Mize, a musician, artist and Jack-of-all-trades, grew up with a similar passion for games. He spent countless hours with Civilization, and when not playing computer games was immersed in sci-fi and fantasy literature. Jason is very competitive and loves strategy, especially when a daring gambit pays off.

Really Big Spiders was founded by Sherman and Jason as a web development company, focusing on web design.  After a few years they turned to the dark side and embraced their true love, games.

We started building the game because we wanted a multiplayer strategy game we could play over the internet. We wanted a game like the strategy games we had enjoyed in the past, but in a multiplayer form we could play together. We lived in different cities, so it was harder to get together and we wanted an activity that we could enjoy on the internet.

We tried several internet games, but we were unsatisfied with all of them. They all had a tremendous difference in power between players who had been in the game for months and new players who had just started. Additionally, most players were members of large alliances, which made it hard for new players to compete.  

After months of trying different games, we decided that it was time we designed and built our own.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Once our campaign is successful, one challenge will be server performance as our game runs with more players than we've ever had. We plan on bringing backers ($20 and up) who get invites to our beta into the game slowly, a few at a time. We can begin this immediately following the Kickstarter completion and continue adding them until we have the full complement of beta players in April. This should be a slow enough growth curve to allow us to identify and address performance issues as we grow. Then we'll have a month or two of beta play to finely tune the game performance before the game opens to the full public in June.

Another possible challenge would be wild success bringing tons and tons of players all at once. We've designed the game to be easily scaled with this idea in mind. Each galaxy will get its own server. We'll run each galaxy with around 500 players in it. If one fills up then we'll close it off and start a new one and run lots of galaxies at once. We're using Amazon and they have a great program for quickly scaling up as you need. We can start a new server and galaxy in about 10 minutes.

FAQ

  • Is the game playable on smart phones and tablets?

    Yes! The game is entirely played on browsers so we have a number of testers who play exclusively from their phones or tablets. It works fairly well. We do not have a mobile app. It’s one of the things that would make a good stretch goal. An app would help with the memory and size constraints on a phone. We also have some improvements planned to make some of the touch and drag features work better.

    Last updated: Sun, Jan 5 2014 1:07 pm EST
  • How will new players get to try out the game without paying for it?

    New players will definitely be able to try the game for free. We have 2 ideas for that. The first idea is to give every new account a few free tokens when they start. That would let them try the game for a few weeks before having to pay-to-play. My concern is that this could potentially lead to multi-accounting. We would have to program in some blocks to make sure this wasn't a viable cheating mechanism.

    My second idea is to create a very short galaxy - a 2 or 3 day galaxy that is free to play in. It would end and automatically begin again. Being just a few days long, it wouldn't be long enough to be worth playing except to try out the game and see what it is like. It wouldn't affect the player ranks and you wouldn't be able to win any medals/awards.

    At the moment, I lean towards the second option. I'd love to hear feedback and thoughts.

    Last updated: Sat, Jan 11 2014 5:15 pm EST
  • How do you stop players from resource farming?

    “Farming” refers to the creation of multiple accounts that all funnel their resources to one master account. Our fix for farming is 2-fold. First we've made the game pay-to-play. If you want to farm a bunch of accounts, you're going to have to pay for the extra accounts. Second, we have a block on sending resources if you haven't spent resources. The amount you can send (or receive) is a ratio of how much you have produced and traded. So if you don't spend anything and send all your resources away, you quickly hit the limit and can't do so anymore. If you spend lots but are given resources without ever sending resources in exchange, you quickly hit the limit. Since we implemented that limit, resource farming hasn't been an issue.

    Last updated: Sun, Jan 5 2014 1:09 pm EST

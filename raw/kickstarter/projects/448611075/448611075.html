Â Game Features and Game Design

The game is designed to provide a 3D environment that feels realistic, even if it is an imaginary location, it is immersive and believable. It uses an excellent 3D gaming engine that is capable of physics, shading, lighting, textures and 3D movement. Visualization effects allow for realistic changes in day and night, weather, sound, and motion.

You play the game in first person perspective and on foot. You will enter rooms and buildings, find secret areas, travel through different periods of time, in the past, present and future. You are able to walk, run, and jump, and can be on land or under water. There will be visual and audible prompts for information, and you will read information that is found in the game to apply to solving puzzles based on clues.

The length and difficulty level of the game is designed so that you will play over many days, perhaps even weeks, and you are able to save your
game and load it later.

The game is designed to provide lighting and shadows, animated 2D items for lights and added detail, advanced shading techniques for realism
of objects within the game, and the building of 3D models for indoor and outdoor areas.

The logic of the game provides for a game that has a linear story in the form of open-world game play. You are moved into a definitive direction
but have the freedom and flexibility to discover this direction in your own terms and on your own time.

The game is built around a level system, similar to mapped levels found in 3D games. The rules of the level and map are open and once again
based upon the ability to use a scripting language. Series of events can be developed in many ways to keep the gamer entertained and challenged.Â 

The Game Concept

The idea for this game is to create a compelling story that draws the gamer in and makes you strive hard to advance to the next part of the game in order to read, listen, and explore.

In the current game world, the mystery story or adventure game often ends up being a point-and-click seek-and-find. This means that often it is completely made of static 2D scenes and areas that are clickable to obtain information, clues, or inventory items. The typical modern first person game revolves around shooting and arriving at new areas.

The goal of my game is to take the vastness and realism of the 3D First Person game and fuse it together with the depth and story and suspense
of a mystery with puzzles and investigation. The story centers around your character, Jesse, who must discover where everyone has gone and why
he has been chosen for this special adventure. Puzzles, clues, and story are centered around information from the Old and New Testaments of the Bible,
along with painstakingly researched historical and current topics.

You, the gamer, will be playing as the character Jesse. As a first person perspective, 3D game, you see things through the eyes of Jesse.
Traditionally, the 3D First Person perspective has been used for shooter games, and in some ways this has been a cliche while also a popular playing style. although FPS games have been a genre where millions of dollars are spent developing the most advanced effects and virtual worlds, they have often been criticized for their growing lack of story and intellectual stimulation. I see this as a wonderful opportunity to immerse the player in more than just their environment. With the mind engaged just as much as the senses, the gamer will be able to imagine walking right into a novel, with the opportunity to experience it rather than to read about it!

Jesse soon discovers that something strange is going on, and all of the people around him have disappeared. As he begins investigating his environment, things become more and more unusual. What is happening? Where did everyone go?

The game involves many elements that are based upon historical events, information that comes from the Old and New Testaments of the Bible, and from extensive research on modern, recent, and historical events. The goal is to present an entertaining experience that is challenging and fun, and also adds value to the time invested. The gamer will learn about interesting times, people, and events in the process of unfolding a fictional story based upon fact. I hope that the game succeeds on its own merits as entertaining and well designed, but it is also important to me that it remains honorable to God, and this is the standard I am holding the project to.

Game Genre


The inspiration of the game is a sensory experience in the form of a psychological thriller. In this style, there are elements of a mystery genre, suspense, and intrigue. These things mean that the game may get creepy or spooky at certain times.


I intend to keep the gamer entertained as they advance through the story, and there may be times where you are surprised and even scared. The game's primary focus will remain on you, as Jesse. It is not driven by other characters that you meet along the way, as is often found in adventure games, but rather on the process of discovery itself, and the emotional, psychological, and spiritual effect that it has on the gamer. You will be given guidance as you move forward, by the "Narrator" character in the game, and you might meet some unwanted enemies in your quest for truth!

There may be times where you feel the need to escape a location, and other times that you may linger for some time, trying to solve one of the game's many challenges.

Adventure is an important element of the game. I hope to inspire the gamer as much as I hope to entertain them.

The Technology


The game uses an advanced form of C++ code, in a language called Dark Basic. The 3D environment has been developed over 7 years and continues
to be advanced and refined. Lighting and shadows can be rendered with a high quality, and reflective surfaces can allow light to pass through as if transparent, providing realistic windows. Motion feels smooth as you walk or run, going up or down stairs, and even underwater. The sound engine can track motion or remain stationary and can be activated by unknown elements in the game to keep the artificial intelligence fluid throughout game play. The game contains the ability to do custom scripting to make actions possible that go beyond the original code.


The 3D rendering process allows for culling, which waits to draw items to the screen until they are needed, in order to use the graphics processing in a measurable manner. 3D lighting of complex areas are 'baked' into the scenes before game play, and there are also live-rendered lights that can be used for other effects. There are animated 2D elements and multiple shading techniques used.

About The Game play


You will explore vast areas looking for clues and investigating information as you move forward through the game. You will search for clues that help unlock areas or gain access to new locations. Not everything is as it appears, and you will find that you may show up in the most unlikely locations. You will find that much of the game provides environmental puzzles, where your surroundings are part of the problem-solving skills required. Learning from your surroundings is also an important element, where you must search, read, and pay attention to the areas around you to gain information that will help you to progress forward.


In many situations, you will be able to see where you are trying to go, but the way to get there won't be immediately obvious. The game play is always ultimately a goal-oriented process that drives you forward to unlock new information.

The Story


"Through The Shadows" is about a young man named Jesse. He has a sense of purpose for his life, and he has a strong desire to make a difference in the world around him. He wakes up one day to find that everyone has disappeared and strange events start to occur. As Jesse sets out on his adventure, he discovers that he will find his place in the lineage of a people throughout history. They are called the Seekers of the Truth, and they have protected the true word of the Old and New Testaments of the Bible and placed crucial information in secret, protected locations that only someone like Jesse is meant to discover at a critical time of need. Jesse will progress through his adventure discovering the information that has been left for him,
and will find that he plays an important role in preserving the truth for future generations to come. He will face an evil opposition that wishes to harm him and to prevent his success, and ultimately to bring the eventual fall of all mankind.


The future of humanity is at stake, and Jesse has been called to do something in this fight against evil.

This is the foundation for the first Episode in what I hope will become a successful series of games in the "Through The Shadows" series.
This first episode, "The Darkness Revealed" provides the important groundwork for the works that can follow.

The music for this game is composed, recorded, mixed, and mastered by the game's designer, Michael Angel (that's me). I have a background in
music, recording, mixing, and mastering. I am working on a full orchestral score for the game. It has a realistic motion picture soundtrack
quality that I hope you will enjoy. The music plays an important role in the game to set the mood, atmosphere, and also memorable themes

The music will be carefully choreographed with the events of the game and also with a predetermined volume, so that is always a part
of the activity of the game without being an interference. In addition to traditional orchestral instruments, it will include other elements,
sound effects, electronic sounds, loops, and sonic textures that support the game's happenings, and I hope that you enjoy the music!

The creation of this game brings its own unique list of challenges. I have already worked out the majority of technical issues surrounding the game. The soundtrack will integrate into the game very well, and all of the primary artistic elements will work nicely. I have spent two years learning the scripting language, helping to work out some of the bugs in the engine and fine-tuning its use for Windows XP, and feel that with careful implementation and writing, it will be everything that I hope for it to be.

I have enlisted a few close friends and scholars to help with some of the fundamental spiritual, technical, and historical elements of the story and this will continue as I near my development goals.

One of the major challenges for this project has been to maintain the importance of its premise as a game that I have created for the Christian gamer, that is also designed to stand on its own merits as an independently developed game of a AAA core game standard. There can be a tendency to dislike entertainment that has an attached religious theme, and I am personally well aware of the stigma attached to the few Christian game efforts made over the years. This is the challenge, as it means overcoming stereotypes while striving for excellence in every technical and artistic manner, and even more so creating a project that holds a high moral standard that serves its creative purpose to such a degree that it is incredibly fun to play.

I believe that the project is well on its way to succeeding in all the above challenges, and one thing that is the biggest challenge of them all is development time.

In a technology world that slows down for no one, I have spent two years in research, testing, studying, and investing in the current technology for this game, and I see a necessary window of time that it needs to be created in. There is the challenge of it being played on the classic gaming PC while there are new platform options every month. It will most likely only remain on PC, so my goal for December 2013 is the target I am confident will remain relevant and possible to complete. With successful project funding, I am able to place this game at the forefront of the work I do over the next year, and I believe the potential for the standard of the final project could be raised purely by the time this can free for me, and the creative and emotional freedom that can come from that time. Just as important, there is nothing more valuable than time when it comes to testing a game, and the technical challenges alone can be overcome with much greater clarity of mind and focus if I have met my funding goal.

Once the project is funded, I will be playing multiple roles as developer and writer and musician. I feel confident that I will succeed with this project with funds in place. I will add a layer of protection to backing up the project most likely with extra drives and at least an additional rendering computer with multiple drives. I have found that the more time available for research, the more I find to incorporate into the depth of the story. My only concern for fulfilling the project on time if funding is reached, is if a natural disaster or other catastrophe caused the project to be ruined. I will try not to think about that aspect at all! :-) I consider it the highest importance to fulfill this project to its greatest standard and to definitely do so on time.

Shyla takes on the world as she unravels the secrets that lie just beneath her skin, revealing an alien God that wishes to live again.

Shyla lives with her father and brother in the woods, her mother disappeared when she was ten years old and never returned. Seven years later a stranger comes to her father from the woods, soon Shyla will begin a journey that will take her into a world she never could have imagined, a world far beyond her comprehension and far beyond limiting conceptions. 

For Shyla, there is a long road ahead, for those who have been watching her since birth, there is a long wait ahead and for the God that is living inside of her body, well for him, his long wait is over.

He will use Shyla to be re-birthed into existence and once again try to rule the universe. The question is…. 

Will Shyla be able to stand up to the tests that are coming her way? 

                                         Will she fulfill her destiny?

                           In due time all the answers will fall into place.

 Just Beneath The Skin is a cinematic/action puzzle RPG revolving around Shyla, whom unknowingly and unwillingly is forced into battling for her life and for the lives of many others.

JBS first started as a book idea, when I was younger I would write away in my notebook story ideas/plots and extend on them if I liked the base. I loved the concept of this game so much that I wrote an entire 150 page novel to really bring the JBS world alive, after several years of revisions and edits to the draft I thought about publishing it. Considering I know the story inside and out from what I envisioned it was more of a video game/anime then a book. So with that JBS for the PC was born, starting from scratch it is where it is today because of how much I love this story.

I also expanded on the JBS world and wrote two sequel books that not only bring you into more brand new worlds but continue where the story left off each book, pulling you in and making you want more.

                              Why RPG old school style?

In the era that I grew up in I played video games that were extremely hard, not only were they hard but you actually had to acquire a decent amount of skill to pass each level or a group of skills to beat a whole game. For me this is what video games represent, something to conquer by gaining skills, you see the challenge is what is most thrilling for me and most people I would assume. 

I wanted to bring games back to where they used to be, hard, hard and fun yet rewarding, without having to have a full 3D interface to enjoy it. Remember Ninja Turles for NES? How about Aladdin for Sega? These titles bring one thing to mind for me, SKILL! As you had to have skill to play or even beat these games. Not just that but these games did have bugs and perhaps there were things we didn't like about the game. Still we played anyways and for years we will continue to play, in any video game we play and adapt to the environments that are presented to us. 

With JBS it's all about adapting to the environment as it may change continuously and if you don't pay attention, you may get lost or confused, which is where the "Acquiring Skill" mode comes into play.

                              What will you find in JBS?

So what's in JBS? Puzzles, puzzles, puzzles, I love puzzle games as well as horror/survival but JBS isn't a horror game so I went with a more "surreal" experience to compensate. Dreamy and beautiful environments surround you only to keep you from the absolute truth, nothing in JBS is as it seems, at least almost nothing..... 

Besides puzzles there are many foes that Shyla must fight against in order to survive as she progresses on her journey. You see in order for the God inside Shyla to gain energy, Shyla must draw blood and never stop. As the game progresses you will be able to transform into Caldon (the ancient alien God inside of Shyla) during battle and use his extreme powers to surpass all who stand in their way. 

So we have puzzles, fighting and very deep plot? Interesting combination eh? What else is in here? Oh you know just stuff that most people who play RPG's love, check the features section below for full details and example images.

 3 different story lines to discover, 3 different lives to live

Cinematic Gameplay Style 

(Cut-scenes are a bare essential for any RPG game, with this style though I offer a more "Cinematic" view for the players enjoyment). 

 Stage Select

(I really love Devil May Cry and the Stage Select system they have, that feature def. inspired me to have something similar for JBS. This feature offers a small insight on what awaits the player in the levels ahead, possible rewards for accomplishing quests or side quests and what boss Shyla may have to face somewhere in the level.) 

 Complicated puzzles

(Puzzles are puzzles, they are intended to be hard, confusing and maybe even frustrating to those who can't figure them out. The early resident evil games were inspiration for the kind of puzzles found in JBS).

Manual Stat boosting capability

(Who doesn't love being able to manually boost your own stats instead of having to battle hundreds of foes to accomplish the same thing? I think everyone loves manual stat boosting, with that I just had to put it in because sometimes in JBS there aren't many foes around to practice on).

Resolution options 

(I always try to think "how can I make this more entertaining for the player" and with this I felt an interactive resolution changer was needed. Of course if you put the game in fullscreen from the beginning, this scene makes no difference but if your playing windowed it can help when playing on high resolution monitors.)

SBS (Sideview Battle System) and ATB (Active Time Battle System)

(Who could forget the old Final Fantasy and Breath of Fire battle scenes, well I can't and for one I really love the style, I wanted to stick to the old school gameplay style thus this was born).

Spirit Alignment System 

(Shyla’s spirit is fragile and as you progress in the game you will have choices to make, ones that will determine just how light or dark your soul becomes. For Shyla the end result, whether she succeeds to re-birth Caldon or not all depends on the status of her spirit, if she dwells in the darkness too much she may be consumed and disappear forever but on the other hand if she resides in the light she may even be freed from the burden placed upon her).

***Status will be displayed on main in-game menu***

Light (work in progress)

 Dark (work in progress)

Journal 

(Shyla stores/records main-quest and side-quest information in her Journal, you can access the journal from the in-game menu).

 Side-Quests 

(Extending the possibilities for various game play experiences, side-quests have started to be implemented offering in game cheat codes, secret item, secret accessories, etc. for successfully completing quests).

Quick save and Auto-save (for saving on the go when it’s dire)

(Sometimes you get really into the game and forget to save, if you can save anytime that is but sometimes you just can't. That's something I really didn't like in a lot of games, was the lack of ability to save whenever or even have an autosave save for you just in case. Well you don't have to worry about that in JBS, an autosave occurs every time you change rooms, exit menu or start a new level. Not only that but you can quick save anytime when not in a cut-scene by simply pressing *S*).

30+ stages to explore and conquer (for starters)

Live the life of a lawyer in a vast, open world city where you must rise to the top of your game - however you choose to play

    "If a law is unjust, a man is not only right to disobey it, he is obligated to do so"

A release date for the game will be revealed in a further update after we have begun full-time work on the project and have a sense of your interest in it.

Awesome, you found us! Below we have a quick overview of the project. Want to know more? Keep reading. We hope you love what you hear! (psst, head over to Twitter and hit follow - help spread the word! We are also over at crowdfundingforum.com if you want to join in the discussion with the community about 'Lawsuit' and loads of other crowd funding projects!

Lawsuit is an open world game that will bring a new law based genre to your screens. You play the part of a young lawyer tasked with uncovering and stopping a series of dark dealings on wall street - revolving around the actions of a corporate raider. Explore the city, fight in court and break your opponents - however you see fit.

Interested? Click the play button above and the magic of the internet will display a short video where we can tell you just what we are trying to achieve - then read on to find out where you fit in.

(Oh, by the way, take a look at our brand new website and blog - we will be posting updates about our team and project on there as well as here on Kickstarter!)

Lawsuit (working title!) is an open world game based in a reimagined New York city. You will take control of a lawyer trying to find his way in the vast urban landscape (30 x 30 miles of skyscrapers, parks and courtrooms) as you take on cases for one of 5 law firms (with side missions, of course) and try to raise to the top of the city's most lucrative profession. It is entirely up to you how you play, aggressively push witnesses until they break or carefully piece a story together - as long as the jury are on your side, you can win however you want. This is a big project and we want to get it perfect - from the living city and its inhabitants to the fierce courtroom dramas and battles. This is why we do not want to set a release date too early on in the process - you do not want to play a rushed game! After we have begun full time work on the project with the 'Kickstart' it needs from you we will be able to gather a realistic estimate of when the game will be finished. At the moment, we are expecting to spend at least the best part of 2014 to get it to a standard that we are happy with - this may seem a long way away but - with your rewards arriving much earlier - we promise that it will be worth the wait.

Randomisation and Simulation

Our team (we will get to them later) wanted to stay away from linear storytelling as much as we could - so we built randomisation into the city. Each and every person you see, talk to or meet will have their own characteristics which determine how they act, react and behave. Meaning that not only will the cases be different as you play through the storyline (even multiple times), the characters you meet will also have their personalities change - you must adapt to win. Side missions (other than backer designed ones) will also be randomly created on-the-fly by our system to give an almost limitless array of possibilities in terms of cases for you to take on.

Each and every person does not only have their own characteristics, they also have their own life. Jobs, family and routines all play a part in the game. Different routines mean different witnesses to each crime and even the city itself can be affected if inhabitants working in certain fields become unhappy. Are the top dogs on wall street corrupt? Be prepared for a host of financial cases. Is a top celebrity mixing with the wrong people? Be prepared to make a killing. Literally. All cases react to the city and vice versa.

Always changing, always new.

Mechanics

Think of Lawsuit as a cross between 'GTA' and 'Heavy Rain'; the open world exploration aspects of GTA that allow you to travel, uncover witnesses and of course expose evidence for you to present in court combined with the high pressure decision making aspects of 'Heavy Rain' as you choose which questions to ask, which evidence to show and how you will dictate your own path to victory. 

This aspect of choice plays a massive role in Lawsuit. There is never one way to victory or one correct question to ask. You, the player, must choose which order to present evidence, which questions to ask and how to back them up. Characters will react realistically based on their individual characteristics and the choices that are made will all have consequences and repercussions. In following updates we will add write ups of some cases that didn't quite make it into the game (to avoid giving any hints of how to win the cases that did make it) outlining how each one could play out for different players and how they could impact the rest of the game.

There is no 'game over' screen, no 'mission failed' - just choices and decisions that you must live with. Could your failures leave an innocent man in prison - or a guilty man on the streets?

'AI' plays a big part too, our judges will think and act intelligently and the inhabitants of our city will all have independent personalities and thoughts about you. We have also built an information distribution system embedded into the core of the game - meaning your actions and any other important information will reverberate through the city realistically. Information is passed on from person to person - bringing more life and realism to how the city will react to your actions. Feared or respected, you will have to earn your place.

Our team may be small, yet we see this as an advantage. We have retained the individuality and personal touch that has been lost in endless 'big name' titles. We have one very talented coder and two 'creative types' that will make everything look great. Together we also have awesome connections with the music industry so the soundtrack will be as top notch as the game (grab it as a reward! Hint Hint) - more on that in a later update.

You can see us as small, you can see us a connected. Either way, it is more than enough to deliver a great experience.

Why trust us?

We understand that trusting a team that you don't know maybe a little hard to justify - but trust is a two way street. We are handing this project to you to 'kickstart' it - each and every person that pledges will become a part of the team in one way or another. Don't see us as a three person team trying to take on the industry. See us as a growing community lead by us - for you. The forum, blog and Kickstarter page will be constantly updated letting you know how our baby is coming along until the day it sees the light.

If you are still sceptical, feel free to leave a comment or PM me with any information you require (even if you just fancy a chat) - I am happy to oblige for our amazing backers! Also, check out our website at the top where we will be keeping a public blog. We admit, we have not worked on a project on this scale before - but we all have experience in our fields with work published on the web, on IOS and Android. Trust us, if you as the community do your part, we as a team will do ours.

Our Plan

We intend to distribute the game through Steam - a link to the greenlight campaign will be published in an update when it goes live (as well as any other PC / Mac distribution platforms we can get onto such as GOG) our own website and, if we can get the licensing, maybe even the playstation network! The game will be available for Windows, Mac and Linux for the moment but, as I said, we hope to grow that list. 

We do not want to release too many stretch goals too early, we want to keep some surprises for later if enough interest is shown - but here is a few we think you will be excited about. 

Oculus Rift - £45,000

We love the idea of the Oculus Rift and think it fits in perfectly with both our open world environment and courtroom settings. Imagine actually exploring our re-imagined New York City through your own eyes as if you were there before emerging yourself even more in the courtroom battles. For an extra £5,000 we can get the Oculus Rift support finished for the initial release of the game. Like our Multiplayer stretch goal (see below) we will add this stretch goal no matter how much money is pledged - yet reaching these goals will enable us to get them finished for the initial release and not as an update months later.

Multiplayer - £60,000

We already have big plans for the single player Lawsuit game - but with multiplayer we believe we are revolutionising another area of gaming. The same fully simulated city with a randomised case and a limited time to gather your evidence and witnesses. This is no place for the button mashing found in big-publisher shooters. It is time for a multiplayer PvP that requires intelligence and tactics under real-time pressure. Genuine skills and thought needed. There will also be a ranking system - beat a top lawyer and you will rack up points faster than if you beat a small time associate on our leaderboards.

Why is multiplayer one of the first stretch goals? Because we want it to happen! It will cost more time and money for multiplayer than we could possibly expect you to pledge, but we want it so bad we are willing to take the hit ourselves to create a truly awesome experience.

Multiplayer will always get made no matter how much money you pledge - this stretch goal will just allow us to bring it to you much quicker and allow the community to mold it into the experience we all want it to be through the beautiful communication Kickstarter allows us to have with you.

For anyone wondering about the possibility of seeing our game on the playstation network, that will not be listed as a stretch goal - if we get the go-ahead from Sony then we will make it happen, no extra funds required from you!

Its your money, we won't hide anything from you. Below is a full overview of where the money is going. It may look cheap for a game of this scale - but bear in mind that we are funding this ourselves as well.

This is only an overview of where your pledges will go. 

Software (e.g Unity Pro game engine) - £2,000

Hardware (e.g Motion Capture equipment) - £14,000

Unexpected expenses (everything that is left over from this will be put back into kickstarter projects) - £5,000

Voice Acting - £3,000

Keeping our very small working area paid for and warm - £1,000

Music and Soundtrack - £5,000

Kickstarter reward fulfillment - £2,000

Supporting the team on the job (lots of coffee is needed for a project this size!) - £1,000

Initial hiring of servers to begin building and testing multiplayer - £4,000

Advertising and outreach - £3,000

Any other expenses will be paid for by our own funds - All of your money will go into the project, meaning none of it will be used to pay salaries. 

We have already begun work on the game and have many of the core coding mechanics in place. But who wants to look at code? We are working as hard as possible to get something playable up and running so that you get a chance to see the game in action ASAP. Next, however, we want to make the world as beautiful as it is vast and alive. Our main goal is to make the world feel like New York - and what comes to mind when you think of New York? Skyscrapers.

These are very, very early models (and textures!) of New York and its courtrooms that we have been using as placeholders for testing code. We know that - for the immersive world that you and me both want - we will need to do much better! For now - imagine the streets filled with people, shops and conspiracies for you to crack.

Thank you for your precious time (and money... hopefully!) - after seeing the success of Satellite Reign (of which we are proud backers) we have great hope for the space in the market for immersive, open world games. We truly hope that you will allow our idea to come to light. You will be hearing a lot from us over the next few weeks - and we hope we can hear a lot from you! Again, feel free to communicate however you can (comment, PM, twitter, messenger pigeons) with any questions you want.

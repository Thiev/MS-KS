An epic multi-player arena space shooter for small gang combat. Currently being built with cutting edge HTML5. Join the adventure!

Welcome!

Straight away I must say thank you for checking out this project.  Regardless of your support level, your interest in this game and the space genre is worth more!  Please share with everyone you know.  Check out our official page www.factionsofki.com and like us on  facebook.com/factionsofki.

Project Definition:

Factions of Ki is a tactical space shooter.  This means that gameplay is not all about twitch based flight and lasers.  Instead, we have a mouse flight system (similar to freelancer) coupled with scenarios that require team work and strategy.  The player must out maneuver their opponent and place their teammates in tactical positions to score hits on their opponents all in real time.  The guns will autotrack the selected target and deal damage similar to MMO style combat.  A pilots position and relative velocities will play key roles in how much damage is dealt to the enemy.  

The game is also built around arena combat (similar to league of legends).  This small gang (5v5) combat is extremely engaging.  In many games, if teams grow beyond 7 players the matches become a free for all.  No one follows orders, and tactics can not be brought to bear on the enemy (exclude 10 hour blood bath fleet fights of EVE Online).  Enter Factions of Ki. Gameplay is designed to last 25-30 minutes while players make coordinated attacks to capture control points, complete / destroy mission objectives and defend against enemy forces.  Join up with your friends, activate your guns and get ready for an explosive experience!

Motivation:

My passion is to play and experience space games!  Games such as Colony Wars (PS1), Priveteer, Freelancer, Homeworld, Tachyon The Fringe, Earth And Beyond (Beta Tester), SWTOR and Eve Online have all shaped my intense desire to be a space pilot.  I love this genre.  However, very little currently exists on the market to wet my appetite.  Eve Online is fantastic, but I am bored with sitting in a station fitting a ship and flying all over the universe for modules.  Then grouping up for a 4 hour fleet battle where one sittings for 3 hours and then dies after 15 minutes of combat.  It is a fantastic game but it is best played in the background or while trading stocks!  I wanted something more intense...A 30 minute game session with my friends before bed or multiple sessions to burn a lazy Saturday.  With the recent death of Jumpgate Evolution and Black Prophecy the only choice left was to start making my own game.  Therefore, I present this project in it's current state for you to enjoy and help bring to life!

Why Kickstarter?

I think Sean from "Kinetic Void" (check out his team's kickstarter project!) said it best, I'll paraphrase!  "Frankly no game company will touch this genre.  It's dead to them.  So we need crowd funding to create the games that space fans want to play."  So there you have it...Instead of starting with massive funding and burning in a fire of corporate hullabaloo, Factions of Ki is designed to start with a core of loyal fans and grow as the player base increases. This indie start-up method gives you, the players, the best chance to see the product come to life.  Then by expanding the completed game we can sustain the brand on a long term path!  

Using Kickstarter allows us to create our initial player base while gathering funds for the financial needs listed below.  We hope that your contribution to this project is not simply monetary.  We want the backers to be engaged in the development process over the coming months to help shape the experience of the final product!

The Bigger Picture:

Consider a solar system divided into sectors.  Each arena battle takes place between rival factions in one of these sectors.  The winning faction of that battle receives a portion of control of that sector.  Once a faction has 80% control of a sector, the sector produces small attribute bonuses that are spread among all the players of the controlling faction.  Expand this to several thousand sectors and 4 factions and we have a macro game dynamic where every small battle has consequence.  Some factions and players may choose to fight for sectors with speed bonuses, while others choose damage.  A voting system will be used for your faction to choose what direction to take.  Will you be loyal to the directed path or change alliance? How will you affect the outcome?

Why HTML 5?

Pushing the boundaries is always incredibly engaging.  The cutting edge of HTML 5 is allowing content and games to be rendered directly to the browser.  The game is served straight to a player without the need for client downloads, updates, patches or browser plug-ins (no adobe, no silverlight, no java, no flash and no unity).  Riding this new wave of innovation is the definition of web development.  If people don't utilize and test the cutting edge, then we hold back the next big thing.  I am excited to use HTML 5.  I hope you find this new technology equally amazing!  Again, check out the dev-blog for specifics on how we are using HTML 5 elements to create Factions Of Ki.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Difficultly #1: Art

As an engineer the calculations, logic, game flow, physics and networking are much easier to handle than spending hours building an artistic 3D model. So my first hurdle will be accomplished by using the funds you provide to purchase content from artists who are millions of times more talented than myself! I look forward to bringing you high quality content and shiny new ships to fly and destroy.

Difficulty #2 - Servers

Networking for a multiplayer game is expensive. The intention is to fund longevity through a content system where players can purchase aesthetic items and playable ships yet not be able to advance themselves with money. For the initial launch, the funds you contribute will help us provide these servers and keep the game active for about a year.

Difficulty #3 - Fanbase

Space fans are loyal, but space gamers are a rare breed. Very few companies put space shooters on the production table and even fewer produce a playable product. My passion stems from this lack of recent content in the space genre. As space fans we need to show our support for the genre and the other brave indie developers helping us to fly among the stars! Connecting with this small core of loyal space pilots is going to be a challenge. However, it is a challenge I want to attack! Having an indie start means we can grow with the player base. The initial funds will help us launch and grow the first stages of existence from which there is no limit. Our initial investment is low so we will not be burdened by corporate backers who demand profits! The game content and potential will be limited only by what players can imagine!

FAQ

  • Will this run on PC, Mac or Linux?

    Yes! Currently you need to have Google Chrome on any system to run the demo at the official page. However, all three will be supported at launch so long as you have a HTML 5 capable browser. These include Google Chrome v27+, Mozilla and Safari (For users with Snow Leopard). The important thing is that we must have browsers that support webGL for graphics content.

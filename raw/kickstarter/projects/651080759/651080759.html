Hey fans and possible backers,

First and foremost, this is not going to be an online flash based game. It'll be on the same quality level as old Lucas Arts adventure games. It'll most likely be sold through Steam and direct download after it's release.

That being said, I've been tossing this idea back and forth in my head for quite some time now, and finally got to work on it in May of 2011. The idea is to have a download-able game in the series of Reincarnation on the scale of such classics as the Monkey Island series, Sam and Max Series and just about any Lucas Arts adventure game.

I've done a kickstarter project in the past to get dolls made of Vile the demon character from the game, and it was very successful. Therefore, I felt I'd give it another try with this project.

So, the idea behind this project is that I'll be having a downloadable game in the Reincarnation series which I'll be able to sell on the internet. The game will be a much longer and a more entertaining version of what I've been making for free. I'm planning on having over 50+ scenes to explore, a wide selection of characters to interact with in hell and on earth, different Reincarnies to find, and a wide array of silly puzzles to figure out in-order to finish your quest.

  • Each character will be fully voiced.
  • Each level will be beautifully rendered.
  • Characters will have plenty of animations for all their actions.
  • 2D + 3D cut scenes.

Reincarnation Series:

It was originally going to be an online comic, but I wanted to make it interactive. So, I looked to the inspiration of my love for classic adventure quest games. Hence, I merged the two ideas to finally release a free point & click game on 10/29/2008 called Reincarnation: A Demon's Day Out. It started getting a lot of good feedback and some followers which made me want to make another one. Then another. And Another. And so on... Now with 10+ free games circling the internet, I feel it's time I release a mega game!

The Root of All Evil will have over 4 hours of game play if you are really good (or using a walkthrough) and answer a lot of questions about the world that Vile lives in. The story begins after Vile wakes up from a wild, partying bender and finds Hell to not be as he had left it. Why should I give you any money for this game?

Good question! I have been doing game design since 2005 with my first release of Griswold the Goblin. So far, all my games have been free for anyone to play on internet. I've released over 15 Point and Click adventure games and several other well received genres as well.

If you are or have ever been a fan of point and click adventures, this is the project you want to support. It'll contain all the great humor you would expect from a game of this genre, but it will have a dark twist!


Things to expect from the game you help produce:

  • Highly animated intros, cut-scenes, and other graphics.
  • Well thought out puzzles ranging from easy to difficult.
  • Four (4) chapters to explore.
  • Tons of jokes.
  • Unlockable achievements.
  • Hours of game-play with save/load features.
  • Flawless coding.
  • Excellent voice acting.

FAQ

  • Will you sign any of the physical rewards I get upon request?

    Yes. Just let me know after the project is finished and I'll sign whatever you have ordered.

    Last updated: Mon, May 14 2012 2:28 pm EDT
  • Will the interface be the same as the free versions?

    No. The series is getting a complete makeover as far as gameplay and interface is concerned. The inventory with be completely redone to suit the rest of the game. Nothing will be on the screen to give it more of a playable movie feel.

    Last updated: Wed, May 16 2012 6:20 pm EDT
  • Will it be available on Linux?

    We will probably be exporting with Unity Pro and will have to wait and see if they end up making a support for the Linux OS. Anything we can export to, we will.

    I just recently read some stuff on Wikipedia that Unity plans on games being able to be played on the Linux system. Infact, the wiki article mentions a game that was funded on here called Wasteland 2.

    Last updated: Mon, Jun 11 2012 10:44 am EDT
  • I don't have a credit card, is there another way to donate?

    Yes. Since I believe in this project 100%, I've set up a way to still preorder the game through paypal. You will be put on a special mailing list this way in order to keep up with all the details of the project.

    Last updated: Mon, Jun 11 2012 10:44 am EDT
  • I gotta wait till August 2013 to get all my swag!?

    I gotta wait till August 2013 to get all my swag!?

    No. Once the project is funded, I'll start sending out merchandise that I have in stock. IE: T-shirts, stickers, and dolls.

    August 2013 is just my hopeful due date to have a copy of the game to everyone.

    Last updated: Mon, Jun 11 2012 10:44 am EDT
  • Why only $15,000 when other projects of the same scale are asking way more?

    The $15,000 is the money we need to get the project started without making a dime our self. It's going to go to getting the software and the different ports we will need for PC, Mac, Android, Linux, and iPhone.

    So, that's why we only have it at 15k. However, by all means try to get it pass that price so that we will have money left over to do more with this project (and be able to pay everyone working on it too!)

    Last updated: Mon, Jun 11 2012 10:44 am EDT
  • I noticed you only had the mac or pc version at the 15 dollar reward, can I replace that with the iphone, linux, or any other version?

    Yes. You will be able to download the game in any version we have available.

    Last updated: Mon, Jun 11 2012 10:44 am EDT
  • Will there be a DRM free version of the game?

    Yes.

    Last updated: Mon, Jun 11 2012 10:44 am EDT

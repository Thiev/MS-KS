You are a Sorcerer Lord ruling a small village from the heights of your stone tower. You dream of conquering the planes and ending the War of Ascension at last. Your knowledge and power are great, but they must become greater still if you are to overcome your foes. Your empire must expand, your skill increase, your legions swell. This plane and all others will bow to your will!To put that in more quantifiable terms: Worlds of Magic is a classic 4X strategy game set in a fantasy universe. It follows in the footsteps of the game that, in many ways, defined the fantasy 4X genre: MicroProse's Master of Magic.

Primary gameplay takes place on the world map. From there you can move your armies to explore new territory, defend key positions or attack your enemies. You can gain control of additional cities by conquering them or founding new ones yourself. You manage the cities under your control, building new buildings to improve them, dedicating your work force to one task or another, and creating new military units. Your armies can be made up of units you've trained, heroes and mercenaries you've hired, and even powerful summoned creatures. When two enemy armies collide gameplay is moved to the tactical battle map where combat is played out turn by turn. During gameplay you will also research new spells, manage your economy, and deal with diplomatic situations all in an attempt to gain supreme control of the universe.

Worlds of Magic will not only seek to capture the charm of the timeless classic that serves as its inspiration, but also go beyond it in a number of areas.

Additional Factions: We want to put as many playable races as we can in Worlds of Magic. Needless to say, however, adding a new faction requires a lot of artwork. Each faction has units and buildings that are completely unique. The funding for additional factions will go to getting the artwork we need in the amount of time we have.

Additional Roaming Creatures: What are roaming creatures? Well, they're creatures that... well... roam. That is to say, they are creatures that don't fit into any society. Basilisks, cockatrice, dragons, drakes and giant snakes... (We're sure you get the idea.) Again, adding them to Worlds of Magic is going to require additional artwork (and a bit of extra code). You might find one guarding a dungeon or run into one just wandering the countryside. It most cases it will end in battle, but the very wise (or very prepared) may be able to convince the beast to join their ranks. (You can get some of them to join your army.) Roaming Creatures are one of the things we really like and we want to put more of them in the game!

Choose from 10 Sorcerer Lords or create your own: Worlds of Magic offers players ten different pre-created Sorcerer Lords to play as or face as AI opponents, all with unique skills, powers and knowledge. If you prefer you can also create a custom Sorcerer Lord allowing you to select the circles of magic and different disciplines you will use to overthrow the planes.

Over 300 spells sorted into 12 magic circles: Spells in Worlds of Magic are sorted into twelve different Circles focused on the energies that fuel the spell or the spell's effects. Become a life mage or summoner, master all the spells of earth or just those that destroy. The choice is yours. Add spells to your spellbook through research, take them by conquest or find them in unexplored ruins.

Choose from a large number of disciplines: Not all sorcerer skills can be found in a spell book. Select disciplines to give your Sorcerer Lord the powers you need for conquest. Learn the secrets of Alchemy and turn mana into gold. Become an Artificer and make powerful magical artifacts with ease. Or learn the art of war and become a powerful Warlord.Â 

Spellbook full of magic

A Fantasy Setting: Worlds of Magic is set in a fantasized medieval world filled with magic and fanciful creatures. You will see cites of Elves, Dwarves and Undead. You can discover lost shrines and fallen temples guarded by towering giants or fearsome dragons. The world will span a number of different planes each of which can be explored and conquered.

8 Playable Factions: Select one race to be the founders of your empire. Overthrow others and make them serve you or raze their cities to the ground. Each faction has unique units along with it's own strengths and weaknesses. Dwarves are lords of the forge and make powerful weapons, armor and even constructs. Elves are lovers of nature and nature itself serves to protect them. Each has their own feel, their own flavor.

Undead as a Playable Race: We wanted to offer a truly unique feel for undead in Worlds of Magic. Not just humans dressed up like zombies. The undead pay no taxes, eat no food, don't want to get a villa down by the beach. They have one goal: The extermination of all life. Choosing to play as Undead is going to alter the entire game play experience. We want it to be something with a truly unique feel.

Tactical Combat: Battles in Worlds of Magic take place on a tactical combat map. Trained units, summoned creatures and hired allies will all join the ranks of your armies. You will be able to support your units with powerful spells. Summon more creatures to the battlefield or blast your enemies with a devastating fireball.

Arena Mode: Want to hone your tactical combat skills without playing a full game? Good news! That's exactly what arena mode allows you to do. Build an army right on the battle map. You can play with and fight against any unit in the game. Test unit abilities, try out new strategies, or just prove to your buddies what a brilliant tactician you are!

D20 Combat Mechanics: Thoroughly play tested, balanced, and offering in-depth mechanics the D20 OGL rule set makes a perfect base for turn-based tactical combat. Worlds of Magic makes use of the rule set to offer units that are unique and flavorful while at the same time ensuring that none are under or over powered.


Units gain experience: All military units in Worlds of Magic gain experience. As they do so they gain levels and become more and more powerful. Even the lowliest of units can become an effective fighting force given time and patience.

Over 80 different military units: Each faction offers a number of trainable military units. In addition the world is filled with wandering monsters and powerful guardians. Face them in battle or try to convince them to join you cause.

Hire heroes and champions to lead your armies: Worlds of Magic offers a large number of hand crafted heroes and champions for you to recruit as well as randomly generated ones that can rarely be promoted from normal military units. They can gain more experience than other units and can be equipped with a number of magical artifacts. Use them to lead you armies or join them together into an elite corps.

Procedurally Generated Terrain: Every game of Worlds of Magic can be unique. The entire game universe is generated procedurally and can be altered with a number of settings, be given a completely random key or generated based on a key entered manually. You can experience completely new worlds each game, share keys with your friends or enjoy the same game world each time. It's up to you.

Huge Maps: Want to spend days conquering the universe? Worlds of Magic can let you do that. Create games with hundreds of thousands of accessible world tiles. Build hundreds of cities and explore thousands of world features, such as forgotten dungeons and lost cities. Just how big a universe do you want to overthrow?

7 planes of existence to explore and conquer: Explore the Prime Material Plane (a world much like our own) and discover magic portals to the elemental planes of existence. Dive into the Shadow Plane and face the undead legion or head to the Radiant Plane and build a paradise. Expand you empire into the realms of Air, Earth, Fire and Water. Don't just conquer the world, conquer the planes!

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Time. One of the greatest challenges we have to face is getting all the features we want in Worlds of Magic by our target date. Needless to say, as we hit more and more stretch goals there will be more and more artwork to be completed before the game's final release. Now, that will be offset to some extent by the fact that we will be able to hire more artists. However, we have to make sure that any newcomers share the same artistic vision for the game as our current team. Although none of this should delay our Beta version, which we plan to have ready by 4Q 2013, it may delay the final release of the game simply because of the additional workload. We will certainly do our best to hit our deadlines, but in our minds quality is more important that a set in stone release date. We feel that, even if there should be a slight delay, the wait will be worth it. We will, of course, keep the community updated and let them know as we reach development milestones.

Mr. Utility is an action adventure platforming game with strategic elements and gameplay tweaks thrown in to add some depth and variety. The aim is to get you engrossed in the very engaging gameplay and  applying graphics.

The story

Mr Utility returns home one day to find Fluffytown empty and in ruins. After discovering a tattered note hinting at the location of his beloved Mayor Mr Utility ventures back into the dangerous world to rescue the Mayor and the rest of the townsfolk. 

After developing for just under a year, the game is now in it’s second round of early alpha stage and is almost ready for you to play with all feedback addressed and glitches fixed (Hopefully)

Check out the first ever concept level Pierre used to pitch and recruit Team Utility.

Once the game is finished it will consist of 7 levels including the hub world. The alpha demo consists of 2 levels the tutorial and dungeon level, two weapons ‘The cage trap’ and ‘The dynamite’.

Features

Huge Levels To explore

Add-on Content. ( Downloadable Levels and Bosses to explore and battle. )

Online Leaderboards. ( Try and get the best play through times for levels. And compete for the best time with people all over the world. ) SPEED RUNS ARE BACK!

Character Customisation. ( Customise Mr Utility how you please with hidden items found in levels and from rewards for quests. )

Free Camera Control. ( Take control of the camera and take screenshots of this beautiful world and its inhabitants. And share them with your friends. ) 

Achievements.

Controller Support. ( Not a pc gamer? Not to worry play Mr Utility with a Controller. Plug in and Play )

Choose your load out. ( Mr Utility finds many items along the way but he can only take three items into a level. Choose the right items for the right situation. )

The tutorial level – This is the first level in Mr. Utility. This is where you will get to grips with the controls and goals of the game. The new tutorial level has been completely revamped from the first demo released, with all new graphics and level design.

The Dungeon level - This is the second level from the demo where Mr. Utility must save the townsfolk of Fluffy Town. This new dungeon level you will encounter darker environments and the level of difficulty has been ramped up. 

Team Utility are big fans of music in games like Super Mario and Zelda.To achieve this level of quality we need your help to get us there. Have a listen to what we have so far! 

Mr Utility offers a brand new and exciting way to control and deploy items to defeat your enemies. On top of this brand new control scheme Mr Utility is also grounded in video gameplay tradition. 

While most platforms require you to navigate the player, we require you to move the player and also pay very close attention to his surroundings making sure he is not in danger and if he is, finding the right tool for the job.    

Platforms

The game will be available on Mac OSX, Windows, and Linux. On Mac OSX and Windows the game can also be played with a XBOX 360 controller.

3D Platforming 

Mr Utility runs and leap across huge gaps and get to hard to reach places, evade the bad guys, and explore every nook and cranny of the each level in full 3D.

Side Quest 

Whilst in Fluffy Town you will come across plenty of friendly and witty townsfolk who need your help. Something as simple as delivering a letter to collecting the most interesting of items there is plenty to do and explore.

Puzzle and Items

Mr Utility will come across many items on his journey. Wield them to battle enemies and solve puzzles and reach new areas.

The items in this game are essential; they are Mr Utility’s main way of defeating and evading his enemies. At the moment we currently have five, one of them is still in development. We would love you our ‘backers’ to be involved with our next item, brainstorm ideas. Be apart of the development process.

Pierre Joshua – Game designer (Creator)

Location: London, United Kingdom

Favourite Game: Zelda Windwaker

Pierre fell in love with the video game industry the first time he played Super Mario World 2 on the original gameboy. Pierre then decided to get into the video game industry through animation. He studied as animator for three years.  After graduating in 2011 he decided to start his own company and work on the ideas and projects that he was passionate about. Early this year Pierre decided it was time for the world to meet Mr Utility.

Cristian Alexandru Geambasu - Programming

Location: Bucharest, Romania

Favourite Game: The Witcher

Cristi knew he wanted to be a video game developer from a young age.He started to learn programming on his own and after a couple of years. he met Pierre and decided to help him bring Mr Utility to life.

Johan Hallen, - Programming

Location: Malmoe, Sweden.

Favorite game: Crash Bandicoot

Hallen's first console was a Sega Genesis. he started programming around 13 years old. Hallen mostly self-taught and watched a lot of lectures online from different universities and read a lot in the field of programming. Hallen got into game development a couple of years back when searching for a good game engine to build his first game from, and found Unity. A great game engine with a lovely community. Some years later Hallen saw a post on the Unity forums asking for a programmer for an untitled game that had a very unique and cartoony look and he thought, what the hell, I'll help this guy out. And that's how he met Pierre.

Aaron David Kaminer – 3D artist

Location: Warner robins, Georgia. usa

Favourite game: Mega Man

Aaron have been interested in game development since he was a child. Aaron finally began to learn and create games in 2006. he started with free 2d engines where he quickly learned game development and design processes. soon after he picked up an interest in 3d game design. He has spent the past 6 years learning everything he could about 3d modelling. Aaron has worked on nearly one dozen games in his time developing game assets. Aaron saw an opportunity to work side by side with several talented people who had an original idea and he jumped on the team.

After many discussions on the how long the development of the game would take, the team has agreed that 7 levels would take around 7 months to complete (One level and month ) But the rest of the year would be used for testing,ironing out the glitches and really polishing the game.

Once we have a substantial script, we would add in voice talent. Adding new levels to the game would defiantly be a direction we would go. We would also port the game on the Ouya console. FAQ

  • I can’t get the demo to open on my mac?

    No problem! just follow these simple steps:

    Step one: All you have to do is go to your system preferences located in your apple menu in the top left hand corner of your screen.
    Step two: Click on Security & Privacy
    Step three: Click the General tab.
    Step four: Click on the lock icon at the bottom left corner of the window (only if you can’t do the next step)
    Step five: Select the 'Anywhere' option.
    Step six: Close the window and relaunch Mr Utility. Enjoy!!!

An amped sci-fi platforming adventure sure to cater to the Metroid/Mega-man fan in everyone. Except this time, with friends!

Welcome

Video games nowadays come in all shapes and sizes. We have been continuously surprised as new features and better graphics push the envelope year after year. However, I'm sure we have all noticed that the diversity of genres between new releases is pretty sparse with 2 or 3 new First Person Shooters, Fantasy MMOs, or gruesome RPGs being released every year.

Although we have nothing against these genres, we feel that pushing the envelope isn't always where the best ideas lay. This is why we are launching this project, A visit to our roots never hurt and we feel that building from the ground up is sometimes better then trying to rake our brains on creating the next best thing.

And that's what we're here for, to see if there are any like minded individuals out there that'd want us to help begin real development of this project.

About Exodae

The Game

Exodae is our take on the side-scrolling platformer genre that was all the rage a few years back. Most notable titles of this style being Super Metroid and several of the Mega-Man games. While these series have evolved since then, perhaps not in the best of ways. We're sure a majority of the fans that  like these types of games agree with us that perhaps these gems were good as they were and didn't need to move to 3-D planes.

But back to Exodae, the biggest feature of Exodae will be its 4 - player cooperative multi-player. When you play a game with someone else, its experience changes entirely. Playing with 4 is just down right awesome. Exodae will feature multiple paths through each campaign, some available to only 1 player, others to only 4.This way every squad can choose their own way through the story. The players will also have to interact with each other to clear obstacles and navigate the deadly catacombs. It will almost be a different game each time you play, depending on who you choose to be your trust worthy comrades. So choose your squad well soldier! 

The Story
In the year 2030, the Earth finally made contact with an extraterrestrial race, who introduced themselves as the Karr. They appeared to be a timid species, having highly developed technology, but having no apparent need for force, making us believe that the universe was overall peaceful.

Blinded by their proposition of shared space travel technology in return for being allowed to immerse themselves in our culture as a study venue, we eagerly welcomed them to the planet. However, during the years of the seemingly prosperous alliance, we failed to notice the Karr secretly delving into the chasms of the Earth, preparing to launch an attack from where we never would've expected. Home.

A valiant effort was put forward, but the Humans were no match for the Karr who had been planning the attack for decades. The Humans were forced to leave their homes, fleeing into outer space. After the Human Exodus from their planet, they claimed it as dead, but used the newly gained technology to establish several space colonies. Since then the humans have dedicated themselves to ensuring that similar events do not befall any other sentinent species in the universe. A lifelong war had begun.

A century later, players will take control of a small squad of Space Marines, part of an intergalactic defense squad established by the Humans to trace and prevent the Karr from committing repeat offenses when possible.

While on a routine cruise, the troopers intercept a distress signal of Human origin, surprisingly sent from the Earth itself. Which was still believed to be dead, stripped of its resources by the Karr. The signal warranted enough interest for the approval to investigate, and the troopers change course to check it out.

What they discover is a surprise and will be up to the players to sculpt and mold the story into an adventure of their own.

Community Development

Everyone's had that moment where we thought that a game or feature of a game could have been handled or done differently, perhaps changed or even completely removed.

We'd like to prevent as many of those moments as possible by introducing our Community Development Program. All of our backers will be given a vote to how/which concepts and designs of the game should be used/included or changed/removed.

Depending on the backing tier you have, the more content you're given access to customize and have a vote on. This way we make sure that everyone knows what they're backing.

As daunting a task as this may seem like, its simply a matter of catering to our audience. We figure why bother even trying to make a game if you're not paying attention to the people who are actually going to play it?

The Documentary

EVERYTHING we do will be available for the community to see and give input on, from designing enemy characters to how we fall alseep at our desks after a long nights work. We want our backers to feel just as involved in the game development process as we are. A thing that many gamers have yet to experience. From the actual video documentary, to facebook, twitter and website updates, we want to make sure your in the know.

Community Development Tiers

There's a few ways we've set up our Development site to help backers get their word in.

Backer Vote: EVERYONE that backs even just 1$ is given the right to vote on a major features/content. Once the concept is honed down well enough, we open the idea to the backers to decide if its worth it. This way, even the little guys have a say.

Developer Vote: Backers at the 100$ level are given access to the back-end developer forum, where the more technical aspects of the game are discussed. This section is also where, depending on your access level, you will be allowed to create and suggest your OWN content ideas for the game for us to discuss.

Side Quests: 150$ Backers are given to ability to create and suggest small side quests to be put in the game.
(For example: Having Four players have to time and correctly shoot 4 targets while falling down a chasm to enable the power-up at the bottom for them to collect)

Map Concepts: 500$ Backers are allowed to suggest  how portions of the in-game world should be designed and handled.
(For example: You think that the Underwater level should have a sunken ship section for exploration)

Enemy Concepts: 1000$ Backers will be allowed to create and suggest an enemy type. Given the enemies in the game (Karr) almost any and all enemy types are viable here.
(For example: You had a nightmare about a giant shark eating lizard mouse? Let's see if we can get him the game then.)

Item/Ability Concepts: 5000$ Backers are also allowed suggest and brainstorm up types of gear and items the players should be allowed to acquire, as well as different abilities to be of use to them.
(For example: Grappling Hooks, Triple Jumping, Throwing Spears, etc)

Story Concepts: (Only Available if Certain Stretch Goals are Made)
Backers will get to work with our Storywriter(s) to brainstorm how the main campaign will unfold. As well as suggest events and plots for the community to discuss on.
(For example: Players crossing a bridge too slow should have it collapse on them and force them to take a detour, including a more difficult boss fight)

Main Character Designs: (Only Available if Certain Stretch Goals are Made) 
Backers will get to work with developers to decide how certain NPC's and story characters will interact with the game.

Game Design Input: (Only Available if Certain Stretch Goals are Made) 
Backers will be able to suggest actual features that they feel should be in the games. The ultimate removal of "I would've done that differently moments"

We feel that this is a perfect way to make sure that once again... you guys get the game that YOU want to play.

Who Are We?

Shonorukyo Tsuno, LLC is merely a small group of gamers trying to follow the dream that every normal gamer has had at some point, to create their own.

While the world is set on trying come up with new styles and genres, we think that a visit to the retro styles that introduced us to the wonder of video games in the first place is in order from time to time.

Which is our original goal with this title, Exodae. Bring some good old fashioned gameplay styles back with a touch of the new modern styles to  what we hope will be a game to remember.

Stretch Goals

With our minimal budget request of 100K, we do realize that the rewards section may seem a bit bland to some of you. That's because we're not asking for a comfortable amount of cash to even quit our day jobs yet. While 300K would be the ideal amount for us and a staff to work full-time on the project, we  realize that an ideal situation isn't always going to be available.

However! If the backers decide it to be so and we reach any of our stretch goals, we have more content and bonuses for everyone! I know we keep repeating this, but its true! You are making sure that you get what YOU want.

Closing

With all the above being said, we thank you sincerely for taking the time to look at our project. We hope that this can be a community project that not just ourselves, BUT EVERYONE can look back on and say "Yes Sir, I was part of that."

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Our largest and most daunting challenge first and foremost is definitely gaining the trust of any would be backers. We're aware that we don't have any large titles under our belts and are relatively young to the entire development scene, but we feel that our biggest driving factor is really well.. just our drive. The fire and desire that only a dedicated youth can have fuels us and we hope that its enough for people to see that we really are ready to create this masterpiece of a project. We've spent the past 2 months shelling out our own money for this project and using our limited free time to create the small bit we have so far, but it could be something fantastic and meaningful if we had the support of other like minded individuals.

With that being said, if we should amazingly even just amass the minimum budget we've asked for, we have a plan to keep the game under the budget while still being able to produce it in the time period we've promised. We've gone over every scenario from just hiring one or two extremely talented full time media designers to making all of the work contracted out and even gone the route of researching the possibility of enlisting the help of talented college students from the local art & design universities of Las Vegas who are looking to expand their portfolio and be part of something big.

But even with all these preparations and planning, we know the largest key challenge will be to finish on time. The biggest chance of this not happening would be us getting carried away in the excitement of embarking on such a great and life changing adventure for all of us that designing this game will be. Through-out the entire process of this, we will be continuing to work our own jobs to help fund the project as best as we can to ensure that any unpredictable hurdles such as loss of a server or other hardware along with important information doesn't happen. That means if we want to fund a back up of all of our files, it'll be paid for by ourselves. We want to make sure that the backers of this project's money goes directly to what they want, development and design of the game.


As for the rewards, we're specifically kept the physical reward amounts to a minimum to along with the minimum budget to ensure that we don't end up spending all of the backer money on rewards. This is just the barebones of the project however, should we reach the ideal funding amount or even greater, we have prepared the additional reward systems for our backers should they decide that they believe in our project as much as we do.

We've also met with colleagues from our own experiences in life, for example our Technical School that two of us attended here in Las Vegas actually does T-Shirt designs and printing for an extremely discounted price for its students and alumni. Thus saving a large host of funds that can again be put forward towards development of the game.

Now aside from all of these operational challenges the ultimate obstacle will be the interaction with all of the backers themselves during development. We've promised them an inside story to the entire process and even input allowed to the more dedicated backers. We're aware that there will be people who feel like they've got the greatest idea ever thought of and feel like it ABSOLUTELY MUST be put in the game and will be disappointed if it doesn't pass, but to avoid direct confrontation, we've set-up a system where all backer development ideas must be approved by their peers before we even begin to look at it. This way, even if the feature or design seems obsolete to us, we will see that it is supported by the people who will play the game. Should the system prove successful, the only thing we'd have to worry about is knowing when the point where "enough is enough" and that trying to cram more content into an already bustling project would be detrimental.

Through all of these preparations we feel that the central point of the project will be achieved and that is allowing everyone to help create what is now THEIR dream too and no longer just OURS.

WHAT IS RUN KITTY RUN?

Loosely based on a fast-paced Warcraft III custom game scenario of the same name, Run Kitty Run is an independent Cocos2D game, currently in development for the iPhone and iPad.

As a semi-top-down 2D running game, where speed and reaction time matters, the basic premise of the game is simple: Kittens must run to the end of each level's maze and dodge the dogs along the way. Use items, scare dogs,turn invisible or better yet,turn into a flaming fur ball! Do what it takes to save time and accomplish each level's goals..foras you progress, paths get tighter, and the dogs get nastier.

Run Kitty Run will be characterized by cute hand-drawn art, colorful environments, playful music andfast-paced singleplayer & multiplayer action that will keep each player on their toes as they work together, or work against each other to reach the goal. It will take queues from some inspirations such as The Legend of Zelda: 4 Swords, Tilt to Live , and Mario Kart.

In-game prototype (meaning non-final graphic. All graphics found here are original creations. Please check the FAQ to learn more about how these graphics came about :) ) Please note that a big part of this Kickstarter campaign is for improving the visual design and animation so all the art isn't final.

HOW DO I ENJOY THE GAME?

CHOOSE YOUR KITTEN

Choose from a number of different kittens with their own special traits and abilities. Traverse the map through a mix of tilt-controls inspired by (and based on) Tilt to Live and quick-access buttons for items and abilities. Watch out as a simple touch from a dog could leave your poor kitten unconscious.

Initial set of hand-drawn sprites. They are fully animated (may lack frames to smoothen out the animation though). Design and names are not final! If you noticed that those last 2 kittens look familiar, please check the FAQ

AVOID THE DOGS
Different kinds of dogs mean different kinds of danger! Run, dodge, and evade through the dozens of patrolling dogs, escape from aggressive dogs who chase you, and pass-by unnoticed through sensor dogs. Dogs will be in varied breeds and behavior through different areas!

UTILIZE ITEMS

Pick up items, visible or hidden, through each level and use them to your advantage. Throw balls, get a burst of speed, be invincible, turn into a ghost, anything can happen!

PLAY ALONE

Play alone and complete the goals in all 50 levels of theCampaign Mode,or play through 10Classic Modelevels and be the fastest kitten in theLeader Boards. Achievements enthusiasts won't be disappointed as there are lots to collect. (note: amount of levels are subject to change based on project success and app store game updates).

PLAY WITH OTHERS

Get a persistent character, level-up and upgrade your traits and abilities and play with others through three different modes. Play together in Cooperative Mode and help each other reach the goal. Save unconscious kittens, or continue to the goal with 1 less kitten. Play in Competitive Mode anduse your abilities and items against each other. Be the first to complete the level's goal in Competitive Race or be the last kitten standing in Competitive Arena.


Illustration of save & be-saved mechanic in Cooperative mode. Kittens who save others in need will be rewarded in their play through.

WHY MAKE IT?

I thoroughly enjoyed my time playing this type of game when I came across it in WC3. Play with a bunch of friends, run through a maze and avoid the dogs, get speed boosts, and if someone goes down by hitting a dog, you choose to risk go back saving him, or just gun for the end without him.

It was good simple fun, certainly worth a few laughs, but I always felt that it could have been better. As much fun as I had back then, it had its down-sides: the visuals weren't suitable, music could be better, too much set up was needed to arrange a game between friends, and there simply wasn't enough features to make people play it again and again.

I believe bringing this type of arcade gameplay from PC to handheld and expanding on it would make for a really fun & entertaining experience. There's a lot that can be done, if time permits, I also want to include a Pac-Man mode in the game since the engine would be perfectly built for it. I hope to make this worth everyone's time; I certainly wouldn't make something I don't wish to enjoy myself, nor make something I wouldn't be proud of making. With all that's mentioned above, I hope to bring this game to life, and in its complete form to the iOS platform.

WHY KICKSTARTER?

Prototype Demo

Preface. Growing up, I have always been interested in being able to make something that people could enjoy. I used to make games on pen and paper and let other kids play them. I want to be able to do the same on the mobile platform and this game will set the pace for it. It's my first game for the iOS and with it, I'm striving to bring the whole experience of the game I planned into the platform. Not a feature less.

Obstacles. I'm a developer with so-so drawing skills and no experience with composing music. I self-funded as much as I can and bought all the development and production tools I needed and decided to start developing the game a few months back. With no money to get someone else on board, I figured that I would just wing it with the art and music when the time came, thinking that I'd be able to provide good art and music with enough hard work and effort.

Alas, working alone on it for 4 months now, I have to face reality and admit that I won't be able to achieve it in a timely manner and with quality, or even not at all. I was drawing at a turtle's pace and it's been taking time better spent on completing the code.

Truthfully, I'm scared of leaving things unfinished and losing all my work when the time comes that I can't support it anymore. I'm very tired, and admittedly, even just a show of community support can help lift my spirits and make me push forward.

Enter Kickstarter. Kickstarter allows me to be free of my own limits in my vision for the game. Funds from Kickstarter will be used to pay for the proper creative professionals I need (mainly a graphics artist and a sound engineer/musician), and possibly fund more production tools to enable the game to reach the planned level of quality.

I need to get a professional who can draw and animate well, to either "fix" or replace my current set of drawings. As you can see throughout the Kickstarter campaign, I am definitely quite lacking in the art department. Other similar campaigns have the luxury of showing beautiful illustrations...having beautiful videos...both of which I can't do so I made do with what I can muster up..but I know its not enough in today's standards. It's really important to me that the game is not only fun to play, but also looks and sounds amazing, but I need your support to achieve this goal.

I've been working with a very talented game music composer, MisfitChris, who will compose tunes for the soundtrack of the game. Along with him, 2 other composers, Oxygenstar and Disasterpeace could be included in the project as well. I urge you to check out their work, they're simply amazing. Your support will make sure that the soundtrack is of the best quality upon release.

Any funds raised over the goal will allow me to get another developer on board and be able to increase the production value of the game beyond initially planned (more levels, more items, more characters, and hopefully even an android port) and to polish and optimize the code (I want it to perform well during times the screen is filled with players and enemies)

I hope that as a (potential) supporter, you're as excited for this as I am! Huge thanks for reading until the end! Please read the FAQ for more detailed information. I purposely didn't cover everything in detail as it might be too long. If you still have some questions, feel free to ask them below :)

Cheers!

Allen

  • To which games can I compare this to?

    Ahthis is quite hard to answer. It does take to the Run Kitty Run game in WC3, but only in mechanic, and only in cooperative mode. The visuals, sounds, controls, and other game modes are totally different. It is influenced by different games as well: Zelda: 4 Swords in the way that it is about multiple players who are in coopetition (cooperative competition), Tilt to Live wherein you have to react fast in order to survive and last longer, Mario Kart wherein you race with others, with boxes that give you random power-ups (getting a box starts a roulette for selecting the power-up, and possibility is affected by commonness: more common power-ups appear more often).

    Last updated: Thu, Nov 17 2011 12:09 pm EST
  • Can you explain the game modes (more) clearly?

    2 Singleplayer modes:

    Campaign mode composed of mini-objectives and goals per level that you have to achieve with efficiency in order to get the best possible score. Its for people who want something to complete, like getting a perfect score in each level in games such as Cut the Rope. Most of the achievements can be unlocked in this mode, and some achievements unlock in-game content as well, so finishing the campaign mode is a must.

    Classic mode composed of big levels & mazes where you have to reach the end as fast as you can without dying too much. You have to get through the maze in a couple of passes, and each pass gives you harder and varied enemies. Its for people who like time trials and dont mind playing alone. Players can compare best times through online leaderboards per maze.

    3 Multiplayer modes:

    Cooperative mode Its basically classic mode with other people. Completing levels in this mode means that you have to cooperate to survive. Hitting a dog leaves you unconscious, while touching an unconscious kitten revives them.

    Competitive race Almost same as cooperative mode, but with friendly-fire turned on, and theres no need to save other kittens, but rather, it would be to your advantage to cause them to faint somehow. Push them towards enemies or use power-ups directly against them. This is a race to the finish.

    Competitive arena This is a death-match arena where you use your abilities and random power-ups to cause each other to faint. Point-based or life-based last-man-standing.

    In all multiplayer modes, you get a persistent version of each character you use. Participating in game mode objectives and completing the game modes earn you points that help you improve your character so they are naturally better, or their ability is better.

    Last updated: Thu, Nov 17 2011 12:09 pm EST
  • What are the controls for the game?

    For now, you control the game with Tilt and Buttons. The tilt controls are similar to the very tight tilt controls of Tilt to Live and is calibrate-able so the player has full control of their movement. Tilt gives full reflex control to the player, and this is very crucial to the game. It also allows you to see more of the game; you wouldn't want to die because your finger was blocking your view of a dog right? Buttons would be for interacting with the environment and using items & abilitie and are designed for quick-tap access

    Last updated: Thu, Nov 17 2011 12:09 pm EST

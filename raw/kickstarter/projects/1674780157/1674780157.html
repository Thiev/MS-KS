UPDATE #1 - The first one

Unbreakable: Scorched Earth is the first PC game from Labyrinth Interactive, an equally brand new company from Stafford. We're all keen gamers and have been waiting for an opportunity to make a game to be proud of for years, and that opportunity is here right now.

We began production three weeks ago and have already created a lot of the assets and script that we had planned for the first release. However, we are limited by the amount of money we have, and as a result there are a lot of problems that we have had to overcome by using software we've had to learn on the spot, meaning that we can't bring it to the same quality as planned.

The money you pledge to us will be put toward a number of things - we would like to present our game to the Steam Greenlight, which will help us to sell Unbreakable to people who may never have heard of it. Software-wise, we need the money for Z-Brush (used for sculpting that would allow us to create better quality characters) and Flash, which would give us the room to create a professional looking menu and HUD (heads up display). Finally, with five programmers all working on code for the same game, we've been using a version control system to make sure everything works as soon as it is included into the engine. However, due to the amount of work we have already put in, as well as backups in case something goes wrong, we have need of another internal hard drive, which will allow us to continue working on bigger and more complex code for the final product.

With all this, we would have the chance to build something truly unique, to rival all other indie game companies. We want to make a great first impression, and with your help we'll be able to do this. Thank you for your time, and we hope you can help!

And now for a little information into the game itself...

Unbreakable: Scorched Earth is set on the fictional continent of Arthos, a large stretch of land and sea surrounded by the Maelstrom Belt to the south and impassable mountains stretched across the rest. Man and elf co-existed peacefully for generations, each committing their best works to the others for the sake of peace and prosperity throughout Arthos.  

But a darkness has swept over the valleys in the far east, engulfing the elven kingdom in flame.  

The Dracorians, a race of dragon tamers, have started a war that threatens to cover the continent in the ash of the fallen. The elves, their vast expanse of land nothing but charred remains, have taken a stand in their last remaining city of Sylva. To prevent the advance of the Dracorian horde, three towers have been raised, creating a barrier that protects the rest of Arthos from harm; one in the Elven city, one in the northern fortress of Ellental, and one on the coast to the south. Each tower harbors a crystal, and while all three remain intact the Dracorians are unable to send their army in its entirety. Unfortunately, the barrier is not impenetrable. Each tower requires protection against the still formidable force that threatens them, forcing the elves to call on the race of Men to aid them.  

As the Dracorians rally for one final assault, man and elf stand together to prepare for a battle that will determine whether Arthos braves the storm, or suffers the same fate as the east.

GAMEPLAY

Unbreakable is a third person hack and slasher with a difference. We’re combining real-time strategy and role playing elements in order to create an experience that allows you to play the way you want. 

The aim of the game is simple; do whatever you can to protect a Runestone in each stronghold (starting with Ellental’s Fortress this December). Buy and place units to defend your position, construct towers to help you on the battlefield or simply wade into the fray to utilise the meaty combat that each character is capable of. Invite up to three friends to assist your plight, or play alone. It’s all up to you.

Our goal is to create and maintain an experience like no other, and to do this we're going to be releasing the game in three installments - the first will include our first character, Felicia, the Runekeeper, as well as our first stronghold, Ellental's Fortress. Over the year we will be adding to the content, with another three characters currently planned (all with different playstyles), and two extra maps with unique mechanics. We'll also be adding to the existing maps with each update - the hub world will become overburdened as the storyline progresses to give the impression that the world is still moving around you, and a lot can happen in a few months! 

Felicia: The Runekeeper

Years before the Dracorian invasion, a young girl visited Sylva with dreams of harnessing the earthern powers that Elves are renowned for possessing. Her human roots caused a stir amongst the elders of the art; with the exception of one, who witnessed her potential one day as she practiced. Felicia has an uncanny connection to the earth, something that came about after years of obsession in the art. Now a young woman, Felicia has mastered the use of protective magic. When Sylva became the final bastion of the Elves, she used her abilities to protect those seeking refuge behind its walls, gaining the respect of the many who deemed her unworthy of her gifts. Nicknamed the Runekeeper by her peers, Felicia has taken to travelling between the three Strongholds in a bid to assist wherever possible, keeping the very real threat of the Dracorians at bay. After hearing of an impending threat to the mountain fortress of Ellental, Felicia has made her way there and is preparing for the first real challenge of her life. With little time before an attack, she sleeps lightly and practices often, consulting the scrolls given to her by the Sylvan Elves to enhance her understanding of the threat.

Ellental's Fortress - The Northern Pass Stronghold

The mountains in the north are a perilous labyrinth of steep drops and enclosed spaces. Murderous wildlife have reduced once thriving settlements to ghost towns, feeding on the bodies of adventurers unwittingly travelling through. Ellental sits defiantly in the centre of the rocky maze, scars etched into the cobblestone to remind its inhabitants of the dangers outside the walls. For years it has been a sanctuary for the lost, the welcoming and proud community within dedicated to the protection of those who need it. Men and women born in Ellental are difficult to provoke, but once angered they are formidable warriors; the harsh surroundings have given mountain-folk skin as thick as the walls surrounding the city, coupled with the strength to level small buildings. As a result, the Elves chose to share the construction of a stronghold with them – with Ellental’s defensive capabilities and the Elves’ intuition, the Fortress is the strongest of the three and stands ready to halt the Dracorian advance.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

There will always be huge risks when working on a project of this size; in some cases we have already succumbed to these. But we have overcome things like the LAN capabilities failing, and one piece of code messing up the version control, each time managing to come back from it. As a result, we are confident that we can power through any problems that may come our way.

First of all, because of the business model we are using, we never plan to release more than we know we can handle. As it stands, we are ahead of schedule, which means that we can spend a lot of time near the end polishing Unbreakable and even adding mechanics and models in that were not planned for. This time also gives us chance to fix any problems we run into with good time to do so, meaning that we will never release an unfinished product.

If anything DOES happen that we cannot fix before the first release date, we will first determine whether it is of paramount importance or not. If it is not, the code/asset won't be included in that release, instead being worked on and sent out in a later version/patch. If it is important however, all our work will go into working out the problem and fixing it, and a worst case scenario may lead to a slightly later release date.

We will never release a game that we do not believe is ready, and will always try to focus test every aspect to a fault.

FAQ

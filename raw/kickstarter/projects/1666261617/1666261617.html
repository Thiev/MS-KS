Play@EDU will create three cross-platform video games to teach college courses using enthralling storylines and awesome gameplay.

Play@EDU is a startup educational technology company in the Atlanta metro area whose goal is to develop and publish mobile educational video games. Our company was founded by a small group of college teachers who desired to maximize the learning benefits of educational technology in the their courses. Technology is already everywhere, and our philosophy is to utilize it instead of banning it from the classroom. We saw an unmet need in colleges and universities for pairing high quality mobile video games with course content in order to increase student success. Research shows gameplay can increase memory, critical thinking, and, of course, motivation to learn (read the M.I.T. whitepaper here). Higher Education needs polished video games that have deep learning integration, can send student learning analytics to the instructor, and that can be used inside and outside of class whenever the student wishes to learn. Play@EDU is the solution to all three of these areas of need. We make fun games. It just so happens that players will learn something in the process!   

Our team of artists, designers, programmers and learning science specialists have decades of combined industry experience. In addition to our talented in-house team, our studio will collaborate with well-established international studios as well. Some of these studios include Monkeypuzzle Games, Riffel Interactive Media, and Gskinner, one of the most capable interactive development studios in North America. These firms have the kind of backend expertise needed for our very unique project. While our main focus is on the mobile platform, iOS and Android, we will also offer our games via web browser.

We are passionate about exciting video games with compelling story-lines. Please join in this project with us! 

Our project is ambitious, but it is completely doable with your backing. Over the course of the next twelve months, play@EDU will make three mobile video games for core college courses, as well several casual games for quick practice (play one below). Through collaboration between our team of creatives and designers, college faculty and students, and learning science specialists, we will build the next level of educational technology.

Who is this project for? While our games are geared towards traditional or non-traditional college students, anyone will enjoy playing our games because the learning is woven into the gameplay. Also, teachers or institutions who wish to enhance the learning in their courses should be interested in backing our project. Watch the video below to see real students learning using video games, such as Civilization. 

Anyone will be able to download our games from the various app stores and play them on their devices. However, our games are unique in that they will be able to send analytics to participating college and university's learning management systems, such as Blackboard, Moodle, Canvas, or Desire2learn. Teachers will then be able see how the games are helping their students learn in the course whether in-class or outside of class. Our games will not replace excellent teachers; they will serve as learning tools for innovative 21st century educators who want to step out and try new approaches to delivering course content and enhancing critical thinking skills. However, you do not need to be a student to get addicted to our games!     

Kickstarter backing is essential in order to accomplish this project. Due to the required educational specialization and backend customization for this particular project, crowd funding is the best way to maintain creative freedom over our games instead of going the traditional investor route.     

The project rewards timelines are located in right sidebar below, but rewards will start shipping in July 2014. All of the money you contribute in backing will go straight to project production. Our project budget does not pay salaries, but money will go towards paying project team members for work contributed to the project and, of course, all of the terrific rewards for our Kickstarter backers. Back us today and get unique rewards exclusive to our Kickstarter campaign. 

Play@EDU will start off with three fantastic mobile video games that can be accessed on Android, iOS, and web browser. The goals and gameplay of all three games will be based on three core college courses that would be typical for a Freshmen year: Biology I, Algebra I, and Introduction to Psychology. 

Man of Earth

Synopsis: The world is a war-zone between the mechanical and the natural. As The Scientist, you must protect that which matters the most to you, both as a man of science and as a family man. The struggle for balance pushes you into choices and actions that will result in extreme consequences. Circumstances force you to choose between your ideology and your relationships. 

Gameplay: In this action adventure RPG, Man of Earth utilizes basic concepts of Biology, as well as principles of Ecology and Botany, to place you in a world of chaos. You must use your knowledge to perform experiments, as well as use clues and journals to uncover the mysterious life of The Scientist in order to progress.

Shudder

Synopsis: A twist on the classic murder mystery, Shudder places you, a psychologist, at the center of a swanky dinner party gone awry where you must distinguish the murderer from the victims in a timely fashion to save as many lives as you can. Can you read and diagnose the other partygoers and locate the psychotic murderer before they’re killed off one by one? All is not as it seems in Shudder. In the end, it is not only the sanity of others that you will be evaluating! You’d do best to catch on quickly!

Gameplay: In this strategy RPG mystery game, you play Alex, a psychologist who must analyze and evaluate the other remaining dinner guests. Alex will be forced to unravel the mysteries of each individual's psyche using clues gathered from the house and from individual discussion. As truths are revealed, artifacts and links are added to the psychological bridge work.

The Multi

Synopsis: There exists in the shadows a secret society that spans the time of humankind, yet cannot be found in any history book. Who are the Multi and what is their mission? As young archaeologist Rachel awakens from slumber and finds herself lost among ancient ruins, she asks these very questions. But she’ll get much more than the answers she seeks. Use clues and solve puzzles to figure out what’s going on and discover an entirely new world, as well as an entirely new you along the way. Enter the world of The Multi! 

Gameplay: In this platform game, you play as Rachel navigating through the mysterious ancient ruins. The Multi is puzzle heavy and relies on acquiring artifacts, utilizing formulas, slaying adversaries, and outwitting traps in order to conquer levels.

Dr. Daniel Haynes, Ph.D. is the CEO and Co-founder of play@EDU. He will be the acting Executive Producer and will oversee the day-to-day project management tasks of all aspects of the game development cycle. Daniel earned his Ph.D. in Religion from the University of Nottingham in the United Kingdom in 2011, and he has taught undergraduate and graduate Philosophy and Religion courses at the university level for almost eight years. Daniel also has a background in Information Technology and Web Design, which led to his work as a Technology Enhanced Learning Coordinator at Georgia Gwinnett College in the Atlanta area. He has a passion to better connect the current digital generation of students with educational technology, such as awesome mobile video games!

Michael Parker is a Co-founder and Vice-President of Game Design at play@EDU. He will manage the creative team of Game Designers, Writers, and Graphic Artists. Michael is a lifetime gamer with interests in multiple game genres. Writing and designing enthralling games is his passion in life. Over the last couple of decades it seems like the quality of many mainstream games has gone down. One way Michael wants to counter this trend is to write fun and challenging story lines with rich and solid game design. He has concepts and designs for numerous games.

Dr. Roy Marquez, Ph.D. is an Educational Technology Consultant at play@EDU focusing on how to enhance learning through gameplay. He will work hands-on with our game design team to weave solid learning principles into gameplay. Also, he will coordinate with our faculty fellows to bring essential course content to each game. Roy earned his doctorate in Instructional Technology from the University of Central Florida. He has worked most recently at Eastern New Mexico University, and prior to that, he was at the University of Central Florida in the capacity of Technology Specialist for the College of Education and the Center for Research in Education, Arts, Technology and Entertainment. Roy also has worked in the public school system as well as in the corporate sector with several educational technology companies. His current interests include looking at the relationship between the choice of instructional technology and media, assistive technology, and student achievement, particularly using the medium of video games.

Dr. Adam Hutcheson, Ph.D. is an Experimental Psychologist at play@EDU and an Assistant Professor of Psychology at Georgia Gwinnett College in the Atlanta area. His work at play@EDU will focus on the effects of video games on the mind from a learning perspective. His insights into the psychology of learning will be critical as our design team crafts gameplay for each of our games. After receiving his bachelor of arts degree from the University of Alabama in 2002, Adam went to graduate school at the University of South Carolina and obtained a Ph.D. in the field of Experimental (Cognitive) Psychology. His research includes spatial abilities, human memory processes and human interaction with new technology, such as video games, GPS and augmented reality.

Marcos is a native Brazilian, owner of Riffel Interactive Media, and has worked for 20 years with TV motion graphics, art and animation. He has been a game creator since 2000 (web games, flash games, mobile games) with 20+ apps published in the Apple Appstore. Several of his games have ranked #1 in the Appstore in Brazil and abroad.

Also, they will help debug our code and perform other quality control functions in the development cycle. Gskinner is an internationally recognized developer of rich interactive software for large corporate clients and leading agencies deployed on the web, desktop, mobile / devices and installation. Led by CEO and Chief Architect Grant Skinner, they have developed successful solutions for Adobe, Microsoft, Disney, Coca Cola, Nike, and others. They will assist with specialized aspects of the project as well as provide quality control. Their specialties are: Flash / Flex / AIR / iOS / Android / HTML5 development, usability & consulting. Rich applications, games, interactive experiences, installations, mobile & devices.

MonkeyPuzzleGames is a mobile game development studio and will be doing the bulk of our video game programming. They have been building websites and creating Flash projects since 1999. Their lead programmer is a published author and former finalist in the Flash film festival. MonkeyPuzzleGames has completed projects for DreamWorks, Epson, Motorola, Channel 4 TV and a host of other well known brands. They were also asked to create a series of 8 fun, mini-games to complement an online course and help to reinforce certain key points that the students would be tested on. 

Along with the games, they created a main character and a story to join the 8 games together. The result was these 8 Flash games, which utilized external XML files to store the questions that would be asked within the game. Not only did the students have to complete the game, but they had to use information from the course to complete tasks in the correct order. At the end of the game, they received a summary based on how many of the key points they successfully remembered.

Risks and challenges Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

Every project has risks and challenges, particularly when the little guy is trying to get a quality product out to the public, but obstacles can always be overcome with the right mindset and a dedicated team. Play@EDU has both the right mindset and a world-class team. Since we are combining game development and publishing on this project, the challenge will be to make sure the design team, our consultants, and the developers all work in harmony and on schedule. Our budget is lean, but we have included a standard buffer to make sure we can handle any surprises. Since we are paying for contract hourly work instead of salaries, there will be more accountability built into the project timeline.

On the development side of the project, GSkinner, Monkeypuzzle Games and Riffel Interactive Media all have a solid record of top quality and efficient game development. Our project tracking software and a state-of-the-art video conferencing and collaborative office space will streamline project communication and the whole game development cycle.

Slightly more challenging will be to create quality graphics that are also light enough to perform well on the mobile platform, particularly since we will use a HTML 5 solution instead of the easier and faster flash platform, which is slowly dying out in official support. Using vector formatted graphics and leaning on the experience of our developer teams will help navigate us through to the next level of game creation using HTML 5.

Finally, the biggest challenge will be to create fun games that have learning woven into the very fabric of the game instead of an add-on to the gameplay that is prevalent in many "educational" video games that are available on the market. This is why play@EDU has brought on board two experts in the field of learning science and video games, as well as partnering with three college professors with over 50 years of combined teaching experience. The game designs of Shudder, The Multi and Man of Earth will be created from scratch in conjunction with these specialists. The risk is that we will hit road blocks in the design process, but our working experience with the learning science specialists thus far has presented innovative methods and perspectives to address game design issues. We anticipate only forward movement once we have Kickstarter backing to increase the level of consultation.

Most importantly, we will be utilizing student input throughout the development cycle in order to address quality control issues before they become an issue and set back the production schedule. Play@EDU has completed extensive planning with our developer teams in order to establish the current project timeline of twelve months plus three months of testing. Of course, any setbacks in the project will be communicated with our Kickstarter backers.
